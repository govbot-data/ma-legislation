40 SECTION 8. Chapter 190B, as so appearing, is hereby amended by inserting after section
41 5-507 the following sections:-
42 Section 5-601. Definitions:
43 As used in this section, the following words shall have the following meaning
44 unless the context clearly requires otherwise:
45 “Adult”, an individual 18 years of age or older.
46 “Coercion”, the use of force or threats to persuade someone to do something.
47 “Decision-maker”, an adult who seeks to execute, or has executed, a supported
48 decision-making agreement with 1 or more supporters under this chapter.
49 “Executed”, a supported decision-making agreement that is signed by both the
50 decision maker and all supporters in accordance with requirements set forth in this chapter.
51 “Supported decision-making”, the process of supporting and accommodating the
52 decision-maker, without impeding the self-determination of the decision-maker, in making life
53 decisions, including, but not limited to: (i) decisions related to where the decision-maker wants
54 to live; (ii) the services, supports, financial decisions and medical care the decision-maker wants
3 of 14

55 to receive; (iii) whom the decision-maker wants to live with; and (iv) where the decision-maker
56 wants to work.
57 “Supported decision-making agreement”, a voluntary, written agreement, written in
58 plain language that is accessible and understood by the decision-maker and entered into by the
59 decision-maker with one or more supporters pursuant to section 5-602 used to support decision-
60 making.
61 “Supporter”, an adult who meets the requirements of section 5-602 and has
62 executed a supported decision-making agreement with a decision-maker.
63 Section 5-602
64 (a) A decision-maker may voluntarily enter into a supported decision-making agreement
65 with 1 or more supporters.
66 (b) The decision-maker may amend or terminate a supported decision-making agreement
67 at any time pursuant to section 5-602(3)(e).
68 (1) Any person, eighteen years of age or older, may be a supporter.
69 (2) A person shall not serve as a supporter in a supported decision-making
70 agreement when:
71 (i) The decision-maker previously made, or makes, an allegation of elder abuse
72 against the supporter.
4 of 14

73 (ii) The decision-maker has obtained or obtains a Protection from Abuse Order
74 against the supporter or a harassment prevention order against the supporter under chapter 258 of
75 the General Laws.
76 (iii) The supporter is the subject of a civil or criminal order prohibiting contact
77 with the decision-maker;
78 (iv) The supporter has been removed as the conservator of the decision-maker,
79 based upon a finding that they did not act in the conservatee’s best interest; or
80 (v) The supporter is found criminally, civilly, or administratively liable for abuse,
81 neglect, mistreatment, coercion, or fraud.
82 (3)(a) Except as limited by a supported decision-making agreement, a supporter
83 shall do all the following:
84 (i) Respect the values, beliefs, and preferences of the decision-maker.
85 (ii) Act honestly, diligently, and in good faith.
86 (iii) Act within the scope identified by the decision-maker.
87 (iv) Support and implement the direction, will, and preferences of the decision-
88 maker.
89 (v) Maintain confidentiality of any information obtained by a supporter, unless the
90 decision-maker specifically authorizes its disclosure.
91 (vi) Exercise only the authority granted to the supporter in the supported decision-
92 making agreement.
5 of 14

93 (b) Except as limited by a supported decision-making agreement, a supporter
94 may, with the consent of the decision-maker, provide to the decision-maker decision-making
95 assistance regarding the decision-maker’s affairs, including, but not limited to: (i)
96 communicating decisions and understanding information about, options for, the responsibilities
97 of and the consequences of decisions; (ii) accessing, obtaining and understanding information
98 that is relevant to decisions, necessary for the decision-maker to manage their affairs, including,
99 but not limited to, medical records, including protected health information under the Health
100 Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (Pub. L. 104-191); psychological records;
101 financial records to the extent provided in Subsection (c); educational records under the Family
102 Education Rights and Privacy Act of 1974 (20 U.S.C. s. 1232g), or information protected by 42
103 U.S.C.A. s. 290dd-2, 4 C.F.R. Part 2; as well as other educational records including
104 Individualized Education Programs, transcripts, a transition plan, a student summary of
105 performance and any other materials as requested; (iii) ascertaining the wishes and decisions of
106 the decision-maker, assisting in communicating those wishes and decisions to other persons and
107 assisting to ensure the decision-maker’ wishes and decisions are implemented; and (iv)
108 accompanying the decision-maker and participating in discussions with other persons when the
109 decision-maker is making decisions or attempting to obtain information needed to make
110 decisions.
111 (c) A limited power of attorney is required for a supporter(s) to access financial
112 records, including bank records of the decision-maker without the decision-maker. A supporter
113 may accompany a decision-maker and participate in discussions with banking personnel when
114 the decision-maker is making decisions or attempting to obtain banking related information
115 needed to make decisions.
6 of 14

116 (d) Unless the supporter has a valid legal authorization to do so and the action is
117 within the scope of their authority, a supporter shall not do either of the following: (i) make
118 decisions for, or on behalf of, the decision-maker, or (ii) sign documents on behalf of the
119 decision-maker.
120 (e) A supporter shall not participate in any life decision in which they have a conflict of
121 interest. This includes, but is not limited to, any decision in which the supporter, his or her
122 immediate family or partner, a business organization in which he or she is serving as officer,
123 director, trustee, partner or employee has a financial interest or other direct and substantial
124 interest in the outcome.
125 (f) A supporter shall only be authorized to assist the decision-maker in accessing,
126 collecting or obtaining information that is relevant to a decision authorized under the supported
127 decision-making agreement and to which the decision-maker agrees that the supporter should
128 have access pursuant to a written authorization in accordance with the applicable federal or state
129 privacy laws. The decision-maker may withdraw the authorization at any time in whatever
130 manner is authorized by applicable state or federal law. A supporter shall keep confidential any
131 information obtained in the process of assisting the decision-maker.
132 (g) A decision-maker may bring a civil action for damages and equitable relief,
133 including injunctive relief, resulting from a violation of this section or a regulation promulgated
134 under this section in any court of competent jurisdiction.
135 (1) The existence of a supported decision-making agreement shall not preclude a
136 decision-maker from seeking personal information without the assistance of a supporter.
7 of 14

137 (2) To be valid, a supported decision-making agreement shall be signed and dated
138 by the decision-maker and each applicable supporter in the presence of a notary public. The
139 decision-maker may use reasonable modifications, such as assistive technology or physical
140 assistance, to sign the agreement.
141 (3) Evidence of undue influence or coercion in the creation or signing of a
142 supported decision-making agreement shall render the supported decision-making agreement
143 invalid.
144 (4) A supported decision-making agreement shall be personalized by the decision-
145 maker to reflect the decision-maker’s personal circumstances. A supported decision-making
146 agreement shall:
147 (i) be in writing in plain language that is accessible and understood by the
148 decision-maker;
149 (ii) identify the decision-maker and all supporters;
150 (iii) describe the types of decisions with which each supporter shall assist the
151 decision-maker. If the decision-maker wants assistance to access their medical records, the
152 agreement must specifically reference that the supporter shall have access to protected health
153 information under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (Pub. L. 104-
154 191). If the decision-maker wants assistance to access their education records, the agreement
155 must specifically reference that the supporter shall have access to protected education records
156 under the Family Education Rights and Privacy Act of 1974 (20 U.S.C. s. 1232g) or information
157 protected by 42 U.S.C. a. s. 290dd-2, 4 C.F.R. Part2; as well as other education records including
8 of 14

158 Individualized Education Programs, transcripts, a transition plan, a student summary of
159 performance and any other materials;
160 (iv) indicate that all supporters agree to assist the decision-maker in making such
161 decisions, to respect the decision maker's decisions and to assist the decision-maker in
162 communicating such decisions;
163 (v) state that supporters shall not make decisions for the decision-maker;
164 (vi) indicate that the decision-maker may amend or terminate the supported
165 decision-making agreement at any time and for any reason subject to the requirements of section
166 5-602(3)(e);
167 (vii) list contact information for the disabled persons protection commission, the
168 elder abuse hotline and all programs providing services to the decision-maker; and
169 (viii) include a statement from each supporter that they understand and accept
170 their roles, responsibilities and limitations as outlined in section 5-602(3)(a).
171 (5) A person who receives the original or a copy of a supported decision-making
172 agreement shall rely on the agreement and recognize a decision, request or communication made
173 with the decision-making assistance of a supporter as the decision, request or communication of
174 the decision-maker. A written confirmation from the decision-maker may be required affirming
175 that the supported decision-making agreement presented is current.
176 (6) A person or entity that, in good faith, acts in reliance on a decision made
177 pursuant to a supported decision-making agreement shall not be subject to civil or criminal
178 liability or to professional discipline.

179 (7)(a) The supported decision-making agreement shall remain in effect until it is
180 terminated or expires.
181 (b) A supportive decision-making agreement shall be terminated as follows:
182 (i) On any termination date set forth in the agreement but no more than a term of
183 three years, unless extended by agreement, in writing, of the decision-maker and supporters,
184 pursuant to section 5-602 with terms of execution.
185 (ii) A supporter may terminate participation in a supported decision-making
186 agreement at any time, by written notice to the decision-maker and any other supporters to the
187 agreement; provided, however, that if the agreement has more than 1 supporter, the agreement
188 shall remain valid as to all other supporters.
189 (iii) A decision-maker may terminate a supported decision-making agreement at
190 any time by written notice to all supporters to the agreement.
191 (iv) Notice of the suspension of a supporter pursuant to section 5-602 shall be
192 given in writing and delivered in hand to said person or his or her attorney or sent by registered
193 mail to said person at his or her residence or his place of business. Such notice so given,
194 delivered or sent shall automatically suspend the authority of such person to perform the duties
195 of his or her office or employment until he or she is notified in like manner that his or her
196 suspension is removed.
197 (v) The death or incapacitation of the decision-maker.
198 (vi) In the event that the decision-maker becomes subject to a guardianship and
199 conservatorship order, the scope of the supported decision-making agreement may be limited in
10 of 14

200 whole or in part by the probate and family court to those decisions reserved for the decision-
201 maker after the issuance of the guardianship or conservatorship order.
202 (8)(a) The disabled persons protection commission, an elder protective services
203 agency, the department of developmental services, the department of mental health or any person
204 interested in the decision-maker's welfare may petition the probate and family court to revoke or
205 suspend the role of the supporter within one or more supported decision-making agreements on
206 the grounds of abuse, neglect, exploitation or failure of the supporter to follow their roles and
207 responsibilities resulting in serious physical, emotional, or financial harm to the decision-maker,
208 or on the grounds that the supporter is not qualified pursuant to section 5-602(2)(b). The court
209 shall provide notice to the decision-maker and supporter and provide an opportunity for the
210 decision-maker and supporter to be heard. The court shall revoke or suspend the role of the
211 supporter within the supported decision-making agreement, in whole or in part, if the court
212 makes a finding under any grounds set forth in this paragraph. The court shall not order a
213 supported decision-making agreement to remain in effect over the objection of the decision-
214 maker. If the decision-maker is indigent, the court shall appoint counsel for the decision-maker
215 upon the filing of any petition under this paragraph.
216 The court shall tally the number of individuals who appear before the court on
217 supported decision-making agreement issues. A report of the court’s findings shall be submitted
218 annually, as of June 30, to the clerks of the senate and house of representatives, the joint
219 committee on committee on children, families and persons with disabilities and the senate house
220 committees on ways and means.
