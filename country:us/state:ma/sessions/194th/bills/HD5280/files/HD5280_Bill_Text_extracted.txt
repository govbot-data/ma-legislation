ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT INCENTIVE PROGRAM
About the Program
EDIP aims to help Massachusetts become the top choice for businesses when deciding where to grow.
The program fosters the creation of quality full-time jobs in every corner of the Commonwealth by
responsibly awarding attractive tax credits to businesses committed to expanding in Massachusetts.
EDIP offers state tax credits in support of businesses investing in capital projects that create jobs
throughout the Commonwealth. EDIP tax credits are also offered as an incentive to companies that
relocate operations to Massachusetts from other states, and in some limited cases, to companies that
maintain operations in Massachusetts instead of moving jobs out of the Commonwealth.
EDIP Reforms
The Mass Leads Act, signed into law on November 20, 2024, included significant reforms to EDIP that,
along with administrative improvements implemented by MOBD, have already made the program more
efficient, attractive, and effective. These reforms included:
• Expanding refundable tax credit availability to broaden the pool of applicants able to take
advantage of the tax credits.
• Allowing companies to claim credits against their entire tax liability in a given year.
• Allowing companies to claim EDIP tax credits in tandem with the 3% manufacturers investment
tax credit.
• Increasing flexibility by decoupling local incentives from EDIP tax credits.
• Developing clear eligibility and evaluation criteria to increase transparency.
• Consolidating a lengthy, multi-phase application process into a single application with 60% fewer
questions.

LOCAL ONLY INCENTIVES
About Local Only Incentive Projects
The Economic Assistance Coordinating Council (EACC) is required to approve Tax Increment Financing
(TIF) and Special Tax Assessment (STA) agreements executed prior to the effective date of the Mass Leads
Act (November 20, 2024). During Fiscal Year 2025 (between July 1, 2024 and November 20, 2024), the
EACC approved four local tax incentive agreements between a municipality and a company committing
to make investments and grow jobs in that city or town. Two of the four projects (50%) were located in
Gateway Cities. Although the EACC no longer approves local tax incentive agreements, MOBD continues
to provide technical assistance to municipalities seeking to support local economic development
through local tax incentives.

MASSACHUSETTS VACANT STOREFRONT PROGRAM
About the Program
The Massachusetts Vacant Storefront Program aids municipalities in their efforts to revitalize their
downtown and commercial areas by encouraging the occupancy of long-term vacant storefronts. The
program allows municipalities to use refundable tax credits as a tool to attract businesses to prominent
vacancies within a community’s downtown and/or commercial area.
Vacant Storefront Program Reforms
This year, the Vacant Storefront Program has also undergone significant reforms. The most significant
change is the introduction of an annual municipal allocation of Vacant Storefront Program tax credits and
the elimination of Vacant Storefront Districts. Municipalities must now apply annually for an allocation of
tax credits that can be used to support one or more Vacant Storefront Projects within the identified area
in the community. This process replaces the Vacant Storefront District. Other noteworthy reforms
include:
• Reduced the required vacancy period from 12 months to 6 months
• Eliminated the strict 50/50 match requirement from municipalities
• Increases flexibility of tax credit awards to exceed $10,000 per project
• Expiration of tax credits if not used during the year awarded
