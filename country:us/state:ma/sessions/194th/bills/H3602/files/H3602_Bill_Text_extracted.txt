HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 3602
By Representative Worrell of Boston, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3602) of
Christopher J. Worrell for an investigation by a special commission (including members of the
General Court) relative to establishing a statewide Massachusetts Caribbean American cultural
center in the city of Boston. Tourism, Arts and Cultural Development.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act creating a commission establishing a Massachusetts Caribbean American Cultural
Center.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. (a) There shall be a special legislative commission established pursuant to
2 section 2A of chapter 4 of the General Laws to investigate and study the feasibility of
3 establishing a statewide Massachusetts Caribbean American Cultural Center in the City of
4 Boston.
5 (b) The commission shall investigate and study various methods for establishing a
6 Massachusetts Caribbean American Cultural Center in the City of Boston, including, but not
7 limited to, the creation of a nonprofit organization to oversee the construction and management
8 of the Massachusetts Caribbean American Cultural Center. The commission shall consider the
9 representation of the over 300-year history of Caribbeans in the Commonwealth of
10 Massachusetts and shall evaluate the costs associated with each method.
11 SECTION 2. (a) The commission shall be composed of the following members:
1 of 2

12 (1) three members of the House of Representatives, three of whom shall be of Caribbean
13 descent or represent a district with a significant population of Caribbean people, two of whom
14 shall be appointed by the Speaker of the House of Representatives, and one of whom shall be
15 appointed by the House Minority Leader;
16 (2) three members of the Senate, three of whom shall be of Caribbean descent or
17 represent a district with a significant population of Caribbean people, two of whom shall be
18 appointed by the Senate President, and one of whom shall be appointed by the Senate Minority
19 Leader;
20 (3) one person to be appointed by the Mayor of the City of Boston;
21 (4) one person to be appointed by the Governor; and
22 (5) not more than fifteen additional members from across the Commonwealth of
23 Massachusetts, whose appointments shall be considered and approved by three members of the
24 House of Representatives and three members of the Senate. The commission shall elect two co-
25 chairs from among its members.
26 (b) All appointments to the commission shall be made no later than June 30, 2026.
27 SECTION 3. The commission shall submit its findings, including any recommendations
28 for legislation, to the clerks of the House of Representatives and the Senate no later than
29 December 31, 2026.
