HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, September 25, 2025.
The committee on the Judiciary, to whom were referred the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 1580) of Jennifer Balinsky Armini,

abuse of minors; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1732) of
Kenneth I. Gordon and Natalie M. Blais relative to the statute of

governmental and charitable tort liability; and the petition (accompanied
by bill, House, No. 1833) of John J. Lawn, Jr., relative to remedies for
survivors of childhood sexual abuse, reports recommending that the
accompanying bill (House, No. 4539) ought to pass.

An Act enhancing remedies for survivors of childhood sexual abuse.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 4C of chapter 260 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by striking out the first paragraph and inserting in place
3 thereof the following paragraph:-
4 Civil actions alleging a defendant sexually abused a minor may be commenced at any
5 time after the acts alleged to have caused an injury or condition occurred.
6 SECTION 2. Said chapter 260 is hereby amended by striking out section 4C 1/2 and
7 inserting in place thereof the following section:-
8 Section 4C½. Civil actions alleging that a defendant negligently supervised a person who
9 sexually abused a minor or that a defendant’s conduct caused or contributed to the sexual abuse
10 of a minor by another person may be commenced at any time after the acts alleged to have
11 caused an injury or condition occurred. For the purposes of this section, “sexual abuse” shall
12 have the same meaning as in section 4C.
1 of 4

13 SECTION 3. Said chapter 260 is hereby further amended by inserting after section 4C ½
14 the following section:
15 Section 4C ¾. Notwithstanding any provision of law which imposes a period of
16 limitations to the contrary and the provisions of any other law pertaining to the filing of a notice
17 of claim or a notice of intention to file a claim as a condition precedent to commencement of an
18 action or special proceeding, every civil claim or cause of action brought against a party alleging
19 sexual abuse of a minor, as that term is defined in section 4C, or negligent supervision
20 contributing to the sexual abuse of a minor under section 4C ½, or a predecessor statute that
21 prohibited such conduct at the time of the act, which is barred as of the effective date of this
22 section because the applicable period of limitations has expired or the plaintiff previously failed
23 to file a notice of claim or a notice of intention to file a claim, is hereby revived, and action
24 thereon may be commenced no later than 2 years after the effective date of this section. In any
25 such claim or action, dismissal of a previous action, ordered before the effective date of this
26 section, on grounds that such previous claim was time barred or for failure of a party to file a
27 notice of claim or a notice of intention to file a claim, shall not be grounds for dismissal of a
28 revival action pursuant to this section.
29 Notwithstanding any provision of law which limits the liability or damages for any
30 organization or employer or their officers, directors, trustees, employees or volunteers, every
31 civil claim or cause of action brought against a party alleging sexual abuse of a minor, as that
32 term is defined in section 4C, or negligent supervision contributing to the sexual abuse of a
33 minor under section 4C ½, or a predecessor statute that prohibited such conduct at the time of the
34 act, which was subject to limits on liability or damages at the time of the act, shall not be subject
35 to any limitations for revived actions brought pursuant to this section.
