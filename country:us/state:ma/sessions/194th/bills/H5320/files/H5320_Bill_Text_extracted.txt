HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, April 2, 2026.
The committee on Consumer Protection and Professional Licensure, to
whom were referred the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 319) of
James C. Arena-DeRosa relative to certain credit card fees; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 320) of Brian M. Ashe relative to
consumers of tickets; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 325) of
David Biele and others relative to the sale of alcoholic beverages at certain
venues; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 332) of Daniel Cahill
for legislation to regulate internet gaming; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 357) of Michael S. Day for legislation to regulate hemp-
derived beverages like alcohol; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 375) of Colleen M. Garry relative to credit card surcharges; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 427) of Jack Patrick Lewis and David
Paul Linsky relative to the occupational therapist interstate licensure
compact; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 447) of James M.
Murphy for legislation relative to credit card surcharges; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 448) of Brian W. Murray and others for
legislation relative to the purchase of event tickets; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 450) of Steven Owens relative to
protections for solar customers; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 454) of Edward R. Philips and Jacob R. Oliveira relative to credit card
surcharges; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4019) of Shirley
B. Arriaga relative to compensation for warranty service labor; and the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No, 4159) of Colleen M. Garry
relative to credit card surcharges, reports recommending that the
accompanying order (House, No. 5320) ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
TACKEY CHAN.
