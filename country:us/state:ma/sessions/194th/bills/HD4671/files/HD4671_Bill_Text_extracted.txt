An Act relative to labeling food from mRNA-treated animals and aquaculture.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 94 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the following section:-
2 Section 330. (a) As used in this section, unless the context clearly requires otherwise, the
3 following terms shall have the following meanings:
4 “Aquaculture”, the culture or husbandry of marine organisms by any person.
5 “food or food products”, any agricultural, horticultural, forest or other product of the soil
6 or water that has been grown, harvested or produced wholly within the commonwealth. Such
7 products shall include: fruits, vegetables, eggs, dairy products, meat and meat products, poultry
8 and poultry products, fish and fish products, grain and grain products, honey, nuts, preserves,
9 maple sap products, apple cider, fruit juice, ornamental or vegetable plants, nursery products,
10 flowers, firewood, fermented agricultural products and Christmas trees.
1 of 2

11 “Livestock”, cattle, equines, goats, members of the genus Lama, rabbits, sheep and
12 swine.
13 “mRNA vaccine material”, a substance included in a vaccine to stimulate the production
14 of antibodies and provide immunity against disease by introducing messenger ribonucleic acid
15 that corresponds to a viral protein.
16 “Poultry”, all domesticated birds.
17 (b) A label shall be placed on a food or food product offered for sale in the
18 commonwealth derived from aquaculture, livestock or poultry that received mRNA vaccine
19 material.
20 (c) The label shall: (i) contain a clear and concise statement that the food or food product
21 contains mRNA vaccine material; and (ii) be conspicuously and prominently placed in order to
22 be easily seen by the consumer.
23 (d) The department of public health may conduct examinations and investigations of food
24 or food products to ensure compliance with this section. If the department finds that a food or
25 food product is not properly labeled as required by this section, the department shall order the
26 stop of sales of the food or food product until it is labeled in accordance with this section.
27 (e) The department of public health shall promulgate regulations, pursuant to chapter
28 30A, implementing this section.
