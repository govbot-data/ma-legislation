18 SECTION 4. Chapter 69 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the following
19 new section after section 1T:-
20 Section 1U. (a) As used in this section, the following words shall, unless the context
21 clearly requires otherwise, have the following meanings:
22 “Evidence-based literacy instruction”, literacy instruction that is: (i) grounded in
23 scientific research methods; and (ii) demonstrated through well-designed and well-implemented
24 studies to produce significant and positive effects on student learning outcomes.
25 “Five research-based areas in reading instruction”, reading instruction that includes, but
26 shall not be limited to, phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary and comprehension.
27 “High-quality curriculum”, curriculum reviewed and approved by the department.
28 (b) Districts shall select high-quality curricula to meet the curriculum frameworks
29 pursuant to section 1E for kindergarten through grade 3 literacy that: (i) include instruction in the
30 five research-based areas in reading instruction; (ii) utilize and are based upon evidence-based
31 literacy instruction; and (iii) improves competency in the areas of vocabulary development and
32 oral language.
33 (c) The department shall provide tools and resources to aid districts in providing adequate
34 professional development aligned with evidence-based literacy instruction and scientifically-
2 of 6

35 based reading research for literacy teachers, paraprofessionals, and reading specialists in grades
36 pre-kindergarten through third grade.
37 (d) The department shall create and maintain: (i) A list of department-approved, high-
38 quality professional development programs and vendors aligned with evidence-based literacy
39 instruction best practices and scientifically-based reading research; (ii) A set of online training
40 modules available and free to all teachers that provide training on the foundational practices and
41 pedagogy aligned with evidence-based literacy instruction best practices and scientifically-based
42 reading research; and (iii) a list of high-quality curricula. Said lists and modules shall be updated
43 as often as practicable to remain current with best practices and new curriculum releases.
44 (e) (1) A district may apply to the department for a waiver to use kindergarten through
45 grade 3 literacy instruction curriculum that does not appear on the department’s list of high-
46 quality curricula and that has not already been reviewed by the department.
47 (2) In determining whether to approve a district’s waiver for a curriculum that is not
48 already on the department’s list of high-quality curricula and that has not already been reviewed
49 by the department, the department shall assess whether the district’s curriculum utilizes
50 evidence-based literacy practices and is high-quality.
51 (3) A district shall report to the department during the waiver period any student data as
52 requested.
53 (4) Curriculum used on a waiver that is assessed and approved by the department and
54 deemed to meet the requirements of this section shall be allowed to continue as an approved
55 literacy curriculum used for instruction in the district following the expiration of the waiver
56 unless the waiver is rescinded pursuant to paragraph (7).

57 (5) A district that receives a waiver pursuant to this subsection shall publicly post to its
58 website any report submitted to the department with information required pursuant to paragraph
59 (2) and curriculum samples.
60 (6) The department may audit approved waivers at any time. The audit shall include, but
61 shall not be limited to: (i) an in-depth review of curriculum materials; (ii) instructional
62 documentation; and (iii) instructional practices in the classroom.
63 (7) The department may rescind approval of a waiver based on audit findings before the
64 expiration of the waiver.
65 SECTION 5. Said Chapter 69 is hereby amended by inserting after section 1U, the
66 following new section:-
67 Section 1V. (a) (1) Annually, not later than August 15, the department shall identify a list
68 of data requirements that districts shall report on related to curricula consistent with evidence-
69 based literacy instruction as defined by section 1U of this chapter. The department shall publish
70 the list of data requirements on the department’s website.
71 (2) Annually, not later than October 31, each district shall report to the department the
72 data required by the department pursuant to paragraph (1).
73 (b) Annually, not later than December 31, the department shall aggregate data collected
74 from each district pursuant to subsection (a), prepare and publish a report on the aggregated data
75 and file the report with the clerks of the house of representatives and the senate and the joint
76 committee on education. The department shall publish the report on the department’s website.

81 SECTION 7. Said chapter 71 is hereby further amended by inserting after section 57A
82 the following 2 sections:-
83 Section 57B. (a) For the purposes of this section and section 57C, the term “screening
84 assessments” shall mean tools and assessments that can be used to identify students at risk for
85 poor academic outcomes as determined by the department.
86 (b) The department, in consultation with the department of early education and care, shall
87 issue guidelines to districts requiring screening procedures and protocols for reading ability and
88 progress in literacy skills for all students in kindergarten through grade 3 using screening
89 assessments. The guidelines shall include a threshold at which a student is determined to be
90 “significantly below” the grade level benchmark. The commissioner shall annually publish data
91 on which screening assessments are employed by each district to conduct reading screening.
92 Section 57C. (a) Each district shall assess each student’s reading ability and progress in
93 literacy skills from kindergarten through at least grade 3, using a screening assessment approved
94 by the department. The frequency of the screening assessment shall be determined by the
95 commissioner but shall be no less than twice per school year. The screening assessment of the
96 student’s reading ability and progress shall be consistent with section 2 of chapter 71B, the
97 department’s dyslexia guidelines issued pursuant to section 57A and literacy guidelines issued
98 pursuant to section 57B.

99 (b) If a screening assessment determines that a student is significantly below relevant
100 grade level benchmarks in specific literacy skills, the district shall determine which actions
101 within the general education program will meet the student’s needs, including, but not limited to,
102 differentiated or supplementary evidence-based reading instruction and ongoing monitoring of
103 progress. Not later than 30 days after a screening result that determines a student is significantly
104 below the relevant grade level benchmark, the district shall inform the student’s parent or
105 guardian of the screening results and the school’s response and shall offer the opportunity for a

107 SECTION 8. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, if a school
108 district does not teach a high-quality curriculum, as defined by section 1U of chapter 69 of the

111 such curriculum.
112 SECTION 9. School districts shall begin reporting required pursuant to paragraph (2) of

115 secondary education shall submit the first report required pursuant to subsection (b) of section
116 1V of chapter 69 of the General Laws.
