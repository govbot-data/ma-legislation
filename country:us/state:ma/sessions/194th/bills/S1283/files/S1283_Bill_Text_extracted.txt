By Mr. Velis, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 1283) of John C. Velis for legislation
to protect orders issued on behalf of a minor so that the order remains in effect until the
expiration date of the order. The Judiciary.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act regarding on behalf of orders.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 209A of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official
2 Edition, is hereby amended by adding the following new section:-
3 Section 9A. Any on behalf of order or order relative to minor children, remains in effect
4 after the minor reaches the age of majority unless otherwise ordered by the court. Upon reaching
5 the age of majority, the plaintiff may appear at court on the date and time the order is to expire,
6 and the court shall determine whether or not to extend the order for any additional time
7 reasonably necessary to protect the plaintiff or to enter a permanent order.
8 SECTION 2. Chapter 258E of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official
9 Edition, is hereby amended by adding the following new section:-
10 Section 11A. Any order issued on behalf of a minor remains in effect after the minor
11 reaches the majority unless otherwise ordered by the court. Upon reaching the age of majority,
12 the plaintiff may appear at court on the date and time the order is to expire, and the court shall
