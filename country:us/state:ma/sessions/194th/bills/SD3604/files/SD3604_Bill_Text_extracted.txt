Re: Annual Report of Wiretap Interceptions to the General Court Pursuant to
G.L. c. 272, § 99R
Dear Mr. Hurley:
Pursuant to G.L. c. 272, § 99R, I hereby submit this report to the General Court on behalf of the
Attorney General’s Office for the 2025 calendar year.
During 2025, the Attorney General submitted two (2) applications for electronic surveillance
warrants to Justices of the Superior Court. The Attorney General submitted (6) subsequent
applications for renewal. All the applications for electronic surveillance were granted by Justices
of the Superior Court. The resulting warrants authorized electronic surveillance for up to fifteen
days. The designated offenses for which electronic surveillance was conducted included
violations of: G.L. c. 94C, §§ 32A, 32E, 34, 40; G.L. c. 265 § 25; G.L. c. 271, §§ 16A, 17; and
G.L. c. 274, § 7.
During 2025, a total of 30,712 interceptions were made pursuant to these warrants. As a result,
139 indictments were issued.
In 2025, there were no jury trials that included the presentation of electronic surveillance;
therefore, there were no convictions resulting from wiretap warrants.
