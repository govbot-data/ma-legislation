An Act relative to cimex lectularius.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 186 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting the
2 following new section:-
3 Section 32. Cimex lectularius.
4 (a) For purposes of this section the following words shall have the following definitions,
5 unless the context clearly requires otherwise:-
6 “Control” means the process required by a professional pesticide applicator in an attempt
7 to eliminate or manage an infestation of bed bugs by poisoning, spraying, fumigating, trapping or
8 by any other recognized and lawful pest-elimination method, including repeated applications of
9 any treatment, particularly to areas where bed bugs are likely to congregate, provided that the
10 department of public health may recommend and issue standards on treatment methods as they
11 see fit based on the availability of updated information and science. Control of bed bugs shall be
12 deemed completed if there has been no evidence of bed bug activity for thirty (30) days after the
13 last application of any treatment.
1 of 3

14 “Infestation” means the presence of bed bugs, or signs of their presence, in a quantity
15 large enough that the tenant of a dwelling unit has knowledge, or should have knowledge, of the
16 presence of bed bugs in the dwelling unit.
17 “Certified Applicator” shall have the meaning provided in section 2 of chapter 132B.
18 (b) Any tenant with actual knowledge of the presence of bed bugs in said tenant’s
19 dwelling unit shall: (1) immediately provide notice to the owner in writing after becoming aware
20 of the presence of bed bugs; (2) allow the owner, as well as its agents, inspectors, certified
21 applicators, and contractors, to enter the dwelling unit to perform inspections repairs,
22 exterminations, and applications, upon not less than 24 hour notice; (3) comply with all protocols
23 and instructions afforded to tenant by the certified applicator, including without limitation,
24 encasing and sealing personal property in plastic bags, laundering personal property, removing
25 personal property which may be to be infested with bed bugs; and (4) remove personal property
26 identified for removal by the certified applicator. No tenant shall interfere with the certified
27 applicator’s performance of any extermination or inspection. Any tenant that fails to provide
28 access to the dwelling unit, interferes with any inspection or extermination, or fails to comply
29 with the protocols and/or instructions of the certified applicator, shall be responsible for any
30 actual and consequential damages incurred by the owner as a result of such denials.
31 (c) Within 5 business days of receiving a notice under subsection b, the landlord shall
32 commence an extermination protocol and notify the tenants and owners of any surrounding units;
33 provided, that if a certified inspector fails to observe an infestation of bed bugs, the owner shall
34 have no further obligation under this section. The owner shall perform all treatments and
2 of 3

35 exterminator services as may be required by the certified applicator until the bed bug infestation
36 is resolved.
37 An owner shall be deemed to have constructive knowledge of an infestation of bed bugs
38 in the event the owner is aware of an infestation of bed bugs in any surrounding unit. If an owner
39 has actual or constructive knowledge of the presence of bed bugs in a vacant unit or otherwise
40 prior to the signing of a lease for a new tenant, the owner must take action under this subsection
41 or the lease shall be void.
42 SECTION 2. Section 127Q. Cimex Lectularius Awareness
43 The department of public health shall prepare informational materials that describes bed
44 bugs, how to avoid infestations and legal rights and obligations imposed on tenants and
45 landlords. Said informational materials may contain additional information that the department
46 deems necessary and shall be updated by the department as new information concerning bed
47 bugs becomes available.
48 SECTION 3. The executive office of housing and livable communities may promulgate
49 regulations pursuant to section 32 of chapter 186, as inserted by section 1 of this act.
