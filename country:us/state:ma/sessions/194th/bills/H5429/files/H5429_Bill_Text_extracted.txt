To the Honorable Senate and House of Representatives,
Pursuant to the provisions of Article II, Section 8, Paragraph 1, Clause (2) of the
Amendments to the Constitution, as appearing in Article LXXXIX of said Amendments, I am
filing for your consideration the attached legislation entitled “An Act providing injured on duty
compensation to Chelmsford Firefighter Nicholas Spinale.”
Nicholas Spinale, an active-duty fire fighter for the Chelmsford Fire Department and a
Support Branch staff member at the Massachusetts Firefighting Academy, was tragically injured
on April 7, 2026, while supporting fire fighter training. The Academy is run by the Department
of Fire Services (DFS), and trains over 13,000 fire fighters at every level of experience across the
state each year. The Academy provides training at no cost to municipalities and relies on
instructors and support staff, who, like Mr. Spinale, are active-duty or retired fire fighters. Mr.
Spinale has performed critical, life-saving work in both roles and deserves our steadfast support
as he recovers.
This legislation ensures that Mr. Spinale will receive full pay and benefits and maintain
his full-time position with the Chelmsford Fire Department while he recovers from his injuries.
All costs will be borne by the state.
Every day, fire fighters bravely step into dangerous situations to keep us safe. Academy
staff are vital to the professional training and development that prepare them for these duties.
Mr. Spinale exemplifies this courageous spirit. He deserves the support this legislation will
provide as he recovers. I therefore urge your prompt consideration of this bill.

An Act providing injured on duty compensation to Chelmsford firefighter Nicholas Spinale.
Whereas, The deferred operation of this act would tend to defeat its purpose, which is to
establish forthwith, injured in the performance of duty benefits for a certain employee of the
Chelmsford fire department who is incapacitated as a result of a hazardous workplace injury,
therefore it is hereby declared to be an emergency law, necessary for the immediate preservation
of the public convenience.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, Nicholas
2 Spinale, a firefighter in the town of Chelmsford, shall be eligible for leave from the town without
3 loss of pay pursuant to section 111F of chapter 41 of the General Laws, retroactive to April 7,
4 2026, for the period during which he is incapacitated from duty as a Chelmsford firefighter due

9 provisions of chapter 152 of the General Laws; provided further, the benefits granted in this
10 section shall be reduced by the amount of workers’ compensation received by Nicholas Spinale
11 under said chapter 152. Nicholas Spinale shall be eligible for such injury leave compensation
