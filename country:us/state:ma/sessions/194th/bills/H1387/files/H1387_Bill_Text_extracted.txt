By Representative Markey of Dartmouth, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1387) of
Christopher M. Markey and others relative to reimbursement for certain acute hospitals that
qualify as low historic relative price hospitals. Health Care Financing.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act to increase reimbursement for low historic relative price hospitals.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 6D of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
2 section 9 the following section:-
3 Section 9A. (a) For the purposes of this section, “low historic relative price hospital”
4 shall mean an acute hospital (i) with an average statewide relative price across all carriers during
5 a 5-year period of less than 0.90, and (ii) that is either corporately independent or is corporately
6 affiliated with 2 or more acute hospitals but negotiates carrier contracts separately and on its own
7 behalf. The commission, in consultation with the center, shall annually publish a list of acute
8 hospitals that qualify as low historic relative price hospitals under this section.
9 (b) The commission shall establish a rate equity target to advance the equitable
10 reimbursement of low historic relative price hospitals:
1 of 2

11 (1) For the benchmark cycle of calendar years 2026 to 2029, inclusive, a carrier shall not
12 pay any in-network low historic relative price hospital a payment rate that is less than 15 per cent
13 below the average relative price of all acute hospitals in the carrier’s network;
14 (2) For the benchmark cycle of calendar years 2029 to 2032, inclusive, the average
15 annual reimbursement rate increase from a carrier to a low historic relative price hospital shall be
16 not less than 2 per cent above the health care cost growth benchmark;
17 (3) For the benchmark cycle of calendar years 2032 to 2035, inclusive, the average
18 annual reimbursement rate increase from a carrier to a low historic relative price hospital shall be
19 not less than 1 per cent above the health care cost growth benchmark; and
20 (4) Beginning in the benchmark cycle of calendar years 2035 to 2038, inclusive, and
21 beyond, the average annual reimbursement rate increase from a carrier to a low historic relative
22 price hospital shall be not less than the health care cost growth benchmark.
