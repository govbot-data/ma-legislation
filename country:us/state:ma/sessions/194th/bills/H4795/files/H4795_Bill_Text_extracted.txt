45 SECTION 8. Said charter is hereby further amended by striking out section 4.2 and
46 inserting in place thereof the following section:-
47 Section 4.2: Powers and Duties
48 The town manager shall be the chief administrator of the town and shall be responsible to
49 the select board for the proper administration of all town affairs placed in the town manager’s
50 charge by this charter. The powers and duties of the town manager shall include, but not be
51 limited to:
52 (i) managing, supervising, and being responsible for the efficient and coordinated
53 administration of all town functions as may be authorized by this Charter, by-law, town meeting
3 of 8

54 vote or the select board, including all appointed officials, employees, department heads and their
55 respective departments;
56 (ii) unless otherwise required by law, this charter or by-law, managing and
57 coordinating the administrative activities of all town agencies; provided, however, that elected
58 officers or their representatives shall be required to meet with the town manager at reasonable
59 times to effect coordination and cooperation among all town agencies;
60 (iii) appointing and removing a police chief, fire chief and town clerk pursuant to the
61 policy established by the select board; provided, however, that the police chief shall serve under
62 section 97A of chapter 41 of the General Laws and the fire chief shall serve under section 42 of
63 chapter 48 of the General Laws; provided, however, that an appointment made by the town
64 manager shall be confirmed by the select board not more than 15 days after the date on which the
65 town manager files notice of the action with the select board; and provided further, that failure
66 by the select board to confirm an appointment in that 15-day period shall constitute rejection of
67 the appointment.
68 (iv) appointing and removing department heads, officers and subordinates and
69 employees and other appointed members of town government for whom no other method of
70 appointment or removal is provided in this charter or by-law; provided, however, that an
71 appointment made by the town manager shall be confirmed by the select board not more than 15
72 days after the date on which the town manager files notice of the action with the select board;
73 and provided further, that failure by the select board to confirm an appointment in that 15-day
74 period shall constitute rejection of the appointment;
4 of 8

75 (v) acting as a negotiator for all collective bargaining agreements to which the select
76 board is a party, subject to ratification by the select board;
77 (vi) conducting or reviewing annual performance evaluations of all employees
78 subject to the town manager’s or the select board’s appointment and consulting with elected and
79 appointed boards to contribute to the preparation of the evaluations of department heads
80 associated with the boards;
81 (vii) fixing the compensation of all employees appointed by the town manager within
82 the limits established by the overall approved budget, the personnel by-laws, the town’s wage
83 and classification schedule and collective bargaining or other agreements;
84 (viii) attending all regular and special meetings of the select board unless excused
85 at the town manager’s request and having a voice, but not a vote, in all discussions;
86 (ix) attending all sessions of the town meeting and answering all questions
87 directed to the town manager that are related to the office of the town manager or concerning
88 which the town manager possesses the relevant information;
89 (x) assuring that all laws, this charter, by-laws, town meeting votes and directives
90 of the select board that require enforcement by the town manager or employees subject to the
91 town manager’s direction and supervision are faithfully carried out;

94 (xii) coordinating the preparation of the town’s annual report;

116 SECTION 17. Section 6.5 of said charter is hereby amended by striking out paragraph
117 6.5.1 and inserting in place thereof the following paragraph:-
118 6.5.1 The finance committee shall, upon receipt of the proposed budget from the town
119 manager, consider in public meetings detailed expenditures for each town department and
120 agency and may confer with representatives of each town agency in connection with the
121 committee’s review and consideration. The finance committee may require the town manager or
122 any town agency to furnish the committee with such additional information as the committee
123 may deem necessary to assist the committee in its review and consideration of the proposed
124 budget. Not less than 14 days before the opening of the spring town meeting, the finance
125 committee shall file with the town clerk a report containing the committee’s proposed budget and
126 comments or recommendations regarding differences between the committee’s proposed budget
127 and the proposed budget submitted by the town manager. Not less than 14 days before the
128 opening of the spring town meeting the report shall be made available to voters of the town by
129 publication on the town’s website and by leaving copies of the report in not less than 2 public
130 places in the town. Copies of the report shall be made available to voters at the opening of the
131 spring town meeting. The failure to file the report with the town clerk in a timely manner or to
132 publicize the report by posting on the town’s website or in 2 public places in the town shall not
133 prohibit the town meeting from voting on the budget and shall not affect the validity of any vote
134 taken on the budget at town meeting.
