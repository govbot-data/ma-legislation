HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, January 15, 2026.
The committee on Environment and Natural Resources, to whom were
referred the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 989) of Jack Patrick
Lewis and others relative to the use of animals in product testing; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1055) of Thomas M. Stanley for
an investigation by a special commission (including members of the
General Court) relative to the status of wild striped bass in the
Commonwealth; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1056) of
Thomas M. Stanley relative to the Atlantic striped bass; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3935) of Jay D. Livingstone and others
relative to ivory and rhinoceros horn trafficking; and the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 4512) of Adrian C. Madaro relative to
modernizing the governance of port authority parks in the East Boston
section of the city of Boston, reports recommending that the accompanying
order (House, No. 4928) ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
CHRISTINE P. BARBER.
