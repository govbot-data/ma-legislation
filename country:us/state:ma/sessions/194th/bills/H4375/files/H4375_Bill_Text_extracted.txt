To the Honorable Senate and House of Representatives,
I am pleased to submit for your consideration the DRIVE Act, An Act to preserve and
advance Massachusetts’ competitiveness in discovery, research, and innovation for a vibrant
economy. With this legislation, we are making a transformative investment in Massachusetts’s
world-class research and innovation economy. By supporting discovery, research, and innovation
across our institutions, we will sustain the Commonwealth’s competitive edge, preserve and
create jobs, strengthen local economies, and ensure that the benefits of scientific progress thrive.
Massachusetts has long been a global leader in research and innovation, with our
institutions shaping the course of modern science and technology. Our state has been the home of
breakthroughs such as the invention of the telephone, the world’s first painless surgery under
anesthetic, the guidance computers that made the Apollo moon landings possible, cutting-edge
plan genetics and soil microbiome research, wearable technology and sustainable plastics, the
foundational science behind life-saving gene therapies, and more. Massachusetts is the global
leader in innovative research and the discovery of lifesaving cures.
Research funding is also foundational to the Massachusetts economy – it’s economic
infrastructure here. A new report by the UMass Donahue Institute’s Economic & Public Policy
Research Group analyzed the impact of federal research and development funding in
Massachusetts. The report found that the $8.57 billion in annual federal research and
development funding that Massachusetts receives supports 81,300 jobs, $7.8 billion of income,
and generates more than $16 billion in economic activity. The funding also represents an
excellent return on investment.
1 of 3

Massachusetts is home to one in every 10 jobs in research and development in the United
States. Two out of every five of the jobs created by this funding are in sectors outside of research
and development, including: construction, food services, health care, retail and administrative
support.
Yet Massachusetts’s leadership and success are not guaranteed. Other states and countries
are investing aggressively in their own research and innovation economies, seeking to compete
for the projects, partnerships, and talent that have long made Massachusetts a destination for
discovery. Our research institutions face growing pressure to modernize infrastructure, retain
early-career talent, and remain competitive in attracting top-tier projects. Steep cuts to federal
research funding, including reductions from the National Institutes for Health (NIH), National
Science Foundation (NSF), and National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA),
combined with caps on indirect spending, threaten the researchers and institutions that have
anchored our innovation economy and pushed scientific discoveries forward.
To ensure Massachusetts remains a global leader in research and to protect this critical
sector for our state’s economy, I am putting forward a coordinated, forward-looking proposal
that includes this legislation and executive action. This proposal, if enacted, will create jobs
across industries from construction and food services to health care and education. And in the
face of uncertainty from the federal government, this proposal will protect one of the things that
makes Massachusetts so special – our global leadership in health care, innovation, and
discovering cures to help families across the world.
At the core of the legislation are two major funding mechanisms designed to ensure that
Massachusetts can continue to drive progress even as the research landscape evolves:
• The Research Resilience Fund, which will provide $200 million in multi-year
support for research projects at Massachusetts hospitals, universities, and independent research
institutions. These funds will support high-value initiatives with clear public benefit, scientific
promise, and significant economic and workforce impact. This legislation would expand the
allowable uses of the Federal Match Fund of interest earnings generated by the state’s record-
high Stabilization Fund balance while preserving our ability to meet all commitments for
existing matches and take advantage of future funding opportunities.
• The Research Catalyst Fund, which will serve as a vehicle to accept and distribute
philanthropic contributions and matching funds from industry. Donors will be able to direct
funds toward particular institutions or projects or contribute to a shared pool. These dollars will
be used to complement public investments.
Both the Research Resilience Fund and the Research Catalyst Fund will be governed by
the Research Opportunity Review Board established in this bill: a group of experts in higher
education, research, philanthropy, health care, and industry.
2 of 3

The legislation also establishes a separate Public Higher Education Bridge Funding
Reserve, a $200 million investment funded through Fair Share revenue to support the state’s
public colleges and universities. This funding includes:
• $80 million in flexible campus funding for research-related expenses
• $50 million to retain and extend positions for graduate students, postdocs, and
early-career researchers
• $20 million to support regional research partnerships and joint ventures, with a
focus on expanding access and opportunity statewide
Complementing this legislative framework, I have issued an executive order establishing
the Advisory Commission on Advancing Research and Discovery in Massachusetts. This
Commission will provide a statewide forum for long-term strategy, bringing together leaders
from academia, industry, and government to evaluate trends, propose new models for
collaboration and sustainability, and recommend new initiatives to keep Massachusetts at the
forefront of innovation.
This legislation underscores our commitment to the economic engine of Massachusetts’s
scientific research and discovery sector, and with these steps, the Commonwealth can build a
more resilient research ecosystem that supports both innovation and long-term economic growth.
I look forward to working with you to advance this critical investment in our shared future.
Respectfully submitted,
Maura T. Healey,
Governor
