HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, November 6, 2025.
The committee on Housing, to whom was referred the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 1493) of David F. DeCoste and others

RICHARD M. HAGGERTY.

An Act allowing movable tiny houses as permanent residential dwellings and accessory dwelling
units.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 1A of chapter 40A of the General Laws, as amended by chapter 358
2 of the acts of 2020, is hereby further amended by inserting after the definition of “MBTA
3 Community” the following definition: -
4 “Movable tiny house", a transportable dwelling unit built on a frame or chassis with not
5 more than 400 square feet, excluding lofts, projections from bay windows, open decks, porches
6 or exterior utility and storage compartments, intended for residential use on an individual house
7 lot where single-family dwellings are allowed or as an accessory dwelling unit.
8 SECTION 2. Section 3 of said chapter 40A, as so appearing, is hereby amended by
9 adding the following 5 paragraphs: - The Massachusetts Department of Transportation, registry
10 of motor vehicles and the Secretary of the commonwealth shall establish a new category for
11 movable tiny houses. The registry of motor vehicles shall establish a process to allow cities and
12 towns of the Commonwealth to opt-in and apply for, issue and cancel certificates of title for
13 movable tiny houses that meet all additional safety and equipment requirements established by
1 of 3

14 the registry of motor vehicles as well as reasonable local zoning requirements including, but not
15 limited to, septic systems established in 310 CMR 15.000, if applicable, site plan review,
16 regulations concerning dimensional setbacks and the bulk and height of structures; provided,
17 however, that the registration fee for said certificates of titles for movable tiny houses and
18 manufactured movable tiny houses shall be $100.
19 A “movable tiny house” shall be permanently affixed to a chassis approved by the
20 Massachusetts Department of Transportation capable of carrying the total weight of the structure
21 and the exterior width shall not exceed any dimension prohibited by the Massachusetts
22 Department of Transportation for movement on a public way or highway; provided further, that a
23 “movable tiny house” shall be built in conformance with the regulations and standards of the
24 state building code but shall not be required to have insulation in walls, floors and total ceiling
25 assembly exceeding R13, R19 and R25 respectively.
26 A movable tiny house may be placed on a lot as defined in section 1A of chapter 40A of
27 the General Laws and lived in for not less than 180 days before an on-site inspection; provided,
28 however, that after 180 days, a movable tiny house shall be required to have an established
29 address, energy and water source and wastewater system; provided further, that after 180 days
30 the authority having jurisdiction of said lot shall provide for the inspection of the moveable tiny
31 house by either the local authority having jurisdiction or a certified member of a third-party
32 inspection agency and if the moveable tiny house meets the standards of the inspection a
33 certificate of occupancy shall be issued.
34 Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, a city or town may establish
35 less restrictive rules and regulations than are permitted by this section; provided, however, that a
2 of 3

36 city or town shall not establish design criteria having the effect of excluding movable tiny
37 houses.
38 A city or town may incentivize hosted parking of movable tiny houses with tax
39 abatements or other tax incentives.
