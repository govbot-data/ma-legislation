By Ms. Comerford, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 2348) of Joanne M. Comerford,
Michael D. Brady, James B. Eldridge, James K. Hawkins and other members of the General
Court for legislation to use blue envelopes to facilitate better interactions between police officers
and persons with autism spectrum disorder. Transportation.

In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act facilitating better interactions between police officers and persons with autism spectrum
disorder.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 6C of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
2 section 56 the following section:-
3 Section 56A. (a) As used in this section, the following words shall, unless the context
4 clearly requires otherwise, have the following meanings:
5 “Blue envelopes”, blue-colored envelopes designed either by the department of state
6 police or the registry of motor vehicles, in consultation with the department of state police, the
7 Massachusetts Chiefs of Police Association Incorporated and either Advocates for Autism of
1 of 2

8 Massachusetts or any other organization that advocates for persons with autism spectrum
9 disorder, that
10 (i) provide written information and guidance on the outside of the envelope regarding
11 ways to enhance effective communication between a police officer and a person with autism
12 spectrum disorder;
13 (ii) include instructions for ease of access to the envelope, including, but not limited to,
14 attaching the envelope to the sun visor on the driver’s side of the vehicle; and
15 (iii) can hold a person's motor vehicle operator's license, registration and insurance
16 identification card.
17 (b) The registrar of motor vehicles shall make available, upon request, said blue
18 envelopes to a person with autism spectrum disorder or the person's parent or guardian.
19 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect as of July 1, 2026.
