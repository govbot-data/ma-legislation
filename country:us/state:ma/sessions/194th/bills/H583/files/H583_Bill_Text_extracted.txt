An Act to improve student and staff attendance and performance by ensuring air quality
monitoring in schools throughout the commonwealth.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1: Relative to the installation and operation of air quality monitoring (AQM)
2 systems in public schools throughout the Commonwealth. For purposes of providing students
3 and staff with a safe and healthy environment in which to learn and work, and to curtail ongoing
4 student and staff absences related to illness, provisions of this legislation shall apply to all public
5 schools with students between grades K-12 on or before January 1, 2026.
6 The provisions included herein are required to ensure the appropriate utilization of AQM
7 systems previously installed in a select number of schools throughout the Commonwealth.
8 Schools that do not yet possess AQM systems shall be required to install such monitoring
9 systems and begin data collection on or before January 1, 2026.
10 SECTION 1(a): for purposes of this section and section 2 inclusive of this act:

11 (1) “school facility” means any permanent building or portable structure, building, or
12 commercial space owned, rented, operated, leased by a local or regional board of education,
13 including, but not limited to, donated space being used as a classroom or any other space for
14 educational services.
15 (2) “hours of school activity” means any time throughout the day when students, staff and
16 community members occupy a school facility.
17 (3) “multipurpose spaces” means rooms and spaces within an educational setting that can
18 be or are used for multiple purposes, including but not limited to meetings, instructional
19 activities, dining, and social gatherings.
20 (4) “Air Quality Monitor (AQM)” is an instrument for the data collection and
21 measurement of carbon dioxide gas (CO2), carbon monoxide gas (CO), PM10 (Airborne
22 Particulates less than 10 microns in size), PM2.5 (Respirable Particulate Matter less than 2.5
23 microns in size), VOC (Volatile Organic Compounds), Temperature (T), and Relative Humidity
24 (RH%).
25 (5) “allergen” means a substance that causes an allergic reaction.
26 (6) “Mechanical ventilation unit” means the intentional fan-driven flow of outdoor air
27 into a building.
28 (7) “ventilation verification assessment” means an assessment performed by certified
29 third-party ventilation contractors.
30 (8) “district officials” means those in authority who have responsibility for the safety,
31 welfare, and orderly conduct of the District and campus community, or who have had such

42 SECTION 2: Relative to standards for acceptable air quality in a school building.
43 Regarding appropriate action or equipment required to meet current industry standards for school
44 buildings as determined by a certified third-party ventilation contractor:
45 1) Standards for Acceptable Air Quality shall be determined for the purpose of
46 application to all measurements collected via AQM systems and ventilation assessments.
47 2) Data resulting from any AQM systems or assessments shall be made available publicly
48 via an accessible database.
