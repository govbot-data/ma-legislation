Re: Facility Inspection – Essex County Correctional Facility, Middleton
Dear Sheriff Coppinger:
The Massachusetts Department of Public Health’s, Bureau of Climate and Environmental Health (BCEH)
reorganized and consolidated its seven programs into six divisions. The Community Sanitation Program is now
the Division of Environmental Health Regulations and Standards (EHRS). Our division continues to support
correctional facilities and has added expertise for indoor air quality assessments for schools and other municipal
and state facilities. EHRS also used this opportunity to revise and update the format of the correctional bi-
annual inspection reports. This revised format clarifies new and repeat deficiencies outlined in regulation 105
CMR 451.000 and organizes deficiencies based on the 451.100, 451.200 and 451.300 series of provisions, as
well as other conditions that may constitute a threat to health and safety.
Throughout this process we understand there has been a delay in receiving your inspection report and we
appreciate your patience and understanding as we finalize the new report template. As you receive your reports,
please feel free to contact me if you have any questions or concerns.
Sincerely,
Amy Riordan, MPH
Senior Advisor, EHRS, BCEH

Re: Facility Inspection – Essex County Correctional Facility, Middleton
Dear Sheriff Coppinger:
The Massachusetts Department of Public Health (Department) Division of Environmental Health Regulations and
Standards (EHRS) conducted an inspection of the Essex County Correctional Facility on October 22 and 23, 2024
accompanied by Lieutenant Steve Wilkins, Environmental Health and Safety Officer, in accordance with Department
regulations 105 CMR 451.000: Minimum Health and Sanitation Standards and Inspection Procedures for Correctional
Facilities.
The inspection identified 312 total deficiencies: 142 deficiencies under the Required Standards (.100 and .200 series), 51
repeat deficiencies under the Required Standards, 80 deficiencies under the Recommended Standards (.300 series), and
39 repeat deficiencies under the Recommended Standards.
Overview
Section 1 provides details of all deficiencies, including repeat deficiencies, found during the inspection. These
are categorized by Required Standards, Recommended Standards, or additional applicable regulatory standards.
Section 2 provides information on areas that EHRS found to be compliant.
Section 3 documents the areas that EHRS did not inspect.
Section 4 provides information on submitting a Plan of Correction for the identified deficiencies.
Section 5 outlines observations and recommendations related to the inspection.

3. The Vocational Education Building continues to be under construction and is not housing inmates. EHRS
observed the conditions listed below, and if left uncorrected, may lead to health and safety concerns when
brought back on-line to house inmates:
a. Cement board water damaged behind remaining wall tiles in the showers of the VOKE 1 Bathroom;
b. Back exterior wall rotted near the emergency exit in VOKE 2;
c. Wall rotted near the emergency exit on the first floor VOKE Hallway;
d. Exterior door not rodent and weathertight at the end of the first floor VOKE hallway;
e. Exterior emergency exit ramp for Upper Voke damaged;
f. Extension cord running through a window in the Warehouse # 0911; and
g. Roof leaking.
4. During the Kitchen Inspection:
a. EHRS noted either detergent or water with no sanitizing solution within all sanitizing buckets and the 3-
compartment sink while the kitchen was actively in use. It was determined that soap was continuing to
be incorrectly placed in sanitizing buckets and the sanitizing dispenser was not dispensing any sanitizing
solution. Sanitizing solution was manually made, however, EHRS strongly recommended:
i. Additional kitchen staff be trained on how to properly mix the sanitizing solution;
ii. Kitchen staff develop a sanitizing distribution procedure to ensure sanitizing solutions of the
proper concentration are distributed within the sanitizing buckets throughout the kitchen; and
iii. Kitchen staff develop a procedure to test the sanitizing solution when made as well as
throughout each shift to ensure the proper concentrations are maintained.
b. EHRS observed clean and dirty kitchen uniforms strewn about the inmate Changing Room # 1027. EHRS
recommended that a new uniform distribution policy be developed to more clearly separate clean and
dirty uniforms.

c. Numbering the offices, bathroom, and exam room throughout the M.A.P. area. The entire vocation building

3. The Vocational Education Building continues to be under construction and is not housing inmates. EHRS
observed the conditions listed below, and if left uncorrected, may lead to health and safety concerns when
brought back on-line to house inmates: The entire vocation building is undergoing a very large DCAM
upgrade. Upon completion of the renovation, it is expected that all of the below issues will be remedied by
DCAM or the Department. Estimated completion spring 2025

f. Extension cord running through a window in the Warehouse # 0911; and
g. Roof leaking.
4. During the Kitchen Inspection:
a. EHRS noted either detergent or water with no sanitizing solution within all sanitizing buckets and the 3-
compartment sink while the kitchen was actively in use. It was determined that soap was continuing to
be incorrectly placed in sanitizing buckets and the sanitizing dispenser was not dispensing any sanitizing
solution. Sanitizing solution was manually made, however, EHRS strongly recommended:
i. Additional kitchen staff be trained on how to properly mix the sanitizing solution; the contracted
vendor had the dispensing machine fixed by an outside vendor. All contracted vendor staff were
instructed on the proper way to mix and to measure the sanitizer. They also are conducting daily
checks of the solutions via strips to ensure proper levels.
ii. Kitchen staff develop a sanitizing distribution procedure to ensure sanitizing solutions of the
proper concentration are distributed within the sanitizing buckets throughout the kitchen; and
The contracted vendor now mixes and checks all sanitizing buckets prior to dispersal to the
inmate workers.
iii. Kitchen staff develop a procedure to test the sanitizing solution when made as well as
throughout each shift to ensure the proper concentrations are maintained. The
contracted vendor has put in place testing procedures for the initial mix and for spot
checks via strips throughout the day.
b. EHRS observed clean and dirty kitchen uniforms strewn about the inmate Changing Room # 1027. EHRS
recommended that a new uniform distribution policy be developed to more clearly separate clean and
dirty uniforms. Kitchen staff have developed and implemented a distribution plan that clearly identifies
and separates clean, new uniforms from soiled uniforms.
