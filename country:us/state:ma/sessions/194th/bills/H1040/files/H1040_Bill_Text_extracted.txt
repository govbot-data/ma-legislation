An Act ban the use of tire-derived materials in playgrounds and playing fields in Massachusetts.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Whereas, the use of tire-derived materials, including but not limited to tire crumb rubber,
2 ground tire rubber, shredded tires, rubberized asphalt, pour-in-place surfacing, recycled tire chips
3 and tire-derived aggregates in playgrounds, playing fields, and landscaping has raised concerns
4 about potential health and environmental hazards;
5 Therefore, be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
6 Commonwealth of Massachusetts in General Court assembled, and by the authority of the same,
7 as follows:
8 Section 1. For the purposes of this act "Tire-derived materials" shall mean any material
9 derived from the processing of discarded tires, including but not limited to: tire crumb rubber,
10 ground tire rubber, shredded tires, rubberized asphalt, recycled tire chips, and tire-derived
11 aggregates.
1 of 2

12 "Playground" shall mean any area designed for children's play, including but not limited
13 to: Playgrounds in schools, parks, and residential areas; playgrounds in childcare facilities and
14 early childhood education centers;
15 "Playing field" shall mean any area used for athletic activities, including but not limited
16 to: sports fields for baseball, soccer, football, and other sports.
17 Section 2. No person shall install, construct, or maintain any playground or playing field
18 surface that contains tire-derived materials. This prohibition shall apply to both new and existing
19 playgrounds and playing fields.
20 Section 3. This act shall not apply to existing playground or playing field surfaces that
21 were installed prior to the effective date of this act.
22 Section 4. The Department of Environmental Protection shall be responsible for
23 enforcing the provisions of this act. The Department of Public Health] shall provide guidance
24 and recommendations regarding safe surfacing materials for playgrounds and playing fields.
25 Section 5. If any provision of this act or its application to any person or circumstance is
26 held to be invalid, such invalidity shall not affect other provisions or applications 1 of this act
27 which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application.
