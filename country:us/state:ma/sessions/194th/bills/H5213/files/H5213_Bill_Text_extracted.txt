HOUSE . . . . . . . . No. 5213
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
________________________________________
HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, March 12, 2026.
The committee on Financial Services, to whom were referred the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 1106) of Daniel Cahill relative to home investments;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1114) of Mike Connolly, Antonio F. D.
Cabral and Samantha Montaño for legislation to establish a Massachusetts public
bank; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1122) of Michael S. Day relative
to the use of credit trigger leads; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1145)
of Michelle M. DuBois relative to regulating certain mortgages; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 1177) of Carlos González relative to community
reinvestment goals for banks; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1183) of
Richard M. Haggerty relative to mortgage licensing fees; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 1221) of Sally P. Kerans and Sal N. DiDomenico relative to
investments by entities of the Commonwealth; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 1264) of Samantha Montaño and Christine P. Barber relative to identifying
all weapon of mass destruction entities in which the Public Fund holds investments;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1332) of Jeffrey Rosario Turco relative
to shared appreciation mortgage agreements; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 1335) of Steven Ultrino relative to unclaimed property; the petition (accompanied
by bill, House, No. 1348) of Bud L. Williams and Jacob R. Oliveira for legislation to
establish a loan loss guarantee program within the Massachusetts Development
Finance Agency for certain community development financial institutions and other
nonprofit and non-traditional lenders; and the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 1349) of Bud L. Williams relative to improving access to banking for
microbusinesses and small businesses and for an investigation by a special
commission (including members of the General Court) relative to improve access to
commercial insurance for microbusinesses and small businesses, reports
recommending that the accompanying order (House, No. 5213) ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
JAMES M. MURPHY.
