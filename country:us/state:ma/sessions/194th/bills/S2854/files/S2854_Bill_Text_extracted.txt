58 following section:-
59 Section 51B. (a) Any person of good moral character, who: (i) is a graduate of a master’s
60 level dental therapist education program, or its equivalent, approved by the Commission on
61 Dental Accreditation; (ii) passes a comprehensive, competency-based clinical examination that is
62 approved by the board and administered by a recognized national or regional dental testing
63 service that administers testing for dentists and other dental professionals or equivalent
64 examination administered by another entity approved by the board; and (iii) obtains a policy of
65 professional liability insurance and shows proof of such insurance as required by rules and
66 regulations shall, upon payment of a fee to be determined annually by the commissioner of
67 administration under the provision of section 3B of chapter 7, be licensed as a dental therapist
68 and be given a certificate to practice in this capacity. A licensed dental therapist shall have
69 practiced under the direct supervision of a supervising dentist for a minimum of 1,000 hours
70 before practicing under general supervision pursuant to a collaborative management agreement.
71 For the purposes of this section, "general supervision" shall mean supervision of
72 procedures and services based on a written collaborative management agreement between a
73 licensed dentist and a licensed dental therapist but not requiring a prior exam or diagnosis by a
74 supervising dentist or the physical presence of a supervising dentist during the performance of
4 of 14

75 those procedures and services unless required by the supervising dentist in the collaborative
76 management agreement.
77 (b) An applicant for licensure as a dental therapist in the commonwealth must graduate
78 from a master’s level dental therapist education program, or its equivalent, approved by the
79 Commission on Dental Accreditation. All dental therapy educational programs in the
80 commonwealth must include at least one licensed dentist as an instructor. The board shall
81 provide guidance for any educational entity or institution that may operate all or some portion of
82 a dental therapy program, or may collaborate with other educational entities, including but not
83 limited to universities, colleges, community colleges, and technical colleges, to operate all or
84 some portion of a dental therapy program. The board shall provide guidance to develop
85 mechanisms to award advanced standing to students who have completed coursework at other
86 educational programs accredited by the Commission on Dental Accreditation. All education
87 programs must prepare students to perform all procedures and services within the dental therapy
88 scope of practice as set forth in this section. As determined by the board and established through
89 regulations, dental therapists may be authorized to perform additional procedures through
90 continuing education courses approved by the board.
91 The educational curriculum for a dental therapist educated in the commonwealth shall
92 include training on serving patients with special needs including, but not limited to, people with
93 developmental disabilities including autism spectrum disorders, mental illness, cognitive
94 impairment, complex medical problems, significant physical limitations and the vulnerable
95 elderly.

141 the remaining hours or years, whichever is longer, under direct supervision in the
142 Commonwealth prior to practicing under general supervision.
143 (d) Pursuant to a collaborative management agreement, a dental therapist licensed by the
144 board may perform all acts in the Commission on Dental Accreditation's dental therapy
145 standards. Dental therapists shall have the authority to perform an oral evaluation and assessment
146 of dental disease and formulate an individualized treatment plan as authorized by the supervising
147 dentist in the collaborative management agreement. A dental therapist may dispense and
148 administer the following medications within the parameters of the collaborative management
149 agreement and with the authorization of the supervising dentist: non-narcotic analgesics, anti-
150 inflammatories and antibiotics. The authority to dispense and administer shall extend only to the
151 categories of drugs identified in this paragraph and may be further limited by the collaborative
152 management agreement. A dental therapist is prohibited from dispensing or administering
153 narcotic analgesics. A dental therapist may oversee not more than 2 dental assistants, but shall
154 not oversee public health dental hygienists.
155 After entering into their first collaborative management agreement with a supervising
156 dentist, dental therapists shall practice under direct supervision for not less than 1,000 hours.
157 After completing at least 1,000 hours of practice under direct supervision, dental therapists are
158 authorized to perform all procedures and services listed in the Commission on Dental
159 Accreditation's dental therapy standards, as set forth in regulations by the board, under general
160 supervision if authorized by a supervising dentist pursuant to a written collaborative agreement.
161 Pursuant to a collaborative management agreement, a dental therapist may provide
162 procedures and services permitted under general supervision when the supervising dentist is not
8 of 14

163 on-site and has not previously examined or diagnosed the patient provided the supervising
164 dentist is available for consultation and supervision if needed through telemedicine or by other
165 means of communication. If the supervising dentist will not be available, arrangements shall be
166 made for another licensed dentist to be available to provide timely consultation and supervision.
167 A dental therapist may not operate independently of, and may not practice or treat any
168 patients without, a supervising dentist. A dental therapist is prohibited from practicing without
169 entering into a collaborative management agreement with a supervising dentist.
170 (e) The board shall establish appropriate guidelines for a written collaborative
171 management agreement. A collaborative management agreement shall be signed and maintained
172 by the supervising dentist and the dental therapist and shall be submitted annually to the board.
173 The agreement may be updated as necessary. The agreement shall serve as standing
174 orders from the supervising dentist and shall address: (i) practice settings; (ii) any limitation on
175 services established by the supervising dentist; (iii) the level of supervision required for various
176 services or treatment settings; (iv) patient populations that may be served; (v) practice protocols;
177 (vi) record keeping; (vii) managing medical emergencies; (viii) quality assurance; (ix)
178 administering and dispensing medications; (x) geographic distance limitations; (xi) oversight of
179 dental assistants; and (xii) referrals for services outside of the dental therapy scope of practice.
180 The collaborative management agreement shall include specific protocols to govern situations in
181 which the dental therapist encounters a patient who requires treatment that exceeds the
182 authorized scope of practice of the dental therapist. The supervising dentist is responsible for
183 directly providing, or arranging for another dentist or specialist within an accessible geographic
184 distance to provide, any necessary additional services outside of the dental therapy scope of

185 practice needed by the patient. A supervising dentist may have a collaborative management
