Resolve providing for an investigation by a special commission relative to the strengths and
sustainability of the Commonwealth’s emergency food network.
1 Resolved, that a special commission is hereby established to study the sustainability of
2 eligible emergency food providers, known as food pantries, in the Massachusetts Emergency
3 Food Network Assistance Program (MEFAP). The commission shall study the strengths of food
4 pantry programs and the challenges they face regarding the adequacy of state support, workforce
5 development, organizational sustainability, governance, succession planning, and capacity to
6 meet resident needs. The commission shall compare state practices of providing emergency food
7 assistance with state practices of providing emergency housing assistance.
8 Resolved, that the commission shall make recommendations to address the capacity of
9 the emergency food network, specifically the network of food pantries, regarding: (i) workforce
10 development, training, apprenticeship opportunities, (ii) policies and technical assistance to
11 support organizational sustainability, governance and succession planning, (iii) assessment of
12 emergency food needed to meet the commonwealth’s demand, (iv) implications of increased
1 of 3

13 need on projected purchasing and impact on state funding and private fundraising, and (v) the
14 frequency of food pantry use by Massachusetts residents.
15 Resolved, the special commission shall consist of: 3 people who shall be representatives
16 of food pantries from different geographic areas representing urban, suburban and rural
17 communities, 3 of whom shall be representatives of food banks representing communities not
18 represented by pantry program members, 2 of whom shall be food pantry program participants, 1
19 of whom shall be the commissioner of the Massachusetts department of agricultural resources or
20 a designee, 1 of whom shall be the commissioner of transitional assistance or a designee, 1 of
21 whom shall be a member of the Massachusetts food system caucus to be appointed by the chairs
22 of the caucus, 1 of whom shall be a member of the Massachusetts food systems collaborative, 1
23 of whom shall be a member of the house of representatives appointed by the speaker, and 1 of
24 whom shall be a member of the senate appointed by the senate president.
25 Said commission shall ensure that its meetings shall be open to the public, notes will be
26 taken and made available. The appointment of the commissioners shall occur within 30 days
27 after the effective date of this resolve. The commission shall convene its first meeting no later
28 than 45 days after the bill’s enactment. The commission shall meet as often as needed to
29 complete its report.
30 Within 18 months of enactment, said commission shall report to the clerks of the house of
31 representatives and senate, the chairs of the joint committee on agriculture, the chairs of the joint
32 committee on children, families, and persons with disabilities; and the chairs of the joint
33 committee on elder affairs on the results of its investigation and its recommendations, including
34 proposals of legislation or regulations necessary to carry its recommendations into effect.

35 The special commission shall dissolve 30 days after the submission of the report, unless
36 upon a two-thirds vote of the Commission, a continuance is deemed necessary to complete the
37 purpose of the Commission. The time frame of said continuance must be specified in the motion
38 to extend time of operation.
