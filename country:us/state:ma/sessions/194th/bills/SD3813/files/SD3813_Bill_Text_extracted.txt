REPORT OVERVIEW
Massachusetts General Law, Chapter 18, Section 2 (B)(s) requires DTA to file an annual
report detailing the numbers of recipients exempt from the Electronic Benefit Transfer
(EBT) card photo requirement and the reasons for such exemptions.
DEPARTMENT OF TRANSITIONAL ASSISTANCE MISSION
DTA’s mission is to assist and empower low-income individuals and families to meet their
basic needs, improve their quality of life, and achieve long-term economic self-sufficiency.
DTA offers a comprehensive system of programs and supports to help individuals and
families achieve greater economic self-sufficiency, including food and nutritional
assistance, economic assistance, and employment supports.
EBT PHOTO REQUIREMENT
In August 2013, the Legislature passed a law requiring the use of photo identification on
each newly issued, and reissued, EBT card. The requirement covered recipients who are 18
or older that are designated to be the “head of household” on a DTA assistance case.
Other classes of recipients are exempted from the requirement as follows:
• 60 years of age or over • Disabled
• Blind • Victims of domestic violence
DTA must also comply with federal laws. The United States Department of Agriculture
Food and Nutrition Service (USDA-FNS) requires that heads of households who do not
receive Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) benefits on their own behalf be
exempted (e.g., some relative caretakers of minor children). Exemptions based on
religious beliefs also must be allowed.
