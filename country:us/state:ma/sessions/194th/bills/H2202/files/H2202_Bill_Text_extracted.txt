78 SECTION 13. Section 108 of chapter 175 of the General Laws is hereby amended by
79 adding the following:-
80 "13. No individual policy of short-term or long-term disability insurance providing
81 income replacement benefits that is issued or renewed in the Commonwealth shall discriminate
82 against persons disabled due to a mental health condition. “Mental health condition” is defined
83 as any condition or disorder involving mental illness or substance use that falls under any of the
84 diagnostic categories listed in the mental, behavioral or neurodevelopmental disorders section of
85 the international classification of disease, as periodically revised. Discrimination includes:
86 1. Limiting benefits or excluding coverage for disabilities on the basis that the disability
87 results from a mental health condition as defined herein.
88 2. Limitations on benefits or coverage exclusions for any disability resulting from a
89 mental health condition that are not applied equally to all disabilities."
90 SECTION 14. Section 110 of chapter 175 of the General Laws is hereby amended by
91 adding the following subsection:-
92 "Q. No certificate of coverage under a group policy of short-term or long-term disability
93 insurance providing income replacement benefits that is issued or renewed in the Commonwealth
94 shall discriminate against persons disabled due to a mental health condition. "Mental health
95 condition" is defined as any condition or disorder involving mental illness or substance use that
96 falls under any of the diagnostic categories listed in the mental, behavioral or
97 neurodevelopmental disorders section of the international classification of disease, as
98 periodically revised. Discrimination includes:

99 1. Limiting benefits or excluding coverage for disabilities on the basis that the disability
100 results from a mental health condition as defined herein.
101 2. Limitations on benefits or coverage exclusions for any disability resulting from a
102 mental health condition that are not applied equally to all disabilities."
103 SECTION 15. Nothing established in the preceding sections, Sections 13 and 14, of this
104 act, shall be construed to prohibit periodic medical exams or other procedures reasonably
105 calculated to determine whether or not a disability exists, or continues to exist; provided said
106 examinations, procedures or protocols are comparable and no more frequent or onerous than
107 those required for disabilities caused by physical disorders.
