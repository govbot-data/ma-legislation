SENATE DOCKET, NO. 1177 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2156
By Ms. Jehlen, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 2156) of Patricia D. Jehlen, John F.
Keenan, Patrick M. O'Connor, James B. Eldridge and other members of the General Court for
legislation to increase access to menstrual products in public buildings. State Administration and
Regulatory Oversight.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act to increase access to menstrual products in public buildings.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 7C of the General Laws s hereby amended by inserting after
2 section 72, the following section:-
3 Section 73.
4 a. As used in this chapter, the following words shall have the following meanings unless
5 the context clearly requires otherwise:
6 “Menstruating individuals,” any person who menstruates.
7 “Disposable menstrual products,” products used by menstruating individuals including,
8 but not limited to, sanitary napkins, tampons, and underwear liners.
9 b. The Division of Capital Asset Management and Maintenance shall maintain, in all real
10 property owned, rented or otherwise occupied by public agencies of the Commonwealth, free
1 of 2

11 disposable menstrual products in both public and private restrooms for use by menstruating
12 individuals, whether they are employees or members of the general public. Such products shall
13 be provided at no charge. Such products shall be available in a convenient manner that does not
14 stigmatize any persons seeking such products.
15 SECTION 2. Chapter 15A of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
16 section 27 the following section:-
17 Section 27A.
18 a. As used in this chapter, the following words shall have the following meanings unless
19 the context clearly requires otherwise:
20 “Menstruating individuals,” any person who menstruates.
21 “Disposable menstrual products,” products used by menstruating individuals including,
22 but not limited to, sanitary napkins, tampons, and underwear liners.
23 b. Each public institution of higher education shall provide disposable menstrual products
24 to menstruating individuals in the restrooms of any such institution’s building at no cost. The
25 disposable menstrual products shall be available in a convenient manner that does not stigmatize
26 the individual seeking such products.
