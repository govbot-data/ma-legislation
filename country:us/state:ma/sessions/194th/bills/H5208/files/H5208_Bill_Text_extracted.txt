The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
________________________________________
HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, March 12, 2026.
The committee on Labor and Workforce Development, to whom were
referred the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2091) of David F.
DeCoste, Marcus S. Vaughn and Marc T. Lombardo relative to the
verification of employment eligibility of employees through the federal
employment authorization e-verify program; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 2136) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others relative to
employers under paid family medical leave; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 2156) of Edward R. Philips relative to providing hazard
pay and protection for essential workers during a declared state of
emergency; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2173) of Adam
J. Scanlon relative to the structure of the Commonwealth Employment
Relations Board; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2186) of
Marcus S. Vaughn for legislation to establish a special commission
(including members of the General Court) to study and report on the policies
and procedures of the Department of Unemployment Assistance to prevent
and address unemployment fraud; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 2187) of Marcus S. Vaughn that the Department of Unemployment
Assistance online unemployment benefits portal have a status bar on every
individual’s profile displaying the progress of the applicant’s claim; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4069) of Andres X. Vargas
relative to the Department of Unemployment Assistance; and the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 4116) of Rita A. Mendes relative to
building permits and job site safety, reports recommending that the
accompanying order (House, No. 5208) ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
PAUL MCMURTRY.
