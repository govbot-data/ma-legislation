Legislative Mandate
The following report is hereby issued pursuant to Line Item 4590-0915 of Chapter 9 of the Acts of
2025 as follows:
“provided further, that not later than August 18, 2025 and monthly thereafter, the
department shall submit a report to the joint committee on public health and the house
and senate committees on ways and detailing the status of the Pappas Rehabilitation
Hospital for Children; provided further, that the report shall include, but not be limited to: (i)
a summary of the types of services and programs available through the hospital including,
but not limited to, medical, recreational and educational services; (ii) a summary of any
reductions or terminations of services for patients and rationales for each change; (iii) the
census at the hospital on January 1, 2025; (iv) the number of admittances per month since
January 1, 2025; (v) the number of discharges per month since January 1, 2025; and (vi) the
total number of staff employed at the facility, delineated by profession including, but not be
limited to, teachers, nurses, administrative staff and other professionals”
Report
(i) Summary of Services
Medical
Medically Complex Care: Patients admitted primarily for management of medical
and rehabilitative needs that require hospital level care.
Medical / Behavioral Service: Patients admitted with combined medical
complexity and behavioral health challenges
Caregiver Short-Term Respite: Patients admitted to provide respite for patients and
families currently cared for at home.
Therapeutic / Recreational
Physical Therapy: Targets mobility, strength, balance, and pain reduction through
exercise, manual techniques, and other modalities.
Occupational Therapy: Focuses on daily living skills, adaptive strategies, and
environmental modifications to support independence.
Speech-Language Therapy: Addresses communication, cognition, and swallowing
disorders, often critical after stroke or brain injury.
2

Psychosocial Support: Includes counseling and behavioral therapy to manage
emotional challenges and promote mental well-being including complimentary
alternative medicine
Recreational Therapy: includes equine and aquatic therapy, community integration
transitions and exposures through community trips.
Educational
Special education services are made available through DESE’s Special Education
in Institutional Settings (SEIS) which provides a basic academic program and
special education services aligned to each student’s IEP goals and objectives in
coordination with each student’s school district.
(ii) Service Reductions
Service Reduction/Termination Type Service Reduction/Termination Reason
Aquatic Therapy Critical incident from July 9, 2025. The pool
located at the Pappas Rehabilitation Hospital for
Children leaked over 15,000 gallons of water over
the prior week. A structural engineer assessed
the pool and noted numerous wide- spread
localized cracks that are allowing water leakage
and have evidence of corrosion and deterioration
that is occurring within the pool walls.
Pool was fully drained on July 10. Repair options
are currently being developed by a structural
engineer. Pool is 15 years post life.
10/3/25 Update:
Given the structural deterioration, cost of repairs,
and extensive building system deficiencies,
DCAMM states bringing the pool back into service
would be cost-prohibitive and is not
recommended.
A rough estimate provided by DCAMM for
demolition and reconstruction is $85M.
Construction of the new pool is $61M based on
$1,500/sf in 2025, escalated to 2028 and $600/sf
for demolition and site preparation is $24M.
3

(iii) Census at the hospital on January 1, 2025
On January 1, 2025, there were 39 patients admitted to the hospital, comprised of 37 long-
term care patients and 2 care-giver respite patients. The department will provide these
sub-categories of census on an ongoing basis to assist the Legislature further in its review.
