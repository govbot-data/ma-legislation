An Act relative to property tax classification, the senior homeowner property tax exemption, and
the personal property tax exemption in the city of Boston.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1: Notwithstanding section 23 of chapter 59 of the General Laws, or any other
2 general or special law to the contrary, for fiscal year 2025 the tax rates in the City of Boston shall
3 be the rates that would have occurred were a 181.5% shift in place at the time of their setting.
4 The residential tax rate for property in the City of Boston, which applies to residential real
5 property and open space, shall be $11.03 per $1,000 of value, and the commercial tax rate for
6 property in the City of Boston, which applies to commercial real property, industrial real
7 property, and personal property, shall be $26.92 per $1,000 of value. In order to go into effect,
8 this section must be enacted by the General Court prior to March 1, 2025.
9 SECTION 2: Notwithstanding section 1A of chapter 58 of the General Laws or any other
10 general special law to the contrary, the commissioner of revenue shall further adjust the
11 minimum residential factor of the City of Boston determined under said section 1A of said
12 chapter 58 for fiscal years 2026, and 2027, if adoption of such factor for any such year would
1 of 5

13 result in the residential property class bearing a higher percentage of the total property tax levy
14 than the percentage of the total property tax levy imposed on the residential property class in
15 fiscal year 2024. The new minimum residential factor for such a year shall be (a) for fiscal year
16 2026, 50 per cent subject to such adjustment upward as may be required to provide that the
17 percentage of the total tax levy imposed on any class of real or personal property shall not
18 exceed 180 per cent of the full and fair cash valuation of the taxable property of the class divided
19 by the full and fair cash valuation of all taxable real and personal property in the City of Boston,
20 (b) in fiscal year 2027, 50 per cent subject to such adjustment upward as may be required to
21 provide that the percentage of the total tax levy imposed on any class of real or personal property
22 shall not exceed 178 per cent of the full and fair cash valuation of the taxable property of the
23 class divided by the full and fair cash valuation of all taxable real and personal property in the
24 City of Boston, (c) in fiscal year 2028 and thereafter, the determination of the minimum
25 residential factor shall be conducted in accordance with the provision detailed in section 1A of
26 chapter 58. Payments made under section 38H of chapter 59 of the General Laws, and similar
27 provisions of special acts, that are included in the tax levy for purposes of determining the
28 minimum residential factor under said section 1A of said chapter 58 shall be so included in the
29 determination of any factor under this section.
30 SECTION 3: Notwithstanding the provisions of chapter 30B of the General Laws, or any
31 other general or special law, and subject to appropriation by the City of Boston, in each fiscal
32 year in which the total tax levy imposed on the class of commercial property exceeds one
33 hundred seventy five per cent of the full and fair cash valuation of that class divided by the full
34 and fair cash valuation of all the taxable real and personal property in the City of Boston, the
35 City of Boston is authorized to expend up to fifteen million dollars to make grants and provide
2 of 5

36 programs for the purpose of supporting small businesses impacted by the shift of the tax levy
37 beyond the aforementioned one hundred seventy five per cent. The term “small business” as used
38 in this section shall mean a business with its principal place of business located in Boston, with
39 fewer than fifty employees or less than five million dollars in annual revenue.
40 SECTION 4: Notwithstanding clause 41C of section 5 of chapter 59 or any other general
41 or special law to the contrary, with respect to real property in the City of Boston the following
42 factors determining exemption for real property under clause 41C of section 5 of chapter 59 shall
43 be adjusted as follows: (1) increasing the sum of $500 contained in the first sentence of clause
44 41C to $1500; (2) increasing the amounts contained in subclause (B) of said first sentence
45 whenever they appear in said subclause from $13,000 dollars and from $15,000 dollars to fifty
46 percent of Area Median Income as adjusted for household size, as is published annually by the
47 U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development, as required by Section 8 of the Federal
48 Housing Act of 1937 (42 USC Section 1437f), as amended, and the relevant year of the
49 calculation shall be the most recent figure available as of July 1 of the start of the fiscal year to
50 which the exemption is sought; and (3) increasing the amounts contained in subclause (C) of said
51 first sentence whenever they appear in said subclause from $28,000 dollars to not more than
52 $80,000 and from $30,000 to not more than $110,000. These adjustments shall not be further
53 modified by the second sentence of clause 41C of section 5 of chapter 59.
54 SECTION 5: (a) Notwithstanding section 5 of chapter 59 of the General Laws or any
55 other general or special law to the contrary, the City of Boston shall be permitted to allow for an
56 exemption from taxation of personal property not in excess of $30,000 of value.
3 of 5

57 (b) The city council of the City of Boston, with the approval of the mayor, shall vote to
58 establish a minimum value of personal property subject to taxation. Said value shall be subject to
59 limitation pursuant to subsection (a).
60 SECTION 6: Only in the event that the tax rates for the City of Boston for fiscal year
61 2025 are not changed by March 1, 2025, pursuant to Section 1 of this act, from the tax rates
62 certified in December of 2024, and due to the fact that the residential tax levy in fiscal year 2025
63 increased from 41.67% of the overall tax levy to 44.07% of the overall tax levy, the largest
64 percentage increase since 2007, the last year the City was authorized by statute to have a
65 classification shift exceeding 175%, the City of Boston shall be authorized to issue rebates to
66 those taxpayers who received the residential exemption in fiscal year 2025. In no event shall
67 these rebates be issued in fiscal year 2025 unless the following conditions are met: the
68 appropriation for said rebates would not cause the fiscal year’s Generally Accepted Accounting
69 Principles (GAAP) Unassigned Fund Balance to go below 15% of the fiscal year’s GAAP
70 General Fund Operating Expenditures, while maintaining a Statutory/Budgetary Unassigned
71 Fund Balance at 10% or higher of Statutory/Budgetary Operating Expenses. These rebates shall
72 be a uniform amount of money for each eligible taxpayer to be paid out of a pool of money
73 appropriated by the City of Boston for this express purpose. The amount of the rebate shall be
74 calculated,reviewed and approved by the City Council with the submission of an appropriation
75 order,by the Collector-Treasurer of the City of Boston with the approval of the mayor of the City
76 of Boston. This rebate shall be in addition to any exemptions allowed under chapter 59;
77 provided, however, that in no instance shall the taxable valuation of such property after all
78 applicable exemptions and this rebate be reduced below ten per cent of its full and fair cash
4 of 5

79 valuation, unless that is explicitly provided for in any specific section of chapter 59. In order to
80 go into effect, this section must be enacted by the General Court prior to July 1, 2025.
81 SECTION 7: Effective Date. Sections 1,2,3, and 6 of this act shall take effect
82 immediately upon passage. Sections 4 and 5 of this act shall take effect upon passage and be
83 applicable to fiscal year 2026 and all following fiscal years.
