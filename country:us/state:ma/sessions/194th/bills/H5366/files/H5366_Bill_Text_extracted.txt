3 Section 40. (a) For the purposes of this section and section 102 of chapter 71, the

6 “Personal electronic device”, any portable electronic device that is capable of: (i)
7 providing voice communication, text messaging or other data communication between 2 or more
8 parties or devices; or (ii) connecting to the internet, a smartphone or other personal electronic
9 device or a cellular or Wi-Fi network. “Personal electronic devices” shall include, but shall not

13 “School day”, the time from which the first student arrives at the school until the
14 dismissal of the students from the last class of the day; provided, that “school day” shall include:
15 (i) any time between classes, during lunch, during other non-instructional times and in any

20 a school day; provided, that a district shall determine whether a specific category of activity shall

30 (iii) provisions on activities that a school may consider to be a school-sponsored activity;

32 day or other methods of rendering personal electronic devices inoperable or unavailable during

58 SECTION 2. Chapter 71 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the following
59 2 sections:-
60 Section 102. (a)(1) Each district shall have a policy to prohibit student use of personal
61 electronic devices during the school day. The policy shall include, but shall not be limited to:
62 (i) provisions to prevent student use of school-issued or school-sanctioned devices for
63 personal use;
64 (ii) provisions to prevent student use of personal electronic devices during the school day;
65 (iii) provisions on activities that a school may consider to be a school-sponsored activity
66 at which a school may prohibit student use of personal electronic devices;
67 (iv) options for secure storage of student personal electronic devices during the school
68 day or other methods of rendering personal electronic devices inoperable or unavailable during
69 the school day;
70 (v) at least 1 method for parents, guardians and caregivers of students to contact students
71 during the school day; provided, that there shall be considerations for emergency situations;
72 (vi) at least 1 method for a student to contact their parent, guardian or caregiver during
73 the school day; provided, that there shall be considerations for emergency situations; and
4 of 18

74 (vii) enforcement provisions to ensure student compliance with the policy; provided,
75 however, that the enforcement provisions shall contain practical safeguards to prevent
76 inequitable discipline; and provided further, that discipline for noncompliance with a school’s
77 policy on personal electronic devices shall not include the expulsion or suspension of a student
78 solely due to said noncompliance.
79 (2) The policy shall specify the method the district shall use to prohibit student access to
80 personal electronic devices. The following methods shall be deemed sufficient to prohibit student
81 access to personal electronic devices: (i) secure storage of personal electronic devices during the
82 school day; (ii) the use of technology pursuant to subsection (d) of section 40 of chapter 69 that
83 renders the personal electronic device inoperable; or (iii) any another method approved by the
84 department.
85 (3) Each district shall annually notify the parents and guardians of all students attending
86 the school of the policy as determined by the department, pursuant to paragraph (2) of subsection
87 (e) of section 40 of chapter 69.
88 (b) Notwithstanding subsection (a), a district policy may authorize a student to use a
89 personal electronic device if:
90 (i) used in accordance with an individualized education program or an education plan
91 implemented pursuant to section 504 of the federal Rehabilitation Act of 1973, 29 U.S.C. 701 et
92 seq.; provided, however, that a district shall authorize a student to use a personal electronic
93 device only to the extent necessary under the individualized education program or plan;
94 (ii) necessary under any accommodations pursuant to state or federal law, including, but
95 not limited to, Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act, 42 U.S.C. 12131 et seq.; provided,
5 of 18

96 however, that a district shall authorize a student to use a personal electronic device only to the
97 extent necessary under the accommodation;
98 (iii) a healthcare provider provides in writing that the use of a personal electronic device
99 is necessary to treat or monitor a health condition of a student; provided, however, that a district
100 shall authorize a student to use a personal electronic device only to the extent necessary under
101 the healthcare provider’s written notice;
102 (iv) the district determines that there is an emergency and the use of a personal electronic
103 device is necessary; provided, however, that a district shall authorize a student to use a personal
104 electronic device only to the extent necessary during the emergency; or
105 (v) the superintendent of a district, or a designee, provides in writing that the use of a
106 personal electronic device is necessary for language access for a student, including, but not
107 limited to, translation, interpretation or transliteration purposes; provided, however, that a district
108 shall authorize a student to use a personal electronic device only to the extent necessary to
109 provide language access for a student to adequately understand educational materials and
110 participate in activities during the school day.
111 (c) The policy, and any standards and rules enforcing the policy, shall be prescribed by
112 the school committee of a municipality, regional school district or vocational technical school in
113 conjunction with the superintendent or, in the case of a commonwealth charter school, the board
114 of trustees.
115 (d) Nothing in this section shall limit a district from setting restrictions or standards for
116 the use of personal electronic devices on school grounds or during school-sanctioned activities
117 that are outside of the school day.

118 Section 103. (a) Each district shall provide instruction on the social, emotional and
119 physical risks and harms of social media use consistent with content standards in the curriculum
120 frameworks adopted by the board of elementary and secondary education.
121 (b) The department, in consultation with the office of the attorney general and the
122 department of public health, shall provide guidance and recommendations to assist districts with
123 developing and implementing effective instruction on social media use and shall make such
124 guidance and recommendations publicly available on the department’s website. Guidance and
125 recommendations may include, but shall not be limited to: (i) curriculum resources; (ii) guidance
126 on developing community norms regarding cell phone and social media use; (iii) guidance for
127 educating parents or guardians on managing their child’s social media use; (iv) recognizing
128 warning signs of the harmful effects of social media use; and (v) other available resources.
129 Guidance and recommendations may be reviewed and regularly updated to reflect applicable
130 research and best practices.
131 (c) The department shall assist schools in providing: (i) instruction on the social,
132 emotional and physical risks and harms of social media use by facilitating access to high-quality
133 curricular materials; and (ii) professional development training, including the provision of
134 trainings, seminars, conferences and materials, for educators to use in teaching.
135 (d) Nothing in this section shall require a school district to require instruction on social
136 media use for every year of school; provided, however, that social media use education and
137 instruction shall be utilized during appropriate age levels in curricula, as determined by the
138 school district.

139 (e) The department may provide trainings, seminars, conferences and materials for
140 educators to use in the teaching of social media use in person or through the use of synchronous
141 or asynchronous audio, video, electronic media or other telecommunications technology.
142 SECTION 3. The General Laws are hereby further amended by inserting after chapter
143 93L the following chapter:-
144 Chapter 93M
145 ONLINE PROTECTION
146 Section 1. As used in this chapter, the following words shall, unless the context clearly
147 requires otherwise, have the following meanings:
148 “Account”, a unique profile for a user of a social media platform.
149 “Addictive social media feed”, the presentation of content to users where the social media
150 platform employs algorithms that analyze user data or information on users to select content for
151 users and includes any of the following addictive features:
152 (i) infinite scrolling, which shall include: (A) continuously loading content, or content
153 that loads as the user scrolls down the page without the need to open a separate page; and (B)
154 seamless content, or the use of pages with no visible or apparent end or page breaks;
155 (ii) push notifications or alerts sent by the social media platform to inform a user about
156 specific activities or events related to the user’s account;
8 of 18

157 (iii) displays of personal interactive metrics that indicate the number of times other users
158 have clicked a button to indicate their reaction to a user’s content or have shared or reposted the
159 user’s content;
160 (iv) content generated by an algorithm based on a user’s activity on the social media
161 platform; or
162 (v) auto-play video or video that begins to play without the user first clicking on the
163 video or on a play button for that video.
164 “Connected account”, an account on a social media platform that is directly connected to
165 the user’s account, including, but not limited to, a friend, follower or similar connection with
166 access to view the user’s content.
167 “Parent”, a parent or legal guardian of a minor.
168 “Push notification”, an automatic electronic message displayed on a user’s personal
169 electronic device, as defined in section 40 of chapter 69, when the social media platform is not
170 actively open or visible on the personal electronic device that prompts the user to check and
171 engage with the social media platform.
172 “Social media feed”, the presentation of content to users of a social media platform.
173 “Social media platform”, a public website, online service, online application or mobile
174 application that displays content primarily generated by users and allows users to create, share
175 and view user-generated content with other users; provided, that “social media platform” shall
176 not include email, cloud storage, SMS, MMS, RCS or similar text messaging
177 telecommunications services or document viewing, sharing or collaboration services.
9 of 18

178 “User”, an individual who, through an account, accesses or uses either a social media
179 feed or a social media platform.
180 Section 2. (a)(1) To protect the health and wellness of a minor under 14 years of age,
181 including, but not limited to, mental and behavioral health, a social media platform shall prohibit
182 a minor under the age of 14 from being a user of a social media platform.
183 (2) A social media platform shall: (A) terminate a user under the age of 14; (B) allow a
184 user under the age of 14 to request to terminate the account; (C) allow the confirmed parent of a
185 user under the age of 14 to request the termination of the user’s account; and (D) permanently
186 delete all personal information held by the social media platform related to the terminated user
187 unless there is a legal requirement to maintain the information.
188 (3) A parent of a user under the age of 14 may request access to the age assurance or
189 verification system data submitted by the user to the social media platform. Not later than 5
190 business days after receipt of the request, the social media platform shall provide a parent access
191 to the age assurance or verification system data submitted by the user. No social media platform
192 shall provide data other than what was submitted for the purposes of verifying the age of the user
193 to any parent.
194 (b)(1) To protect the health and wellness of a minor who is 14 or 15 years of age,
195 including, but not limited to, mental and behavioral health, a social media platform shall prohibit
196 a minor who is 14 or 15 years of age from being a user of a social media platform unless the
197 social media platform receives verifiable consent from the parent for the minor to become a user.
198 (2) A social media platform shall: (A) terminate a user who is 14 or 15 years of age if the
199 parent’s consent has not been provided for the minor to create or maintain an account on the
10 of 18

200 social media platform; (B) allow a user who is 14 or 15 years of age to request to terminate the
201 account; (C) allow the confirmed parent of a user who is 14 or 15 years of age to request the
202 termination of the minor’s account; and (D) permanently delete all personal information held by
203 the social media platform related to the terminated user unless there is a legal requirement to
204 maintain the information.
205 (3) A parent of a user who is 14 or 15 years of age may request access to the age
206 assurance or verification system data submitted by the user to the social media platform. Not
207 later than 5 business days after receipt of the request, the social media platform shall provide a
208 parent access to the age assurance or verification system data submitted by the user. No social
209 media platform shall provide data other than what was submitted for the purposes of verifying
210 the age of the user.
211 (4) A social media company shall, for users who are 14 or 15 years of age, set default
212 safety settings to prioritize the health and wellness of users 14 and 15 years of age to ensure an
213 age appropriate experience on the social media platform, including, but not limited to, safety
214 settings that shall:
215 (i) restrict the visibility of the account of a user 14 or 15 years of age to connected
216 accounts;
217 (ii) require age verification to change user settings;
218 (iii) require age verification to access sensitive content, including age-restricted content
219 on a social media feed and messages;
11 of 18

220 (iv) only allow a user who is 14 or 15 years of age to share content to connected
221 accounts; and
222 (v) restrict direct messaging capabilities for a user who is 14 or 15 years of age to only
223 allow direct messaging to connected accounts.
224 (5) To protect the health and wellness of a minor who is 14 or 15 years of age, including,
225 but not limited to, mental and behavioral health, a social media platform shall prohibit a minor
226 who is 14 or 15 years of age from being a user of an addictive social media feed.
227 (6) To protect the health and wellness of a minor who is 14 or 15 years of age, including,
228 but not limited to, mental and behavioral health, no social media platform shall send notifications
229 to a minor who is 14 or 15 years of age between the hours of 12:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. eastern
230 standard time.
231 (7) No social media platform shall withhold, degrade, lower the quality or increase the
232 price of any product, service or feature to a user who is 14 or 15 years of age due to the social
233 media platform not being permitted to provide an addictive social media feed to the user.
234 Section 3. (a) A social media platform shall implement an age assurance or verification
235 system to determine whether a current or prospective user on the social media platform meets the
236 age requirement pursuant to section 2. To the extent practicable, the age assurance or verification
237 system shall consist of the best technology available to reasonably and accurately identify a
238 current or prospective user’s age.
239 (b) A social media platform shall implement a review process to allow users to appeal the
240 social media platform’s age determination pursuant to subsection (a). The user may submit
12 of 18

241 documentary evidence electronically to the social media platform to establish that the user meets
242 the age requirements of section 2. Not later than 3 days after receipt, a social media platform
243 shall review the documentary evidence submitted by the user and shall make a determination on
244 the appeal.
245 (c) Any data or information gathered by the social media platform for: (i) use in the age
246 assurance or verification system pursuant to subsection (a); (ii) any appeal of determination
247 pursuant to subsection (b); or (iii) the purpose of verifying parental consent pursuant to section 2
248 shall be segregated by the social media platform and shall remain confidential. The data and
249 information gathered for the purposes in clauses (i) to (iii), inclusive, shall not be used for any
250 other purpose by the social media platform.
251 (d) No social media platform shall share any data related to a minor user and their
252 LGBTQ+ protected status or any other protected status pursuant to chapter 151B.
253 Section 4. (a) A social media platform shall publicly and conspicuously post to the social
254 media platform’s website the number of: (i) users processed using the age assurance or
255 verification system pursuant to section 3; (ii) users granted access to the social media feed due to
256 the age determination appeal process under section 3; (iii) users denied access to the social media
257 feed due to the user not meeting the age requirements under section 2; (iv) users granted access
258 to the social media feed after providing the social media platform with verifiable parental
259 consent under section 2; (v) account user age verification review requests received under section
260 3; and (vi) accounts subsequently terminated for not meeting age requirements due to account
261 user age verification review requests.

262 Section 5. (a) A violation by a social media platform of this chapter shall be deemed an
263 unfair or deceptive act or practice in trade or commerce under chapter 93A.
264 (b) A social media platform found to be in violation of section 2 shall be punished by a
265 civil fine of not more than $5,000 per violation; provided, that a social medial platform shall be
266 in violation of section 2 for each user account not in compliance with section 2.
267 (c) A social media platform violation of section 4 shall be punished by a civil fine of not

273 that the definition includes public websites, online services, online applications or mobile
