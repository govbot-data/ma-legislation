An Act relative to the purchase of out-of-state creditable service.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Notwithstanding any special or general law, or rule or regulation to the contrary, section
2 3 of chapter 32 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition, is hereby
3 amended in subdivision (4) by inserting after the third paragraph the following paragraph:-
4 Notwithstanding the foregoing, if a retirement board accepts payment from a member for
5 the purchase of out-of-state service and issues to the member an estimate of the creditable
6 service to be granted thereby, such payment shall be deemed validly accepted and the member
7 shall be credited with the years of service so estimated for all purposes of this chapter. A
8 subsequent determination that the member may have been entitled to a retirement allowance
9 from another state shall not operate to divest the member of such creditable service, provided
10 that the board had accepted payment at the time of the estimate.
