Dear Chairs Rodrigues, Michlewitz, Velis, and Domb,
Section 2WWWWW of Chapter 29 of the Massachusetts General Laws requires
the Executive Office of Health and Human Services (EOHHS) to submit to the House
and Senate Committees on Ways and Means and the Joint Committee on Mental Health
and Substance Use and Recovery a report on the revenue and expenditure activity within
the Behavioral Health Access and Crisis Intervention Trust Fund.
The Behavioral Health Access and Crisis Intervention (BHACI) Trust Fund supports a
statewide, payor-agnostic community behavioral health crisis system including, but not
limited to, all necessary costs to support: (1) a behavioral health access line to connect
individuals to behavioral health services, including clinical assessment and triage (the
Behavioral Health Help Line); and (2) a statewide system to deliver behavioral health
crisis intervention services 24 hours per day and 7 days per week in mobile and
community-based settings, available to all residents without regard to insurance.
The BHACI Trust Fund has supported the development and operation of the Behavioral
Health Help Line (BHHL) beginning in January 2023. In addition to trust expenditures,
$17.1M was expended from the general fund in FY23 to support the costs of

implementing and operating the BHHL. In FY24, $6.0M was expended from the general
fund to support the costs of implementing and operating the BHHL.
The BHACI Trust Fund helps support the statewide, payor-agnostic crisis intervention
system that launched in January 2023 (Q3 of FY23). MassHealth’s behavioral health
vendor, the Massachusetts Behavioral Health Partnership (MBHP) manages provider
reimbursement for crisis intervention services provided to uninsured, Medicare, and self-
insured individuals. The general fund pays for the first $6.9M of crisis intervention costs
each year for uninsured and Medicare-insured individuals, and then the BHACI Trust
Fund pays for any costs exceeding $6.9M.
Additionally, MBHP managed reimbursement for commercially-insured individuals
before the publication of the Division of Insurance bulletin in June 2023 mandating
commercial coverage of crisis intervention services, and during the interim period as
providers updated their contracts with commercial insurers. The FY24 payment amount
from the trust fund includes reimbursement for delayed invoices to EOHHS for costs of
DOI commercially-insured individuals in FY23.

Increased spending in FY25 and FY26 is anticipated as initial implementation delays are
