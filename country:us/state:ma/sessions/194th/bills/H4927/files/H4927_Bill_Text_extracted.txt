An Act to relative to protecting access to applied behavior analysis services in schools.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 71B of the General Laws is here by amended by adding the
2 following section:-
3 Section 18. (a) As used in this section, the following words shall, unless the context
4 clearly requires otherwise, have the following meanings:-
5 “Applied behavior analysis”, evidence-based therapeutic interventions to improve
6 socially significant behaviors and address behavioral challenges.
7 “Department”, the department of elementary and secondary education.
8 “Licensed applied behavior analyst”, an individual who, by training, experience and
9 examination, meets the requirements for licensing by the board and is duly licensed to engage in
10 the practice of applied behavior analysis in the commonwealth.
1 of 4

11 “Private instructional personnel”, applied behavior analysis professionals contracted by
12 parents or guardians to provide services in educational settings.
13 “Registered behavior technician”, a professional certified in behavior analysis by the
14 Behavior Analyst Certification Board , working under the supervision of a licensed applied
15 behavior analyst or board certified behavior analyst.
16 (b)(1) All public, charter and private schools shall provide access to applied behavior
17 analysis services for students with Autism Spectrum Disorder diagnosis. Applied behavior
18 analysis services shall be delivered on school premises by a: (i) licensed applied behavior
19 analysts; (ii) board certified behavior analysts; or (iii) registered behavior technicians. Registered
20 behavior technicians shall be supervised by a licensed applied behavior analyst or a board
21 certified behavior analyst.
22 (2) School districts shall not deny access to any qualified applied behavior analysis
23 provider, including private instructional personnel contracted by a student’s parent or guardian;
24 provided, that the qualified applied behavior analysis provider meets the requirements of this
25 section.
26 (c) All public, charter and private schools and school districts shall collaborate with
27 private applied behavior analysis providers to integrate their services into the student’s academic
28 environment.
29 (d)(1) All public, charter and private schools and school districts shall be prohibited from:
30 (i) imposing additional requirements on applied behavior analysis providers beyond
31 licensure or certification pursuant to chapter 112 or the behavior analyst certification board;
2 of 4

32 (ii) denying applied behavior analysis providers, including private instructional personnel
33 access to classrooms or other school facilities;
34 (iii) charging parents or providers additional fees for applied behavior analysis services
35 delivered in school settings.
36 (2) School staff, including teachers and administrators, shall not interfere with or
37 obstruct applied behavior analysis providers in the performance of their duties.
38 (e)(1) Registered behavior technicians delivering applied behavior analysis services in
39 schools shall be supervised by a licensed applied behavior analyst or board certified behavior
40 analyst, with supervision conducted in accordance with national certification standards.
41 (2) The supervising licensed applied behavior analyst or board certified behavior analyst
42 shall be responsible for ensuring the quality and fidelity of applied behavior analysis
43 interventions.
44 (f)(1) Applied behavior analysis providers shall complete all state-mandated background
45 checks and provide proof of licensure or certification to school districts.
46 (2) A public, charter and private school may request necessary documentation applied
47 behavior analysis providers, but not delay services longer that 45 days due to providing
48 necessary documentation.
49 (g)(1) Parents, guardians or applied behavior analysis providers may file a complaint with
50 the department if a school or school district denies access to applied behavior analysis services.
51 (2) The department shall investigate all complaints and may impose penalties or
52 corrective actions on non-compliant schools or districts.
3 of 4

53 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect on July 1, 2025 and shall apply to all applied
54 behavior analysis services delivered on or after this date.
