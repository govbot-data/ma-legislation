An Act authorizing the Massachusetts Water Resources Authority to provide sewer services to a
certain parcel of land in the town of Sharon.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Subject to receipt of all approvals required under section 2 and following
2 satisfaction of all procedures adopted by the Massachusetts Water Resources Authority,
3 including the payment to the Authority of an entrance fee, the Authority may provide sewer
4 services through the town of Walpole to the property located in the town of Sharon, consisting of
5 approximately 3.07 acres of land located at the intersection of Route One- General Edwards
6 Highway and High Plain Street, which is more particularly described by the Sharon Board of
7 Assessors as Lots 2R and 4R General Edwards Highway and identified on Sharon Assessors
8 Map 116 as Lots 4 and 5, which property is currently under contract for sale to Lang’s Century
9 Services or its nominee, successor, or assignee; provided, however, that discharges shall not
10 exceed 10,000 gallons per day unless an additional volume is approved by the Authority. The
11 Authority may allow the property to be used and serviced by the sewer system to the same extent
1 of 2

12 as parcels of property located within the district currently served by the authority. All costs and
13 expenses incurred for engineering, design and construction, inclusive of all supervision, labor,
14 equipment and materials, relative to the sewer tie-in shall be paid by Lang’s Century Services, or
15 its nominee, successor or assignee.
16 SECTION 2. The sewer connection to the town of Walpole’s infrastructure and the
17 provision of sewer services by the authority for any discharges shall commence only after the
18 authority’s board of directors has voted to approve the connection after having first made the
19 findings set forth in paragraph (c) of section 8 of chapter 372 of the acts of 1984 and having
20 made such other determinations in accordance with applicable policies of the authority and after
21 all required approvals have been received including, as applicable, the approval of the
22 community of origin, transporting community, all other local and state regulatory bodies where
23 required and the authority’s advisory board.
24 SECTION 3. This act shall take effect upon passage.
