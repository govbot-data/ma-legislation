By striking out all after the enacting clause and inserting in place thereof the following:–
1 SECTION 1. As used in this act, the following words shall, unless the context clearly
2 requires otherwise, have the following meanings:
3 “Appurtenant structure”, any equipment, systems, parts and structures that are attached to
4 or pertinent to the design, operation or function of assets.
5 “Asset”, any common flood prevention structure and equipment that is used to control
6 flows of water or whose function is necessary for the proper operation of structures used to
7 control flows of water including, but not limited to, dams, tide gates, flood gates, solar panels
8 and floats.
9 “Cutler bridge”, the 107 Cutler highway vehicular bridge identified by the Massachusetts
10 Department of Transportation as bridge number R05061 located in the city of Revere and all
11 equipment and assets attached to the bridge that are pertinent to highway and bridge operations
12 including, but not limited to, 8 box culverts structurally built into the Cutler bridge and trash
13 racks affixed to the upstream side of the bridge for the purpose of protecting the 8 box culverts
14 from large debris, sediment and other build up.

15 “Emergency maintenance” or “emergency repairs”, any immediate repairs or
16 maintenance work necessary to address unforeseen issues that prevent the proper operation and
17 function of an asset.
18 “Flood gate”, a type of gate classified as a common flood prevention structure that, when
19 operating as designed, can be opened or closed to control the flow of water for Town Line brook
20 or Linden brook.
21 “Reconstruction”, any project or construction work that would: (i) disable or disrupt the
22 operations of an asset or appurtenant structure for more than 30 days; or (ii) require demolishing
23 or replacement of more than 50 per cent of the existing asset or appurtenant structure.
24 “Repair”, any project or construction work undertaken to return dysfunctional assets and
25 appurtenant structures to their intended and fully operational state; provided, however, that a
26 “repair” shall not disable or disrupt the operations of the assets and appurtenant structures for
27 more than 30 days.
28 “Routine maintenance”, any project or inspection work undertaken while an asset or
29 appurtenant structure is operating as intended or operating in a reasonably adequate manner in
30 which no major functions are disabled or disrupted; provided, however, that such project or
31 inspection shall be undertaken with the purpose of maintaining the intended functions of and
32 ensuring the proper operation of an asset or appurtenant structure.
33 “Tide gate”, a type of gate classified for the purposes of this act as a common flood
34 prevention structure that, when operating as designed, allows water to flow freely when the tide
35 sets in one direction but closes automatically and prevents the water from flowing in the opposite
36 direction.

37 SECTION 2. (a) All assets, appurtenant structures and other structures used to control the
38 flow of water at, into or out of the Town Line brook or Linden brook shall be under the care,
39 custody and control of the department of conservation and recreation. Such assets, appurtenant
40 structures or other structures shall include, but shall not be limited to, the tide gates attached to
41 the 107 Cutler bridge; provided, however, that such assets, appurtenant structures or other
42 structures shall not include the 8 box culverts structurally built into the Cutler bridge and trash
43 racks affixed to the upstream side of the Cutler bridge to protect the 8 box culverts from large
44 debris, sediment and other buildup. The department shall be responsible for the inspections,
45 emergency maintenance, routine maintenance, reconstruction and repairs to the assets and
46 appurtenant structures.
47 (b) The department of conservation and recreation shall: (i) not less than twice annually,
48 conduct routine maintenance and inspections of the assets and appurtenant structures identified
49 in subsection (a) to identify any damage, wear and tear or loss of functionality of the structures;
50 (ii) be responsible for, and conduct any routine maintenance on, the assets and appurtenant
51 structures identified in subsection (a), as required, to ensure that they are functioning properly;
52 and (iii) conduct any routine repairs, reconstruction and emergency repairs on the structures
53 identified in subsection (a), as required, to ensure that they are functioning properly.
54 (c) The department of conservation and recreation shall be responsible for the costs of the
55 routine maintenance, repairs, reconstructions, inspections and emergency repairs for the assets
56 and appurtenant structures identified in subsection (a).
57 (d) The department of conservation and recreation shall report the results of any
58 inspection conducted pursuant to subsection (b) to the Massachusetts Department of

75 Department of Transportation. The Massachusetts Department of Transportation shall be
76 responsible for the inspections, emergency maintenance, routine maintenance, reconstruction and
77 repairs to the assets and appurtenant structures.

80 structures identified in subsection (a) to identify any damage, wear and tear or loss of
