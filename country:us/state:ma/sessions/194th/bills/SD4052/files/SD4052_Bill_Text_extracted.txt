Dear Chair Rodrigues, Chair Michlewitz, Mr. Hurley and Mr. Carroll:
Massachusetts General Law Chapter 29, Section 2KKKKK requires that the Executive Office of Health
and Human Services (EOHHS) provide a report detailing all money transferred, credited or deposited into
and from the Nonpublic Ambulance Service Reimbursement Trust Fund (the Trust Fund), as well as the
reasons for any unspent funding and an analysis of the uniform assessment paid by nonpublic ambulance
providers.
In May 2023, EOHHS implemented a pause in the ambulance assessment. This pause was instituted
because CMS issued an Informational Bulletin on February 17, 2023, reiterating certain federal
requirements that pertain to health care-related taxes such as the nonpublic ambulance assessment,
including a prohibition on so-called “hold harmless” arrangements established entirely by non-state
actors.
This pause was lifted in March 2024, in conjunction with a new requirement that providers attest that they
were not parties to any hold harmless arrangements.

During the period July 1, 2024 through June 30, 2025, The Nonpublic Ambulance Service
Reimbursement Trust Fund collected a total of $32.3M in assessment revenue, received $34.2M in FFP,
for a total of $68.6M in total inflows. For this same period, The Nonpublic Ambulance Service
Reimbursement Trust Fund paid a total of $68.5M in supplemental payments (outflows).
Trust Fund expenditures are not consistent every quarter due to the timing of payments to providers,
which results in unexpended balances in the Trust at the end of each state fiscal year. The timing of
revenue receipts can vary by quarter, which may cause a negative balance in each quarter. Due to this, the
trust The Nonpublic Ambulance Service ending balance for the year is ($0.77M) for the year.
EOHHS has paid supplemental payments in anticipation of the payment of assessment payments that are
owed.
