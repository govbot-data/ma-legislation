Dear Attorney General Andrea Campbell, Senate Clerk Michael Hurley, and House Clerk Timothy Carroll:
Per Chapter 151 of the Acts of 2007 of the Massachusetts Session Laws (the "Act"), the Pension Reserves
Investment Management Board {"PRIM") is required to annually report PRIM's divestment from Sudanese
restricted securities. PRIM Staff provides investment managers with quarterly lists of restricted securities.
The managers are required to divest from said securities according to the timeline laid out in the Act,
which requires that 50% of PRIM's restrictive holdings be divested within 6 months of the security being
added to the restriction list and 100% divested within 12 months of the security being added.
Per subsection {b) of Section 3 of the Act, PRIM reports that no securities of companies on the scrutinized
companies list were acquired by PRIM in 2025. Per subsection (d) of Section 3 of the Act, PRIM reports
that investment managers with indirect holdings of restricted securities have not removed restricted
companies from their funds (except as part of their trading strategy-not because of divestment concerns)
nor have these managers created similar actively managed funds which exclude these restricted
securities. Per Section 4 of the Act, attached is the most recent scrutinized companies list, effective
January 1, 2025.
If you have any questions regarding this letter or scrutinized companies list, please do not hesitate to
contact Riya Shah, Investment Compliance Analyst, at {617)-946-8426 or via email at
rshah@mapension.com.
