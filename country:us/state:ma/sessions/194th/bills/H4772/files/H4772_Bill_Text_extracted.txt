An Act authorizing the Division of Capital Asset Management and Maintenance to grant
easements in certain parcels of land to the city of Lowell..
Whereas, The deferred operation of this act would tend to defeat its purpose, which is to
authorize forthwith the division of capital asset management and maintenance to grant certain
permanent and perpetual easements in the city of Lowell for conservation and recreation
purposes, therefore it is hereby declared to be an emergency law, necessary for the immediate
preservation of the public convenience.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. (a) Notwithstanding sections 32 to 37, inclusive, of chapter 7C of the
2 General Laws or any other general or special law to the contrary, the commissioner of capital
3 asset management and maintenance, in consultation with the commissioner of conservation and
4 recreation, may grant permanent and perpetual easements located in the city of Lowell, over,
5 under and through portions of property owned by the commonwealth under the care, control and
6 use of the department of conservation and recreation for conservation and recreation purposes, to
7 the city of Lowell, solely for the purposes of construction, maintenance, access, operation,
8 replacement, repair, patrol, abandon and removal of outfall structures and laying of mainline
9 drains.
1 of 4

10 (b) The grant of easements pursuant to subsection (a) shall be subject to the requirements
11 of: (i) this section and sections 3 through 5, inclusive; and (ii) any additional terms and
12 conditions the commissioner of capital asset management and maintenance, in consultation with
13 the commissioner of conservation and recreation, may prescribe.
14 (c) The portions of land, over, under and through which the easements may be granted
15 are more particularly described as 3 parcels of land located along the Merrimack river within the
16 city of Lowell, containing approximately 6,750 square feet of land shown as “Permanent
17 Easement” on a plan of land entitled “Bunker Hill Outfall Easement Plan” dated December 2024,
18 prepared by CDM Smith and on file with the city of Lowell. The 3 parcels are a portion of the
19 land described in an Order of Taking recorded in the Middlesex north registry of deeds in book
20 3830, page 70, which the commonwealth obtained for recreation and conservation purposes. The
21 parcel is further described in a plan of land entitled “Lowell Canal System” recorded in the
22 Middlesex north registry of deeds in plan book 157, plan 69.
23 (c) The terms of any easement granted pursuant to this section shall require that the
24 easement holder restore the surface condition to the equivalent or better condition as determined
25 by the department of conservation and recreation within 24 months after initiation of installation,
26 maintenance, repair and other work in the easement area.
27 SECTION 2. (a) The boundaries of any property interests to be conveyed pursuant to
28 section 1 shall be determined by the commissioner of capital asset management and
29 maintenance, in consultation with the commissioner of conservation and recreation after the
30 completion of a survey.
2 of 4

31 (b) The grantee shall assume the cost of any appraisals, surveys and other expenses
32 deemed necessary by the commissioner of capital asset management and maintenance pursuant
33 to section 1.
34 SECTION 3. (a)(1) As a condition of the conveyance described in section 1, the city of
35 Lowell shall compensate the commonwealth through improvements to the department of
36 conservation and recreation property in the vicinity of the property described in subsection (c) of
37 section 1.
38 (2) Said improvements shall have a value: (i) equal to or greater than the fair market
39 value of the property described in section 1, or its value in use as proposed, whichever is greater,
40 as determined by an independent appraisal; or (ii) in a sum equal to the fair market value of the
41 property or its value in use as proposed, whichever is greater, as determined by an independent
42 appraisal; or through some combination thereof.
43 (b) All money paid to the commonwealth by the city of Lowell pursuant to the
44 conveyance authorized by section 1 shall be deposited in the Conservation Trust established in
45 section 1 of chapter 132A of the General Laws.
46 (c) Improvements required pursuant to subsection (c) of section 1 by the city of Lowell
47 shall be agreed to in advance by and completed to the satisfaction of the commissioner of
48 conservation and recreation.
49 SECTION 4. The value of the property described in section 1 shall be determined by an
50 independent professional appraisal prepared in accordance with the usual and customary
51 professional appraisal practice by a qualified appraiser commissioned by the commissioner of
52 capital asset management and maintenance, in consultation with the commissioner of
3 of 4

53 conservation and recreation. The commissioner of capital asset management and maintenance
54 shall submit the appraisal and a report thereon to the inspector general for review and comment.
55 The inspector general shall review and approve the appraisal, and the review shall include an
56 examination of the methodology utilized for the appraisal. The inspector general shall prepare a
57 report of the review and file the report with the commissioner of capital asset management and
58 maintenance, and the commissioner shall submit copies of the appraisal, the report thereon and
59 the inspector general’s review and approval and comments, if any, to the house and senate
60 committees on ways and means and the chairs of the joint committee on state administration and
61 regulatory oversight at least 15 days prior to the execution of the conveyance authorized by this
62 act.
63 SECTION 5. (a) No instrument conveying, by or on behalf of the commonwealth, an
64 easement described in section 1 shall be valid unless such instrument provides that the easement
65 shall be used solely for the purposes described in section 1. The easement instrument shall
66 include that in the event that the easement ceases to be used by the city of Lowell, or its
67 successors or assigns, for the purposes described in section 1, the easement shall revert to the
68 commonwealth under the care, control and use by the department of conservation and recreation,
69 upon the terms and conditions that the commissioner of capital asset management and
70 maintenance, in consultation with the commissioner of conservation and recreation, may
71 determine.
72 (b) If the easement reverts to the commonwealth, any further disposition of the easement
73 shall be subject to sections 32 to 38, inclusive of chapter 7C of the General Laws and the prior
74 approval of the general court.
