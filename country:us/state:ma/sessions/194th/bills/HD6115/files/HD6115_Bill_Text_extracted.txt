II. DIVISION OF OCCUPATIONAL LICENSURE (DOL):
AGENY OVERVIEW
About DOL
The Division of Occupational Licensure (DOL) is an Agency within the Office of Consumer
Affairs and Business Regulation. DOL oversees 26 boards and a number of other programs,
which together license and regulate more than 500,000 individuals and businesses to practice
some 100 trades and professions in the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. DOL also licenses
and regulates Private Occupational Schools and oversees the Office of Public Safety and
Inspections.
Mission
DOL’s mission is to protect the public’s health, safety and welfare by licensing qualified
individuals and businesses to provide services to consumers, while ensuring the fair and
consistent enforcement of licensing laws and regulations. DOL also seeks to promote consumer
protection, a fair and competitive marketplace, and engage in education and outreach events.
Boards, Offices and Units
Introduction
DOL is organized into two core units: Boards of Registration; and the Office of Public Safety
and Inspections. These two units perform the core licensing and regulatory functions of the
Agency. Through a shared-services operational model, these units are supported by several other
offices including the Office of Investigations, Office of Legal Counsel, Office of Prosecutions,
Accounting Unit and the Technology, Analytics, and Digital Transformation Unit.
Boards of Registration
DOL's boards of registration carry out the Agency's mission by licensing individuals and
businesses in more than 100 trades and professions. Board members include licensed
professionals, as well as members of the public, who serve on their respective boards on a
voluntary basis. In addition to licensing qualified individuals, DOL boards of registration
investigate and resolve consumer complaints with the assistance of the Agency’s enforcement
units.

In addition to the boards of registration listed above, DOL also manages the Office of Private
Occupational School Education (OS). OS is responsible for the licensure and oversight of
private occupational schools operating in Massachusetts. Occupational schools are institutions
that offer courses, for profit or by charging tuition, for the purpose of educating and preparing
individuals for a business, trade, technical or industrial occupation, or any other vocational
purpose. OS is supported by a 12-member Advisory Council, consisting of representatives from
other state agencies, consumer advocacy organizations, and the occupational school industry.
The Advisory Council is chaired by the Undersecretary of Consumer Affairs and Business
Regulation (OCABR), DOL’s parent agency.
Office of Public Safety and Inspections
DOL’s Office of Public Safety and Inspections (OPSI) is responsible for the oversight of certain
infrastructure safety standards and the regulation of certain industries in Massachusetts. It assists
DOL in achieving the Agency’s mission of protecting the public’s health, safety, and welfare
through the inspection, investigation, and sanctioning of the activities and industries that it

Office of Investigations
The Office of Investigations (OI) serves as DOL’s front line of consumer protection. The formal
consumer complaint process is initiated when a consumer contacts or files a complaint with OI.
OI staff conduct investigations into allegations of unlicensed practice or professional misconduct
and inspects certain types of business establishments.
Office of Prosecutions
The Office of Prosecutions (OP) prosecutes complaints filed against licensed individuals and
businesses, as well as individuals and businesses engaged in the unlicensed practice of a trade or
profession falling under DOL’s jurisdiction.
Office of Legal Counsel
The Office of Legal Counsel (OLC) consists of three units: Board Counsel; Hearing Counsel;
and Public Records. Board Counsels provide legal advice to their assigned boards, offices, and
units as it relates to the performance of their day-to-day responsibilities, including the issuance
of licenses, the disciplinary process, and the conduct of board meetings. Hearings Counsel, also
known as Hearing Officers, generally conduct adjudicatory hearings between licensees or
unlicensed individuals and businesses alleged to have violated licensing statutes and regulations,
and the relevant board of registration. The Public Records Unit consists of two Records Access
Officers and other staff who process, review, and distribute records pursuant to requests from the
public.
Accounting
The Accounting Unit focuses on collecting and managing all revenue generated by the Agency.
DOL collects between $40 million and $48 million annually in over 275,000 individual
transactions for all of the licenses, permits, and inspections overseen by the Agency.
Technology, Analytics, and Digital Transformation Unit
The Technology, Analytics, and Digital Transformation Unit focuses on managing three legacy
technology platforms: Accela, My License One (MLO), and Inspection and Permitting System
(IPS) utilized by both the Boards of Registration and the Office of Public Safety and Inspections.
This Unit is currently developing an agency-wide digital transformation through the
implementation and deployment of a modern Licensing, Permitting, and Inspection solution.

• Licensing Platform Unification and Modernization Project: DOL currently operates
on three different software platforms in support of Agency programs. These three
platforms perform similar functions but cannot be cohesively integrated. In FY2022,
DOL secured capital funding to develop a target Business Capability Model (BCM) that
represents DOL’s core capabilities and identifies DOL’s differentiated capabilities when
compared against market offerings readily available in the industry. In continuation of the
Technology Modernization effort in FY2024, the DOL team completed a readiness
assessment, extensively mapping business processes and analyzing data set
documentation. The project team developed business process maps, which will inform
the business process redesign necessary to guide the configuration of the new platform
and provide the foundation for developing the core solution requirements.
