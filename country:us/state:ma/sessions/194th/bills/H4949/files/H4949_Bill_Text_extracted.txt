123 (b) There shall be within the division of health insurance a health insurance access bureau
124 overseen by a deputy commissioner, whose duties shall include, but not be limited to, subject to
125 the direction of the commissioner of insurance, rate review of premium rates for health benefit
126 plans offered, issued or renewed in the commonwealth, administration of the division's statutory
127 and regulatory authority for oversight of the small group and individual health insurance market,
128 oversight of affordable health plans, including coverage for young adults, as well as the
129 dissemination of appropriate information to consumers about health insurance coverage and
130 access to affordable products. The deputy commissioner shall: (i) protect the interests of
131 consumers of health insurance; (ii) encourage fair treatment of health care providers by health
132 insurers; (iii) enhance equity, access, quality and affordability in the health care system; (iv)
133 guard the solvency of health insurers; (v) work cooperatively with the health policy commission
134 and the center for health information and analysis to monitor health care spending; and (vi)
135 prioritize affordability of health insurance products during rate review.
7 of 38

136 (c) The deputy commissioner shall develop affordability standards to consider during rate
137 review; provided, however, that the commissioner’s review of a carrier’s rates shall adhere to
138 principles of solvency and actuarial soundness. Such standards shall consider the following:
139 (i) affordability for consumers, including the totality of costs paid by consumers of health
140 insurance for covered benefits including, but not limited to, the enrollee’s share of premium, out-
141 of-pocket maximum amounts, deductibles, copays, coinsurance and other forms of cost sharing
142 for health insurance coverage;
143 (ii) affordability for purchasers, including the totality of costs paid by purchasers of
144 health insurance including, but not limited to, premium costs, actuarial value of coverage for
145 covered benefits and the value delivered on health care spending in terms of improved quality
146 and cost efficiency; and
147 (iii) the impact of proposed rates on the commonwealth’s performance against the health
148 care cost growth benchmark established in section 9 of chapter 6D.
149 (d) The deputy commissioner shall review data and documents submitted to the division
150 including, but not limited to, any materials submitted as part of rate reviews, to examine the
151 causes of premium rate increases and excessive provider price variation.
152 (e)The commissioner shall appoint, at a minimum, the following employees: a first
153 deputy, a general counsel, a chief health economist, a chief actuary, a chief research analyst, and
154 a chief examiner. The appointed employees shall devote their full time to the duties of their
155 offices, shall be exempt from chapters 30 and 31 and shall serve at the pleasure of the
156 commissioner. The commissioner may appoint and remove additional employees, including
157 deputies, economists, analysts, examiners, assistant actuaries, inspectors, clerks and other
8 of 38

158 assistants as the work of the bureau may require. Such additional employees shall perform such
159 duties as the commissioner may prescribe.
160 (f) The commissioner shall make and collect an assessment against the carriers licensed
161 under chapters 175, 176A, 176B, 176E, 176F and 176G to pay for the expenses of the bureau.
162 The assessment shall be at a rate sufficient to produce $2,000,000 annually. In addition to that
163 amount, the assessment shall include an amount to be credited to the General Fund which shall
164 be equal to the total amount of funds estimated by the secretary of administration and finance to
165 be expended from the General Fund for indirect and fringe benefit costs attributable to the
166 personnel costs of the bureau. The assessment shall be allocated on a fair and reasonable basis
167 among all carriers licensed under said chapters. The funds produced by the assessments shall be
168 expended by the bureau, in addition to any other funds which may be appropriated, to assist in
169 defraying the general operating expenses of the bureau, and may be used to compensate
170 consultants retained by the division. A carrier licensed under said chapters shall pay the amount
171 assessed against it within 30 days after the date of the notice of assessment from the
172 commissioner.
