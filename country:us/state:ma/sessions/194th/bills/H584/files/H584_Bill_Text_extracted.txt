R. Gaskey and others relative to sports teams at public schools. Education.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act to ensure fairness and safety in school sports.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 71of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition,
2 is hereby amended by inserting the following section: -
3 Section 1E.
4 (a) No public school shall require any student athlete on a single-sex sports team to
5 participate in a match, or any part thereof, against an opposing team that includes one or more
6 athletes of the opposite sex. No penalty, including but not limited to loss of playing time or loss
7 of starting status, shall be imposed by a public school on such a student athlete for refusing to
8 play in such a match.
9 (b) No coach of a single-sex public school sports team shall be penalized by that school
10 for forfeiting a match against an opposing team because such opposing team includes one or
11 more athletes of the opposite sex.
1 of 2

12 (c) The Department of Elementary and Secondary Education, the Massachusetts
13 Interscholastic Athletic Association, or any equivalent school organization or an entity or board
14 that provides athletic officials, including, but not limited to, referees and umpires, for school
15 sports or athletic events shall not penalize any public school, single-sex public school sports
16 team, coach of such a team, or student athlete on such a team, for forfeiting or refusing to play in
17 a match against an opposing team because such opposing team includes one or more athletes of
18 the opposite sex, except that such a forfeit may be recorded as a loss.
19 For the purposes of this section, a “single-sex team” is any team that is designated as
20 either a girls’ team or a boys’ team, as opposed to a team that is designated as co-ed.
