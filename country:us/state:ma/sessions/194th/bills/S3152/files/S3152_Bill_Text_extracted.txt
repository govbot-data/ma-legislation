An Act establishing an ecologically-based mosquito management program in the Commonwealth
to protect public health.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 252 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024 Official Edition,
2 is hereby amended by striking out section 2 and inserting in place thereof the following section:-
3 Section 2. (a) As used in this chapter, the following words shall, unless the context
4 clearly requires otherwise, have the following meanings:
5 “Adulticiding”, the use of pesticides intended to kill adult mosquitoes.
6 “Disease vector”, a species of mosquito that amplifies or transmits an illness, including
7 Eastern Equine Encephalitis or West Nile Virus, among human or other animal populations by
8 salivary gland fluid injection during biting.
9 “Larviciding”, the use of pesticides intended to kill larval or pupal mosquitoes.
10 “PFAS”, a class of fluorinated organic chemicals containing at least one fully fluorinated
11 carbon atom.
1 of 5

12 “Source reduction”, reducing or eliminating the ability of larval habitats to support the
13 development of larvae, including, but not limited to, removing artificial sources of standing or
14 stagnant water and restoring natural flows and wetland systems to improve access by predators,
15 water quality and overall aquatic health.
16 (b) There shall be a mosquito management board, in this chapter called the board, which
17 shall consist of:
18 (1) 1 employee of the department of environmental protection designated by its
19 commissioner who shall serve as chair; 1 employee of the department of agriculture designated
20 by its commissioner; 1 employee of the department of fish and game designated by its
21 commissioner; and 2 employees of the department of public health designated by its
22 commissioner, 1 of whom shall have expertise in environmental health; and
23 (2) 6 members designated by the secretary of energy and environmental affairs,
24 including: 1 person representing a statewide land conservation organization; 1 person
25 representing a regional or statewide river protection organization; 1 person representing
26 beekeepers; 1 person representing a group that works to protect native pollinators; 1 person who
27 is an expert in pesticides and mosquito control; and 1 person who is an ecological risk assessor
28 with a background in ecotoxicology. Each member designated in this paragraph shall be
29 designated for a term of 4 years, except for persons designated to fill vacancies, who shall serve
30 for the remainder of the unexpired term, and shall be eligible for reappointment; provided,
31 however, that no member shall serve for more than 2 full terms.
32 The designation of any member of said board may be revoked at any time by the
33 designator, and in such case or in case of the resignation or disability of any member, their
2 of 5

34 successor shall be designated in the same manner as in the original designation. Members shall
35 be entitled to reasonable travel and other expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
36 (c) The board shall regulate and oversee all disease vector mosquito management
37 activities in the commonwealth and prepare a science-based statewide integrated pest
38 management mosquito-borne disease management plan, hereinafter called the plan. State
39 agencies and subdivisions, including but not limited to mosquito control districts, mosquito
40 control projects and municipalities, shall adhere to the plan when conducting mosquito
41 management activities. The plan shall provide an ecologically sustainable approach to mosquito
42 population management that includes, but is not limited to, the following provisions:
43 (1) preventative measures to protect humans and livestock from mosquito-borne diseases,
44 including surveillance, monitoring and public education on eliminating breeding sites and taking
45 personal protective actions, which shall be emphasized in the plan;
46 (2) source reductions, which shall be the preferred management measures; and
47 (3) to the extent necessary and to be implemented only after preventative measures and
48 source reduction efforts have been implemented, larvicides and adulticides; provided, however,
49 that aerial application of mosquito-control adulticides is prohibited; provided further, that
50 ground adulticide applications shall be limited to locations where mosquito-borne disease
51 is detected in mosquitoes, other animals or humans in the current year; and provided further, that
52 the use of larvicides or adulticides containing PFAS or other fluorinated compounds is
53 prohibited.
54 (d) The board shall post a public notice of availability of the draft plan in the
55 Massachusetts environmental policy act office’s environmental monitor and provide a 60-day
3 of 5

56 public comment period. The board shall duly consider and respond to comments received and
57 finalize the plan. The plan shall be updated every 3 years, or more frequently, with public
58 review. Annual operational plans and practices of any public entity conducting mosquito
59 management activities shall be consistent with the statewide plan and revised annually based on
60 efficacy data and mitigation of non-target impacts.
61 (e) Additionally, the board shall: (i) annually review and certify integrated pest
62 management mosquito management plans submitted by mosquito districts, municipalities and
63 any other public agency conducting mosquito management activities; (ii) ensure consistency
64 with the statewide plan; (iii) create policies and oversee procedures for monitoring and
65 responding to vector-borne diseases; (iv) advise the governor when a public health emergency
66 arises due to disease-bearing mosquitoes imminently threatening to public health; (v) authorize
67 and carry out investigations; (vi) carry out disease-bearing mosquito management programs
68 when a significant area of the state is affected; (vii) provide education materials on mosquito
69 borne diseases, best practices for eliminating artificial standing water and personal protection
70 measures to avoid mosquito bites; (viii) ensure that all municipalities are provided with access to
71 mosquito surveillance upon request and payment of reasonable fees; (ix) establish and maintain a
72 process for municipalities and landowners to opt out of larviciding or adulticiding, except
73 following a public health emergency declared by the governor that includes a finding that
74 disease-bearing mosquitoes are present at high or critical risk levels determined by the
75 department of public health in the locations to be treated; (x) keep appropriate records of board
76 operations and activities as well as all mosquito management efforts in the commonwealth; and
77 (xi) annually, on or before November 1, publish on the website of the department of
4 of 5

78 environmental protection a report on the activities and expenditures of the board and mosquito
79 control districts or projects and the efficacies and impacts of such work.
