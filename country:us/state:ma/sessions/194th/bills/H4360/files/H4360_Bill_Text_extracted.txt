An Act requiring human trafficking recognition training for certain hospitality workers.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 140 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after section 6B the
2 following section:-
3 Section 6C. (a) For the purposes of this section, “qualified accommodation” shall mean a
4 hotel, motel, lodging house or bed and breakfast establishment, as those terms are defined in
5 section 1 of chapter 64G; provided, that “qualified accommodation” shall not include
6 accommodations exempt from the room occupancy excise established in section 2 of chapter
7 64G.
8 (b) Every licensed innholder operating a qualified accommodation shall require every
9 employee of the qualified accommodation to complete a human trafficking recognition training
10 program. The training program shall be approved by the attorney general and shall be provided
11 by organizations or providers approved by the attorney general and included on the list pursuant
12 to paragraph (1) of subsection (d). The training program may be incorporated in the innholder’s
13 existing training programs.
1 of 2

14 (c) Every licensed innholder operating a qualified accommodation shall post in plain
15 view, in the lobby or common area and in any public restroom of the qualified accommodation, a
16 written notice developed by the attorney general pursuant to subsection (e).
17 (d)(1) The attorney general shall make available a list of approved human trafficking
18 recognition training programs that meet the criteria of paragraph (2) for use by a licensed
19 innholder operating a qualified accommodation pursuant to subsection (b); provided, that a
20 training program may be developed by a federal, state or nonprofit organization.
21 (2) Human trafficking recognition training programs required under this section shall
22 include, but shall not be limited to, training on: (i) the nature of human trafficking; (ii) how
23 human trafficking is defined pursuant to sections 50 and 51 of chapter 265; and (iii) how to
24 identify a human trafficking victim, as defined in section 20M of chapter 233.
25 (e) The attorney general shall develop a standard written notice to be posted by every
26 licensed innholder operating a qualified accommodation pursuant to subsection (c); provided,
27 that the standard written notice shall include the national human trafficking hotline telephone
28 number and other resources as determined by the attorney general.
29 (f) The attorney general shall promulgate regulations necessary to implement this section.
