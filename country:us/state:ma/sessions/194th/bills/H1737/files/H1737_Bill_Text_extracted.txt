An Act relative to historic homes.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 266 of the General Laws, as appearing in 2016 Official Edition, is hereby
2 amended by inserting after section 95 the following new section:-
3 Section 95A.
4 In this section, “historic home” shall mean any dwelling house listed listed in the State
5 Register of Historic Places established and maintained by the Massachusetts Historical
6 Commission pursuant to G. L. c. 9, section 26C or identified by a plaque identifying the
7 homestead as an historic homestead pursuant to G. L. c. 9, section 27D.
8 Whoever willfully, intentionally and without right removes items, including, but not
9 limited to, copper gutters, copper wiring, aluminum or brick; displaces, destroys, defaces, mars
1 of 2

10 or injures the walls, wainscoting or any other part of any historical home, or the appurtenances
11 there of, by cutting, breaking, writing or otherwise, shall be punished by a fine of not less than
12 $100 and not more than $1000 or by imprisonment for not more than 2 years. Any person
13 convicted under the provisions of this section shall, in addition to the fine assessed, reimburse
14 the historic home owner for the total amount of damage incurred.
