This is a report of the Massachusetts Commission on Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender
Aging (MA LGBT) as required by M.G.L. Chapter 3, Section 71.
Commission Activities: Calendar Year 2023
In 2023, the Commission focused on community engagement and feedback in addition to
advocating for statewide policies.
Listening Session
In September 2023, in lieu of a commission meeting, the Commission held a listening session for
LGBTQ+ older adults and allies in New Bedford. The Commission cosponsored the listening
session with South Coast LGBTQ+ Network and brought together LGBTQ+ older adults,
caregivers, aging service providers with Commission members to address the strengths, needs,
and challenges of community members. This session also served to introduce the community to
the Commission to foster future trust and collaboration.
Takeaways from New Bedford Session:
• Participants named several ways that they get involved in the community but say that
some older adults may not be aware of events that are only posted on social media.
• Safety was noted as a concern in the community as well as housing.
• Some participants mentioned wanting more access to LGBTQ+ friendly healthcare.
Policy Advocacy
The Commission gave continued attention to LGBTQ+ and aging related policies and legislation
in the Commonwealth and across the country including:
• An Act to improve quality and oversight of long-term care (MA)
o Members of the Commission testified at the Joint Committee on Elder Affairs
hearing in May 2023 to support the inclusion of LGBTQ+ Long Term Care Bill
of Rights in this legislation.
• FDA policy for blood donation for low-risk gay and bisexual men (National)

Logo and Name Change Proposition
The Commission began the process of changing the language used by the Commission from
“LGBT” to “LGBTQ” to be inclusive of individuals who identify as “Queer” or “Questioning.”
This process will involve changing the name of the Commission as well as the logo.
Funding
In the summer of 2023, the Commission received its first year of funding from a Senate
amendment sponsored by Senator Jehlen. The amount was $50,000 for FY2024. This funding
was allocated to the LGBTQIA+ Aging Project to support the work and activities of the
Commission.

• New Commission Members: Julie Watt Faqir (Home Care Aide Council), Barry Callis
(Department of Public Health), Alejandro Marcel (Transgender Older Adult; appointed

Commission Activities: Calendar Year 2024
The year 2024 marked the 10-year anniversary of the Commission. To honor the work that the
Commission has done and look forward to the future, the Commission began the process of
strategic planning.
Strategic Planning
In April 2024, the Massachusetts Commission on LGBTQ+ Aging began the process of strategic
planning to improve the quality of life for older LGBTQ+ individuals over the next five years.
This strategy emphasizes the development of impactful state policies and programs, building
inclusive partnerships with diverse stakeholders, participating in robust advocacy efforts,
guaranteeing equitable access to comprehensive services, and nurturing a strong sense of
community and belonging. This strategic plan will be released in calendar year 2025.
The Commission established a new mission statement and vision statement to guide work in the
years ahead.
• Mission: The Massachusetts Commission on LGBTQ+ Aging enhances the quality of life
for LGBTQ+ older adults in Massachusetts through state policy and program
recommendations, collaborations, partnerships, and advocacy.

• Vision: Massachusetts’ LGBTQ+ older adults and caregivers will have equitable access
to programs, services, benefits, social support, and community that maximize their
quality of life and enable them to thrive as they age.
Through the process of strategic planning, the Commission has identified nine priority areas with
goals and key objectives. Three of these priority areas relate to the operations and advancement
of the Commission, while the remaining six are specific to the community the Commission
serves.
1. Infrastructure for Massachusetts Commission on LGBTQ+ Aging
2. Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DEI) Lens for Commission work
3. Public Visibility and Advancement of Massachusetts Commission on LGBTQ+ Aging
4. Statewide Needs Assessment of LGBTQ+ Older Adults
5. Social Support and Community Engagement for LGBTQ+ Older Adults
6. Affirming and Accessible Healthcare for LGBTQ+ Older Adults
7. Safe and Affordable Housing for LGBTQ+ Older Adults
8. Policy and Advocacy for LGBTQ+ Older Adults
9. LGBTQ+ Cultural Competency Training
Policy Advocacy
The Commission gave continued attention to LGBTQ+ and aging related policies and legislation
in the Commonwealth and across the country including:
• An Act Relative to LGBT and HIV-Positive Seniors in the Commonwealth
• An Act Relative to Massachusetts Home Care Eligibility
• LGBTQ+ Long Term Care Bill of Rights
o On September 26, 2024, Gov. Maura Healy signed An Act to improve quality
and oversight of long-term care into law to improve inclusivity and quality of
care at assisted living and Long-Term Care (LTC) facilities. This piece of
legislation contains language of the LGBTQ+ Long Term Care Bill of Rights
which specifically names protections for LGBTQ+ older adults when seeking
care at these facilities. Staff will now be required to take trainings on the care
of LGBTQ+ older adults and older adults living with HIV. R LTC facilities
are prohibited from discriminating based on a person’s gender identity, sexual
orientation, or HIV status. Furthermore, LGBTQ+ older adults and older
adults living with HIV will have enhanced protections related to admission,
restroom use, room assignments, medical care, and record-keeping.
o The Commission was instrumental in advocating for the inclusion of this
provision. Massachusetts now joins four other states and the District of
Columbia in including a LGBTQ+ bill of rights specific to long-term care.

Listening Sessions
In June, July, and August 2024, the Commission continued to hold community listening sessions
in Worcester and Boston as well as virtually. Listening sessions serve as a foundation for the
work that has been done by the Commission. Incorporating community voices into priorities and
actions has been crucial to the success of this commission.
Takeaways from Worcester Session:
• Concerns were voiced about attitudes towards the LGBTQ+ community in the Central
Massachusetts region.
• Most participants are living alone and had concerns about aging alone without a partner
or support network.
• Housing was named as a major concern for participants.
Takeaways from Boston Session:
• Loneliness is a major concern for LGBTQ+ older adults.
• Resources and allocations can be confusing in the Boston area. There is potential to use
LGBTQ+ friendly community meal sites as a location to distribute information more
systematically.
• Participants suggested creating a physical safe space for gay and transgender older adults
as well as prioritizing funding for transgender health.
• With the success of the Pryde, Boston’s first LGBTQ+ friendly housing for older adults,
participants voiced wanting to continue prioritizing additional LGBTQ+ friendly housing
for older adults.
Takeaways from Virtual Session:
• Transportation is inaccessible in parts of the Commonwealth, especially the Cape region
for older adults.
• Concerns were voiced about aging alone without a partner or family. Ideas were shared
about fun and engaging social activities.
• Participants named affordable housing as a major concern.
Collaboration
The Commission had the opportunity to collaborate with several community partners:
• The Commission provided feedback on the Department of Public Health’s (DPH)
campaign “Care That Fits You.” This campaign seeks to educate and connect gay,
bisexual, and MSM to resources and information related to sexual health. The
Commission provided feedback on imagery, language, and logistical considerations, with
specific consideration of LGBTQ+ older adults, to MORE Advertising, the firm

managing the campaign. This collaboration aligns with the Commission’s priority of
increasing visibility of older adults in DPH’s educational materials.
• The Pryde, Boston’s first LGBTQ+ friendly senior housing complex, opened in June
2024. The Commission, particularly the housing workgroup, kept up to date on the
progress of this new community and distributed information and updates related to
residency availability. This collaboration aligns with the Commission’s priority of
increasing access to LGBTQ+ friendly housing for older adults.
• Dr. Luis Alvarez-Hernandez Assistant Professor, School of Social Work, Clinical
Practice Department, Core Faculty, Center on Emerging Infectious Diseases, at Boston
University presented to the Commission regarding considerations for LGBTQ+ older
adult immigrants in the state of Massachusetts and nationally. This presentation advanced
the knowledge of Commission members related to this specific population as well as
contributed to the Commission's commitment to increasing diversity, equity, and
inclusion.

Workgroup Recommendations and Accomplishments
2024 Strategic Planning Workgroup
In 2024-2025, the Massachusetts Commission on LGBTQ+ Aging developed a 5-year strategic
plan to serve LGBTQ+ older adults, their families, and caregivers. To achieve this, the
Commission formed a strategic planning subcommittee that convened from April 2024 to early
2025 to thoroughly explore current research, demographic trends, and emerging concerns.
Commissioners performed a comprehensive strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats
(SWOT) analysis. Additionally, the Commission hosted listening sessions in New Bedford,
Worcester, Boston, and virtually where LGBTQ+ older adults, caregivers, and family members
as well as aging service providers from Massachusetts actively participated and shared their
insights on the essential services and programs needed as they age. As a result of this process,
Commissioners produced an updated mission statement, vision statement, goals, key objectives,
and identified nine priority areas with goals and key objectives. Strategic planning updates were
provided to the full Commission during quarterly meetings.

Workgroup Activity
The Commission contains several established workgroups each with specific topic areas:
Housing, Legal /Policy Issues, Long Term Supports and Services, Public Health, and Senior
Centers/Community Engagement. In this year, the workgroup meetings focused on goals for the
strategic plan specific to the workgroup’s specific topic and goals. Each workgroup engaged in
the SWOT analysis and met to determine priority areas of importance to the Commission.
