Dear Clerk Carroll, Clerk Hurley and Secretary Gorzkowicz:
I am writing on behalf of the Massachusetts Office for Victim Assistance (MOVA) and the Victim and
Witness Assistance Board, relative to the Massachusetts Victims of Violent Crime Compensation
Program.
The Victims of Violent Crime Compensation Program is a state and federal supported program which
provides financial assistance to or on behalf of victims of violent crime and, in certain cases, to their
families, to cover certain crime-related expenses or losses. Federal funding for this program comes
primarily from the Victims of Crime Act (VOCA), which allocates funds to states from the fines,
penalties, and forfeitures paid to the Crime Victims Fund (CVF).
On July 1, 2025, the Massachusetts Legislature statutorily transferred the administration and funding
management of the program from the Attorney General’s Office (AGO) to MOVA. This transition is
intended to enhance survivors’ access to services and streamline administrative oversight and
compliance with funding stream requirements. MOVA is the identified state administering agency for
VOCA victim assistance funds, which are administered by the Office for Victims of Crime (OVC), the
same office administering Victims of Crime Compensation funds. As a result, MOVA has established
strong working relationships with the staff and leadership of OVC. Moreso, federal requirements
mandate that MOVA-funded programs provide survivors with information and support in applying for
victim compensation, giving MOVA relevant and timely insight into challenges and barriers to accessing
the program and the ability to respond through policy or regulatory change.
Attorney General Andrea Joy Campbell, Chair | Liam T. Lowney, Executive Director

Reporting
Pursuant to item 0840-0100 in §2, c. 9 of the acts of 2025, the Chair of the Victim and Witness
Assistance Board shall submit to the General Court and the Secretary of Administration and Finance a
report detailing the claims submitted to the State Treasurer for payment under item 0840-0004 of said
section, indicating both the number and costs for each category of claim.
This report is reflective of claims submitted to the state for payment from September 1, 2025 through
December 31, 2025. Although Massachusetts G. L. c. 258C was amended as of July 1, 2025 to make
MOVA the agency responsible for administering Victim Compensation, the AGO continued to process
payments through August 31, 2025. MOVA assumed responsibility for processing payments on
September 1, 2025.
In total, there have been 216 claims submitted to the State Treasurer for payment during this timeframe,
totaling $661,981.79. During this time, the top three claim expenses submitted include coverage or
reimbursement for funeral and burial expenses (33 totaling $234,531.08), lost wages (10 totaling
$161,515.71), and mental health support (86 totaling $131,458.34). (Please see Table 1).

This data indicates the prevalence of the most severe forms of victimization, particularly homicide,
assault resulting in disability and loss of work, child sexual assault, and domestic violence. Ensuring
timely responses and resources for these survivors is critical to address not only the immediate financial
impact they are facing, but also to reduce the added stress and secondary victimization experienced, and
to ensure the delivery of comprehensive victim services beyond compensation alone. The growing
volume and complexity of victim compensation claims require additional staff and resources to ensure
timely, high-quality support. MOVA is specifically committed to providing time sensitive responses to
homicide claims seeking compensation for funeral and burial expenses, which require additional staff
time.
Additionally, since assuming responsibility for the victim compensation program, MOVA has
experienced increased operational demands. MOVA assumed responsibility for processing payments
through the state payment system which requires significant staff resources and expertise without any
additional operational funding. MOVA has included a request for additional funding in the FY27 budget
to add staff and necessary resources to support the program. These funds are essential to support the
program effectively and to address the operational challenges encountered to date. MOVA remains
