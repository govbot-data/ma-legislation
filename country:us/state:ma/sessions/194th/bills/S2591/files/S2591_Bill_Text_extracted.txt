39 SECTION 2. Chapter 69 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the
40 following 2 sections:-
41 Section 40. (a) As used in this chapter, the following words shall have the following
42 meanings, unless the context clearly requires otherwise:
43 “Early college”, a statewide joint high school and higher education initiative established
44 through formal partnerships between Massachusetts public school districts and any accredited
45 public, private or municipal institution of higher education located in the commonwealth, for the
46 purpose of providing structured opportunities for high school students, particularly those from
47 communities that are underrepresented in higher education, to earn college credit that is
48 transferable and applicable toward a postsecondary degree or credential while continuing to meet
49 high school graduation requirements.
50 “Designated early college program”, an early college partnership between a public high
51 school and an accredited public, private or municipal institution of higher education officially
52 sanctioned and approved by the state.
53 (b) There shall be an early college joint committee, hereinafter referred to in this section
54 as the ECJC, which shall be responsible for: (i) monitoring and overseeing implementation of
55 early college; (ii) approving statewide goals and standards for early college recommended by the
56 office; and (iii) giving final approval to applicants for designation. The ECJC shall develop
57 recommendations that include, but are not limited to, (i) how to advance the commonwealth’s
3 of 12

58 goals related to the sustainability, growth and accessibility of early college programming; (ii)
59 how to assist the office in establishing and monitoring statewide performance requirements and
60 enrollments; (iii) how to foster collaboration between the department of higher education and the
61 department of elementary and secondary education on the early college initiative; (iv) how to
62 advance the alignment of early college programming with statewide and regional workforce
63 development goals; and (v) how to promote meaningful stakeholder engagement between
64 institutions of higher education and public school districts. The ECJC shall consist of: the chair
65 of the board of higher education; the chair of the board of elementary and secondary education;
66 one member of the board of higher education, to be appointed by the chair; one member of the
67 board of elementary and secondary education, to be appointed by the chair; and the secretary of
68 education. The commissioner of elementary and secondary education and the commissioner of
69 higher education, or their respective designees, shall serve as non-voting members of the ECJC.
70 (c) There shall be an office of early college, herein referred to in this section as the office.
71 The office shall be administered and managed jointly by the commissioner of elementary and
72 secondary education and the commissioner of higher education. Said commissioners shall enter
73 into a memorandum of understanding, subject to the approval of the secretary of education, that
74 governs the administration, coordination, and oversight of the office. The memorandum shall (i)
75 define the respective roles and responsibilities of each department; (ii) establish mechanisms for
76 shared decision-making and accountability; (iii) ensure, to the extent practicable, equitable
77 participation by both departments in the management and strategic direction of the office; and
78 (iv) provide a process for the secretary of education to resolve interagency matters that affect the
79 coordination, oversight or implementation of early college programming. The memorandum
80 shall be reviewed and updated not less than every 3 years, or more frequently as agreed upon by
4 of 12

81 the commissioners. The departments shall publish and maintain an updated description of the
82 memorandum on their websites and shall notify the chairs of the joint committee on education
83 and the joint committee on higher education 30 days in advance of any changes.
84 (d) The office shall: (i) oversee all early college programs; (ii) receive and review new
85 applications for early college designation; (iii) recommend any designations to be awarded to
86 applicants; (iv) provide logistical and technical support to applicants and designated early college
87 programs as needed; (v) track and report on the performance of early college participants in the
88 aggregate and disaggregate by student subgroup; (vi) evaluate the impact of early college on
89 postsecondary access, student success and progress towards meeting statewide goals for early
90 college; (vii) and identify and pursue further innovative approaches.
91 All early college programs shall:
92 (i) help prepare students by providing equitable access to college coursework and
93 pathways that support successful transitioning to and completion of postsecondary education;
94 (ii) encourage the cooperative or shared use of resources, personnel and facilities between
95 school districts, public and municipal colleges, independent colleges or universities, and
96 employers;
97 (iii) emphasize involvement of parents or legal guardians and provide consistent
98 counseling, advising and conferencing with parents and legal guardians so that families and
99 students can make responsible decisions regarding course selection and may track the student's
100 academic progress and success;
5 of 12

101 (iv) develop methods for early identification of potential participating students in the
102 middle grades and through high school and provide outreach to those students to promote
103 academic preparation and awareness of early college programs; and
104 (v) develop strategies to identify and engage currently underserved student populations in
105 higher education.
106 (e) The office shall establish the standards and information it deems necessary to be
107 included in an application for designation as an official early college program and shall develop
108 a process to ensure that designated early college programs continue to meet said standards over
109 time. Such standards shall encourage, where appropriate, alignment between general education
110 courses and career-oriented courses and pathways. In developing and applying such standards,
111 the office shall make reasonable allowance for regional differences, including the distinct
112 challenges and capacities of rural and urban school districts, to ensure equitable access to early
113 college opportunities across the Commonwealth.
114 (f) The office shall develop a streamlined designation application process which shall, to
115 the extent practicable, avoid any unnecessary or duplicative application requirements for
116 applicants who have, in a separate and previous application, been approved as a designated early
117 college program and who plan on utilizing proven and effective early college models and
118 pathways. In developing this process, the office shall consult with and solicit feedback from the
119 early college working group established in subsection (l) and shall consider a model whereby an
120 institution of higher education need not be required to submit an application for designation more
121 than once.
6 of 12

122 (g) The office shall, at least once every 3 years, disseminate information and guidance on
123 statewide and regional labor market trends and conditions to designated early college programs
124 and applicants for designation in order to ensure early college pathways are attuned to statewide
125 and regional labor market needs. As needed, the office shall collaborate with the executive office
126 of labor and workforce development and other regional workforce intermediaries and agencies to
127 determine the extent to which early college pathways are aligned with regional and statewide
128 employment needs and shall update standards for designation accordingly.
129 (h) The office shall, in consultation with relevant stakeholders including, but not limited
130 to, the working group established in subsection (l), develop recommendations for the creation
131 and implementation of early college technical pathways including, but not limited to, pathways
132 offered through vocational-technical schools and programs pursuant to Chapter 74. Such
133 recommendations shall address any technical, regulatory or operational barriers to participation
134 and identify strategies to support the integration of high-quality career and technical education
135 into the early college framework and may include, without limitation, recommendations for
136 amending designation criteria or standards to encourage participation by such programs. Nothing
137 in this section shall be construed to require the inclusion of Chapter 74 programs in early college.
138 (i) Annually, the office shall review the per-credit reimbursements received by public and
139 private institutions of higher education for their participation in a designated early college
140 program. Annually, not later than December 1, the office shall submit recommendations on
141 reimbursement rates for the forthcoming school year to the house and senate committees on ways
142 and means, the joint committee on higher education, and the joint committee on education. In
143 formulating its recommendations, the office may consider several factors including, but not
144 limited to: (i) the site where the course is taught and the modality by which it is taught; (ii)
7 of 12

145 whether the course has extra costs associated with enrollment; and (iii) whether there are
146 minimum numbers of participating students per section offered.
147 (j) The office shall propose, subject to approval by the ECJC, multi-year goals for the
148 commonwealth for the attainment of college degrees and other post-secondary credentials for
149 students participating in early college programs. Such goals shall include, but not be limited to,
150 recommended overall statewide targets and goals as well as goals for increasing support for
151 historically underserved student populations in higher education. The office shall be responsible
152 for ensuring annual, timely public reporting of progress statewide and by districts and schools
153 towards said goals.
154 (k) Annually, not later than September 30, the office shall submit detailed reports on all
155 current and projected expenditures and fiscal operations related to early college to the ECJC. The
156 ECJC may review and comment on the fiscal operations of the office and may issue formal
157 recommendations to the office.
158 (l) The ECJC shall convene a working group for the purpose of advising the office and
159 the ECJC on matters related to the development, implementation and expansion of early college
160 programs in the commonwealth. The working group shall meet no fewer than 4 times annually
161 and include, but not be limited to, representatives from organizations, associations and entities
162 that are directly affected by or have a demonstrated interest in early college programs, including
163 those representing students and families, secondary and post-secondary educational institutions,
164 early college advocacy groups, state government and workforce and community stakeholders.
165 The commissioner of elementary and secondary education and the commissioner of higher
166 education shall determine the scope, structure and procedures of the working group and shall
8 of 12

167 regularly solicit input and recommendations from its members to inform policymaking and
168 strategic planning.
169 (m) Where required, each designated early college program shall enter into an agreement
170 with at least 1 postsecondary institution that provides students with opportunities to receive
171 postsecondary credits during the student's participation in the program. The agreement shall
172 establish each party’s commitments and duties in administering the designated early college
173 program, including but not limited to program coordination, student support, faculty support,
174 career partnership coordinating and performance monitoring. Partner high schools and colleges
175 shall make best efforts to ensure that, consistent with goals and recommendations developed by
176 the MassTransfer program established in section 48 of chapter 15A, college credits automatically
177 transfer to Massachusetts public institutions of higher education upon completion of the program
178 and admittance to one of those public institutions. In addition, partnering high schools and
179 colleges may consider automatic admission to the participating college upon successful
180 completion of the early college program.
181 (n) All early college programs shall be open to all enrolled students, on a space available
182 basis. If the number of applicants exceeds the number of available seats in an early college
183 program, the designated early college program shall conduct a lottery to determine admission.
184 The lottery shall be conducted in a neutral and unbiased manner and shall not have the purpose
185 or effect of discriminating against any student or group of students on the basis of race, color,
186 national origin, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, disability or any other protected
187 characteristic under state or federal law. Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to prevent
188 a school or school district from instituting reasonable prerequisite conditions for students seeking
9 of 12

189 admission to a specialized or advanced course or program, provided that such conditions are
190 designed to ensure student success in said courses or programs.
191 (o) Annually, by December 31, each designated early college program shall file a report
192 with the office of early college, on performance measures, including, but not limited to the
193 following categories:
194 (i) high school graduation rates of participating students;
195 (ii) percentage of participating students who complete the program;
196 (iii) percentage of participating students who gain any postsecondary credits;
197 (iv) credits earned by participating students in early college programs, including
198 percentage of each program incorporating early college to gain at least 12 credits;
199 (v) percentage of participating students who immediately enroll in postsecondary
200 education;
201 (vi) percentage of participating students who persist to a second year in postsecondary
202 education;
203 (vii) percentage of participating students in a 6-year cohort who attain postsecondary
204 degrees; and
205 (viii) college and career outcomes of participating students.
206 Annually, not later than July 1, the the commissioner of elementary and secondary
207 education and the commissioner of higher education shall prepare an aggregate report for the
208 senate and house chairs of the joint committee on higher education and the joint committee on

209 education, and shall establish and maintain a publicly accessible display of the overall success of
210 early college programs, which shall include but not be limited to the above performance
211 indicators and other relevant data.
212 Section 41. (a) Pursuant to regulations to be promulgated by the board of elementary and
213 secondary education, as needed to implement this section, all public high schools shall offer a
214 program to students before the completion of their 12th grade year that incorporates at least one
215 of the following: (1) early college, as defined in section 40; (2) dual enrollment; (3) industry-
216 recognized credentialing in high school; (4) advanced placement courses; (5) international
217 baccalaureate programs; (6) cooperative education programs, or (7) other approaches for college
218 and career programs. A school district may apply to the commissioner for a waiver from the
219 requirements of this section, and the commissioner may grant such a waiver for a period not to
220 exceed 3 years, provided the district demonstrates substantial hardship, including but not limited
221 to significant fiscal or geographic barriers, and submits a written plan outlining the steps it will
222 take to come into compliance with the requirements of this section. Upon expiration, waivers
223 may be renewed at the discretion of the commissioner.
224 (b) The board of elementary and secondary education shall promulgate regulations to
225 implement this section.
