SENATE DOCKET, NO. 2388 FILED ON: 1/17/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1753
By Mr. O'Connor, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 1753) of Patrick M. O'Connor,
Michael O. Moore, Paul K. Frost, David T. Vieira and other members of the General Court for
legislation to prohibit Class C synthetic chemical substance, or agent that has mind altering or
mood changing effects in correctional facilities. Public Safety and Homeland Security.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act strengthening synthetic drug laws in correctional facilities.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 32B or Chapter 94C of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting at the end of subsection (a) the following
3 sentence:-
4 "“Any Class C substance defined in section 31 of this chapter, synthetic chemical
5 substance, or agent that has mind altering or mood changing effects is strictly prohibited in
6 correctional facilities, and the distribution, possession or use of such chemical substances would
7 be a felony in the state of Massachusetts, punishable with minimum jail or prison sentence of a
8 minimum 2.5 years on or after their current prison sentence"
