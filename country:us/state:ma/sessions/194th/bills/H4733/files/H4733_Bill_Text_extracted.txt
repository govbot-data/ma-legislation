HOUSE . . . . . . . . No. 4733
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
________________________________________
HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, November 17, 2025.
The committee on Consumer Protection and Professional Licensure, to
whom were referred the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 321) of
Bruce J. Ayers for legislation to prohibit the practice of body piercing, so-
called, unless licensed by the Department of Public Health; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 331) of Daniel Cahill for legislation to
authorize smoking bars in certain gaming establishments; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 335) of Tackey Chan, Erika Uyterhoeven
and others for legislation to require that real estate brokers fees be paid by
lessors; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 349) of Mike
Connolly for legislation to establish a special commission (including
members of the General Court) to examine repealing restrictions on the
offer and sale of discounted alcoholic beverages; the petition (accompanied
by bill, House, No. 351) of Mark J. Cusack for legislation to authorize cities
and towns to assess a fee for the sale of a liquor license by a licensee to
another licensee; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 356) of
Janel Munoa relative to horse racing or simulcasting licensing in cities and
towns; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 366) of Michael J.
Finn relative to preventing illegal alcohol sales; the petition (accompanied
by bill, House, No. 374) of William C. Galvin relative to tenant agents; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 395) of Steven S. Howitt relative
to regulating lottery funding to certain cities and towns; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 409) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others
for legislation to authorize the comptroller to transfer funds from the Race
Horse Development Fund to the Community Preservation Trust Fund; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 411) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and
others relative to authorizing the Alcoholic Beverages Control Commission
to obtain national criminal background checks for certain felons applying
for alcoholic beverage licenses; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 414) of Kristin E. Kassner relative to outdoor dining; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 417) of Meghan K. Kilcoyne for
legislation to further regulate the notification of liquor licenses; the petition

(accompanied by bill, House, No. 428) of David Paul Linsky relative to
alcohol sales on Thanksgiving; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 434) of John J. Mahoney and Judith A. Garcia relative to apportionment
of lottery revenues; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 443) of
Samantha Montaño for legislation relative to happy hour in the
Commonwealth; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 449) of
Tram T. Nguyen and others for legislation to further regulate broker's fees;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4058) of Manny Cruz relative
to licensure of and negligence by HVAC companies and technicians; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4195) of Colleen M. Garry for
legislation to require that all establishments be required to accept cash for
transactions; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4196) of Colleen
M. Garry relative to the listing of the alcohol by volume for beer selections
on menus; and the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4197) of
Colleen M. Garry relative to notification of alcohol by volume in beers to
be consumed on the premises, reports recommending that the
accompanying order (House, No. 4733) ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
TACKEY CHAN.
