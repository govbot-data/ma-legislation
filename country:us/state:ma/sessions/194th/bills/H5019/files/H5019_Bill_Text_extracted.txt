An Act relative to access to health care.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 32A of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the
2 following section:-
3 Section 35. (a) For the purposes of this section, the following words shall, unless the
4 context clearly requires otherwise, have the following meanings:
5 “Habilitative services”, health care services that help a person keep, learn or improve
6 skills and functioning for daily living.
7 “Habilitative stuttering speech therapy treatment”, speech therapy that helps an individual
8 to keep, learn or improve skills and functioning for daily living.
9 “Rehabilitative services”, health care services that help a person restore or improve skills
10 and functioning for daily living that have been lost or impaired.
11 “Rehabilitative stuttering speech therapy treatment”, speech therapy that helps an
12 individual restore or improve skills and functioning of daily life that have been lost or impaired.
1 of 14

13 “Stuttering speech therapy”, therapeutic care provided to an individual for the treatment
14 of stuttering administered by a licensed speech language pathologist.
15 “Telehealth”, the application of telecommunication technology to deliver speech therapy
16 services at a distance for assessment, intervention or consultation.
17 “Utilization review”, a set of formal techniques designed to monitor the use of or
18 evaluate the medical necessity, appropriateness, efficacy or efficiency of health care services,
19 procedures or settings, including: prior authorization, second opinion, certification, concurrent
20 review, case management, discharge planning or retrospective review, in order to make a
21 determination regarding coverage of the service under the terms of a health insurance policy or
22 an agreement with the department of health and human services.
23 (b) Any coverage offered by the commission to an active or retired employee of the
24 commonwealth insured through the commission shall provide coverage for the cost of: (i)
25 habilitative services that shall provide coverage for habilitative stuttering speech therapy
26 treatment as a treatment for stuttering, regardless of whether the stuttering is classified as
27 developmental; (ii) rehabilitative services that shall provide coverage for rehabilitative stuttering
28 speech therapy treatment as a treatment for stuttering; or (iii) both habilitative services and
29 rehabilitative services under clauses (i) and (ii).
30 (c) Habilitative services and rehabilitative services coverage required under this section
31 shall not be: (i) subject to any maximum annual benefit limit, including any limits on the number
32 of visits an insured may make to a speech language pathologist; (ii) limited based on the type of
33 disease, injury, disorder or other medical condition that resulted in the stuttering; or (iii) subject
2 of 14

34 to utilization review or utilization management requirements, including prior authorization or a
35 determination that the speech therapy services are medically necessary.
36 (d) Habilitative services and rehabilitative services required under this section shall
37 include coverage for speech therapy provided in person and via telehealth. Telehealth coverage
38 required under this subsection shall: (i) not be less than the coverage required for health benefit
39 plans; and (ii) include the use of any communication technology, application or platform to
40 deliver telehealth services, except coverage may be restricted to technology, applications or
41 platforms that are compliant with any applicable privacy provisions of the Federal Health
42 Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, 42 U.S.C. sec. 1320d et seq.
43 (e) Habilitative services and rehabilitative services required under this section shall
44 include: (i) treatment of speech, language, voice, communication, fluency or auditory processing
45 disorder for individual service; and (ii) treatment of speech, language, voice, communication or
46 auditory processing disorder for group service with 2 or more individuals.
