An Act to expand the doula workforce.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 112 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the following section:-
2 Section 298. (a) As used in this section, the following word shall, unless the context
3 clearly requires otherwise, have the following meaning:-
4 A “perinatal doula” or referred to hereafter as "doula”, is a trained professional who
5 provides physical, emotional, and informational support, but not medical care, for pregnant
6 individuals, surrogates, foster care parents and adoptive parents during and after pregnancy,
7 labor, childbirth, miscarriage, stillbirth or loss.
8 (b) There shall be established and set up on the books of the commonwealth a separate
9 fund to be known as the Maternal Health Justice Fund, hereafter called “the fund,” for the
10 purpose of expanding and managing the doula workforce. The fund shall be administered by the
11 department of public health, referred to hereafter as “the department.” The fund shall be credited
12 with: (i) revenue from appropriations or other money authorized by the general court and
13 specifically designated to be credited to the fund; and (ii) funds from non-state entities,
1 of 4

14 including, but not limited to gifts, grants and donations from private entities and local and federal
15 government agencies. Amounts credited to the fund shall not be subject to further appropriation
16 and any money remaining in the fund at the end of a fiscal year shall not revert to the General
17 Fund.
18 (c) The department shall utilize the fund monies to do the following:
19 1. Provide, subject to available funding, compensation for support staff at the
20 department to oversee the doula workforce support efforts.
21 2. Provide, subject to available funding, scholarship monies to individuals seeking
22 doula certification from an approved certification program, as determined by the department in
23 accordance with competencies outlined in (d)(1) and (d)(2). Scholarship awards should prioritize
24 applicants from historically marginalized backgrounds, those demonstrating low income as
25 determined by the department, and those located in geographic areas that lack doula availability.
26 3. Provide, subject to available funding, support to agencies dedicated to maternal
27 health care-related workforce development, doula mentorship and supervision, and fiscal
28 management.
29 4. Provide, subject to available funding, support to existing community-based doula
30 programs that meet the competencies outlined in (d)(1) and (d)(2).
31 (d) The department shall establish and maintain a list of doula certification programs
32 which are eligible for scholarship monies from the fund.
2 of 4

33 1. The department shall establish and maintain a list of competencies which doula
34 certification programs must meet in order to be eligible for scholarship monies from the fund.
35 Such competencies should include, but not be limited to:
36 A. understanding of basic anatomy and physiology as related to pregnancy, the
37 childbearing process, the postpartum period, breast-milk feeding, breastfeeding and chestfeeding;
38 B. capacity to employ different strategies for providing emotional support, education
39 and resources during the perinatal period;
40 C. knowledge of and ability to assist families with a wide variety of non-clinical
41 labor coping strategies;
42 D. strategies to foster effective communication between clients, their families,
43 support services and health care providers;
44 E. awareness of and ability to provide information on integrative health care systems
45 and various specialties of care to address client needs beyond the scope of practice of the doula;
46 F. knowledge of community-based, state- and federally-funded, and clinical
47 resources available to address client needs beyond the scope of practice of the doula; and
48 G. knowledge of HIPAA compliance and client confidentiality;
49 (2) In addition to the above competencies, doula certification programs eligible for fund
50 monies should also offer education in the areas of:
51 A. Health equity;
52 B. Implicit bias;
3 of 4

53 C. racism, including structural, interpersonal, and institutionalized racism;
54 D. reproductive and birth justice;
55 E. cultural sensitivity and humility;
56 F. trauma-informed care, including for survivors of sexual assault or birth trauma;
57 G. parental mental health needs;
58 H. needs of persons with disabilities or disabled persons;
59 I. sexual and gender identities;
60 J. social determinants of health.
61 (3) the department shall publish the list of eligible doula certification programs and
62 instructions for how to apply for scholarship monies publicly on its website. The list shall be
63 updated promptly in response to any changes to the list of certification programs or eligibility
64 requirements.
