1 SECTION 1. Chapter 63 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
2 section 38NN the following section:-
3 Section 38OO. (a) The purpose of this section shall be to attract capital investment to
4 businesses in rural areas of the commonwealth in order to promote the retention and expansion
5 of existing jobs, stimulate the creation of new jobs, and attract new business and industry to rural
6 areas of the commonwealth.
7 (b) For the purposes of this section, the following words shall, unless the context clearly
8 requires otherwise, have the following meanings:-
9 “Affiliate”, an entity that directly or indirectly through one or more intermediaries,
10 controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with another entity. An entity is
11 “controlled by” another entity if: (i) the controlling entity holds, directly or indirectly, the
1 of 14

12 majority voting or ownership interest in the controlled entity; or (ii) has control over the day-to-
13 day operations of the controlled entity by contract or by law.
14 “Closing date”, the date on which a rural growth fund has collected all of the amounts
15 specified by subsection (c).
16 “Credit-eligible capital contribution”, an investment of cash by a person subject to tax
17 under this chapter in a rural growth fund that equals the amount specified on a tax credit
18 certificate issued by the MOBD under paragraph (5) of subsection (c) of this section; provided,
19 however, that the investment shall purchase an equity interest in the rural growth fund or
20 purchase, at par value or premium, a debt instrument that has a maturity date at least 5 years
21 from the closing date.
22 “MOBD”, the Massachusetts office of business development established in section 3A of
23 Chapter 23A.
24 “Investment authority”, the amount stated on the notice issued under paragraph (5) of
25 subsection (c) of this section certifying the rural growth fund; provided, however, that at least 60
26 per cent of a rural growth fund's investment authority shall be comprised of credit-eligible capital
27 contributions.
28 “Jobs created”, newly created positions of employment that were not previously located
29 in the commonwealth at the time of the initial rural growth investment in the rural business
30 concern and that require a minimum of 35 hours worked each week, measured each year by
31 subtracting the number of employment positions at the time of the initial rural growth investment
32 in the rural business concern from the monthly average of employment positions for the
33 applicable year. The monthly average shall be calculated by adding together the number of
2 of 14

34 employment positions existing on the last day of each month of the applicable year and dividing
35 by 12. Such number shall not be less than zero.
36 “Jobs retained”, positions requiring a minimum of 35 hours worked each week that
37 existed prior to the initial rural growth investment. Retained jobs shall be counted each year
38 based on the monthly average of employment positions for the applicable year. The monthly
39 average shall be calculated by adding together the number of employment positions existing on
40 the last day of each month of the applicable year and dividing by 12. Such number shall not
41 exceed the initial amount of retained jobs reported and shall be reduced each year if employment
42 at the rural business concern drops below such number.
43 “Principal business operations”, the principal operations of a business are located at the
44 place or places where at least 60 per cent of its employees work or where employees that are paid
45 at least 60 per cent of its payroll work; provided, however, that an out-of-state business that has
46 agreed to relocate employees using the proceeds of a rural growth investment to establish its
47 principal business operations in a rural area in the commonwealth shall be deemed to have its
48 principal business operations in this new location if it satisfies this definition within 180 days
49 after receiving the rural growth investment, unless the MOBD agrees to a later date.
50 “Rural area”, a municipality with population densities of less than 500 residents per
51 square mile, according to the latest decennial census of the United States.
52 “Rural business concern”, a business that, at the time of the initial investment in the
53 company by a rural growth fund: (i) has less than 250 employees and not more than $10,000,000
54 in revenue for the preceding taxable year; (ii) has its principal business operations in one or more
55 rural areas in the commonwealth; and (iii) is engaged in industries related to manufacturing,
3 of 14

56 plant sciences, services or technology or, if not engaged in such industries, the MOBD makes a
57 determination that the investment will be highly beneficial to the economic growth of the
58 commonwealth.
59 “Rural growth fund”, an entity certified by the MOBD under subsection (c).
60 “Rural growth investment”, any capital or equity investment in a rural business concern
61 or any loan to a rural business concern with a stated maturity at least one year after the date of
62 issuance.
63 (c)(1) The MOBD shall accept applications for approval as a rural growth fund; provided,
64 however, that the application shall include:
65 (i) the total investment authority sought by the applicant under the business plan;
66 (ii) the following documents and other evidence:
67 (A) a copy of the applicant’s or an affiliate of the applicant’s license as a rural business
68 investment company under 7 U.S.C. 2009cc, or as a small business investment company under
69 15 U.S.C. 681; and
70 (B) evidence sufficient to prove, to the satisfaction of the MOBD, that as of the date the
71 application is submitted, the applicant or affiliates of the applicant have invested at least
72 $100,000,000 in non-public companies located in rural areas;
73 (iii) an estimate of the number of jobs created and jobs retained in the commonwealth as
74 a result of the applicant's rural growth investments;
4 of 14

75 (iv) a business plan that includes a revenue impact assessment projecting state and local
76 tax revenue to be generated by the applicant's proposed rural growth investments prepared by a
77 nationally recognized third-party independent economic forecasting firm using a dynamic
78 economic forecasting model that analyzes the applicant's business plan over the 10 years
79 following the date the application is submitted to the MOBD;
80 (v) a signed affidavit from each investor stating the amount of credit-eligible capital
81 contributions each taxpayer commits to make; and
82 (vi) a non-refundable application fee of $5,000.
83 (2) The MOBD shall make an application determination within 30 days of receipt in the
84 order in which the applications are received. The MOBD shall deem applications received on the
85 same day to have been received simultaneously. The MOBD shall not approve more than
86 $100,000,000 in investment authority and not more than $60,000,000 in credit-eligible capital
87 contributions under this section. If a request for investment authority exceeds this limitation, the
88 MOBD shall reduce the investment authority and the credit-eligible capital contributions for that
89 application as necessary to avoid exceeding the limit. If multiple applications received on the
90 same day request a combined investment authority that exceeds this limitation, the MOBD shall
91 proportionally reduce the investment authority and the credit eligible capital contributions for
92 those applications as necessary to avoid exceeding the limit.
93 (3) The MOBD shall deny an application submitted under this section if any of the
94 following are true:
95 (i) the application is incomplete or the application fee is not paid in full;
5 of 14

96 (ii) the applicant does not satisfy all the criteria described in clause (ii) of paragraph (1)
97 of this subsection;
98 (iii) the revenue impact assessment submitted under clause (iv) of paragraph (1) of this
99 subsection does not demonstrate that the applicant's business plan will result in a positive
100 economic impact on the commonwealth over a 10-year period that exceeds the cumulative
101 amount of tax credits that would be issued to the applicant’s investors under subsection (d) of
102 this section if the application were approved;
103 (iv) the credit-eligible capital contributions described in affidavits submitted under clause
104 (v) of paragraph (1) of this subsection do not equal at least 60 per cent of the total amount of
105 investment authority sought under the applicant's business plan; or
106 (v) the MOBD has already approved the maximum amount of investment authority and
107 credit eligible capital contributions allowed under paragraph (2) of this subsection.
108 (4) If the MOBD denies an application, the applicant may provide additional information
109 to the MOBD to complete, clarify, or cure defects in the application identified by the MOBD
110 within 15 days of the notice of denial for reconsideration and determination. If the applicant
111 completes, clarifies or cures its application within 15 days after the date of the notice of denial,
112 the application must be considered complete as of the original date of submission. If the
113 applicant fails to provide the information to complete, clarify or cure its application within the
114 15-day period, the application remains denied and must be resubmitted in full with a new date of
115 submission. The MOBD shall review and reconsider such applications within 30 days and before
116 any pending application submitted after the original submission date of the reconsidered
117 application.

147 (d)(1) There is hereby allowed a nonrefundable tax credit for taxpayers that made a
148 credit-eligible capital contribution to a rural growth fund and were issued a tax credit certificate
149 under paragraph 5 of subsection (c) of this section. The credit may be claimed against the tax
150 imposed by this chapter. The credit may not be sold, transferred or allocated to any other entity
151 other than an affiliate subject to the tax imposed by this chapter.
152 (2) On the closing date, the taxpayer shall earn a vested credit equal to the amount of the
153 taxpayer's credit-eligible capital contribution to the rural growth fund as specified on the tax
