An Act relative to community corrections.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 211F of the General Laws is hereby amended by striking out
2 sections 1 through 6 and inserting in place thereof the following 5 sections:-
3 Section 1. The following terms as used in this chapter shall have the following meanings:
4 “Commissioner”, the commissioner of probation.
5 “Community justice program”, any program or service that is operated by a state, local or
6 private service agency, that has been deemed an appropriate provider of services by the office of
7 community justice programs including, but not limited to, intensive supervision with treatment,
8 community service, pretrial services programs, reentry services programs, programs designed as
9 an alternative to jail or prison, and any other program or service as the commissioner may so
10 direct; provided, however, that pretrial and reentry services programs shall each be a separate
11 track of programming from intensive supervision with treatment as defined herein.
1 of 8

12 “Community justice plan”, a written proposal submitted to the executive director of the
13 office of community justice programs for approval and funding as a community justice program.
14 “Executive director”, the executive director of the office of community justice programs.
15 “Intensive supervision with treatment”, as determined by the office of community justice
16 programs, a community justice program that provides a combination of interventions, including
17 treatment, services and accountability measures for persons assessed to be at moderate or higher
18 risk for recidivism.
19 Section 2. (a) There is hereby established subject to appropriation within the office of the
20 commissioner of probation an office of community justice programs, which shall be used for any
21 criminal justice purpose as determined by the commissioner and shall establish a continuum of
22 community justice programs statewide.
23 (b) The executive director of the office of community justice programs shall be appointed
24 by the commissioner to establish, oversee and operate a statewide continuum of community
25 justice programs.
26 (c) The executive director shall operate subject to the direction and approval of the
27 commissioner. The office shall, to the extent practicable, utilize existing resources of the office
28 of court management for the purpose of avoiding unnecessary duplication.
29 § 3. (a) Any court exercising jurisdiction is authorized to sentence any eligible person to
30 intensive supervision with treatment.
31 (b) A sentence to intensive supervision with treatment shall be imposed as a condition of
32 probation consistent with chapters 276 and 276A. The court may modify the sentence of a person
2 of 8

33 subject to intensive supervision with treatment in the same manner as if the person had been
34 placed on probation.
35 (c) The commissioner shall develop guidelines for the eligibility of persons for intensive
36 supervision with treatment.
37 (d) No person shall be sentenced to intensive supervision with treatment in lieu of a
38 mandatory minimum term of incarceration set by statute.
39 Section 3A. (a) Participation in a pretrial services program may be ordered by the court,
40 in lieu of bail or as a condition of release consistent with sections 57, 58 and 58A of chapter 276.
41 The court may dictate the duration and conditions of the pretrial services program. Any
42 conditions should be imposed to ensure return of the defendant to court or, where permitted by
43 law, to assure the safety of any person or the community.
44 (b) The Massachusetts probation service may utilize pretrial services programs for
45 pretrial supervision consistent with sections 87 and 87A of chapter 276, upon agreement by the
46 person before the court who is charged with an offense or crime.
47 (c) If the sheriff who has custody of a person held on bail under section 57 or 58 of
48 chapter 276 determines that the person would benefit from entering a pretrial services program,
49 the sheriff shall provide a written recommendation of such determination to the court, the
50 commissioner, the prosecuting office and the person or the person’s attorney, where applicable.
51 The prosecuting office may notify any victim of the sheriff’s recommendation upon receipt of
52 such recommendation. If the commissioner or the prosecuting office objects to such
53 recommendation, the commissioner or prosecuting office shall file written objection with the
54 court within 14 days of receipt of such notice. Upon receipt of such objection, the court may set
3 of 8

55 the matter for hearing. After expiration of the time for filing objections and after hearing, if
56 applicable, the court shall either decline to modify its earlier bail order or make an order under
57 subsection (a) of this section authorizing the person’s participation in a pretrial services program.
58 In no event shall the person held on bail be ordered under this paragraph to enter a pretrial
59 services program without that person’s consent.
60 (d) Placement of a person in a pretrial services program shall require victim notification
61 as required under subsection (t) of section 3 of chapter 258B.
62 Section 3B. (a) For any person sentenced to probation supervision who has not been
63 sentenced to intensive supervision with treatment under section 3, the probation department may
64 utilize programs and services offered by the office of community justice programs: (i) for
65 participation in court-ordered programming where such programming is available through the
66 office of community justice programs; or (ii) upon agreement by the person so sentenced.
67 (b) The use of programs and services under subsection (a) of section 3B of this chapter
68 shall not operate as intensive supervision with treatment as defined in section 1.
69 Section 3C. (a) The office of community justice programs may provide reentry services
70 programs, which shall not operate as intensive supervision with treatment as defined in section 1
71 of this chapter, to any person released from incarceration including, but not limited to, any
72 probationer or parolee.
73 (b) Any person who has previously been sentenced to probation supervision, even if that
74 person is no longer being supervised by the Massachusetts probation service, may utilize
75 programs and services offered by the office of community justice programs. The use of programs
4 of 8

76 and services under this section shall not operate as intensive supervision with treatment as
77 defined in section 1.
78 Section 4. (a) The executive director is hereby authorized and directed to develop and
79 implement standards for a contracting process for community justice plans, as follows:
80 (1) A community justice plan shall include:
81 (A) the type of programs and services offered such as, intensive supervision with
82 treatment, pretrial services or reentry services and the interventions to be made therein, such as
83 cognitive behavioral therapy, employment counseling, educational support, etc.
84 (B) a description of the administrative, capital and operating costs of the programs;
85 (C) a description of methods by which the state, local or private service agency shall
86 implement the community justice program with fidelity to evidence-based practices;
87 (D) a description of the knowledge, skill and experience of the state, local or private
88 service agency in the fields of criminal justice, human services and social sciences.
89 (2) Subject to appropriation, the executive director shall select plans for funding. All
90 contracts shall provide that the executive director may suspend funding or may assume
91 administrative responsibility for any community justice programs not in compliance with
92 standards, or if the public safety is threatened. The executive director shall separately contract to
93 provide specialized, age-appropriate reentry services for emerging adults (18-25 years old),
94 released from incarceration including, but not limited to, any probationer or parolee.
5 of 8

95 (3) The executive director shall monitor programs for compliance with the goals of this
96 chapter, and shall provide technical assistance, training and education to providers in developing
97 and operating community justice programs.
98 (b) Subject to an agreement between the commissioner and the secretary of public safety
99 and subject to appropriation, the resources of community justice programs shall be utilized by
100 the parole board for the purpose of parole supervision and the department of correction for the
101 purpose of reentry.
102 Section 5. The commissioner shall submit an annual report no later than January 15 of
103 each year, commencing January 15, 2026, to the governor, the joint committees on the judiciary,
104 mental health, substance use and recovery, public health and public safety and homeland security
105 and the clerks of the house of representatives and the senate. The report shall include but shall
106 not be limited to the following information:
107 (1) a statistical report of the utilization of community justice programs, including a list of
108 all community justice programs operated under the office of community justice programs;
109 (2) the effectiveness of the office of community justice programs in reducing prison
110 commitments, reducing pretrial detention and increasing the court appearance rate and the
111 metrics used to evaluate said effectiveness;
112 (3) fiscal audits on the expenditure of state funds;
113 (4) the results of any investigations into community justice program noncompliance with
114 community justice plans;
115 (4) any other relevant information or recommendations provided by the commissioner.

116 SECTION 2: Section 57 of chapter 276 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024
117 Official Edition, is hereby amended by striking out the last paragraph and inserting in place
118 thereof the following paragraph:-
119 Participation in intensive supervision with treatment pursuant to chapter 211F as pretrial
120 treatment may be ordered by the court, in lieu of bail, or as a condition of release; provided,
121 however, that the defendant shall consent to such participation. The following shall not be
122 admissible against the person in any proceedings: (i) a request to engage in a pretrial treatment
123 program; (ii) a decision not to enter a pretrial treatment program; (iii) any statement made by the
124 person during the course of a pretrial treatment program assessment Evidence that is the direct
