An Act improving campaign finance reporting for statewide ballot questions.
Whereas, The deferred operation of this act would tend to defeat its purpose, which is to
improve campaign finance reporting by statewide ballot question committees, therefore it is
hereby declared to be an emergency law, necessary for the immediate preservation of the public
convenience.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 22A of chapter 53 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by adding the following paragraph:-
3 Blank forms provided for the collection of signatures for an initiative petition or
4 referendum petition, which will be circulated by a person receiving compensation for the
5 collection of signatures, shall include a disclosure, in a manner to be determined by the state
6 secretary, which clearly informs potential signatories that the form is being distributed by a paid
7 signature gatherer.
8 SECTION 2. Said chapter 53 is hereby further amended by inserting after section 22A
9 the following section:-
1 of 5

10 Section 22A1/2. No person shall provide anything of value contingent upon the number
11 of signatures collected for a statewide initiative petition or referendum petition, including, but
12 not limited to, payments on a fee-per-signature basis; provided, however, that the payment of
13 incentive bonuses for obtaining a certain number of signatures shall be prohibited.
14 A violation of this section shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $100 nor more
15 than $10,000; provided, however, that each signature collected in violation of this section shall
16 be a separate offense.
17 SECTION 3. Section 1 of chapter 55 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024
18 Official Edition, is hereby amended by adding the following paragraph:-
19 For the purpose of this chapter, supporting, favoring, promoting or opposing a question
20 submitted to the voters shall include actions to place or oppose placing a question on a statewide
21 election ballot; provided, however, that supporting, favoring, promoting or opposing a question
22 to be submitted to the voters shall not include legislative lobbying as defined in section 39 of
23 chapter 3. The director shall adopt regulations to define actions for the purposes of this
24 paragraph, including the extent to which these actions include polling, research, legal, consulting
25 or other related services; provided, however, that contributions or expenditures for such actions
26 need not be disclosed as provided for in this chapter until such time as petitions in support of a
27 statewide ballot question are made available by the state secretary pursuant to section 22A of
28 chapter 53.

59 SECTION 10. Section 22 of said chapter 55, as so appearing, is hereby amended by
60 striking out the third paragraph and inserting in place thereof the following paragraph:-
61 If the question is a statewide ballot question, the report shall be filed with the director as
62 follows: (1) by the fifth day of each month covering the preceding month until the sixtieth day
63 before the election; (2) on or before the fifth and twentieth day of each month, complete as of the
64 preceding first and fifteenth day of the month, from the sixtieth day before the election until the
65 election; (3) November 20 following the election, complete as of the fifteenth day of the month;
66 and (4) January 20 of each year, complete as of December 31 of the prior year, until all declared
67 liabilities of that person or corporation, association, organization or other group of persons have
68 been discharged.
69 SECTION 11. The treasurer of a statewide ballot question committee shall include in the
70 first report filed with the director of campaign and political finance following the effective date
71 of this act all information required to be reported under chapter 55 of the General Laws dated
72 since the closing date of the last previous report; provided, however, that if there was no previous
73 report by the committee, the treasurer shall include in said first report all such information dated
74 since January 1, 2026.
