An Act establishing a Commission Regarding Distribution of Lottery Revenue.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. There shall be a special commission established for the purpose of making
2 an examination and study relative to the distribution of lottery revenues to municipalities in the
3 commonwealth. The commission shall consist of the following members: the governor, or a
4 designee, who shall serve as chair; the executive director of the state lottery commission, or a
5 designee; 2 members of the house of representatives, 1 of whom shall be appointed by the
6 speaker and 1 of whom shall be appointed by the minority leader; 2 members of the senate, 1 of
7 whom shall be appointed by the senate president and 1 of whom shall be appointed by the
8 minority leader; 1 representative of the Massachusetts Municipal Association; and 11 persons to
9 be appointed by the governor, 1 of whom shall be a mayor of a city in the commonwealth, 1 of
10 whom shall be a select board member from a town in the commonwealth, 1 of whom shall be
11 municipal finance officer, 1 of whom shall be a member of a municipality’s legislative body such
12 as a city councilor, town councilor or town meeting member, 1 of whom shall be a representative
13 from the Massachusetts council on gaming and health, 1 of whom shall be a member of the
1 of 3

14 lottery commission, 1 of whom shall be a representative of the New England Convenience Store
15 and Energy Marketers Association, Inc., 1 of whom shall be a representative from the Retailers
16 Association of Massachusetts, Inc., 1 of whom shall be representative of SEIU local 888, 1 of
17 whom shall be a representative operating as a licensed sales agent in a brick and mortar retail
18 store in the commonwealth, and 1 of whom shall be a representative of the Massachusetts
19 Restaurant Association, Inc.
20 SECTION 2. The commission shall examine the current distribution of lottery revenues
21 and provide recommendations to improve distributional equity of lottery revenues.
22 The commission’s examination shall include an analysis of:
23 the current distribution’s equity by (i) geographic region, (ii) by the size and density of a
24 municipality from rural to urban, (iii) municipality’s median household income and other
25 economic variables, (iv) status of a municipality’s being a Gateway City versus not a Gateway
26 City and (v) other demographic variables that the commission deems of interest;
27 to what extent the current distribution mechanism distributes lottery revenues to
28 municipalities in proportion to municipality’s residents’ spending on lottery;
29 whether the formula equitably reflects the needs of municipalities based on (i)
30 population, (ii) income levels, (iii) property tax base, (iv) reliance on state aid; and (v) other
31 relevant factors; and
32 the impact of lottery revenue distribution on local budgeting and public services;
33 The commission’s recommendations shall include:
2 of 3

34 (1) whether a specific formula should be established for distribution of lottery revenues to
35 municipalities, notwithstanding how unrestricted general government aid is distributed pursuant
36 to section 18C of chapter 58 of the General Laws, to enhance regional and economic equity; and
37 (2) whether such a proposed distribution formula should: (i) apportion state lottery
38 revenue to cities and towns proportionate to lottery sales in those cities and towns; (ii) establish a
39 minimum distribution of lottery revenues to municipalities based on each municipality’s
40 residents’ spending on lottery; and (iii) establish a maximum distribution of lottery revenues to
41 municipalities based on each municipality’s residents’ spending on lottery, including whether a
42 municipality without lottery ticket sale licensees, pursuant to section 27 of chapter 10 of the
43 General Laws, shall receive any distribution of lottery revenues.
44 SECTION 3. Not later than December 31, 2027, the commission shall report to the office
45 of the state treasurer and receiver general and the general court’s joint committee on
46 municipalities and regional government and the joint committee on revenue, the result of its
47 examination and recommendations, together with drafts of legislation necessary to carry its
48 recommendations into effect.
