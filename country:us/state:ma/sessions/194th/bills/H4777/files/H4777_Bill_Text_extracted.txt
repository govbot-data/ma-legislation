HOUSE . . . . . . . . No. 4777
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
________________________________________
HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, December 1, 2025.
The committee on Financial Services, to whom were referred the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1086) of Bruce J. Ayers that
insurance companies be authorized to extend rebates to clients with safe
driving records; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1124) of
Michael S. Day relative to motor vehicle insurance premiums; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 1152) of Michael J. Finn relative to the
licensure of motor vehicle damage appraisers; the petition (accompanied
by bill, House, No. 1166) of Sean Garballey for legislation to further
regulate the rental of motor vehicles; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 1195) of Steven S. Howitt and Samantha Montaño relative to
eliminating surcharges for motor vehicle inspection violations; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 1200) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and
others relative to electronic automobile insurance charges; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 1219) of Mary S. Keefe, Joan Meschino
and others relative to surcharges on motor vehicle insurance premiums;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1220) of Mary S. Keefe,
Patrick Joseph Kearney and others relative to motor vehicle insurance
premiums; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1263) of Rita A.
Mendes relative to reducing racial and socioeconomic inequities in auto
insurance premium pricing; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
1265) of Frank A. Moran relative to auto insurance costs for traditionally
low-and moderate income communities; and the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 1280) of Francisco E. Paulino relative to insurance
coverage for testing and deployment of and school zone safety protocol
for autonomous vehicles, reports recommending that the accompanying
order (House, No. 4777) ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
JAMES M. MURPHY.
