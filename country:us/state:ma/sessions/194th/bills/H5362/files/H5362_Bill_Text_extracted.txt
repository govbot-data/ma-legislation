The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
________________________________________
HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, April 15, 2026.
The committee on Labor and Workforce Development, to whom were
referred the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2071) of Antonio F.
D. Cabral relative to time and a half wages; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 2081) of Manny Cruz relative to minimum gratuity while
dining; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2107) of Carmine
Lawrence Gentile, Daniel M. Donahue and others relative to raising the
minimum wage; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2134) of
Bradley H. Jones, Jr. and others relative to municipal unemployment
insurance; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2148) of Joseph
D. McKenna relative to public health prevailing wage exemptions; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2159) of Orlando Ramos
relative to fair wages on government subsidized construction projects; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2161) of David Allen Robertson
relative to ensuring fair and full employee compensation; and the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 2174) of Adam J. Scanlon relative to
salaries and wages for municipal employees, reports recommending that
the accompanying order (House, No. 5362) ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
PAUL MCMURTRY.

HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 5362
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
House of Representatives, April 15, 2026.
1 Ordered, That the committee on Labor and Workforce Development be authorized to sit
2 during a recess of the General Court to make an investigation and study of House documents
3 numbered 2071, 2081, 2107, 2134, 2148, 2159, 2161 and 2174, relative to wages, compensation
4 and municipal unemployment insurance.
5 Said committee shall report to the General Court the results of its investigation and study
6 and its recommendations, if any, together with drafts of legislation necessary to carry such
7 recommendations into effect, by filing the same with the Clerk of the House of Representatives
8 on or before December 31, 2026.
