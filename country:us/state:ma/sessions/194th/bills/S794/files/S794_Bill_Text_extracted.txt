An Act relative to the fiduciary responsibility of lenders for non-payment of insurance premiums
from escrowed accounts.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Section 4 of Chapter 167E of the General Laws, as so appearing in the 2022 Official
2 Edition, is hereby amended by inserting after subsection (c), the following subsection:-
3 “(d) If a bank requires, as part of its loan policies, that a borrower escrow the insurance
4 premium for property insurance on real estate secured by a mortgage loan and said bank, as a
5 result of neglect, fails to pay the insurance premium on a mortgage loan when the insurance
6 premium is due and there are sufficient escrowed funds on deposit to pay said insurance
7 premium, and if the property owner suffers a loss as a result of this failure, then the bank is liable
8 for the loss; except, however, that with respect to any loss, which would otherwise have been
9 insured, the extent of the liability shall not exceed the coverage limits of any insurance policy,
1 of 2

10 which has lapsed. The bank shall pay the insurance premium and any increased cost for securing
11 a new insurance policy for a period of three (3) years. If the bank is late in paying the insurance
12 premium, the bank shall pay the late fee charged by the insurance company.”.
