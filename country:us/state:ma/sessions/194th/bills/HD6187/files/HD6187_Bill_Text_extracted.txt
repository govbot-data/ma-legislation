Background
Massachusetts employers submit over 30,000 injury reports to the Department of Industrial
Accidents (DIA) each year. The submission of these reports indicates that individual employees
have sustained injuries significant enough to lose five or more days of full or partial wages as a
result of their workplace injury. This loss of income can be devastating, as these individuals are
suddenly deprived of the wages they rely on to support themselves and their families.
However, the true cost of these workplace injuries can extend far beyond just the financial
burden and can have long-lasting or life-altering consequences. Many of those injured are
never able to return to their previous jobs, altering their careers and livelihoods. In the most
tragic cases, some are unable to return to work entirely, their futures irrevocably changed by
preventable accidents.
Many of these occupational injuries, illnesses, and even deaths are avoidable. Proper education
and training for workers and employers is critical in reducing and potentially eliminating these
tragedies. The DIA Office of Safety helps to provide such education.
History of the Workplace Safety Grant Program
The Workers' Compensation Act of 1985 enhanced the powers of the Department of Industrial
Accidents (DIA). Pursuant to Section 3 of Chapter 23E of the General Laws, the DIA Office of
Safety is responsible for establishing programs that include educating and training employees
and employers in recognizing, avoiding, and preventing unsafe or unhealthy work conditions.
In 1986, the DIA created the DIA Office of Safety. The following year, the DIA Office of Safety
issued its first Request for Responses (RFR) for fiscal grant year 1988. Ever since, the DIA Office
of Safety has managed the Occupational Safety Training and Health Education Training
Program, now called the Workplace Safety Grant Program.
The Workplace Safety Grant Program
The DIA Workplace Safety Grant program provides funding to help Massachusetts employers
prevent workplace injuries and foster safe, healthy work environments. The program supports
training, education, and other injury-prevention initiatives for eligible employees and employer
organizations under Massachusetts Workers’ Compensation Law (G.L. c. 152).
Since 1988, the DIA Workplace Safety Grant program has issued an RFR with an annual budget
of $800,000 to support individual and consortium training proposals from Massachusetts
employers. Eligible applicants may receive up to $25,000 per grant, per fiscal year.
2

The DIA Workplace Safety Grant program ensures that all businesses, including small, minority,
and women-owned businesses have the maximum practical opportunity to access funding for
training. The grant selection criteria incorporate an enhanced needs analysis, to help identify
organizations that may face financial barriers preventing them from obtaining high-quality
safety training services.
An Evaluation Committee appointed by the Director of DIA conducts an objective review of all
complete grant applications. Each application is assessed based on its stated training goals, the
number of employees to be trained, the cost effectiveness of the proposed program, and the
clarity and feasibility of the training timeline.
The DIA’s Workplace Safety Grant program requires that applicants either address an existing
history of workplace injury or propose a training program designed to prevent future risk of
injury. Great care is taken to ensure that each application demonstrates a compelling proposal
consistent with the Office of Safety’s evaluation of criteria and overarching program objectives.
3

Executive Summary
The FY25 DIA Workplace Safety Grant program received 148 applications between June 2, 2024,
and June 28, 2024, requesting a total of $2.2 million in funding against an $800,000 budget.
Ultimately, $800,000 was awarded to 139 recipients, supporting safety training for 6,657
employees across all seven workforce regions, at an average cost of $120.00 per participant.
Nine applicants were not recommended due to non-compliance, and eleven recipients later
withdrew, leaving 128 active grantees to fulfill training obligations.
Program Highlights
• Consortium Grants
In FY25, the Workplace Safety Grant program partnered with MassHire and MassBiz to help
launch nine consortium grants enabling multiple organizations across the Commonwealth to
train 1,350 employees at an average cost of $85 per person. The initiative invested $115,177 in
total and expanded safety training access to smaller employers through collaborative region-
based partnerships. Following the close of FY25, the DIA and EOLWD began promoting the
Workplace Safety Grant program to employers as part of a new initiative: SafetyWorks. In
addition to other workplace safety programs under EOLWD, employers can learn more about
this program by visiting www.mass.gov/safetyworks.
• Municipalities
The Workplace Safety Grant program awarded $256,852 to 41 municipalities, including housing
authorities, schools, and public utilities, delivering safety training to 1,691 municipal
employees at an average cost of $150 per person. This municipal training initiative helped to
build safer workplaces and a stronger, more resilient public workforce.
• City Fresh Foods
City Fresh Foods, Inc., is an employee-owned company dedicated to making fresh, healthy and
culturally-relevant meals accessible to all. City Fresh Foods prepares and delivers more than
20,200 meals each day to students, seniors and food insecure communities across eastern
Massachusetts. By sourcing from local farms, City Fresh nourishes vulnerable residents and
strengthens the local food ecosystem.
Under the leadership of instructor Steve Saint-Laurent, City Fresh Foods utilized its $18,000
Workplace Safety Grant to train 212 employees across all three shifts, in both English and
Spanish, at a cost of $85 per person. The client-specific training program delivered targeted
instruction in basic safety for kitchen hazards, burns, slips trips and falls, and driver safety and
4

ergonomics. By engaging both frontline staff and management, City Fresh Foods was able to
create a safer, more resilient workplace.
• Worcester Housing Authority
Worcester Housing Authority (WHA), the second- largest Housing Authority in New England, is
home to more than 15,000 residents across 24 developments and multiple Section 8 sites
throughout Worcester. WHA’s mission is to enhance the community by providing safe,
affordable, and dignified housing for low-income individuals and families. A major focus of its
work centers on long-term, sustainable solutions that break the cycle of poverty through
workforce training professional development, and self-sufficiency programs.
With a $9,000 Workplace Safety Grant, and under the instruction of Safety Trainers and Rick
Staples, 84 employees completed courses in De-escalation, First Aid, CPR, AED, Bloodborne
Pathogens, and Hoisting at a cost of $107 per participant. These training courses reinforced
safe work practices, clarified responsibilities, and reduced the risk of workplace injury,
strengthening the overall well-being of both employees and the residents they serve.
• Preparatory Rehabilitation for Individual Development & Employment, Inc. (PRIDE)
Based in Taunton MA, this nonprofit organization is dedicated to advancing the field of human
services and supporting individuals with disabilities through a variety of innovative programs
and supports. PRIDE offers adult day programs, rehabilitative services, and community-based
supports funded through the Department of Developmental Services. The organization also
partners with local schools through its School-to-Work program, helping students with
disabilities gain meaningful employment skills and transition successfully into the workforce
and operates an Adult Foster Care program, funded by MassHealth, that matches individuals
with welcoming families who provide residential care in safe, supportive home environments.
Their PRIDWRX manufacturing facility provides subcontract packaging and assembly work for
individuals with disabilities, creating opportunities for paid employment and community
inclusion.
With a $5,400 Workplace Training Grant, PRIDE and East Coast Training Consultants
empowered 150 employees through targeted instruction in Workplace De-Escalation
techniques and Defensive Driving classes, further reinforcing a culture of safety and inclusion at
$36 per person.
The Grant Management System- GMS
• With the launch of the DIA's Office of Safety Grants Management System (GMS) in FY24,
the Workplace Safety Grant program achieved a significant advancement in both
efficiency and user experience. The secure online portal allows applicants to complete
and manage each stage of the grant process from application to reimbursement, saving
valuable time for both applicants and administrators. Prior to the launch of the GMS,
5

the Office of Safety operated using index cards and paper files, making this transition a
major modernization milestone.
• Since implementing the GMS, the Office of Safety has reduced overall grant processing
time by more than 30% while significantly improving data accuracy across all
submissions. In FY26, the GMS will undergo additional upgrades to enhance
functionality and improve the end user experience, with a focus on four key areas: class
submission and invoicing, reporting and post-offer withdrawals. These enhancements
will allow us to better operationalize the system and strengthen our ability to track and
analyze data while reducing processing time in these collective areas by approximately
40%. Looking ahead, our long-term vision includes creating an operational link between
the GMS and Finance to streamline contract processing. This integration would reduce
the workload for Compliance and Procurement (C & P) while increasing the overall
efficiency and timeliness of contract execution and vendor payment.
With a continued focus on growth and innovation, the DIA’s Workplace Safety Grant program
seeks to expand its budget and service capacity to further advance workplace safety across the
Commonwealth. The program remains a proven investment in prevention— delivering broad
based, cost effective, training that enhances employer capability, builds organizational
resilience, and supports the Commonwealth commitment to a safe and healthy workforce.
