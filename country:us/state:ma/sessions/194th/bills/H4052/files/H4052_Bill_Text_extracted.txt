HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 4052
By Representative Vitolo of Brookline, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4052) of
Tommy Vitolo and others relative to a specialized stretch energy code. Telecommunications,
Utilities and Energy.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act sparking the construction of 21st century buildings.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 6 of chapter 25A of the General Laws, as amended by section 19 of
2 chapter 239 of the acts of 2024, is hereby further amended by striking out clauses (14) and (15)
3 and adding the following 4 clauses:-
4 (14) develop and promulgate pursuant to chapter 30A, in consultation with the state board
5 of building regulations and standards, a municipal opt-in specialized stretch energy code that
6 includes, but is not limited to, net-zero building performance standards and a definition of net-
7 zero building, designed to achieve compliance with the commonwealth’s statewide greenhouse
8 gas emission limits and sublimits established pursuant to chapter 21N;
9 (15) develop and promulgate, regulations, criteria, guidelines and standard conditions,
10 criteria and requirements that establish parameters for the siting, zoning, review and permitting
11 of small clean energy infrastructure facilities by local government pursuant to section 21;
1 of 2

12 (16) develop and incorporate into the specialized stretch energy code developed and
13 promulgated pursuant to clause (14), in consultation with the board of building regulations and
14 standards, requirements for mandatory solar photovoltaic panel installation for all buildings
15 deemed suitable for installation by the department. Develop and incorporate into the stretch
16 energy code requirements for buildings to be made photo voltaic ready for all buildings deemed
17 suitable for future installation by the department; and
18 (17) the specialized stretch energy base code shall include all provisions of the stretch
19 energy code related to the latest International Energy Conservation Code, together with any more
20 stringent energy-efficiency provisions promulgated more than 36 months prior.
21 SECTION 2. Section 139 of chapter 164 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
22 Official Edition, is hereby amended by striking out, in lines 99 and 100, 137 and 138 and 145
23 and 146, the words “that are not net metering facilities of a municipality or other governmental
24 entity”.
25 SECTION 3. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, any undertaking
26 by a federal, state, county, municipal or quasi-governmental public agency for the planning,
27 acquisition, design, construction, demolition, installation, repair or maintenance of a structure
28 shall comply with the requirement of the specialized stretch energy code, as developed and
29 promulgated pursuant to clause (14) of section 6 of chapter 25A of the General Laws.
