REPORT OVERVIEW
Massachusetts General Law, Chapter 18, Section 2 (B)(s) requires DTA to file an annual report
detailing the numbers of recipients exempt from the Electronic Benefit Transfer (EBT) card
photo requirement and the reasons for such exemptions.
DEPARTMENT OF TRANSITIONAL ASSISTANCE MISSION
The Department of Transitional Assistance ’s mission is to assist and empower low-income
individuals and families to meet their basic needs, improve their quality of life, and achieve
long-term economic self-sufficiency. DTA offers a comprehensive system of programs and
supports to help individuals and families achieve greater economic mobility, including food and
nutritional assistance, economic assistance, and employment supports. Currently, DTA serves
one out of every six people in the Commonwealth including working families, children, elders,
and people with disabilities.
EBT PHOTO REQUIREMENT
In August 2013, the Legislature passed a law requiring the use of photo identification on the
front of each newly issued, and reissued, EBT card. The requirement covered recipients who are
18 or older that are designated to be the “head of household” on a DTA assistance case. Other
classes of recipients are exempted from the requirement as follows:
• 60 years of age or over • Disabled
• Blind • Victims of domestic violence
DTA must also comply with federal laws. The United States Department of Agriculture Food
and Nutrition Service (USDA-FNS) requires that heads of households who do not receive
Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) benefits on their own behalf be exempted
(e.g., some relative caretakers of minor children). In addition, exemptions based on religious
beliefs also must be allowed.
