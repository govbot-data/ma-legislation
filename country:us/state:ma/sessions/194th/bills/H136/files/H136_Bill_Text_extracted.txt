HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 1884 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 136
By Representatives Owens of Watertown and Garballey of Arlington, a petition (accompanied
by bill, House, No. 136) of Steven Owens and Kristin E. Kassner for legislation to further
regulate the use of sludge from a municipal, commercial or industrial wastewater treatment plant
as an agricultural product. Agriculture.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act prohibiting the use and sale of toxic sludge.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 128 of the General Laws , as appearing in the 2022 Official
2 Edition, shall be amended by inserting after section 65A the following new section:-
3
4 Section 65B. (a) For the purposes of this section, the following words will have the
5 following meanings:
6 ''Sludge'', any solid, semisolid or liquid waste generated from a municipal, commercial or
7 industrial wastewater treatment plant, water supply treatment plant, or air pollution control
8 facility or any other such waste having similar characteristics and effects.
9 (b). Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, except as provided in
10 subsection (c), a person may not:
1 of 3

11 (1) Apply to or spread on any land in the State:
12 (i) Sludge generated from a municipal, commercial or industrial wastewater treatment
13 plant;
14 (ii) Compost material that included in its production sludge generated from a municipal,
15 commercial or industrial wastewater treatment plant or septage; or
16 (iii) Any other product or material that is intended for use as a fertilizer, soil amendment,
17 topsoil replacement or mulch or for other similar agricultural purpose that is derived from or
18 contains sludge generated from a municipal, commercial or industrial wastewater treatment plant
19 or septage; or
20 (2) Sell or distribute in the State:
21 (i) Compost material that included in its production sludge generated from a municipal,
22 commercial or industrial wastewater treatment plant or septage; or
23 (ii) Any other product or material that is intended for use as a fertilizer, soil amendment,
24 topsoil replacement or mulch or for other similar agricultural purpose that is derived from or
25 contains sludge generated from a municipal, commercial or industrial wastewater treatment plant
26 or septage.
27 (c) The prohibitions in subsection (b) do not apply to:
28 (1) The disposal or placement at a solid waste landfill of any of the materials that are
29 prohibited from application, spreading, sale or distribution by this subsection;
2 of 3

30 (2) The land application of or the sale or distribution of compost material or other
31 agricultural product or material derived from or containing residuals generated as a result of the
32 processing or cultivation of food, food waste, crops or vegetative material, the brewing of malt
33 liquor, the fermenting of wine or hard cider or the distilling of spirits, including, but not limited
34 to, blueberries, apples, grapes, potatoes, seaweed, fish and seafood and spent grain or malt,
35 provided that such residuals are not mixed with sludge from a municipal, commercial or
36 industrial wastewater treatment plant, septage, sewage or sanitary wastewater prior to or during
37 land application or the production of the compost material or other agricultural product or
38 material; or
39 (3) The land application of or the sale or distribution of compost material or other
40 agricultural product or material derived from or containing sludge resulting from the production
41 of precipitated calcium carbonate.
42 (4) The land application of or sale any of the materials that are prohibited from
43 application, spreading, sale or distribution by this subsection including Materials derived from a
44 composting toilet system defined by 310 CMR 15.289.
