HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 2023 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 4013
By Representatives Gentile of Sudbury and Connolly of Cambridge, a petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 4013) of Carmine Lawrence Gentile, Mike Connolly and James B. Eldridge
relative to for-profit health care and insurance. Financial Services.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act to improve healthcare and reduce consumer costs.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 51 of chapter 111 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting after the second paragraph the following
3 paragraph:-
4 No original license shall be issued to establish or maintain a for-profit hospital or a for-
5 profit clinic providing ambulatory surgery.
6 SECTION 2. Section 51G of said chapter 111, as most recently amended by section 64 of
7 chapter 343 of the acts of 2024, is hereby amended by adding the following paragraph:-
8 (9) No original license shall be granted to establish or maintain a for-profit acute care
9 hospital.
10 SECTION 3. Section 4 of chapter 175 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
11 Official Edition, is hereby amended by adding the following subsection:-
1 of 2

12 (18) Notwithstanding any other general or special law to the contrary, the commissioner
13 shall not grant licenses or certificates of authority to issue policies of health insurance to any for-
14 profit company.
15 SECTION 4. This act shall take effect 18 months following the date of its enactment.
