An Act to expand housing opportunities through homesharing.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1: The General Laws are hereby amended by inserting after chapter 186A the
2 following new chapter:-
3 CHAPTER 186B. Homesharing
4 Section 1.
5 “EOHLC”, the Executive Office of Housing and Livable Communities of the
6 Commonwealth of Massachusetts
7 “Homesharing agreement”, a written agreement between the homesharing provider and
8 the homesharer containing all terms, conditions, obligations and covenants of the homesharing
9 occupancy at the homesharing property.
10 “Homesharing occupancy”, the use or occupancy or the right to use or occupy a room in
11 a homesharing property pursuant to the homesharing agreement, including the right to use the
1 of 6

12 common areas of the homesharing property including fixtures, furniture and equipment located
13 in, on or about the common areas of the homesharing property.
14 “Homesharing provider”, a person who owns and occupies the homesharing property.
15 “Homesharing property”, an owner-occupied property including, but not limited to, an
16 apartment, house, cottage, condominium or a furnished accommodation that is not a short-term
17 rental, hotel, motel, lodging house or bed and breakfast establishment, where: (i) at least 1 room
18 or designated space is rented to a homesharer; and (ii) below-market rate rent is offered in
19 exchange for use and/or domestic services.
20 “Homesharer”, a person who uses, occupies or has a right to use or occupy a room or
21 designated space in a homesharing property pursuant to a Homesharing agreement.
22 Section 2. Homesharing occupancy shall be a legally permissible residential use of a
23 homesharing property. There shall be a voluntary homesharing program under the EOHLC to
24 allow for homesharing occupancy. The program and regulations shall be established and
25 overseen by the EOHLC, but may be administered through a contracted third-party provider.
26 The EOHLC shall establish a homesharing program fee to be paid by the homesharing provider
27 to fund the homesharing opportunity relief fund established in section 3.
28 A homesharing provider may provide for use, by a homesharer, a room for occupancy in
29 exchange for services rendered and/or to reduce the monthly expenses of owning a home. The
30 homesharing property shall be a single unit or home and shall not be a unit otherwise available
31 for rent. A potential homesharing provider and homesharer may enter into a written homesharing
32 agreement to use and share the homesharing property under certain conditions.
2 of 6

33 The EOHLC shall set forth the terms of the homesharing agreement; provided, however,
34 the homesharing agreement, shall include, at minimum, the following:
35 (a) The amount to be paid by the homesharer, to be negotiated between the two parties,
36 taking into account the limited occupancy rights and to include shared utilities, and adjusted for
37 domestic services rendered by the homesharer.
38 (b) Listing of shared access spaces between the two parties, including kitchen,
39 bathrooms, and limitations on private spaces between the two parties.
40 (c) The agreed upon services to be completed by the homesharer, if any, which may
41 include general housekeeping, grocery shopping, meal preparation, yard work, or other basic
42 household tasks in exchange for lowered rent.
43 (1) Said services shall not include health care, medical or other home care services.
44 (2) Said services shall not exceed 7-hours per week and shall not be the primary purpose
45 of the homesharing agreement.
46 (3) A homesharing agreement may opt to exclude said services and may be limited to the
47 sharing of space as reflected in the rent and homesharing agreement, so long as the rent is below
48 market rate.
49 (d) Written agreed upon household rules pertaining to modifications to the premises
50 including painting, remodeling, or other changes to the physical property.
51 (e) Written notice to both participants outlining the processes for termination of
52 agreement as outlined in state regulation.
3 of 6

53 (f) Parties shall be exempt from existing state law pertaining to the relationship between
54 landlords and tenants and the evictions process set forth in MGL Chapter 186 and MGL Chapter
55 239.
56 Section 3. There shall be a separate fund known as the Homesharing Opportunity Relief
57 Fund. There shall be credited to the fund revenue from appropriations or other money authorized
58 by the general court and specifically designated to be credited to the fund, including revenues
59 received from the registration of homesharing households as established by the EOHLC.
60 Proceeds of the fund shall be used to provide assistance to homesharing providers and
61 homesharers for costs related to a failed homesharing agreement or in other emergency
62 circumstances set forth by the EOHLC regulations, including costs related to homesharer
63 relocation.
64 Section 4. The EOHLC shall promulgate regulations, in accordance with section 2 of
65 chapter 30A, for the implementation and enforcement of the homesharing program that are
66 necessary to ensure program safety and oversight.
67 The EOHLC, in developing regulations to oversee the program, shall consider:
68 (1) Existing program administration in peer states;
69 (2) Registration fee minimums and distribution(s) from the relief fund;
70 (3) Compliance with the state sanitary code;
71 (4) Administration of background checks, including, but not limited to, criminal record
72 information checks, credit checks, or other relevant information to ensure safety in homesharing
73 agreements;
4 of 6

74 (5) Standardized notice to homesharing provider and homesharer pertaining to rights and
75 responsibilities, compliance with applicable state laws, and conditions of the program;
76 (6) Model homesharing agreement for use by homesharing provider and homesharer;
77 (7) Administration of the program through third-party non-profit or private organizations;
78 (8) The processes to be followed by a homesharing provider or homesharer to terminate a
79 homesharing arrangement, including notices, timelines, and other considerations as deemed
80 necessary by the department.
81 (8) Additional program requirements which may include trial period between the two
82 parties, regulations for tenancies involving more than one homesharer and other considerations
83 not contained forthwith;
84 (9) Input and guidance from the Executive Office of Elder Affairs for the protection of
85 older adults entering into homesharing agreements.
86 SECTION 2: Chapter 175 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
87 section 4H the following section:-
88 Section 4I. (a) As used in this section, the terms “homesharer,” “homesharing provider”
89 and “homesharing property” shall have the same meanings as Chapter 186B unless the context
90 clearly requires otherwise.
91 (b) A homesharing provider shall maintain a homesharing insurance rider with property
92 and liability and, coverage for any services provided by the homesharer, or guests of the
93 homesharer, insurance of not less than $1,000,000 to cover the homesharing property, unless
94 homesharing provider already maintains equal or greater coverage. Such coverage shall defend
5 of 6

95 and indemnify the operator and homesharers in the building for bodily injury and property
96 damage arising from the homesharing property.
97 (c) A homesharer shall maintain a renter’s insurance policy including both personal
98 property protection and liability of not less than $100,000.
