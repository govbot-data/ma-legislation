An Act relative to requiring premium impact statements.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Prior to the adoption, amendment, or repeal of any health care or health
2 insurance-related regulation, an agency must file with the secretary of state’s office a public
3 notice of the proposed action and include a premium impact statement. In the statement, the
4 agency must consider the impact the proposed action will or will not have on health insurance
5 premium rates in Massachusetts. The agency must afford the public an opportunity to present
6 data, views, or arguments related to the impact statement, and prior to adopting the proposed
7 regulation, the agency must file an amended premium impact statement with the secretary of
8 state’s office.
9 SECTION 2. Any joint committee, house committee, or senate committee may report
10 favorably any bill or petition relative to health care or health insurance so long as that bill or
1 of 2

11 petition shall have first received a premium impact statement conducted by the center for health
12 information and analysis. Any joint committee, house committee or senate committee shall refer
13 all healthcare and health insurance related bills or petitions to an accompanied study order
14 pending a final report by the center for health information and analysis pursuant to this section."
