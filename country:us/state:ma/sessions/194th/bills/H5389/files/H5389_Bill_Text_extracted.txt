An Act authorizing the continued employment of Michael Marchand, the fire chief in the town of
Upton.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, Michael
2 Marchand, the chief of the fire department of the town of Upton, may continue to serve in such
3 position until he reaches the age of 70, the date of his retirement or the date he is relieved of his
4 duties by the select board or town manager, at their discretion, whichever occurs first; provided,
5 however, that he is mentally and physically capable of performing the duties of his office. The
6 select board or town manager may, at the town's expense, require that Michael Marchand be
7 examined annually by a physician designated by the select board or town manager to determine
8 his physical and mental capability to perform the duties of his office.
9 SECTION 2. No further deductions shall be made from the regular compensation of
10 Michael Marchand pursuant to chapter 32 of the General Laws for any service performed
11 subsequent to him reaching the age of 65. Upon his retirement for superannuation, Michael
1 of 2

12 Marchand shall receive a superannuation retirement allowance equal to the allowance that he
13 would have been entitled to receive had he retired upon reaching age 65.
14 SECTION 3. This act shall take effect upon its passage.
