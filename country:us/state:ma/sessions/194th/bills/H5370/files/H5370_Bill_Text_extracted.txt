HOUSE . . . . . . . . No. 5370
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
________________________________________
HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, May 7, 2026.
The committee on Labor and Workforce Development, to whom were
referred the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2078) of Mike
Connolly, Erika Uyterhoeven and others relative to securing the right to
strike for certain public employees; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 2079) of Mike Connolly and Danillo A. Sena for legislation to
provide paid family and medical leave benefits for educators and
municipal employees; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2088)
of Marjorie C. Decker relative to collective bargaining for teachers and
other school employees; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
2093) of Carol A. Doherty and others relative to allowing legislative
employees to unionize; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
2125) of Vanna Howard and others relative to paid family and medical
leave for educators; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2137)
of Patrick Joseph Kearney that the salaries of members of the United
States Coast Guard who are residents of the Commonwealth be paid by the
Commonwealth during the so-called federal government shut-down; the
joint petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2146) of Joseph D.
McKenna, Ryan C. Fattman and Alyson M. Sullivan-Almeida relative to
employment protections for domestic violence victims; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 2164) of Jeffrey N. Roy relative to
certain payment of wages in institutions of higher education; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 2185) of Andres X. Vargas and others
relative to wages and benefits for employees of public institutions of
higher education; and the joint petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
4291) of Hadley Luddy (by vote of the town) relative to exempting the
town of Chatham from the prevailing wage law, reports (under the
provisions of House Rule 27) recommending that the accompanying order
(House, No. 5370) ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
PAUL MCMURTRY.
