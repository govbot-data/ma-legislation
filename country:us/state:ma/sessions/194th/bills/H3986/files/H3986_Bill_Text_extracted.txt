An Act relative to addressing delayed patient discharges.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 111 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
2 section 51D the following section:-
3 Section 51D½. (a) For the purposes of section, the following words shall, unless the
4 context clearly requires otherwise, have the following meanings:
5 “Delayed patient discharge”, a situation where a patient remains in a hospital bed beyond
6 the completion of medical treatment due to unresolved health insurance authorization issues,
7 disputes over coverage or unavailability of in-network post-hospital care providers.
8 “Health insurance company”, a company as defined in section 1 of chapter 175 that
9 engages in the business of health insurance.
10 (b)(1) All health insurance companies operating within the commonwealth shall employ
11 or contract with dedicated care coordinators to facilitate hospital discharge and transition
12 planning for patients requiring post-hospital care at nursing homes, skilled nursing facilities or
1 of 3

13 rehabilitation centers. Dedicated care coordinators shall be available outside of standard business
14 hours, including evenings and weekends, to address health insurance authorization needs and
15 ensure timely patient discharges.
16 (2) All health insurance companies operating within the commonwealth shall: (i) provide
17 hospitals with real-time access to secure electronic platforms containing patient health insurance
18 information relevant to discharge planning; and (ii) issue timely updates to patients and hospitals
19 on the status of discharge authorizations and arrangements.
20 (c) Hospitals shall ensure timely discharge processes by collaborating with licensed
21 health insurance companies and utilizing the secure electronic platforms provided under clause
22 (i) of paragraph (2) of subsection (b).
23 (d) Any health insurance company operating within the commonwealth that fails to
24 comply with the requirements set forth in this section shall be subject to penalties determined by
25 the department, including but not limited to: (i) fines not exceeding $10,000 per documented
26 instance of non-compliance; and (ii) public reporting of non-compliant entities in an annual
27 report published by the department.
28 (e) The department shall promulgate rules and regulations necessary to enforce this
29 section and may conduct audits of hospitals and health insurance companies compliance to
30 determine compliance with this section.
31 (f) The department, in collaboration with the executive office of health and human
32 services, shall prepare an annual report that shall include: (i) the number of documented delayed
33 patient discharges; (ii) compliance rates among health insurance companies and hospitals; and
34 (iii) any recommendations for improving discharge coordination and reducing delays. The report
2 of 3

35 shall be submitted, no later than December 31 of each year, to the chairs of the joint committee
36 on public health and the clerks of the house of representatives and senate.
37 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect on January 1, 2026.
