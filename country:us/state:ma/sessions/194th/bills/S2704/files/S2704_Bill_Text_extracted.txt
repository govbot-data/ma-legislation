An Act assisting low-income students success through work-study.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 15A of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024 Official
2 Edition, is hereby amended by inserting after section 22A the following section:-
3 Section 22B. (a) Subject to appropriation, the Massachusetts Association of Community
4 Colleges shall support education opportunity coordinator positions. The coordinators shall work
5 with low-income students, including, but not limited to, recipients of cash assistance through
6 transitional aid to families with dependent children, who enroll in community colleges across the
7 state for the purpose of earning a certificate or 2-year degree. Each community college shall have
8 at least 1 on-campus education opportunity coordinator. In coordination with student support
9 staff funded through line item 7100-4002 of the general appropriation act, said education
10 opportunity coordinators shall provide focused assistance to recipients, including, but not limited
11 to, developing career plans, identifying a program of study and accessing work study
12 opportunities and funding. The coordinators shall work with recipients, community colleges and
13 case managers at the department of transitional assistance and other state agencies as needed.
14 The Massachusetts Association of Community Colleges shall enter into a memorandum of
1 of 5

15 understanding with the community colleges to ensure coordinators execute the duties of this
16 paragraph.
17 (b) The Massachusetts Association of Community Colleges, in collaboration with
18 relevant state agencies, shall annually, on or before August 1, file a report with the chairs of the
19 joint committee on higher education, the joint committee on children, families and persons with
20 disabilities and the house and senate committees on ways and means that shall include, but not
21 be limited to: (i) information regarding the efficacy of state work study employment placements
22 and related coursework in developing careers for recipients of cash assistance; (ii) the number of
23 recipients of cash assistance enrolled in certificate or 2-year degree programs at community
24 colleges; (iii) the number of recipients of cash assistance enrolled in a certificate or 2-year degree
25 program receiving grants under the Massachusetts Community College Career Pathways Trust
26 Fund established in section 2KKKKKK of chapter 29; (iv) student success rates including, but
27 not limited to, graduation rates, time to complete a degree and grade point averages, of recipients
28 of cash assistance enrolled in a certificate or 2-year degree program; (v) employment rates of
29 former recipients of grants under the Massachusetts Community College Career Pathways Trust
30 Fund 6 months after completing a degree or certificate program; and (vi) employment rates of
31 former recipients of grants under the Massachusetts Community College Career Pathways Trust
32 Fund 1 year after completing a degree or certificate program. No personal identifying
33 information shall be used in the report.

40 SECTION 3. Chapter 29 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024 Official Edition,
41 is hereby amended by inserting after section 2JJJJJJ the following section:-
42 2KKKKKK. (a) There is hereby established and set up on the books of the
43 commonwealth a separate fund to be known as the Massachusetts Community College Career
44 Pathways Trust Fund, hereinafter referred to as the fund, to which shall be credited any
45 appropriations, bond proceeds or other monies authorized by the general court and specifically
46 designated to be credited thereto. The Massachusetts Association of Community Colleges,
47 hereinafter referred to as the Association, shall administer the fund and hold the fund in an
48 account or accounts separate from other funds. Amounts credited to the fund shall be used by the
49 Association to carry out the purposes of the fund as set forth in this section. Any monies
50 unexpended at the end of the fiscal year shall remain in the fund and may be expended by the
51 Association without further appropriation.
52 (b) The fund shall be used to provide and fund state work-study employment
53 opportunities for eligible low-income students enrolled in a certificate or 2-year associate degree
54 program at any community college. Preference shall be given to recipients of cash assistance
55 through transitional aid to families with dependent children.
56 (c) Funds expended shall, to the greatest extent feasible, be for work-study employment
57 opportunities at: (i) the community college; (ii) an off-campus placement that provides career
3 of 5

58 development opportunities that are related to the student’s course of study; or (iii) an off-campus
59 community service placement as defined in subsection (e).
60 (d) The share from funds distributed shall not exceed 80 per cent of the total
61 compensation paid to students, with the exception of jobs at the community college the student
62 attends, or that provide career development opportunities related to the student’s course of study.
63 Employers shall pay the costs of any employee benefits, including all payments due as an
64 employer's contribution under the state workman's compensation laws, federal Social Security
65 laws and other applicable laws. The federal work-study program shall not be used to provide the
66 employer’s share of student compensation.
67 (e) An off-campus community service placement shall include direct service planning,
68 career development or applied research that is designed to improve the quality of life for
69 residents of the community served, particularly low-income residents, in such fields as health
70 care, child care, education, literacy training, welfare, social services, public safety, crime
71 prevention and control, transportation, recreation, housing and neighborhood improvement, rural
72 development and community improvement. Placements shall be identified by the Association
73 through formal or informal consultation with local nonprofit, governmental and community-
74 based organizations.
75 The placement shall not be at an organization for whom a majority of its mission is
76 political activities, including but not limited to electing candidates, influencing ballot questions
77 and raising money for political campaigns.
78 (f) Students shall be paid the rate of pay as compared to entry-level salaries and wages
79 provided other employees engaged in similar work, provided total applicant resources shall be
4 of 5

80 considered to ensure to the maximum extent feasible that students may remain qualified for other
81 federal and state public assistance programs. The Association may make reasonable adjustments
82 to salaries and wages to maximize a student’s participation in other federal and state public
83 assistance programs.
84 (g) Funds shall not be expended under the program to compensate students for hours
85 worked in excess of an average of 15 hours per week over the period of enrollment or 40 hours
86 per week during vacation period. A student shall not be concurrently employed in the same
87 position by the fund and the federal work-study program and exceed the 15 hours per week
88 average.
89 (h) Students may receive academic credit for work experience gained through fund jobs.
90 (i) Monies received from the fund shall not count against a recipient’s income, assets or
91 any other eligibility standard in qualifying for cash assistance benefits.
92 (j) The Association and the department of higher education, hereinafter referred to as the
93 department, shall enter a memorandum of understanding to ensure that funds are properly
94 expended and disbursed. The Association shall enter into agreements with employers,
95 community colleges, the department of transitional assistance and others for the operation of the
96 fund. Said agreements shall include such provisions as the executive office for administration
97 and finance may deem necessary or appropriate to carry out the purposes of this section. Said
98 agreements shall be made available to the department upon request.
99 SECTION 4. The first report required under subsection (b) of section 22B of chapter 15A
100 of the General Laws, as inserted by this act, shall be filed not later than August 1, 2028.
