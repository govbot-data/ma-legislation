By Mr. Feeney, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 129) of Paul R. Feeney for
legislation to require that mandated reporters complete training at least every two years to
recognize and report suspected child abuse or neglect. Children, Families and Persons with
Disabilities.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to training of mandated reporters.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 51A of Chapter 119 of the General Laws is hereby amended by
2 striking out subsection (k) and inserting in place thereof the following subsection:-
3 (k) A mandated reporter who is professionally licensed by the commonwealth shall
4 complete training at least every two years to recognize and report suspected child abuse or
5 neglect. The training shall have a curriculum that has been approved by the Office of the Child
6 Advocate.
7 SECTION 2. Section 5 of Chapter 18C of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding
8 the following subsection:-
9 (i) The child advocate shall be responsible for approving curricula for mandated reporter
10 trainings in accordance with M.G.L. c. 119 § 51A(k). Such curricula shall include, but not be
11 limited to, recognition of the signs of child neglect or abuse and related reporting requirements,
1 of 2

12 bias and cultural considerations in reporting, information on how to address concerns with
13 families and children when those concerns do not rise to the level of requiring a maltreatment
14 report, information about connecting families with needed supports and resources and how to
15 understand what qualifies as neglect.
