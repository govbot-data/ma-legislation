16 SECTION 2. Subsection (g) of said section 18B of said chapter 6A, as so appearing, is
17 hereby amended by adding the following paragraphs:-
18 The State 911 Department is hereby directed, pursuant to the provisions of chapter 30A
19 of the General Laws, to adopt, amend, and enforce regulations to ensure ongoing Quality
20 assurance and Improvement (QA/QI) for all emergency calls in which a cardiac arrest is
21 confirmed by Emergency Medical Services (EMS) personnel and resuscitation is attempted. The
22 regulations shall include, but are not be limited to, the following requirements:
23 a) Audit of Dispatch Calls: Within 60 days following the date of receipt of all 911 or
24 emergency calls received at any PSAP in which resuscitation for cardiac arrest is indicated or
25 attempted or 60 days from the date on which an EMS agency notifies a PSAP of a qualifying
26 incident, whichever is shorter, the dispatch call shall undergo a review and assessment for
27 Quality Assurance and Improvement purposes. An analysis and explanation for reasons for the
28 non-recognition of cardiac arrest and any delays shall be documented as part of the assessment.
29 PSAPs shall submit all complete reviews to the State 911 Department within 10 days of
30 completing the Quality Assurance and Improvement review in a form and manner prescribed by
2 of 6

31 the State 911 Department. The State 911 Department shall develop an electronic portal system to
32 accept submissions of qualifying calls from PSAPs and provide status and feedback regarding
33 the Department’s review.
34 b) Data Collection: The call taking and Quality Assurance and Improvement process
35 must collect key data, as defined by the department, including, but limited to, time intervals from
36 call receipt to “hands on chest”, attempted 911 directed orassisted defibrillation, and EMS
37 arrival.
38 c) Individual Review: Each cardiac arrest call shall undergo an individual Quality
39 Assurance and Improvement review by the PSAP Director or a designated Quality Assurance
40 and Improvement professional, and shall include appropriate feedback and recommendations .
41 d) Review by the State 911 Department: The State 911 Department shall conduct a
42 review of all reported cardiac arrest calls in accordance with AEMPDS certification standards.
43 e) The State 911 Department shall develop remediation and enforcement policies to
44 address PSAP non-compliance.
45 f) Annual Reporting: The State 911 Department shall compile, summarize and make
46 publicly available on a Statewide and PSAP level, on their website Quality Assurance and
47 Improvement reports annually. The reports shall highlight trends and areas for improvement.
48 PSAPs shall be listed by name.
49 g) Training and Improvement: Quality Assurance and Improvement Reports shall be used
50 to identify and address training needs for emergency dispatch personnel and ensure PSAP
51 compliance with regulatory requirements to provide telephone, 911-assisted CPR, and
3 of 6

52 Emergency Medical Dispatch. The department shall establish, and from time to time amend, a
53 list of required trainings and implement appropriate mechanisms to ensure compliance with
54 training requirements.
55 The Department of Public Health shall promulgate regulations to require that all EMS
56 agencies report all instances of cardiac arrest, including those where resuscitation is attempted, to
57 the State 911 Department. This reporting shall include relevant data as determined necessary for
58 Quality Assurance and Improvement and statistical analysis, including but not limited, to patient
59 condition upon first contact with EMS, number of defibrillation attempts, whether or not
60 bystander CPR was in progress, patient status upon arrival at the definitive care facility/ER if
61 transported or indication of pronouncement on scene, if applicable. All reports of incidents
62 reported by EMS agencies shall be forwarded to the PSAP of jurisdiction and, when available, be
63 published in the required portal system.
64 The State 911 Department, in collaboration with the Department of Public Health, shall
65 ensure that data collection and reporting protocols respect the privacy and dignity of individuals
66 and comply with applicable laws and regulations.
67 The State 911 Department shall develop an annual award for up to 3 individuals
68 employed by a PSAP for superior performance of duties that substantially contributed to the
69 survival of victims of out-of-hospital cardiac arrests in the Commonwealth. The State 911
70 Department shall further develop an annual award to be awarded to the PSAP on the basis of
71 high performance and contribution to the survival of victims of out-of-hospital cardiac arrests in
72 the Commonwealth.
