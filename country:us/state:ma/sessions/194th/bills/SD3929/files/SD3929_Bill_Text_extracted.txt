Background
The FY25 state budget included $10,000,000 for grants to Regional Transit Authorities (RTAs) to
advance connectivity between existing public transportation routes: “…that not less than
$10,000,000 shall be expended on grants to regional transit authorities for the creation or
altering of routes that advance connectivity between existing public transportation routes
including, but not limited to: (A) regional transit authority bus routes; and (B) Massachusetts
Bay Transportation Authority routes; provided further, that the Massachusetts Department of
Transportation shall oversee the distribution of said grants to the authorities and shall
establish grant criteria including, but not limited to, the prioritization of: (I) opportunities for
increased ridership; (II) opportunities for increased total mileage covered in a single trip; (III)
opportunities to reach low-income individuals; and (IV) geographic equity.”
Grant Criteria and Review
MassDOT received 10 applications. A review committee comprised of MassDOT staff reviewed
each of the 10 applications and evaluated whether these potential projects aligned with the
legislative intent, using the following scoring criteria:
• Service Plan (15 Points) - Evaluates whether the proposed service promotes
connectivity between transit authorities, the likelihood of effectiveness, and the
thoroughness and potential success of the proposed implementation timelines.
• Budget (5 points) - Evaluates whether expenses are reasonable, eligible, and likely to
lead to a successful project.
• Project Need (10 points) - Evaluates the strength of evidence supporting local or
regional need, with higher scores for proposals citing multiple sources like studies,
rider requests, and data.
• Prioritization (15 points) - Evaluates potential impacts on ridership increases, coverage
expansion, and service to low-income individuals, with special consideration for equity
and accessibility.
• Evaluation (5 points) - Checks for concrete plans to track ridership data and evaluate
service effectiveness.
• Bonus Points (10 points) - Evaluated collaboration and awarded additional credit for
collaborative proposals involving multiple transit authorities, with maximum points
given when all impacted authorities are partners.
In addition to these criteria, the review committee also considered geographic equity to
ensure funding was distributed throughout the Commonwealth.
2

Awards
MassDOT awarded funding for all 10 projects, including full funding for 6 projects and partial
funding for 4 projects.
Mechanisms for Data Collection
MassDOT requests that grantees submit quarterly reports that include data on ridership, other
relevant metrics, and updates on progress.
