An Act authorizing the establishment of a mean tested senior citizen property tax exemption.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Section 5 of chapter 59 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024 Official Edition, is
2 hereby amended by inserting after section 5O the following section:-
3 Section 5P. (a) In a city or town that accepts this section, there shall be a residential
4 property tax exemption for real property for income and age qualified domiciliary homeowners
5 as provided herein. For the purposes of this section, “parcel” shall mean a unit of real property as
6 defined by the board of assessors under the deed for the property and shall include condominium
7 units. The exemption provided for herein may, at the discretion of the city or town be in addition
8 to any other exemptions on the same property allowed by the General Laws.
9 (b) With respect to each qualifying parcel of real property classified as Class one,
10 residential in the municipality, there shall be an exemption from the property tax in an amount
11 set annually as provided for in paragraph (d). The exemption shall be applied to the domicile of
12 the taxpayer.
1 of 4

13 (c) A parcel of real property shall qualify for the exemption under this section if each of
14 the following criteria is met:
15 (i) the qualifying real property is owned and occupied by a person whose income from
16 the prior year would make the person eligible for the credit allowed under subsection (k) of
17 section 6 of chapter 62;
18 (ii) the qualifying real property is owned by a single applicant age 65 or older as of July 1
19 of the fiscal year for which the exemption is sought or jointly by persons either of whom is age
20 65 or above as of July 1 of the fiscal year for which the exemption is sought and the joint
21 applicant is 60 years of age or older;
22 (iii) the qualifying real property is owned and occupied by the applicant or joint
23 applicants as their domicile;
24 (iv) the applicant or at least 1 of the joint applicants has been domiciled and owned a
25 home in the municipality for at least 10 consecutive years before filing an application for the
26 exemption;
27 (v) the assessed value of the domicile is not greater than the prior year’s maximum
28 assessed value for qualification for the credit allowed under subsection (k) of section 6 of chapter
29 62, as adjusted annually by the commissioner of revenue;
30 (vi) the total assets of the applicant or joint applicant do not exceed any asset limitations
31 established pursuant to subsection (e) to determine eligibility for this exemption; and
32 (vi) the board of assessors of the municipality has approved the application.
2 of 4

33 (d) Either the board of assessors of the municipality, or the select board, town council or
34 city council, as decided by the legislative body at the time this section is adopted shall determine,
35 on an annual basis, the exemption amount, which shall not be more than 200 per cent of the
36 maximum amount of the credit allowed under subsection (k) of section 6 of chapter 62 for which
37 the applicant qualified in the previous year.
38 (e) Either the board of assessors of the municipality, or the select board, town council or
39 city council, as decided by the legislative body at the time this section is adopted may establish
40 limits on the total assets that may be owned by an applicant or joint applicant for purposes of
41 eligibility.
42 (f) Any person who seeks to qualify for the exemption provided for under this section
43 shall, on or before April 1 of the applicable tax year, or within 3 months after the bill or notice of
44 assessment was sent, whichever is later, file an application, on a form to be adopted by the board
45 of assessors, along with supporting documentation to demonstrate the income and assets as
46 described in the application. The application shall be filed each year for which the applicant
47 seeks the exemption.
48 (g) The total amount exempted by this section shall be allocated proportionally within the
49 residential tax levy. No exemption shall be granted under this section until the commissioner of
50 revenue certifies a residential tax rate for the applicable tax year where the total exemption
51 amount is raised by a burden shift within the residential tax levy.
52 (h) Prior to acceptance of this section, and annually thereafter prior to the commissioner
53 of revenue’s certification of a residential tax rate for the applicable year, the city or town shall
3 of 4

54 provide and post on its website an estimate of the change to the residential tax rate due to
55 acceptance of this section.
56 (i) An exemption adopted pursuant to this section shall expire 3 years after acceptance;
57 provided, however, that city or town may reauthorize the exemption for additional 3-year
58 intervals by a vote of the legislative body of the city or town.
59 (j) Acceptance of this section may be revoked by an affirmative vote of the legislative
60 body of the city or town and shall take effect 30 days following such affirmative vote.
