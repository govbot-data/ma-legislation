1 SECTION 1. The General Laws are hereby amended by inserting after chapter 23N the
2 following chapter:-
3 CHAPTER 23O.
4 INTERNET GAMING.
5 Section 1. The general court hereby finds and declares that:
6 (1) it is in the best interests of the commonwealth for the commonwealth to operate
7 internet gaming and that it is the intent of the general court to authorize internet gaming within
8 the commonwealth and through reciprocal agreements with other approved jurisdictions;
9 (2) illegal internet gaming channels operating throughout the United States pose a critical
10 threat to the safety and welfare of the citizens of the commonwealth and that a secure and highly
1 of 29

11 regulated policy framework will protect the public and positively benefit state revenues and the
12 commonwealth's economy;
13 (3) the most effective and efficient manner in which the commonwealth can operate and
14 regulate the forms of internet gaming authorized by the provisions of this chapter is to limit the
15 number of authorized operators to those who are licensed;
16 (4) the granting of licenses pursuant to the provisions of this chapter, while maintaining
17 all ownership rights and exercising control through strict regulation of all internet wagering
18 authorized by the provisions of this chapter, constitutes an appropriate exercise by the general
19 court of the power granted it by the commonwealth; and
20 (5) the operation of internet wagering in conjunction with brick-and-mortar casinos
21 serves to protect, preserve, promote, and enhance the tourism industry of the commonwealth as
22 well as the general fiscal well-being of the commonwealth and its subdivisions.
23 Section 2. As used in this chapter the following words shall, unless the context clearly
24 requires otherwise, have the following meanings:
25 "Adjusted gross revenue", the total of all sums actually received by an internet gaming
26 operator from internet gaming operations less the total of all sums paid out as winnings to
27 patrons.
28 "Commission", the Massachusetts gaming commission established under section 3 of
29 chapter 23K.
30 "Internet game", an internet-based game conducted on a computer, mobile device or any
31 other internet device, in which a patron wagers money for the opportunity to win money or
2 of 29

32 something of monetary value. Authorized internet games include, but are not limited to, poker,
33 blackjack, craps, roulette, cards, slots, progressives, jackpots, game show style games, games
34 driven by a random number generator or remote gaming service, peer-to-peer and skill-based
35 games or any other games substantially equivalent to those typically offered at a casino, and any
36 other game approved by the commission.
37 "Internet gaming", the business of accepting monetary wagers on internet games via
38 approved gaming operations that are conducted online in accordance with regulations via a
39 computer, a laptop, a game console, a tablet, a mobile phone or any other digital device that has
40 internet access and game play capabilities.
41 "Internet gaming account", an account established by a patron for the deposit and
42 withdrawal of funds used for internet gaming. A patron may deposit by methods approved by the
43 commission and withdraw funds for internet gaming and other authorized purchases, and to
44 which the operator may credit winnings or other amounts due to or authorized by that patron;
45 provided, however, that such account may be established, verified and funded by the patron
46 remotely through an approved internet gaming platform, and the patron may be permitted to use
47 the same account to participate in internet gaming and mobile or digital sports wagering with an
48 operator licensed to conduct both internet gaming and mobile or online sports wagering.
49 "Internet gaming brand", the names, logos and brands that an internet gaming operator
50 holds out to the public as its internet gaming platform.
51 "Internet gaming operator", a party or parties licensed by the commission to operate
52 internet gaming.
3 of 29

53 "Internet gaming platform", the combination of hardware and software or other
54 technology designed and used to manage, conduct and record internet gaming and the wagers
55 associated with internet gaming.
56 "Internet gaming skin", a distinctly branded internet gaming platform operated by an
57 internet gaming operator, which may encompass a website, mobile application or other portal to
58 the internet gaming platform. The brand may be that of the internet gaming licensee or its
59 affiliate, the internet gaming management vendor or another brand as agreed upon by the internet
60 gaming licensee and its internet gaming management vendor.
61 "Live dealer internet game", an internet game, typically offered in a casino or any other
62 game approved by the commission, conducted by a gaming attendant or gaming equipment or
63 both, in a live internet game studio in which video is streamed to an internet gaming platform
64 and patrons have the ability to communicate game decisions through the internet gaming
65 platform, and may be permitted to interact with game attendants and fellow patrons.
66 "Live internet game studio", a physical location in the commonwealth or in a permissible
67 jurisdiction in accordance with a multi-state internet gaming agreement, which utilizes live video
68 and audio streaming technology to provide an internet gaming licensee with a live internet game
69 simulcast.
70 "Online sweepstakes game", any game, contest or promotion, in which a prize is awarded
71 based on chance, that is available on the internet and accessible on a mobile phone, computer
72 terminal or similar access device, that utilizes a dual-currency system of payment allowing the
73 player to exchange the currency for any prize or award or cash or cash equivalents, and simulates
4 of 29

74 casino-style gaming, including but not limited to, slot machines, video poker, and table games,
75 lottery games and sports wagering.
76 "Permissible jurisdiction", a jurisdiction other than this commonwealth from which
77 wagers on internet gaming may be accepted pursuant to a multi-state internet gaming agreement.
78 Section 3. (a) The commission shall have exclusive jurisdiction to issue, renew, revoke or
79 suspend internet gaming licenses, conduct investigations, impose sanctions and govern all
80 aspects of internet gaming operations in the commonwealth.
81 (b) The commission shall promulgate rules and regulations necessary for the
82 implementation, administration and enforcement of this chapter. The commission may
83 promulgate emergency rules and regulations in accordance with applicable procedures for the
84 promulgation of emergency rules and regulations.
85 (c) The commission shall promulgate regulations to prohibit: (1) the purchase or other
86 use of any personal biometric data for internet gaming or aiding a patron in placing a wager with
87 internet gaming operators; (2) the following advertising, marketing and branding activities: (i)
88 advertisements, marketing and branding in such a manner that it is deceptive, false, misleading
89 or untrue or tends to deceive or create a misleading impression whether directly or by ambiguity
90 or omission; (ii) use of unsolicited pop-up advertisements on the internet or by text message
91 directed to an individual on the list of self-excluded persons; (iii) any form of advertising,
92 marketing or branding that the commission deems unacceptable or disruptive to the viewer

341 Section 17. (a) Each internet gaming operator shall implement a responsible gaming lead

368 of the time selected if the registered patron reaches the loss limit; (3) a limit on the maximum
369 amount of any single wager on any internet game; (4) a time-based limit must be offered on a

371 patron's login to log off, a registered patron may spend engaging in internet gaming, provided
372 that if the time-based limit is reached a registered patron is permitted to complete any round of
373 play or active or prepaid event; and (5) a temporary suspension of a patron's internet gaming
