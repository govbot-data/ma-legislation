HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, January 12, 2026.
The committee on Children, Families and Persons with Disabilities, to
whom were referred the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 220) of
Mindy Domb and Simon Cataldo for legislation to establish a diaper
insufficiency fund; the petition (accompanied by resolve, House, No. 221)
of Mindy Domb and Simon Cataldo for an investigation by a special
commission (including members of the General Court) relative to an
annual allowance to be used to purchase diapers for recipients of the
Transitional Aid to Families with Dependent Children (TAFDC) program;
and the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 295) of Steven George
Xiarhos relative to Department of Transitional Assistance benefits for
diapers, reports recommending that the accompanying bill (House, No.
4911) ought to pass.
For the committee,
JAY D. LIVINGSTONE.

An Act relative to a diaper allowance to meet early childhood needs.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 1 of chapter 117A of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting after the last paragraph, the following
3 paragraph:-
4 Effective September 1st annually, the department shall issue a one-time diaper allowance
5 to eligible recipients for each dependent child under the age of 3, as defined in section 1 of
6 chapter 118 as appearing, subject to appropriation. Supplemental payment per child shall be
7 issued at a later date. The allowance shall be in an amount to be determined in accordance with
8 the amounts available to the department for expenditure under this chapter.
9 SECTION 2. Section 2 of chapter 118 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024
10 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting after the last paragraph, the following
11 paragraph:-
