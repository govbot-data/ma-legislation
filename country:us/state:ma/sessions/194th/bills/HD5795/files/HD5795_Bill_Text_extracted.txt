Supplier Diversity Office
Comprehensive Annual Report
Fiscal Year 2025

To: Governor Healey, Lieutenant Governor Driscoll, Members of the General Court, Cabinet Secretaries,
Agency Heads, Secretariat and Agency Supplier Diversity Officers
From: Bonnie Borch-Rote, Executive Director, Supplier Diversity Office
________________________________________________________________________________________
I am honored to present our Comprehensive Annual Report for Fiscal Year 2025 (FY2025). The SDO’s mission
is to promote diversity in state contracting and increase opportunities for underrepresented businesses.
Consistent with the directives in MGL c. 7, § 61 and Executive Order 599, the SDO provides certification,
programs, training services, and access to business opportunities and resources for Minority (MBE), Women
(WBE), Veteran (VBE), Service-Disabled Veteran (SDVOBE), Disability (DOBE), and Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and
Transgender (LGBTBE)-Owned businesses, as well as small Massachusetts businesses. The SDO also
collaborates with procurement officials, resource organizations, and its Diverse and Small Business Advisory
Board to further its mission.
The accompanying report details the Commonwealth’s diverse and small business programs, including the
Certification, Supplier Diversity (SDP), Small Business Purchasing (SBPP), Affirmative Marketing (AMP,
MCAMP), Veteran Business, and Individuals with Disabilities programs.
In addition to Executive Branch agency results, this report includes narrative descriptions of supplier diversity
programs and/or results from 15 Commonwealth quasi-public/non-executive organizations and seven
constitutional/quasi-public/non-executive organizations participating in quantitative sections of this report.
Here are some of the spending highlights from the attached FY2025 Annual Report:
• Growth in total diverse spending. The Commonwealth spent approximately $4.53 billion with diverse and
small businesses (a 20.39% increase over FY2024), which includes:
• Discretionary (or program) spending by Commonwealth agencies through SDP, SBPP, and
DCAMM’s AMP programs of approximately $3.1 billion (a 25.88% increase over FY2024).
• Nondiscretionary (or non-program) spending of approximately $1.43 billion (a 10% increase from
FY2024).
In addition, this report documents more than $114.2 million from state-funded municipal construction
projects within the SDO’s Municipal Construction programming and more than $400 million in spending
by quasi-public organizations who submitted program narratives.
2

• Benchmark performance. SDP Program participants exceeded spending benchmarks for MBE, WBE, and
SBPP by 29.90%, 86.44%, and 50.9%, respectively. The Commonwealth continued its efforts to meet its
ambitious veteran-owned business benchmark. While 11 participating offices did meet the veteran
spending benchmark, the Commonwealth as a whole did not meet it. However, VBE spending with
participating offices increased by 44.5%. Executive Branch agencies met the DOBE benchmark, exceeding
it by more than $2 million. While spending with LGBTBEs did grow by 5.81% over FY2024, the benchmark
was not met. Thirteen participating offices met the LGBTBE spending benchmark individually, contributing
to the overall growth in spend with LGBTBE firms.
• Spending by race/ethnicity. Overall, MBE spending grew from FY2024 to FY2025 by $174.06 million,
$94.64 million from an increase in direct spending and $79.4 million from growth in business partnership
spending.
Other highlights include:
• Number of certified and recognized businesses. Overall, the SDO added 309 new diverse businesses to
its directory (a 5.2% increase over FY2024).
• Training internal and external stakeholders. The SDO’s training program continued to develop and deliver
sessions to diverse and small bidders, prime contractors, executive agencies, and other public purchasers.
It participated in pre-bid conferences, contractor onboardings, and contract kick-off sessions for buyers.
• Continued compliance efforts. The SDO continued to expand its compliance efforts to ensure the integrity
of diverse and small business programs. The Spending Compliance Verification program
engaged 1,345 unique certified diverse businesses to verify 1,892 reporting line items submitted by prime
contractors. The program verified spend exceeding $377 million in reported spending, a 6% decrease
compared to FY2024.
• Cross Functional Team. The Cross Functional Team (CFT) continued to implement a focused, intentional
approach to promoting opportunities for diverse and small businesses. In FY2025, their efforts resulted in
a 94% increase in SDO certified and/or SBPP participant vendors on the statewide and departmental
contracts promoted by the CFT.
• Expanded outreach. The SDO Programs and Engagement Team conducted more than 200 one-on-one
technical assistance sessions with diverse and small business owners. In addition, SDO staff attended
and/or presented at more than 80 events geared toward assisting small and diverse businesses, agencies,
and other interested parties.
My team and I look forward to continuing the legacy, innovation, and leadership of the SDO as we continue
to promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in the Commonwealth’s public procurement and contracting
processes. I congratulate the SDO Team on another impactful Fiscal Year.

Introduction
This report and the summary of accomplishments included in the introductory letter provide an overview
of the FY2025 activities and accomplishments of the Supplier Diversity Office (SDO). Together, they
present comprehensive information on diverse and small business spending across the wide range of
Commonwealth departments and quasi-public organizations and their programs.
SDO Overview
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts spends billions of dollars each year on goods, services, and
construction. The role of the SDO is to certify, provide resources for, and support diverse and small
businesses in competing for contracts being bid across the Commonwealth. The state’s diversity programs
include firms certified as Minority1 (MBE), Women (WBE), Veteran (VBE), Service-Disabled Veteran
(SDVOBE), Disability (DOBE), and Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender (LGBTBE)-Owned Businesses,
and includes the following programs:
• State Certification Program
• Supplier Diversity Program (SDP)
• Small Business Purchasing Program (SBPP)
• Commonwealth and Municipal Construction Affirmative Marketing Programs (MCAMP)
• Individuals with Disabilities in State Procurement and Contracting Program
1 The term Minority Business Enterprise or MBE is defined in statute as a business that is owned by a racially or ethnically
diverse individual. While the terms Minority and MBE are meant to define an ethnically or racially diverse individual or business
respectively, they are not meant to denote a smaller or lesser status of the individuals or businesses included in this definition.

SDO Organizational Development
SDO Mission Statement
The mission of the SDO is to promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in state contracting by:
1. Certifying diverse businesses (minority, women, veteran, service-disabled veteran, disability, and
LGBT-owned) and small Massachusetts-based businesses;
2. Connecting these companies with business opportunities and resources that enhance their
marketability when bidding on public contracts; and
3. Collaborating with Executive Agencies and public organizations to:
a. Identify and remove barriers for diverse and small businesses; and
b. Increase diverse and small business spending.
SDO Commitment to Staff Diversity
In support of the Governor’s goal of making state government representative of the constituents we serve
and making it a great place to work for all its employees, the SDO:
• Continued its commitment to its Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Vision Statement:
Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) are critical to our organization and to the success of the
diverse and small businesses that we represent. In fact, Diversity is in our name and it is what
unites us! Ultimately, we believe we can only achieve our vision, mission, and goals with a
demonstrated commitment, through words and actions, to DEI. We continuously seek to deepen
our understanding of and perspective on the barriers and challenges facing those we serve, many
of which are rooted in long-standing patterns of social inequities, exclusions, and divisions. Equally
important, when we recruit, hire, and retain diverse talent that reflects the diverse businesses that
we serve and when we foster an environment where the unique skills, capabilities, viewpoints, and
dissenting opinions of all employees are valued, we enhance belonging, empathy, creativity, and
ultimately innovation.
• Continued to prioritize the recruitment, hiring, and retention of diverse candidates to ensure its
staff reflects the diverse businesses the SDO serves; and
• Continued to be one of the most diverse agencies in state government with the following
representation in FY2025:
o 77.8% Women
o 47.5% Persons of Color
o 3.2% Veterans and
o 12.7% Individuals with Disabilities

SDO Innovation
One of the keys to the SDO’s success has been our focus on promoting innovation in everything we do.
Below are some examples of innovative measures implemented by the SDO in FY2025:
Pre-Certification Webcast added to Certrak, the SDO’s Certification Tracking System
During FY2025, the SDO completed systems enhancements to enable better access to SDO Pre-
Certification Training. Until the end of FY2025, the SDO’s Pre-Certification Training was only available in
a live webinar format, requiring applicant businesses to attend a session on specific dates and times.
These classes would often fill completely, making it difficult for some businesses to attend.
To make this training more accessible for business owners, the SDO converted our scheduled live
training into an on-demand webcast, available 24/7. The new Pre-Certification Webcast is integrated
into the application portal, allowing businesses to use one location for both training and certification
applications. While not deployed until July 1, 2025, the creation and implementation of the new on-
demand webcast occurred during FY2025.
The Supplier Diversity Hub
Launched in FY2023 to connect diverse and small businesses to prime contractors and public buyers, the
SDO’s Supplier Diversity Hub was updated in FY2025 to better support reporting functionality. In
FY2025, the SDO enhanced the Hub to streamline goods and services supplier diversity reporting by
departmental prime contracts. Today, contract information is better integrated with the
Commonwealth’s payments to prime vendors on departmental contracts, enabling automated
calculation of supplier diversity commitment amounts.
Additionally, the SDO added framework for enhanced Municipal Construction Affirmative Marketing
Program (MCAMP) and Veteran Business Program (VBP) reporting. These new construction and
design enhancements allow for more detailed reporting of payments issued on MCAMP and VBP
projects. Pilot launches and training for the new enhancements began in FY2026. Widened availability of
the Hub for reporting will continue to phase out cumbersome spreadsheet reports.
SDO Award Ceremony Reinvigoration
The SDO hosted an in-person SDO award ceremony in June 2025, recognizing outstanding work in
FY2024. The event, held at the State House, featured speakers such as the Governor’s Deputy Chief of
Staff for Access and Opportunity Marcony Almeida-Barros and then-Assistant Secretary for the Office
for Administration and Finance Mark Fine.
This year, in addition to benchmark achievements, Executive Agencies received awards based on
increases in direct payments to diverse small businesses. We gave awards to the top three in each
category and included:
8

• Pinnacle Performer Award for the highest percentage increase in direct spend in each diverse
business benchmark category
• Small Business Star Award for the highest percentage increase in direct spend with SBPP
businesses
• Innovation Icon Award for the highest percentage increase in direct spend with both DOBE and
LGBTBE
• Game Changer Award for increased percentage of direct spend in five categories
• Diverse and Small Business Champion for increased percentage of direct spend in
all six categories
The shift to focus more on direct spending highlights the Commonwealth’s ongoing commitment to
growing small and diverse business participation at the prime contracting level. Bridgewater State
University was also recognized as the SDO’s Community Partner of the Year for their commitment to
supplier diversity in construction contracting. The award ceremony was an overwhelming success, and
the SDO looks forward to continuing this tradition.
Conversion of the Individuals with Disabilities Pilot Program into Full Program
In FY2025, the SDO collaborated with the Operational Services Division (OSD) and the Massachusetts
Office on Disability to implement policy and Request for Response language for a new Empowering
Abilities in Contracting and Employment (EACE) Program. Beginning for FY2026, new statewide contracts
will contain EACE Program requirements, expanding the SDO’s impact on participation by individuals
with disabilities in state contracting. Specifically, vendors with sales on these new statewide contracts
will be required to report to the SDO on their efforts to recruit, hire, and retain individuals with
disabilities in their workforce.
This is an expansion of the Pilot (voluntary reporting) into a Full Program with defined requirements for
contractors. Additional information is included in the Individuals with Disabilities Pilot Program section
below. Since this new Program takes effect for FY2026, more detailed information will be provided in
our next Annual Report.

Overview of Diverse and Small Business Spending
In FY2025, the Commonwealth spent approximately $4.53 billion with diverse and small businesses, a
20.39% increase when compared to the previous year.9 Participating departments and organizations
expended funds in two areas:
• Discretionary (program) spending, which represented 68% of diverse and small business spending
and included the SDO’s Supplier Diversity Program (SDP), Small Business Purchasing Program
(SBPP), and DCAMM’s Affirmative Marketing Program (AMP) for Commonwealth Agencies; and
• Non-discretionary (non-program) spending, which accounted for 32% of departments’ diverse
and small business expenditures.
The following sections measure the combined achievements of all supplier diversity programs that exist
in participating organizations through the lens of benchmarks organized by business category – businesses
owned by minorities (MBE), women (WBE), veterans (VBE and SDVOBE), those with a disability (DOBE and
SDVOBE), members of the LGBT community (LGBTBE),10 and small businesses (SBPP).

result from competitively procured contracts.11 The SDO’s programs use policy, benchmark-setting,

and small businesses in these procurement areas.

Non-Discretionary Spending with Diverse and Small Businesses
The Commonwealth makes pension and insurance-related expenditures, awards grants and subsidies,
manages entitlement programs, and maintains loans and special payments, which are considered non-
discretionary spending. While the Commonwealth’s supplier diversity programs do not apply to these
non-discretionary areas of spending, diverse and small businesses are part of that marketplace, so the
SDO also tracks this spending.

In FY2025, combined non-discretionary direct expenditures totaled more than $1.4 billion, a 10% increase
when compared to the prior fiscal year. While most of this spending is the Commonwealth’s direct

Supplier Diversity Program (SDP)
The SDP is established through MGL c. 7, § 58 and § 61 and Executive Order 599 to promote equity of
opportunity in the state procurement and contracting process. The SDP applies to all executive
department procurements for goods and services with annual values exceeding $250,000. The program
institutes policies to encourage participating organizations and their contractors to use SDO-certified
MBEs, WBEs, VBEs, SDVOBEs, DOBEs, and LGBTBEs in their contracts for goods and services.
In consultation with the Governor’s Office for Access and Opportunity, the SDO sets annual benchmark
goals for specific certification categories. These goals are expressed as a percentage of each participating
organization’s discretionary budget. In FY2025, the SDP spending benchmarks were set as follows:

Currently, more than 70 organizations participate in the SDP, falling into one of three groups:
• Executive branch departments in all secretariats
• Non-executive departments and constitutional offices (such as the Office of the Governor, the
Massachusetts Gaming Commission, the Massachusetts Commission Against Discrimination, and
the Disabled Persons Protection Commission)
• Quasi-public entities (such as the Massachusetts Convention Center Authority, the
Massachusetts Housing Finance Agency, and the Cannabis Control Commission)
Only executive departments are required to participate in the SDO’s procurement programs, which
include the SDP and the SBPP. Through continued collaboration and outreach, many non-executive
departments, constitutional offices, and quasi-public organizations voluntarily participate in this annual
report. For quasi-public organizations, the degree of adherence to the SDO’s program rules and
participation in this report varies:

• Some of the Commonwealth’s quasi-public organizations submit narrative program reports,

Participating organizations may use two types of spending to achieve program spending goals:
• Direct spending with MBE, WBE, VBE, SDVOBE, DOBE, and LGBTBE prime contractors
• Indirect spending resulting from business partnerships between the organizations’ contractors
and MBE, WBE, VBE, SDVOBE, DOBE, or LGBTBE vendors used in the contractors’ operations. This
includes subcontracting, as well as other types of business-to-business relationships.
Indirect spending can be further subdivided into the following types:
• Subcontracting, defined as a partnership in which the SDP partner is involved in the provision of
products and/or services to the Commonwealth. Such relationships typically involve a formal
written agreement between the SDP partner and the prime contractor.
• Ancillary Products and Services, a business relationship in which the SDP partner provides the
prime contractor products or services that are not directly related to the prime contractor’s
contract with the Commonwealth (such as maintenance or office supply services). In most cases,
this type of partnership is related to the prime contractor’s general business operations and may
involve a formal written agreement with the SDP partner. In some cases, products and/or services
provided by the SDP partner may contribute to both subcontracting (when used by the
Commonwealth) and ancillary (when used by other customers) spending.
The SDO obtains information about direct spending with certified contractors from the Commonwealth
Information Warehouse (CIW), a repository of the Commonwealth’s financial transactions. Indirect
spending is reported to agencies by their contractors and then compiled and submitted to the SDO. Quasi-
public organizations monitor supplier diversity spending internally and submit their direct and indirect
expenditure information to the SDO for analysis and publication.

Municipal Construction Affirmative Marketing Program (MCAMP)
The Municipal Construction Affirmative Marketing Program (MCAMP) was established for the purpose
of ensuring the fair participation of minority-owned and women-owned businesses on state-assisted
building projects undertaken by political subdivisions of the Commonwealth. The SDO, in consultation
with DCAMM, establishes MBE and WBE participation goals for such projects. The mission of MCAMP is
to educate, monitor, and assist political subdivisions in the attainment and enforcement of MBE and
WBE participation goals for both the design and construction phase of applicable projects.
Prior to July 1, 2020 (i.e., FY2021), MCAMP combined the MBE and WBE goals to provide designers and
contractors with flexibility in using SDO-certified MBE and/or WBE contractors on state-assisted vertical
construction projects. The combined MBE/WBE goal percentage was 17.9% for design and 10.4% for
construction.
Starting July 1, 2020, in its construction project goal setting, MCAMP utilized the general annual MBE
and WBE aspirational goals, as based upon data from the DCAMM disparity study. The following general
annual goals were used in FY2021–FY2022:
• Design: MBE 6.6% and WBE 15%
• Construction: MBE 4.2% and WBE 8.8%
Some projects started during FY2021–FY2022 will continue to be measured against these goals.
Effective July 1, 2022, based on the results of the DCAMM disparity study, MCAMP began using project-
specific MBE and WBE goals, which are set, using DCAMM’s methodology, for individual state-assisted
vertical construction projects subject to SDO oversight. This practice ensures more realistic MBE and
WBE goals for individual projects based on construction trades and geographic availability.
Veteran Business Program (VBP)
Effective January 1, 2025, the SDO issued a three percent veteran business goal for all state and state-
assisted design and construction projects costing more than $150,000, building from guidelines released
in FY2024. To ensure the consistency and validity of the data, the SDO will collect, starting in FY2026,
spend data for all projects. Future SDO Annual Reports will contain more information regarding
VBE/SDVOBE business participation on applicable projects.

Individuals with Disabilities in State Procurement and Contracting Program
In collaboration with the Massachusetts Office on Disability (MOD), the SDO established the SDO Individuals
with Disabilities Program to have overarching goals which are updated every two years. The following section
reports on the success in completing the biennial goals established in FY2024.
Goal #1: Expand inclusion of Disability-Owned Business Enterprises (DOBE) and Service-Disabled Veteran-
Owned Business Enterprises (SDVOBE).
In FY2025:
• The SDO maintained its valued partnerships with Disability:IN, MassAbility, MassHire, the
Massachusetts Veterans Chamber of Commerce (MVCC), the Center for Women and Enterprise
(CWE), and the New England Veterans Business Outreach Center (VBOC) to promote SDO programs
to DOBEs and SDVOBEs.
• The SDO further developed its relationship with the Massachusetts Commission on the Status of
Persons with Disabilities through collaboration on resources for statewide contract vendors
participating in the Individual with Disabilities Pilot Program. The Commission connected the SDO to
many nonprofit entities who provide workforce development support to employers interested in
hiring qualified applicants who identify as having a disability.
• The SDO established new collaborative relationships with the Executive Office on Economic
Development (EOED) and the Executive Office of Veterans’ Services (EOVS) to ensure the veteran-
owned and service-disabled veteran-owned business communities are consistently connected to
ongoing SDO programs and events.
• While the overall number of SDO-certified DOBEs and SDVOBEs slightly decreased (from 376 to 374),
the Individuals with Disabilities Program Coordinator met with entity owners to discuss certification
eligibility and SDO programs throughout the year. Out of the 104 unique introduction meetings, 36
identified as either a DOBE or SDVOBE. Barriers to certification were routinely mentioned in these
conversations, largely centered around fear of disclosure and hesitation to identify as a diverse
business entity. Service-disabled veteran-owned business entities also experienced disruption in
federal certification with the Small Business Administration (SBA), which is a common route to third-
party verification with the SDO.
Goal #2: Finalize the transformation of the Individuals with Disabilities Pilot Program to a Full Program.
In FY2025, the Director of Programs & Engagement and the Individuals with Disabilities Program Coordinator
worked closely with the SDO Legal team and the Operational Services Division (OSD) to finalize policy and
statewide contract language to transform the Pilot into a Program.
The creation of an interagency team allowed for open dialogue and feedback on the feasibility of achieving the
overarching goals of the updated program. These goals were to increase participation of DOBEs and SDVOBEs
in Commonwealth contracting and particularly to increase the representation of people with disabilities working
on state contracts by supporting statewide contract vendors in diversifying their workforces. As a result of the
41

successful data collection under the Individuals with Disabilities Pilot Program, we were able to use historical
participation rates and vendor feedback to shape policy.
In FY2025, there was one remaining contract in the Individuals with Disabilities Pilot Program, FAC114, a
janitorial services statewide contract. In the FY2025 annual employment practices survey responses, most
entities reported having a workforce with fewer than 50 employees but still had voluntary and confidential self-
identification processes for applicants and employees with disabilities.
While many lacked formal policies to provide reasonable accommodation, or a specialized ADA coordinator,
several respondents noted a dedication to attending job fairs and partnering with Commonwealth agencies like
MassHire to find applicants who are veterans and/or have a disability. These responses, in conjunction with the
similar responses received over the lifetime of the pilot program, aided the program development team in
identifying where statewide contract vendors need support and what types of resources would be most
impactful to share with them. The Program Coordinator has created a resource repository for participating
vendors which includes requests for referral to agencies like MassAbility and MOD, agencies that work with
employers to create a more accessible and equitable workplace.
The dedication of these statewide contract vendors to voluntarily report annual disability employment data has
been equally valuable in shaping the finalized policy and program. Out of nine statewide contract vendors who
received contract dollars, five volunteered annual disability employment data to the Pilot Program Coordinator.
An additional seven vendors who had not received contract dollars in FY2025 submitted their own disability
employment data.
With a total of 32 active businesses on the FAC114 vendor list and a total of 12 survey respondents, the SDO
saw a 55.5% response rate for awarded vendors and an overall response rate of 38% in FY2025. While this year’s
overall response rate is slightly below the 43% rate from FY2024, awarded vendors responded at a significantly
higher rate compared to the 40% rate in FY2024. These metrics provide a solid foundation on which the
development team can rationalize program requirements of data submission. Vendors who receive statewide
contract dollars are most likely to respond and could be required to do so, while vendors who did not receive
contract dollars could still be encouraged to submit and will likely do so.
As a result of the Pilot Program, the new Empowering Abilities in Contracting and Employment (EACE) Program
will be effective for FY2026. A summary is provided in the SDO Innovation Section above, and more details will
be reported in the FY2026 Annual Report.
Goal #3: Publish updated program resources and guidance for vendors and Commonwealth departments and
agencies.
In response to the feedback from DOBE/SDVOBE vendors and program partners, the SDO published an updated
Individuals with Disabilities Program webpage. The new page has multiple sections, including resources for
employers, resources for job seekers who identify as having a disability, and a link to the resource repository
created and maintained by the Program Coordinator. The Programs & Engagement team is committed to sharing

promote workplace diversity and equitable access to employment.

Supplier Diversity Program (SDP) Spending Compliance Verification
FY2025 is the third fiscal year that both Operational Services Division (OSD) Statewide Contract Goods and
Services contractors and Health and Human Services contractors were included in the Supplier Diversity
Program (SDP) Compliance Verification.
For reports submitted for FY2025, the SDO Compliance Team sent out verification emails for 2,875 line items
of spend to 1,158 unique certified diverse businesses who served as contractors’ supplier diversity partners. A
total of 769 diverse businesses responded, verifying a total of 1,892 line items of reported spend exceeding
$377 million, a 6% decrease over FY2024. Compared to FY2024, the number of line items verified has
increased from 1,750 to 1,892. The response rate for FY2025 reported spend verification was 66%, a 3%
decrease over FY2024’s verification rate. Please note that diverse partners are strongly encouraged but not
required to respond to verification outreach.
Based on the responses received, the SDO Compliance Team found the following:
Accurate and Understated reports: 1,529 line items of spend (81% of the reports, representing 80% of the
expenditures reported) were confirmed to be accurate or higher than reported, an 8% increase over FY2024.
Of that number, 295 line items (16% of the responses, representing 20% of the reported expenditures) were
understated, and the contractors, in fact, spent more than reported.
Overstated reports: 363 line items (19% of the reports and 19% of the expenditures reported) were found to
be overstated by contractors. All contractors who overstated their spending received notice from the SDO
Compliance Team of the discrepancies identified. For statewide contractors, the SDO Compliance Team also
alerted the OSD Sourcing unit and requested that proof of payment and any corrections, updates, or revisions
be provided to both the SDO and OSD.
Data used for this Annual Report spending section was updated to reflect the spending amounts confirmed by
diverse vendors. All amounts were revised and the corrected spend is reflected in this Annual Report.
