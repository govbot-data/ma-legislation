An Act providing for the structural repairs, hazard remediation and modernization of fire stations
in the city of Fall River.
Whereas, The deferred operation of this act would tend to defeat its purpose, which is to
make supplemental appropriations for fiscal year 2025, which is immediately necessary to carry
out those appropriations or to accomplish other important public purposes, therefore it is hereby
declared to be an emergency law, necessary for the immediate preservation of the public
convenience.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. To provide for certain unanticipated obligations of the commonwealth, to
2 provide for an alteration of purpose for current appropriations and to meet certain requirements
3 of law, the sums set forth in this act are hereby appropriated from the General Fund unless
4 specifically designated otherwise in this section, for the several purposes and subject to the
5 conditions specified in this act, and subject to the laws regulating the disbursement of public
6 funds for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2026. Except as otherwise stated, these sums shall be
7 made available through the fiscal year ending June 30, 2027.

14 that funding shall include, but not limited to, the: (i) inspection and reinforcement of structural
15 elements, including foundations, support beams, walls, staircases and roofs; (ii) repair of ceiling
16 collapses, floor damage and other safety hazards; (iii) compliance with state and local building
17 codes, including fire safety and seismic standards; (iv) full removal and remediation of asbestos,
18 black mold, lead paint and other contaminants; (v) ventilation and filtration upgrades to ensure
19 long-term air quality and safety; (vi) modernization of HVAC, electrical and plumbing systems;
20 (vii) emergency lighting and sprinkler system upgrades; (viii) improvements to energy efficiency
21 and sustainability; (ix) decontamination of showers and equipment washing stations; (x)
22 firefighter-safe rest and recovery areas; (xi) accessible, gender-inclusive living and restroom
23 facilities; and (xii) enhanced emergency communication and dispatch infrastructure; and
24 provided further, that the program shall prioritize funding and construction to fire stations in the
25 city of Fall River that: (a) pose immediate structural safety risks; (b) contain known toxic
26 substances including, but not limited to, mold and asbestos; (c) are in violation of health or fire
27 codes; (d) lack appropriate decontamination or wellness facilities; and (e) demonstrate urgent

29 SECTION 3. (a) For the purposes of this act, the following words shall, unless the
30 context clearly requires otherwise, have the following meanings:
2 of 3

31 “Department”, the department of fire services.
32 “Executive office”, the executive office for administration and finance.
33 “Program”, the Fall River fire station infrastructure and remediation improvement
34 program.
35 (b) The executive office, in consultation with the department and the department of
36 public health, shall establish and administer the Fall River fire station infrastructure and
37 remediation improvement program. The program shall ensure that all fire station facilities within
38 the city of Fall River are structurally sound, free from environmental hazards and adequately
39 equipped to support the health, safety and operational readiness of the city of Fall River fire
40 department.
41 (c) The department of public health shall oversee all environmental remediation work of
42 the program to ensure compliance with applicable health and safety standards. The department
43 shall provide oversight on structural repairs to ensure all improvements meet or exceed state and
44 local code requirements.
45 (d) The executive office, in conjunction with the department, the city of Fall River and
46 project contractors, shall submit an annual report, not later than December 31, on the program
47 established in this act that shall include: (i) detailing project timelines, expenditures, structural
48 evaluations and environmental remediation status of fire stations in the city of Fall River
49 participating in the program; and (ii) any recommendations for improvements to the program.
50 The annual report shall be submitted to the chairs of the joint committee on public safety and
51 homeland security; the chairs of the house and senate committees on ways and means; and the
52 clerks of the house of representatives and senate.
