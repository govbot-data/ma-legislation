An Act relative to the use of video screens while operating a motor vehicle.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 13 of Chapter 90 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2020
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by striking out in lines 27-31 the following words:-
3 No person shall drive any motor vehicle equipped with any television viewer, screen or
4 other means of visually receiving a television broadcast which is located in the motor vehicle at
5 any point forward of the back of the driver’s seat, or which is visible to the driver while
6 operating such motor vehicle.
7 SECTION 2. Subsection (a) of section 13B of said chapter 90 is hereby amended by
8 inserting after the third sentence the following sentence:-
9 No operator of a motor vehicle shall use a mobile electronic device to access social media
10 or use camera functions, including video calls.
1 of 3

11 SECTION 3. Said chapter 90 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the
12 following section:-
13 Section 13D.
14 (a) No motor vehicle registered in the commonwealth shall be equipped with, nor
15 shall be used therein, a television receiver, video monitor, or a television or video screen, or any
16 other similar means of visually displaying a television broadcast or video signal that produces
17 entertainment or business applications, is operating and is located in the motor vehicle at a point
18 forward of the back of the driver’s seat, or is operating and the monitor, screen, or display is
19 visible to the driver while driving the motor vehicle. The operator of a motor vehicle that is not
20 required to be registered in the commonwealth shall not operate a television receiver, video
21 monitor, or a television or video screen or any other similar means of visually displaying a
22 television broadcast or video signal that produces entertainment or business applications that
23 violates the provisions of this section while driving in the commonwealth.
24 The prohibitions contained in this subsection shall not, however, include:
25 1. Electronic displays used in conjunction with vehicle navigation and mapping
26 systems, or as part of a digital dispatch system;
27 2. Closed circuit video monitors designed to operate only in conjunction with
28 dedicated video cameras and used in rear-view systems on trucks, motor homes, and other motor
29 vehicles;
30 3. Television receivers or monitors used in government-owned vehicles by law-
31 enforcement officers in the course of their official duties;
2 of 3

32 4. Visual displays used to enhance or supplement the driver’s view forward, behind,
33 or to the sides of a motor vehicle for the purpose of maneuvering the vehicle;
34 5. A vehicle information display;
35 6. A visual display used to enhance or supplement a driver’s view of vehicle
36 occupants;
37 7. Television-type receiving equipment used exclusively for safety or traffic
38 engineering information; or
39 8. A television receiver, video monitor, television or video screen, or any other
40 similar means of visually displaying a moving image, if that equipment is factory-installed and
41 has an interlock device that, when the motor vehicle operator is performing one or more driving
42 tasks, disables the equipment so that such moving images are not visible to the motor vehicle
43 operator except as a visual display described in subsections 1 through 7. For the purposes of this
44 subsection, “driving task” means all of the real time functions required to operate a vehicle in on-
45 road traffic, excluding the selection of destinations and waypoints, and including steering,
46 turning, lane keeping and lane changing, accelerating and decelerating.
47 (b) Except for displays explicitly authorized in subsection b, no driver of any motor
48 vehicle shall view any motion picture or similar video display while driving.
