To the Honorable Senate and House of Representatives,
I am filing for your consideration a bill entitled “An Act Making Appropriations for
Fiscal Year 2026 to Provide for Supplementing Certain Existing Appropriations and for Certain
Other Activities and Projects.”
This bill is being filed as a companion to the Fiscal Year 2026 budget, which was signed
alongside $130 million in net vetoes designed to ensure that Massachusetts is in the best position
to meet its obligations over the coming year and to respond to economic uncertainties, including
potential federal cuts or economic headwinds.
Because of that, I am requesting $100 million for a reserve to give our administration the
flexibility to address time-sensitive pressures created by federal actions and address unfunded
spending needs for FY26. This would include additional resources for the Department of
Transitional Assistance with the goal of avoiding layoffs that could result without additional
funding. I am also requesting a $30 million transfer to the Housing Preservation and Stabilization
Trust Fund to help the administration respond to the significant uncertainty around federal
housing investments.
I am also seeking a number of administrative tools that will give our administration
greater flexibility in this moment of uncertainty to respond quickly to changing economic
conditions. These tools include:
• Expanded 9C Authority: Authorizing the Administration to make emergency mid-
year budget cuts outside of the Executive Branch under certain conditions.
1 of 2

• Line-Item Transferability: Allowing the Administration to transfer funding
between line-items to help us manage spending effectively throughout the year. This would be a
one-year change capped at 5 percent of the amount appropriated in each line-item.
• MassDOT Capital Salary Flexibility: Authorizing MassDOT on a limited basis to
include salaries of employees assigned to capital projects onto its capital budget.
In order to effectuate one of the vetoes made to the FY26 budget for the Group Insurance
Commission, we are also requesting limited authority to make mid-year health insurance plan
changes for members to address extreme price volatility related to GLP-1 weight-loss drugs. The
high-cost of GLP-1 drugs have put extraordinary pressure on the GIC, and like other states and
private insurers, it is no longer fiscally responsible at this time to cover open access to those
pharmaceuticals. We are asking the GIC board to scale back coverage of those drugs to only
those patients for whom the medication is medically necessary, including those with diabetes.
This will allow us to save $27.5 million in FY26 and will bring health plans offered to members
more in line with what is being offered in the private market.
Finally, we are filing some proposals to give agencies targeted flexibilities, including
transferability for certain line-items within the Executive Office for Housing and Livable
Communities, maintaining access to MassVentures grants for small businesses independent of
accompanying federal funding, and allowing greater flexibility in determining vaccine schedules
for the Childhood Vaccine Program.
Respectfully submitted,
Maura T. Healey,
Governor

An Act making appropriations for the fiscal year 2026 to provide for supplementing certain
existing appropriations and for certain other activities and projects.
Whereas, The deferred operation of this act would tend to defeat its purposes, which are
to make supplemental appropriations for fiscal year 2026 and to make certain changes in law,
each of which is immediately necessary to carry out those appropriations or to accomplish other
important public purposes, therefore it is hereby declared to be an emergency law, necessary for
the immediate preservation of the public convenience.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
