RAFT Legislative Quarterly Report
FY2026, Q1
July 1, 2025 – September 30, 2025
Introduction
The Executive Office of Housing and Livable Communities (EOHLC) submits this report to the House and
Senate Committees on Ways and Means in accordance with legislative language related to line item
7004-9316, the Residential Assistance for Families in Transition (RAFT) program, below:
provided further, that the department shall submit quarterly reports to the house and senate committees
on ways and means detailing the: (1) number of families who applied for assistance; (2) number of
families approved for assistance; (3) minimum, median and average amount of financial assistance
awarded; (4) total amount of assistance awarded to date, including a breakdown by income category;
and (5) number of families falling into each income category.

A household may submit multiple applications for assistance. This can result in an artificially low
approval rate, as some initial applications may time out due to missing documentation but are later
resubmitted and approved. While households can be approved more than once, multiple approvals
within the same quarter are uncommon. Any household that applied or was approved more than once in
a quarter is counted each time.
"Households who applied for assistance" includes all applications received for RAFT, including Letters of
Intent (LOI). LOI applications allow a household to be approved conditionally for RAFT to move to new
housing and return to submit a compete RAFT application when they locate a unit. However,
"Households approved for assistance" and all figures under "Financial Assistance Awarded" include only
RAFT-funded applications and do not include LOI applications. In Q1, an additional 3,791 LOI applications
were approved beyond the RAFT approvals shown. Households with approved LOI applications may, but
do not always, return to apply for RAFT funds.
EOHLC may, as funds are available, fund certain RAFT applications out of the federal Emergency Rental
Assistance (ERA) funding. The section below only includes applications funded by the state RAFT
appropriation.
