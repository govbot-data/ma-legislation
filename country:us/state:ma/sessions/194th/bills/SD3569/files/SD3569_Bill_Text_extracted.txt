Re: Facility Inspection – Essex County Correctional Facility, Middleton
Dear Sheriff Coppinger:
The Massachusetts Department of Public Health (Department) Division of Environmental Health Regulations and
Standards (EHRS) conducted an inspection of the Essex County Correctional Facility on October 15 and 16, 2025
accompanied by Lieutenant Steven Wilkins, Environmental Health and Safety Officer and Captain Jotham Garbin, in
accordance with Department regulations 105 CMR 451.000: Minimum Health and Sanitation Standards and Inspection
Procedures for Correctional Facilities.
The inspection identified 131 total deficiencies: 51 new deficiencies under the Required Standards (.100 and .200 series),
29 repeat deficiencies under the Required Standards, 37 new deficiencies under the Recommended Standards (.300
series), 14 repeat deficiencies under the Recommended Standards.
Overview
Section 1 provides details of all deficiencies, including repeat deficiencies, found during the inspection. These
are categorized by Required Standards, Recommended Standards, or additional applicable regulatory standards.
Section 2 provides information on areas that EHRS found to be compliant.
Section 3 documents the areas that EHRS did not inspect.
Section 4 provides information on submitting a Plan of Correction for the identified deficiencies.
Section 5 outlines observations and recommendations related to the inspection.

SECTION 4: Plan of Correction
This facility does not comply with the Department’s Regulations cited above. In accordance with 105 CMR 451.404,
please submit a plan of correction within 10 working days of receipt of this notice. The Plan of Correction must include:
1. Specific corrective steps to be taken
2. A timetable for the corrective actions for larger projects
3. The date by which correction will be achieved
4. Any interim measures being implemented to ensure the health and safety of incarcerated individuals and facility
staff
5. The plan should be signed by the Superintendent or Administrator and submitted to my attention, at the
address listed above.
SECTION 4: Observations and Recommendations
1. The inmate population was 824 at the time of inspection.
2. During the inspection EHRS staff recommended:
a. Continue with monthly plans for shower drain maintenance to ensure proper function; and
451-25(2)-Essex-Middleton-Report 11-7-2025 Page 7 of 8

b. Continue to work with a professional pest control company to treat drain flies. Prior to treatment,
EHSO’s should assess and document areas throughout the facility that require treatment that is then
shared with the pest control company.
3. During the Kitchen Inspection:
a. EHRS staff noted metal baking sheets/pans and plastic serving trays stacked wet and not dried properly.
EHRS strongly recommended:
i. Both metal baking sheets/pans and plastic trays be separated to be allowed to fully air dry; and
ii. More drying racks be obtained to provide spacing to dry metal baking sheets/pans and plastic
trays.
4. The warewash machine was out-of-order and being serviced at the time of inspection. As an interim measure,
the facility stated they would utilize Styrofoam trays and manually wash, rinse, and sanitize additional dishware
until repairs are made.
5. In 120 Building (F), there were many dirty cells that were unoccupied, particularly in the Behavioral Stabilization
Unit. It is recommended to establish a policy to have cells cleaned out upon exit, for them to be ready upon
move in as needed.

SECTION 4: Observations and Recommendations
1. The inmate population was 824 at the time of inspection.
2. During the inspection EHRS staff recommended:
a. Continue with monthly plans for shower drain maintenance to ensure proper function; and
b. Continue to work with a professional pest control company to treat drain flies. Prior to treatment,
EHSO’s should assess and document areas throughout the facility that require treatment that is then
shared with the pest control company.
3. During the Kitchen Inspection:
a. EHRS staff noted metal baking sheets/pans and plastic serving trays stacked wet and not dried properly.
EHRS strongly recommended:
i. Both metal baking sheets/pans and plastic trays be separated to be allowed to fully air dry; and
ii. More drying racks be obtained to provide spacing to dry metal baking sheets/pans and plastic
trays.
4. The warewash machine was out-of-order and being serviced at the time of inspection. As an interim measure,
the facility stated they would utilize Styrofoam trays and manually wash, rinse, and sanitize additional dishware
until repairs are made.
5. In 120 Building (F), there were many dirty cells that were unoccupied, particularly in the Behavioral Stabilization
Unit. It is recommended to establish a policy to have cells cleaned out upon exit, for them to be ready upon
move in as needed.
