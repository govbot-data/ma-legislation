3 Section 16HH. For the purposes of this section and sections 16II to 16KK, inclusive, the
4 following words shall have the following meanings unless the context clearly requires otherwise:
5 “Abusive treatment”, physical, verbal or mental abuse, including, but not limited to,
6 bullying, directed at or committed by individual home care consumers, surrogates, family
7 members, home care workers, other caregivers and any other individuals present in the home
8 care consumers’ household when the home care worker is providing home care services.
9 “Executive office”, the executive office of health and human services.
10 “Home care agency”, a business, including a sole proprietorship, nonprofit organization
11 or other entity, that: (i) provides home care services to consumers in the consumers’ residences
12 for compensation; (ii) represents itself as a home care agency by name, advertising or
13 communications to the public or uses the terms “home care agency” or “home care” in the
14 entity’s name; or (iii) procures, offers, refers or provides or attempts to provide home care
15 services by matching individuals seeking home care services to entities that provide home care
16 services; provided, however, that “home care agency” shall not include: (A) an entity operated

40 Section 16II. (a) No person or entity shall: (i) establish, maintain, operate or hold itself
41 out as a home care agency; (ii) provide home care services; (iii) or use the words “home care” to
42 describe its services without a home care agency license issued pursuant to subsection (b).
43 Licensure pursuant to said subsection (b) shall be required for home care agencies that directly
44 employ home care workers and home care agencies that contract with entities that employ home
45 care workers.
46 (b)(1) The executive office shall issue for a term of 3 years, and may renew for like
47 terms, a home care agency license to any person or entity that the executive office deems
48 responsible and suitable to establish or maintain a home care agency and meets the requirements
49 of the rules and regulations promulgated pursuant to subsection (e). A license issued under this
50 subsection shall not be transferable or assignable.
51 (2) The executive office may suspend, revoke or refuse to renew for cause a home care
52 agency license in accordance with regulations promulgated by the department pursuant to
53 subsection (e); provided, however, that the office shall provide home care agencies an
54 opportunity for appeal of such suspension, revocation or refusal or renewal.
55 (3) License application and renewal fees shall be established pursuant to section 3B of
56 chapter 7.
57 (c) If the executive office determines a licensee has failed or refused to comply with
58 requirements established under this section or the regulations promulgated thereunder, the
59 executive office may: (i) deny an application for licensure; (ii) modify, suspend or revoke a

60 license; or (iii) issue a fine of not more than $500 for each day of such failure or refusal to
61 comply.

130 Section 16JJ. (a) The executive office, in consultation with the home care worker and
131 consumer abuse stakeholder advisory committee established in subsection (b), MassHealth, the
132 executive office of aging and independence, the department of public health and the PCA quality
133 home care workforce council, shall adopt minimum standards and procedures for addressing
134 abusive treatment of home care workers, personal care attendants and home care consumers. The
135 standards and procedures shall: (i) address abusive treatment, including the connection between

138 consumers; (iii) ensure a safe and healthy work environment for home care workers and personal
139 care attendants; (iv) recognize the privacy rights of home care consumers; and (v) recognize the
140 responsibilities of the consumer-employers of personal care attendants.
