An Act relative to modernizing the pathway to becoming a certified public accountant.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 87A1/2 of chapter 112 of the General laws, as appearing in the
2 2024 Official Edition, is hereby amended by striking out subsection (e) and inserting in place
3 thereof the following subsection:-
4 (e) The educational and experience requirements for a certificate shall be at least 1 of the
5 following:
6 (i) a bachelor’s degree or its equivalent from a college or university approved by the
7 board and 2 years of full-time experience or the equivalent approved by the board;
8 (ii) a bachelor’s degree with 30 semester hours of additional education from a college or
9 university approved by the board and 1 year of full-time experience or the equivalent approved
10 by the board; or
11 (iii) a master’s degree or its equivalent from a college or university approved by the
12 board and 1 year of full-time experience or the equivalent approved by the board.
1 of 3

13 The educational requirements to take the examination required to be passed as a
14 condition for the granting of a certificate shall be set forth in regulations promulgated by the
15 board.
16 SECTION 2. Paragraph (1) of subsection (d) of section 87B of said chapter 112, as so
17 appearing, is hereby amended by inserting after the word “commonwealth;”, in line 33, the
18 following word:- and.
19 SECTION 3. Paragraph (2) of said subsection (d) of said section 87B of said chapter 112,
20 as so appearing, is hereby amended by striking out clauses (A) to (C), inclusive, and inserting in
21 place thereof the following 3 clauses:-
22 (A) is certified or licensed in the other state and is in good standing in the other state;
23 (B) has passed the Unform Certified Public Accountant Examination and has completed
24 the educational requirements listed in subsection (e) of section 87A1/2; or
25 (C) had 4 years of experience in the practice of public accountancy or equivalent,
26 meeting requirements prescribed by the board by rule, after passing the examination upon which
27 their certificate was based within the 10 years immediately preceding their application.
28 SECTION 4. Said subsection (d) of said section 87B of said chapter 112, as so appearing,
29 is hereby further amended by striking out paragraph (3).
30 SECTION 5. Subsection (h) of said section 87B of said chapter 112, as so appearing, is
31 hereby further amended by striking out paragraph (2) and inserting in place thereof the following
32 paragraph:-
2 of 3

33 (h)(2) A person whose principal place of business is outside the commonwealth shall be
34 deemed to have qualifications substantially equivalent to the commonwealth’s requirements for
35 the practice of public accountancy and may engage in the practice of certified public
36 accountancy in the commonwealth, including offering and rendering professional services,
37 whether in person or by mail, telephone or electronic means, if such person holds a valid license
38 as a certified public accountant issued by another state; provided, however, that the person has
39 met the educational and experience requirements listed in subsection (e) of section 87A1/2 and
40 has passed the Uniform Certified Public Accountant Examination or exceeded the licensure
41 requirements of this chapter; provided further, that any person who has passed the Uniform
42 Certified Public Accountant Examination and holds a valid certified public accountant certificate
43 issued by another state on or before December 31, 2025, shall be exempt from the educational
44 requirements in said subsection (e) of said section 87A1/2. Any person who qualifies for the
45 practice privilege pursuant to this subsection may exercise such privilege in the commonwealth
46 without limitation on the period of time within which such person may so practice in the
47 commonwealth if such person remains qualified pursuant to this subsection; provided, however,
48 that such person shall not be required to obtain a certificate or license pursuant to this section,
49 except as provided in this subsection, submit any other notice to the board or obtain a temporary
50 practice permit from or pay any fee to the board.
51 SECTION 6. This act shall take effect on January 1, 2027.
