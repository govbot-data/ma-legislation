An Act to build restorative family and community connection.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Section 36C of chapter 127 of the General Laws of 2022, is hereby amended by striking
2 out the section in its entirety and inserting in place thereof the following:
3 (a) A correctional institution, jail or house of correction shall not: (i) prohibit, eliminate
4 or unreasonably limit in-person visitation of inmates; or (ii) coerce, compel or otherwise pressure
5 an inmate to forego or limit in-person visitation. For the purposes of this section, to unreasonably
6 limit in-person visitation of inmates shall include, but not be limited to, providing an eligible
7 inmate fewer than 2 opportunities for in-person visitation during any 7–day period.
8 (b) A correctional institution, jail or house of correction may use video or other types of
9 electronic devices for inmate communication with visitors; provided, that such communications
10 shall be in addition to and shall not replace in-person visitation, as prescribed in this section.
11 (c) The Commissioner of Correction and administrators of state prisons and county
12 correctional facilities shall allow up to 20 individuals to be eligible to visit an incarcerated
13 person.
1 of 2

14 (d) The Commissioner of Correction and administrators of state prisons and county
15 correctional facilities shall permit individuals to visit more than one incarcerated person in
16 correctional custody, including in the same facility, unless there is an individual determination
17 that such visitation would pose a threat to security or orderly running of the facility. The
18 incarcerated person and the visitor shall be provided with a written explanation of the basis for
19 any such determination, including the objective reasoning and all evidence that was relied upon
20 for the determination. Any such determination may be challenged by an action under Mass.
21 General Laws chapter 249 section 4.
22 e) Nothing in this section shall prohibit the temporary suspension of visitation privileges
23 for good cause including, but not limited to, misbehavior or during a bonafide emergency.
