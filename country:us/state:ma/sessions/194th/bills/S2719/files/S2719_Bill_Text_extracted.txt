SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2719
Senate, November 13, 2025 - Text of the Senate amendment (Senator Tarr) to the House Bill
amending the charter of the town of Ipswich to eliminate the residency requirement for the town
manager (House, No. 4243).
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
1 SECTION 1. The charter of the town of Ipswich, which is on file with the archivist of the
2 commonwealth, as provided in section 12 of chapter 43B of the General Laws, is hereby
3 amended by striking out sections 6 and 7 and inserting in place thereof the following 2 sections:-
4 Section 6. The select board shall appoint a town manager for a term of 3 years. The town
5 manager shall be a person especially fitted by education, training and experience to perform the
6 duties of the office; provided, however, that the town manager shall be appointed without regard
7 to the town manager’s political beliefs. The select board may reappoint an incumbent town
8 manager for successive, 3-year terms of office, as the board may determine to be in the best
9 interests of the town. Before entering upon the duties of the office, the town manager shall be
10 sworn to the faithful and impartial performance thereof by the chair of the select board, the town
11 clerk or a justice of the peace. The town manager shall execute a bond in favor of the town for
12 the faithful performance of the town manager’s duties in such sum and with such surety or
13 sureties as may be fixed or approved by the select board.
14 Section 7. A vacancy in the office of town manager shall be filled as soon as possible by
15 appointment by the select board. Pending the appointment of a town manager or the filling of
1 of 2

16 such a vacancy, the select board may appoint a suitable person to perform the duties of the office
17 temporarily; provided, however, that a temporary appointment shall be made within 30 days of
18 the occurrence of the vacancy and shall be for a period not to exceed 180 days; provided further,
19 that the select board may extend the temporary appointment for an additional period not to
20 exceed 180 days. A temporary town manager appointed pursuant to this section shall serve at
21 compensation to be set by the select board; provided, however, that such compensation shall not
22 exceed the rate of compensation approved for the town manager by the select board.
23 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon its passage.
