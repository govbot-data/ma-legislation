Executive Summary
The HousingWorks Infrastructure Program (“HWIP”) was established in FY24 by the Healey-
Driscoll Administration to support infrastructure needs to increase housing production in
the Commonwealth. Created as a “sibling” program to the MassWorks Infrastructure
Program, HWIP awards capital funds to municipalities and other public entities for a variety
of activities related to infrastructure projects that serves housing units.
HWIP accepted its first round of applications as part of the FY24 round of the Community
One Stop for Growth (“COSG”) and considered applications under the “Infrastructure”
category of the COSG development continuum. The program awards funds based on a
project’s nexus with housing, transportation, infrastructure, and community development
needs. Awarded funds may be used for infrastructure projects associated with housing,
including but not limited to: design, construction, building, rehabilitation, repair, and other
improvements to infrastructure, including, but not limited to, sewers, utility extensions,
streets, roads, curb-cuts, water treatment systems, transit improvements, public parks and
spaces adjacent to planned or proposed housing improvements, and pedestrian and bicycle
ways, to advance projects that support new housing development, preservation, or
rehabilitation, including transit-oriented housing developments.
HWIP Application Rounds
In the first year, HWIP had approximately $25 million in available funds to spend in FY25.
However, as the grants are up to 4 year grants, it allowed EOHLC the opportunity to award
more in the first year to maximize the number of projects supported as the program
launched. For the HWIP inaugural FY24 round through the COSG, HWIP reviewed 39
applications that collectively requested $109,034,228.00. HWIP awarded 15 projects for
$33,528,797.00 which will serve 2,147 housing units.
In the second year, HWIP had approximately $21 million in available funds. Greater
awareness and knowledge of the program more than doubled the number of applications
submitted and amount of funding requested. For the FY25 round of the COSG, HWIP
reviewed 84 applications that collectively requested $253,574,914.00. HWIP awarded 13
projects for $20,252,000.00, which will serve 2694 housing units.
