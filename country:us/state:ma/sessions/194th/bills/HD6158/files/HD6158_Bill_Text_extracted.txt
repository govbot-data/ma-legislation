CHAPTER 140 OF THE ACTS OF 2024
The Fiscal Year 2025 (FY25) General Appropriations Act directed the Department of
Environmental Protection (MassDEP) to develop and administer a grant program for
small businesses to support alternatives to single-use plastics:
2200-0107 …provided further, that not less than $150,000 shall be expended to the
department of environmental protection to develop and administer a grant program
for small businesses to support alternatives to single-use plastics; provided further,
that grants may be used by grantees to purchase biodegradable, compostable or
reusable food service ware, water bottles, bags, packaging or other similar single-use
plastics reduction efforts or activities; provided further, that small businesses located
in low-income communities shall be prioritized in the grant application review
process; provided further, that not later than June 30, 2025, the department shall
submit a report to the senate and house committees on ways and means and the joint
committee on environment and natural resources on the implementation of the grant
program which shall include, but not be limited to: (i) the names and locations of the
grantees; (ii) the grant amounts distributed; (iii) the implemented plastic reduction
efforts through utilization of grant funds; and (iv) if possible, the estimated amount of
single-use plastics reduced
SCOPE
MassDEP provided grants and technical assistance to businesses and non-profits to
replace single use plastic food serviceware with reusables through three programs: the

Page 2
Reduce, Reuse and Repair Micro Grants, the Recycling & Reuse Business Development
Grants, and business assistance organization RecyclingWorks Massachusetts.
1. The Reduce, Reuse, Repair Micro-Grants provide from $3,000 (minimum) to $10,000
(maximum) to eligible businesses, non-profits, regional authorities, municipalities,
and schools/colleges for short-term waste reduction projects (defined as projects
lasting ≤1 year). In the most recent grant cycle, MassDEP awarded four (4) grants to
entities pursuing efforts to reduce single-use plastics. Project descriptions and
grant funds dispersed are listed below:
• Lawrence General Hospital – $10,000 - Replace single-use pitchers and cups
with reusables.
• Colrain Central School and Sanderson Academy – $2,012 - Reusable Serving
Supplies for Two Elementary School Cafeterias
• Women’s Lunch Place, Boston – $5,000 - Replacing single-use dishware at
women's shelter with reusables.
• Berkshire Environmental Action Team - $9,440 - Replacing dishware at event
space and potentially loaning to other events.
2. The Recycling and Reuse Business Development Grant Program provides grants to
Massachusetts businesses and non-profit organizations to expand recycling, reuse,
and composting infrastructure in Massachusetts. The grants range from $50,000 to
$400,000, and target specific priority materials such as food materials, mattresses,
textiles, construction & demolition materials and bulky reusable items. In the most
recent grant cycle, MassDEP awarded two (2) grants to organizations focused on
promoting reusable food service ware, as follows:
Re:Dish – $150,000 - supporting expansion of company servicing small
businesses by collecting, washing, sanitizing, re-delivering, and tracking
reusable food serviceware replacing single-use products.
https://www.redish.com/
• Recirclable LLC – $70,000 - reusable takeout container purchasing Customer
Service Platform software to broaden the reach of reusable takeout
containers in food service. https://www.recirclable.com/
3. RecyclingWorks in Massachusetts (RecyclingWorks) is a recycling assistance
program funded by MassDEP that helps businesses and institutions reduce waste
and maximize recycling, reuse, and food recovery opportunities. MassDEP
contracted with RecyclingWorks ($45,000) to provide a number of services focused
on reducing single-use plastics, including:
