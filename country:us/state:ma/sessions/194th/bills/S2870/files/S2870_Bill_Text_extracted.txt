An Act to provide a sustainable future for rural schools.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 2 of chapter 70 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting after the definition “Required net school
3 spending” the following definition:-
4 “Rural school district”, a school district with both of the following: (i) a student density
5 of not more than 35 students per square mile and (ii) a per capita income of less than the average
6 annual estimated, state-wide per capita income.
7 SECTION 2. Chapter 71 of the General Laws is hereby amended is hereby amended by
8 adding the following section:-
9 Section 100. (a) There is hereby established and set upon the books of the commonwealth
10 a separate fund known as the Rural Schools Aid Fund. The fund shall be credited with: (i)
11 appropriations or other money authorized or transferred by the general court and specifically
12 designated to be credited to the fund; (ii) funds from public and private sources, including, but
13 not limited to gifts, grants and donations; and (iii) any interest earned on such money. Amounts
1 of 8

14 credited shall not be subject to appropriation and shall be expended by the department of
15 elementary and secondary education to support the long-term fiscal health of rural school
16 districts to be administered by the department in accordance with this section.
17 (b) A qualified school eligible for funding are towns and regional school districts,
18 excluding vocational schools, independent agricultural, technical schools and charter schools;
19 provided, that a school district shall be eligible for rural school aid if a school district has a
20 student density of not more than 35 students per square mile and an average annual per capita
21 income of not more than the average annual per capita income for the commonwealth for the
22 same period; provided further, that rural school aid shall be allocated equitably in the following
23 priority order: (i) school districts serving less than 11 students per square mile; (ii) school
24 districts serving not more than 21 students per square mile; and (iii) school districts serving not
25 more than 35 students per square mile;
26 (c) Annually, not later than the first day of December, the department shall submit a
27 report to the house and senate committees on ways and means detailing: (i) its recommendations
28 for additional adjustments to the rural school aid calculation for the upcoming fiscal year to
29 improve the accuracy and equity of the student density component and the per capita income
30 component; and (ii) the calculation and planned distribution of funds to school districts; and
31 provided further, that funds distributed from this section shall not be considered chapter 70 aid
32 for the calculation of the minimum required local contribution for the upcoming fiscal year.
33 (d) Every 5 years, the department shall determine the adequacy of funding for the
34 purposes of this section and recommend to the general court any necessary adjustment.

101 SECTION 10. Said chapter 71 is hereby further amended by inserting after section 16I
102 the following 3 sections:-
103 Section 16J. (a) There is hereby established and set upon the books of the commonwealth
104 a separate fund known as the School District Shared Services and Regionalization Grant Fund.
105 The fund shall be credited with: (i) appropriations or other money authorized or transferred by
106 the general court and specifically designated to be credited to the fund; (ii) funds from public and
107 private sources, including, but not limited to gifts, grants and donations; and (iii) any interest
108 earned on such money. Amounts credited shall not be subject to appropriation and shall be
109 expended by the department to fund a grant program for the study, planning and implementation
110 of school district regionalization and shared services efforts to be administered by the department
111 in accordance with this section; and provided further, that grant funds awarded pursuant to this
112 section shall be distributed evenly over a 3 year period.
113 (b) A qualified entity eligible for funding are towns and regional school districts,
114 excluding vocational schools, independent agricultural, technical schools and charter schools;
115 provided, that a school district shall be eligible for a school district regionalization and shared
116 services grant if it is considering forming, is in the process of forming or has formed within the
117 past 5 years a regional school district or shared services; provided that school district
118 regionalization and shared service grants shall be allocated equitably in the following priority
119 order: (i) school districts with significant enrollment decline as defined by the department of
120 elementary and secondary education; (ii) school districts where existing school space is
6 of 8

121 underutilized; and (iii) school districts where the regionalization or shared services proposal will
122 produce significant expansion of available academic resources and supports as a result of cost
123 savings or economies of scale.
124 (c) Annually, not later than the first day of February, any district receiving funds under
125 this item shall submit a report to the department outlining the progress the district has made in
126 studying, planning or implementing regionalization or shared services.
127 (d) Annually, not later than the first day of December, the department shall submit a
128 report to the house and senate committees on ways and means and the joint committee on
129 education detailing the calculation and planned distribution of funds to school districts; provided
130 that funds distributed from this section shall not be considered chapter 70 aid for the calculation
131 of the minimum required local contribution for the upcoming fiscal year.
132 (e) Every 5 years, the department shall determine the adequacy of funding for the
133 purposes of this section and recommend to the general court any necessary adjustment.

154 potential impacts on students requiring special education services or other supports; and (v) any
155 other potential barriers to regionalizing, establishing a superintendent union, or districts sharing
156 any services. Further, the report shall make recommendations to address any potential barriers to
157 regionalizing, establishing a superintendent union, or districts sharing any services. The office of
158 shared services and regionalization shall submit its report to the clerks of the house of
159 representatives and senate, the house and senate committees on ways and means, and the joint
160 committee on education no later than December 31, 2026.
