HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, June 1, 2026.
The committee on Veterans and Federal Affairs, to whom were referred
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3819) of James Arciero and
others relative to no cost hunting and fishing licenses for 100 percent
disabled veterans; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3820) of
James Arciero and Raymond L. Mascola relative to eligibility of non-
Massachusetts origin veterans for state veteran bonus awards; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3821) of James Arciero and others for an
investigation by a special commission (including members of the General
Court) relative to the feasibility of establishing a veterans' hall of fame; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3822) of James C. Arena-DeRosa
relative to enhancing public service pension for veterans; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3823) of James C. Arena-DeRosa relative
to authorized uses of veterans assistance funds in cities and towns; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3824) of Shirley B. Arriaga for
legislation to provide for the appointment of full-time veterans' agents or
directors of veterans’ services in certain cities and towns; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3825) of Shirley B. Arriaga relative to the
flag of the commonwealth being flown at half-staff; the petition
(accompanied by resolve, House, No. 3826) of Shirley B. Arriaga for an
investigation by a special commission (including members of the General
Court) on women veterans and the long-term effects of combat on
motherhood; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3830) of Natalie
M. Blais, Mindy Domb and Joanne M. Comerford relative to the
reimbursement of cities and towns for veterans' benefits; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3837) of David F. DeCoste relative to
employment preference for veterans at the Massachusetts Port Authority;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3838) of Patricia A. Duffy
and Natalie M. Blais relative to the fee for filing certificates of organization
for veteran owned businesses; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
3840) of Colleen M. Garry relative to fraudulent representation of an
accredited veterans service officer, accredited agent or attorney; the petition

(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3841) of Colleen M. Garry relative to the
calculation of benefits of veterans receiving public housing and other public
assistance; the petition (accompanied by resolutions, House, No. 3843) of
Carmine Lawrence Gentile, Natalie M. Higgins and Mary S. Keefe for the
adoption of resolutions memorializing the Congress of the United States to
pass and send to the states for ratification a constitutional amendment to
restore the first amendment and fair elections to the people; the petition
(accompanied by resolutions, House, No. 3844) of Carmine Lawrence
Gentile and others for adoption of resolutions memorializing the Congress
of the United States to admit Washington, D.C. into the Union as a state of
the United States of America; the petition (accompanied by resolutions,
House, No. 3845) of Carmine Lawrence Gentile and others for the adoption
of resolutions memorializing the Congress of the United States to ratify the
proposed amendment to the Constitution of the United States for federal
regulation of child labor; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
3846) of Carmine Lawrence Gentile, James B. Eldridge and Mary S. Keefe
relative to reviving, continuing and further regulating the citizens
commission established (under Chapter 322 of the Acts of 2018) concerning
a Constitutional amendment to secure government of the people; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3847) of Jessica Ann Giannino
for legislation to recognize and provide assistance for military service-
related injuries from agent orange and burn pits; the petition (accompanied
by bill, House, No. 3850) of Tara T. Hong and others relative to establishing
a veterans service dog program; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 3852) of Steven S. Howitt relative to veteran health, opportunity,
notification, observance and respect; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 3854) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others relative to establishing
a Vietnam War commemoration fund; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 3856) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others relative to increasing
the reimbursement of burial expenses for veterans; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3857) of Patrick Joseph Kearney relative
to the definition of veteran; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
3858) of Patrick Joseph Kearney relative to no cost hunting and fishing
licenses for 100 percent disabled veterans; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 3859) of Meghan K. Kilcoyne relative to veterans' benefits;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3861) of Michael P.
Kushmerek relative to enhancing burial expenses for veterans; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3863) of Kate Lipper-Garabedian relative
to COLA adjustments for veteran benefits; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 3864) of Kate Lipper-Garabedian relative to public

employee benefits for military service members on active duty; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3865) of Kate Lipper-Garabedian relative
to reimbursement to armed service members; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 3866) of Kate Lipper-Garabedian that the Board of
Registration in Nursing be authorized to establish a waiver program for
military personnel who satisfactorily complete medical training offered
through the military; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3868)
of John J. Marsi and others that veterans benefits payments shall not be
considered income for determining eligibility for other state services; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3870) of Paul McMurtry relative
to the definition of veteran; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
3872) of Kelly W. Pease and Dennis C. Gallagher relative to the mission of
the Office of the Veteran Advocate; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 3874) of Bridget Plouffe relative to the Veterans’ Bonus
Division; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3875) of Sean Reid
relative to the establishment of a veterans research trust fund; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3876) of David Allen Robertson for
legislation to provide for the furnishing of headstones for deceased
members of the National Guard; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 3877) of David Allen Robertson relative to retirement benefits for
veterans; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3879) of Adam J.
Scanlon relative to providing for a prisoner of war income tax exemption;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3880) of Adam J. Scanlon
that cities and towns be authorized to provide courtesy retail parking spaces
designed for non-handicapped veterans; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 3881) of Adam J. Scanlon relative to increasing access to
medical care for veterans by providing use of commuter rail, subway, bus,
shuttle and boat services at no cost; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 3882) of Danillo A. Sena relative to the military family
advocacy program, domestic violence and child abuse and neglect; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3883) of Alan Silvia and others
relative to disability benefits for veterans; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 3884) of Todd M. Smola and Steven George Xiarhos relative to
emergency medical transportation provided to veterans; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3885) of Todd M. Smola relative to
veterans’ benefits for surviving spouses; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 3887) of Jeffrey Rosario Turco and David F. DeCoste for
legislation to include certain members of the National Guard in the
definition of veterans; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3889)
of Steven George Xiarhos and Joseph D. McKenna relative to extending

veterans benefits to surviving spouses; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 4002) of Kip A. Diggs relative to benefits for disabled veterans
and their families; and the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4479)
of Lindsay N. Sabadosa for legislation to rescind all previous calls for a
federal constitutional convention, reports (under the provisions of House
Rule 27) recommending that the accompanying order (House, No. 5466)
ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
JOSEPH W. MCGONAGLE, JR.
