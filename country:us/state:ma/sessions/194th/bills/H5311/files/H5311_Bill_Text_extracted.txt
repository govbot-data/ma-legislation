HOUSE . . . . . . . . No. 5311
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
________________________________________
HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, April 2, 2026.
The committee on Election Laws, to whom was referred the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 799) of Shirley B. Arriaga and others
for legislation to further regulate voter registration rolls; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 800) of Bruce J. Ayers for legislation to
ensure that polling places are accessible to handicapped and elderly voters;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 802) of Donald R.
Berthiaume, Jr., for legislation to require the showing of valid photo form
of identification to election officials prior to voting; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 807) of Tackey Chan and David Holway
relative to political contributions by certain public employees; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 811) of Mike Connolly for legislation to
authorize cities and towns to provide for citizen-funded election
campaigns for municipal offices; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 812) of Mike Connolly relative to transparent political campaigning;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 815) of Gene John Cass
relative to town committee elections and membership; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 816) of Michael S. Day relative to
political campaign contributions; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 817) of Vincent Lawrence Dixon relative to the disclosure of the
political party offices' campaign contributions; the petition (accompanied
by bill, House, No. 818) of Marjorie C. Decker relative to the disclosure of
donations by major policy makers engaging in political activity; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 819) of Mindy Domb relative to
random placement of candidate names and removal of incumbent
information on primary and state ballots; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 821) of Daniel M. Donahue for legislation to create a
payroll deduction for voluntary contributions to not-for-profit social
welfare organizations and candidate or other political committees; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 824) of Paul J. Donato relative
to political designations; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
826) of Paul K. Frost relative to candidates for public office reporting

public assistance; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 827) of
Paul K. Frost relative to the voting procedures of presidential electors; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 833) of Colleen M. Garry
relative to the identification requirements for voters; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 834) of Carmine Lawrence Gentile and
others relative to same day voter registration; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 839) of Russell E. Holmes relative to increasing voter
registration and participation of people incarcerated for felony convictions
to help prevent recidivism; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
840) of Russell E. Holmes relative to special election scheduling; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 842) of Steven S. Howitt for
legislation to increase the safety and security of unattended ballot boxes;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 843) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr.
and others relative to the voting procedures of presidential electors; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 844) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr.,
and others relative to political contributions by investment advisors doing
business with public entities; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 845) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others relative to the campaign
finance reporting cycle; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
849) of David Henry Argosky LeBoeuf and Steven Owens relative to
inactive voters and municipal census administration; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 850) of David Henry Argosky LeBoeuf
relative to electioneering communication expenditures; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 851) of David Henry Argosky LeBoeuf
for legislation to ensure a complete, accurate, and equitable census count
and to create a census equity commission (including members of the
General Court) to ensure that censuses accurately and completely count all
residents of the Commonwealth; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 853) of Kate Lipper-Garabedian and Jason M. Lewis relative to the
composition of the boards of registrars of voters; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 857) of James M. Murphy for
legislation to establish a special commission (including members of the
General Court) to investigate ways to increase voter participation in
primary elections; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 859) of
Kelly W. Pease for legislation to further regulate ballot request
notifications; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 863) of
Orlando Ramos for legislation to establish same-day voter registration; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 864) of Orlando Ramos relative
to mail-in ballots; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 865) of
David M. Rogers for legislation to further define felony convictions as

relates to the disqualification of voters; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 867) of Jeffrey N. Roy relative to campaign contributions at
bake sales, farmer’s markets, or community festivals by political party
committees; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 871) of
Kenneth P. Sweezey for legislation to further regulate primary elections;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 873) of Chynah Tyler
relative to voting rights for convicted felons; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 874) of Erika Uyterhoeven for legislation to restore voting
rights to incarcerated felons; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
875) of Erika Uyterhoeven and Lindsay N. Sabadosa relative to limiting
political spending by foreign-influenced corporations; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 877) of Marcus S. Vaughn relative to
choosing presidential electors and the process which governs them; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 878) of Marcus S. Vaughn
relative to the availability of voter lists; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 880) of Tommy Vitolo relative to mailing of early voting
ballots by city and town clerks; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 882) of Susannah M. Whipps relative to political designations and
terminology; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3918) of
Christine P. Barber (with the approval of the mayor and city council) that
the city of Somerville be authorized to provide voting rights in municipal
elections for certain residents aged 16 and 17 years old; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 4033) of Erika Uyterhoeven (with the
approval of the mayor and city council) relative to providing for legal
voting rights in municipal elections for non-citizen residents of the city of
Somerville; the joint petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4095) of
Natalie M. Blais and Paul W. Mark (by vote of the town) that the town of
Conway be authorized to provide legal voting rights in municipal elections
for residents of said town aged 16 and 17 years old; the joint petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 4096) of Lindsay N. Sabadosa and
Joanne M. Comerford (with the approval of the mayor and city council)
relative to amending the charter of the city of Northampton to authorize
residents sixteen years or age or older to vote in preliminary and city
elections; the joint petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4097) of
Lindsay N. Sabadosa and Joanne M. Comerford (with the approval of the
mayor and city council) relative to amending the charter of the city of
Northampton to authorize resident non-citizens to vote in preliminary and
city elections; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4132) of
Michelle L. Ciccolo (by vote of the town) that the town of Lexington be
authorized to grant legal residents with local voting rights in municipal

elections; the joint petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4148) of
Aaron L. Saunders and Joanne M. Comerford (by vote of the town)
relative to lawful noncitizen permanent resident voting in the town of
Shutesbury; the joint petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4190) of
Mindy Domb and Joanne M. Comerford (with the approval of the town
council) that the city known as the town of Amherst be authorized to
extend local voting rights for lawful permanent residents residing in said
town; the joint petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4209) of Natalie
M. Blais and Joanne M. Comerford (by vote of the town) that the town of
Sunderland be authorized to provide legal voting rights in municipal
elections for residents of said town aged 16 and 17 years old; the joint
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4223) of Sean Garballey, Cindy
F. Friedman and David M. Rogers (by vote of the town) that the town of
Arlington be authorized to grant legal voting rights in municipal elections
to town residents aged 16 and 17 years old; the joint petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 4241) of Natalie M. Blais and Paul W.
Mark (by vote of the town) that the town of Whately be authorized to
provide legal voting rights in municipal elections for town of Whately
residents aged 16 and 17 years old; and the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 4713) of Carmine Lawrence Gentile, Simon Cataldo and
Michael J. Barrett (by vote of the town) relative to ranked choice voting in
the town of Concord, reports (under the provisions of House Rule 27)
recommending that the accompanying order (House, No. 5311) ought to
be adopted.
For the committee,
DANIEL J. HUNT.
