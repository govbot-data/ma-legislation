By Representatives Williams of Springfield and Davis of Great Barrington, a petition (subject to
Joint Rule 12) of Bud L. Williams and Leigh Davis for the issuance of an annual proclamation
by the Governor to designate August 22 as Elizabeth Freeman day of equality, healing, and
remembrance. State Administration and Regulatory Oversight.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act establishing Elizabeth Freeman Day of Equality, Healing and Remembrance in the
Commonwealth.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 6 of General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after section 15FFFFFFF the
2 following section:-
3 Section 15GGGGGGG. The governor shall annually issue a proclamation setting apart
4 August 22 as Elizabeth Freeman Day of Equality, Healing, and Remembrance, in recognition of
5 the courageous contribution of Elizabeth Freeman, an illiterate and enslaved Black woman -
6 Known in 1781 as "Mum Bett" - who sued for her freedom at great personal risk and won, and
7 who's case, Brom and Bett v. Ashley, was a spark that ignited the legal end of slavery in
8 Massachusetts by demonstrating the power of the 1780 Massachusetts Constitution, which
9 declares that all are born free and equal.
