HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 4510 FILED ON: 3/17/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 4320
By Representatives Moran of Lawrence and Scanlon of North Attleborough, a petition (subject
to Joint Rule 12) of Frank A. Moran, Adam J. Scanlon and others for legislation to establish a
special task force (including members of the General Court) to review and report on vocational
admissions policies. Education.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act establishing a vocational admissions task force.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, there shall be a
2 Vocational Admissions Task Force to examine and make recommendations on (1) Chapter 74
3 admissions policies, (2) the collection, dissemination, and analysis of district-level and statewide
4 data on Chapter 74 admissions and waitlists, and (3) the standard for review and enforcement of
5 said policies and data.
6 SECTION 2. The task force shall consist of 15 members: the house and senate chairs of
7 the joint committee on education, or their designees, who shall serve as the co-chairs of the task
8 force; the task force shall also consist of the house and senate chairs of the joint committee on
9 labor and workforce development, a member of the general court appointed by the senate
10 minority leader; a member of the general court appointed by the house minority leader; the
11 commissioner of elementary and secondary education, or a designee, 1 member appointed by the
12 Massachusetts Association of Vocational Administrators (MAVA), 1 member appointed by the
1 of 2

13 Alliance for Vocational Technical Education (AVTE), 1 member appointed by the Massachusetts
14 Career & Technical Educators Organization (MassCTE), 1 member appointed by the
15 Massachusetts Association of School Committees (MASC), 1 member appointed by the
16 Massachusetts Association of School Superintendents (MASS), 1 member appointed by the
17 Massachusetts Association of Regional Schools (MARS), 1 member of the Gateway Cities
18 Legislative Caucus, and 1 member appointed by the Massachusetts Municipal Association
19 (MMA).
20 SECTION 3. The first meeting of the task force shall take place within 60 days of the
21 effective date of this act. The task force shall conduct at least one public hearing to receive
22 testimony from members of the public. The task force shall file a report containing its findings
23 and recommendations, including legislative recommendations, if any, with the clerks of the
24 house and senate not later than 12 months following the first meeting of the task force. The
25 Department of Elementary Education shall not promulgate or otherwise amend its regulations
26 relative to Chapter 74 admissions prior to the completion of the task force’s report, nor shall the
27 Department take any other action prior to the completion of the task force’s report that would
28 otherwise alter, amend, or affect the admissions policies of Chapter 74 schools and programs for
29 the 2025-2026 or 2026-2027 admission cycles or school years. Any regulatory, policy, or
30 enforcement action taken by the Department relative to Chapter 74 admissions policies between
31 March 12, 2025 and the release of the task force report shall be deemed to be in violation of this
32 Section.
33 SECTION 4. This act shall be effective upon passage.
