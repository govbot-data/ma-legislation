Massachusetts Hate Crimes, 2024
Executive Summary
Notes on Data
This report is written in accordance with Section 34 of Chapter 22C of the Massachusetts General Laws. The 2024 Hate Crime Report for Massachusetts
was compiled using data voluntarily supplied by police departments across the Commonwealth. The Executive Office of Public Safety & Security
(EOPSS) received a total of 467 reports of incidents of hate crime (down from 557 in 2023) from 82 municipal police departments, 13 campus police
agencies and the Massachusetts State Police. In addition, 295 agencies submitted “zero reports” (indicating that they had experienced no bias-motivated
incidents) and there were 37 non-reporting agencies. In 2024, reporting agencies and zero-reporting agencies covered approximately 98% of the
population of Massachusetts. After receipt and state data quality checks, EOPSS submits all Massachusetts hate crime data to the FBI for further
analysis, verification, and inclusion in its annual Hate Crime Statistics publication. EOPSS continues to verify and update hate crime reports after
submission to the FBI, and as a result, the data in this report may not match the FBI’s published data.
Data was collected utilizing the National Incident Based Reporting System (NIBRS). 2020 was the last year that the FBI collected UCR or Summary
Reporting System (SRS) data, fully transitioning to NIBRS for the 2021 data collection. Agencies report Hate Crimes by exporting data from their
records management system (RMS) in accordance with the FBI’s NIBRS program and uploading their files to EOPSS, or by manually entering pertinent
data fields through EOPSS’ Sidearm data portal. EOPSS then extracts the required data for analysis and forwards it to the FBI. It is important to note
that several of the categories displayed in this report are only available in NIBRS, so comparisons to prior years may be misleading or incomplete as
certain data elements were previously not captured via UCR.
Bias Motivation
A “hate crime,” which can be referred to interchangeably as a “bias crime” or “bias-related / motivated crime,” is not reported as a stand-alone offense,
but rather as part of a separate criminal violation, ranging from vandalism to harassment to violent crimes. Of the 481 hate crime offenses reported in
2024, the most frequent offenses were Vandalism / Damage / Destruction of Property (38%), which is often graffiti or the defacing of property and
Intimidation (21%). Simple Assault and Aggravated Assault incidents both had a significant portion of offenses accounting for 19.3% & 12.9%
respectively. In terms of violent crime, there were 62 bias-motivated Aggravated Assaults reported in 2024, which is up slightly from the 59 reported
in 2023. In 2024, as in 2023, there were 0 bias-motivated homicides, after reporting 2 in 2021. Historically, Vandalism and Intimidation are the top
two offenses reported during bias incidents and in recent years have each accounted for approximately 30% of offenses. (See Table 1.)
In 2024, similar to years past, prejudice against race / ethnicity or national origin was the most widely reported bias motivation representing 42.7% of
the total (down from 45.1% in 2023). Bias against religious groups surpassed bias against sexual orientation with 34.3% (up from 24.6% in 2023).
Sexual orientation was third with 16.3% of the total (down from 19.7% in 2023). (See Figure 1 & Table 2.)

Victims and Offenders
As in previous years, males were the most frequent victims (63.9% of known victims). This number has been in the 50-60% range for the past several
years whereas prior numbers reached 70% of victims being reported as male as recently as 2012. The known offenders of hate crimes continue to be
predominantly male with 77.4% of offenders identified as males. Females were victims in 36.1% of incidents where the sex of the victim was identified.
Females as a percentage of offenders where sex was determined accounted for 22.6% (down from 24.8% in 2023). (For a breakdown of victims and
offenders by gender, please refer to Tables 14 and 16.) When the race of the victim was determined by reporting agencies, whites were the most
frequent racial category as victims 60.4% (up from 58.3% in 2023). White offenders accounted for 70.9% of known offenders down from 73% in
2023). African American victims of both genders comprised 33.3% of the total victim population, up from 32.6% the previous year. African American
offenders comprised 25.6% of offenders, up from 24.2% the previous year. (See Table 15.)
Victims were reported to have some type of injury in 13.7% of cases. This is consistent with last year’s 13.6% and above past years, where typical
numbers hovered around 7-8% of incidents resulting in some injury. Of the 64 total injuries, 14 resulted in a major injury of some type which is the
exact same number as in 2023. (See Table 17.)
Location
Bias incidents reported by police agencies in Massachusetts occur in a variety of locations, both public and private. In 2024, 26.8% of incidents
occurred at a residence or home, while an additional 14.6% occurred on a highway or street. Combining all school categories resulted in 13.3%, which
is exactly the same as 2023. (See Table 11.) Other location types with 10 or more incidents were other / unknown; church / synagogue / temple /
mosque; restaurant; parking / drop lot / garage; Government / public building; commercial / office building; and park / playground. Religious locations
were reported 30 times or in 6.2% of incidents. This is a decrease from the 35 reported in 2023, however a significant increase from the 15 incidents
reported in 2022.
Targets
Individuals and private property were the primary targets for hate crimes in the Commonwealth, with 77% of the reports, down from 77.7% in 2023.
Business and Government targets were the second most frequent targets in 2024 with 7.9% of the reports. (See Table 10.)
Hate Crimes Historical Perspective
Over the past 25 years, the numbers and the categories of bias motivations have remained fairly consistent. Between 2000 and 2002, approximately
500 bias motivations were reported each year. From 2003 through 2018, the number declined to an average of about 360 incidents per year and has
remained extremely consistent over this extended period. Over this time-period, the most frequently reported bias motivations have remained similar:
Anti-Black bias has consistently been most frequent, followed by Anti-Gay, Anti-Semitic and Anti-White. The 557 incidents reported in 2023 were

the highest reported since 2002 and was a 26.5% increase over 2022. 2024 saw a significant decrease of 16% from 2023. The most notable change

Definitions
Hate Crime Any criminal act to which a bias motive is evident as a contributing factor. The MA Hate Crime Reporting Act of 1991
covers bias on account of race, religion, ethnicity, handicap, gender, or sexual orientation. Hate crimes can be reported
only by law enforcement agencies. See M.G.L. c. 22C, § 32 for more details.
Zero Report An official report by a police department stating no hate crimes were reported to the department during the reporting
period.
Non-reporting agencies Local police departments, and other law enforcement agencies, which are not in compliance with the provisions of the
Reporting Act. These agencies failed to indicate how many, if any, bias crimes occurred in their jurisdiction.
Reporting agencies Local police departments, as well as other law enforcement agencies, who have voluntarily reported the incidence of
identified bias crimes in their jurisdiction. Agencies can comply with the Reporting Act by reporting the number of bias
crimes, even if zero.
Reports by Law Enforcement Agencies
In the statistics that follow, it should be noted that a single incident report may include multiple offenses, bias motivations, victims or offenders. As a
result, totals in some categories may exceed the number of reports. Similarly, some reports omit information in certain categories, resulting in totals
lower than the number of incident reports collected.
Hate crimes were reported by a total of 97 different law enforcement agencies. A total of 467 hate crime reports were filed by these agencies, which
included 481 separate offenses and 510 reported biases. Boston reported 149 hate crimes in 2024, which is down from 192 in 2023. Other agencies
that reported 10 or more hate crimes were: Newton (43), Cambridge (34), Natick (12), Danvers (10) and Salem (10). (See Figure 7 and Table 19.)

Categories of Bias Reported
Of the 510 bias motivations for particular incidents reported, race / ethnicity / national origin bias constituted the largest category of reported motives,
with 42.7% of the total (down from 45.1% in 2023). Offenses related to religious affiliation were the second most prevalent, with 34.3% (up from
24.6% in 2023). Offenses motivated by sexual orientation were the third most frequent category, with 16.3% of the total (down from 19.7% in 2023).
. Although there was a significant decrease in the total number of reported Hate Crimes, Anti-Jewish incidents did increase by 23.5% vs 2023.

Categories of Crimes Reported
Of the 481 total offenses reported, Vandalism / Destruction of Property was the most frequently reported with 183 offenses, representing 38% of the
total. Intimidation was the second most frequently reported offense, with 101 incidents representing 21% of the total offenses. Assaults also formed
a significant portion of incidents, accounting for 19.3% and 12.9% respectively. Combining Aggravated and Simple Assaults would yield 155 assaults
where offenders physically confronted a victim, which is a decrease compared to last year’s total of 161.

Victim and Offender Profile
An Individual / Person (including private property) was the target in 77% of cases (down from 77.7% in 2023). Business and Government targets were
second, with 7.9%. Religious targets were chosen in 22 incidents or 3.9% of incidents (up from 2.7% the previous year).
In the 366 NIBRS incidents where the victim’s race was reported, whites were victims 60.4% of the time (up from 58.3% in 2023), while African
Americans were victims in 33.3% of cases (down from 32.6% in 2023). The victims were male in 63.9% of cases. This year, the most common age
groups reporting hate crimes were 41-45 (11.4%) and 21-25-year-olds (10.7%). (See Table 8 & 13.) In 2023, the most frequent age groups reporting
were 21-25 with 14.24% and 31-35 with 14%.
The perpetrators of hate crimes were white in 70.9% of cases (down from 73% in 2023) and African American in 25.6% of cases (up from 24.2% in
2023), in the 254 cases where this information was identified. The perpetrator was male in 77.4% of known cases (up from 75.2% in 2023), and of the
cases where the offender(s)’s age group was identified, the most frequently reported age group was 31-35 with 14.7. In 2023, the most frequent age
group was 11-15 with 15.2%.

Characteristics of the Incidents
The most common location for hate crime incidents to occur in 2024 was a residence / home (26.8%, up from 24.6% in 2023); followed by highway /
road / street / sidewalk (14.6%, down from 18.3% in 2023) and School – College / University (7.3%) which is down by two (2) incidents in 2023 (37
incidents in 2023 and 35 in 2024). (See Table 11.)
Weapons of some type were reported in 142 incidents, which is up from 141 in 2023. In cases where the type of weapon was identified, hands / fist /
feet were used the most frequently (47.2%, down from 48.2% in 2023). Other weapons were the second most frequently listed in 25.4% of cases. Some
type of firearm was utilized in 4 incidents, up from 2 in 2023 and significantly down from 2021 where a firearm was used in 11 incidents. (See Table
12.)
In 2024, there were 64 incidents where injuries were reported. Minor injuries were reported in 50 incidents. This year, there were some other major
injuries. There were 4 Broken Bones, 2 lacerations, 2 internal injuries, 3 other major injuries, 2 victims lost consciousness and 1 incident where a
victim lost teeth. (See Table 17.)
