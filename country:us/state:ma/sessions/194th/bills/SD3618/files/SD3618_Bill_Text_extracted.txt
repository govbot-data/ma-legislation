Re: Facility Inspection – Middlesex County Jail and House of Corrections, Billerica
Dear Sheriff Koutoujian:
The Massachusetts Department of Public Health (Department) Division of Environmental Health Regulations and
Standards (EHRS) conducted an inspection of the Middlesex County Jail and House of Corrections on December 2, 3, and
5, 2025 accompanied Captain Shane Cassidy, Assistant Deputy Superintendent; Captain Kara Turco; and Sergeant Sean
Lee, Environmental Health and Safety Officer, in accordance with Department regulations 105 CMR 451.000: Minimum
Health and Sanitation Standards and Inspection Procedures for Correctional Facilities.
The inspection identified 376 total deficiencies: 179 new deficiencies under the Required Standards (.100 and .200
series), 61 repeat deficiencies under the Required Standards, 108 new deficiencies under the Recommended Standards
(.300 series), 24 repeat deficiencies under the Recommended Standards, 4 new deficiencies under 105 CMR 451.402(B)
(other conditions that may constitute a threat to health or safety).
Overview
Section 1 provides details of all deficiencies, including repeat deficiencies, found during the inspection. These
are categorized by Required Standards, Recommended Standards, or additional applicable regulatory standards.
Section 2 provides information on areas that EHRS found to be compliant.
Section 3 documents the areas that EHRS did not inspect.
Section 4 provides information on submitting a Plan of Correction for the identified deficiencies.
Section 5 outlines observations and recommendations related to the inspection.

SECTION 5: Observations and Recommendations
1. The inmate population was 679 at the time of inspection.
2. At the time of inspection, the EHRS received a complaint that inmates housed in HSU are not afforded
recreational time outside of their cells during inclement weather due to the Tier Gym being closed. The EHRS
recommends a procedure be developed to ensure compliance is maintained with 105 CMR 451.212 Recreational
Opportunities during all weather conditions.
3. During the kitchen inspection:
a. Hot meat sauce from the serving line was placed directly into the walk-in cooler in large, covered pans.
The EHRS stated their concerns with the ability for the food to cool properly and safely. The EHRS
recommended cooling procedures be implemented to ensure food is cooled in accordance with 105
CMR 590; FC 3-501.14 Cooling which requires food to be cooled:
i. Within 2 hours from 57ºC (135ºF) to 21ºC (70°F); and
ii. Within a total of 6 hours from 57ºC (135ºF) to 5ºC (41°F) or less.
b. The EHRS recommended obtaining additional dry storage racks to store serving trays, baking sheets, and
additional kitchen equipment in a self-draining position.
c. The warewash machine was not in use and the area was under renovations. The facility is manually
washing/sanitizing dishes and using Styrofoam trays until renovations are complete.

1. Specific corrective steps to be taken

SECTION 5: Observations and Recommendations
1. The inmate population was 679 at the time of inspection.
2. At the time of inspection, the EHRS received a complaint that inmates housed in HSU are not afforded
recreational time outside of their cells during inclement weather due to the Tier Gym being closed. The EHRS
recommends a procedure be developed to ensure compliance is maintained with 105 CMR 451.212 Recreational
Opportunities during all weather conditions.
3. During the kitchen inspection:
a. Hot meat sauce from the serving line was placed directly into the walk-in cooler in large, covered pans.
The EHRS stated their concerns with the ability for the food to cool properly and safely. The EHRS
recommended cooling procedures be implemented to ensure food is cooled in accordance with 105
CMR 590; FC 3-501.14 Cooling which requires food to be cooled:
i. Within 2 hours from 57ºC (135ºF) to 21ºC (70°F); and
ii. Within a total of 6 hours from 57ºC (135ºF) to 5ºC (41°F) or less.
b. The EHRS recommended obtaining additional dry storage racks to store serving trays, baking sheets, and
additional kitchen equipment in a self-draining position.
c. The warewash machine was not in use and the area was under renovations. The facility is manually
washing/sanitizing dishes and using Styrofoam trays until renovations are complete.
