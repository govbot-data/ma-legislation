RE: Settlements and Judgments Report —Fiscal Year 2026 Quarter 1
Dear Chair Rodrigues and Chair Michlewitz:
Pursuant to Section 2 of Chapter 9 of the Acts of 2025, the Office of the Comptroller hereby
submits to the House and Senate Committees on Ways and Means a report on payments from the
Settlements and Judgments reserve (1599-3384) for the first quarter of Fiscal Year 2026.
The Settlements and Judgements reserve entered Fiscal Year 2026 with a $1 million
appropriation included by the Legislature in the General Appropriation Act. The chart below
shows the funding and spending for the first quarter of Fiscal Year 2026.

As the table shows, the Quarter 1 spending was fully covered by appropriated funds. The
remaining $792,338 in the Settlements & Judgments reserve is likely to be exhausted in the first
weeks of Quarter 2. When the appropriated funds are exhausted, we will continue to pay
Settlements & Judgements up to the amount allowed by the Spend in Anticipation mechanism.
For Fiscal Year 2026, the Spend in Anticipation mechanism set $20,306,986 as the maximum
obligation available for Fiscal Year 2026. Given that the $5,977,479 is counted against that
total, the incremental capacity provided by Spend in Anticipation is $14,329,507. If Fiscal Year
2026 expenditures follow recent patterns, this will provide adequate capacity for several months
to come. Amounts paid under the Spend in Anticipation will require a supplemental
appropriation in the current fiscal year.
A detailed report is attached that provides information on payees, amounts, and the associated
department or agency for settlements and judgments paid during Quarter 1 from appropriation
account 1599-3384. This information is also posted quarterly on the Comptroller’s transparency
website, CTHRU, which may be accessed via this link, https://www.macomptroller.org/cthru.
