An Act to establish a perinatal behavioral health care workforce trust fund.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 6A of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after Section 16GG the
2 following new section:
3 Section 16HH. (a) As used in this section, the following words shall have the following
4 meanings:
5 “Medically underserved populations”, populations that have: (i) too few
6 primary care, behavioral health, or perinatal healthcare providers;(ii) a high infant
7 mortality rate; (iii) high rates of poverty; or (iv) high elderly population.
8 “Perinatal”, the period of time from pregnancy up until one year following birth.
9 “Perinatal individual”, an individual that: (i) is either pregnant or is within 12 months
10 from the date of giving birth; (ii) is a biological parent or an adoptive or foster parent who is
1 of 3

11 within 12 months from assuming custodial care of a child; or (iii) has lost a pregnancy due to a
12 still birth, miscarriage or a medical termination within the previous 12 months.
13 “Perinatal behavioral health care workforce”, health care providers in the field of mental
14 or behavioral health, including substance use disorders, who are acting in accordance with the
15 laws of the commonwealth and who focus on perinatal health in perinatal individuals.
16 “Secretary”, the secretary of health and human services.
17 (a) The secretary shall establish a trust fund to address the perinatal behavioral health
18 workforce; this fund shall be referred to as Perinatal Behavioral Health Care Workforce Trust
19 Fund.
20 (b) The secretary shall administer funds from said trust fund through grants to entities to
21 establish or expand educational or training programs designed to grow and diversify the perinatal
22 mental and behavioral health care workforce.
23 (c) The secretary shall promulgate regulations or guidelines as necessary to develop and
24 implement the grant application process and eligible uses of grant funds pursuant to this section;
25 provided that the grant funds prioritize programs serving medically underserved populations or
26 operating in areas with significant racial, ethnic or geographic disparities in perinatal health
27 outcomes;
28 (d) The secretary shall make public an annual report on
29 1) the number of grants awarded to entities in the preceding fiscal year and the amount of
30 each such grant;
31 2) the nature of entities receiving grant funds;
2 of 3

32 3) the persons or organizations participating in educational or training programs provided
33 by the entities receiving grant funds, provided that all information is stratified by demographics,
34 including but not limited to age, race, ethnicity, gender identity, language, and zip code;
35 4) the impact the entities’ use of grant funds on healthcare shortages;
36 5) the impact the entities’ use of grant funds on the sustainability of the perinatal mental
37 and behavioral health workforce; and
38 6) the populations, communities and individuals served pursuant to such grants, provided
39 that all information is stratified by demographics, including but not limited to age, race,
40 ethnicity, gender identity, language, and zip code.
