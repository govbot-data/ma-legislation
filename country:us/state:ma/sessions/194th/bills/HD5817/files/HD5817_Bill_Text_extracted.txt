FY’26 Legislative Report: Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner Fellowship
Program
In calendar year 2023, the Executive Office of Health and Human Services (EOHHS), Tan
Chingfen Graduate School of Nursing, a program of the University of Massachusetts Chan
Medical School (UMass Chan GSN) and the Massachusetts League of Community Health
Centers (MLCHC) launched the $11.6 million Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner
(PMHNP) Fellowship Program for recent PMHNP Graduates and current PMHNP Students
funded through item 1599-2026 of the acts of 2021. See appendix I for authorizing statutory
language. The PMHNP Fellowship Program aims to increase the community health center
(CHC) PMHNP workforce pipeline and improve access to psychiatric care in CHCs, including by
supporting recruitment of fellows with linguistic and cultural competencies responsive to the
populations those CHCs serve. See appendix II for PMHNP Fellowship Program eligibility and
prioritization information.
This report includes information on (I) the number of psychiatric mental health nurse practitioner
applicants and participants; (II) participant retention; (III) efforts to diversify the psychiatric
mental health nurse practitioners; and (IV) all program expenditures.

III. Efforts to diversify the psychiatric mental health nurse practitioners:
UMass Chan GSN and MLCHC have worked to expand the PMHNP pipeline serving CHCs by
recruiting bilingual candidates and candidates whose linguistic, cultural, or community
backgrounds may support care in the populations served by participating CHCs. The program
maintains comprehensive demographic tracking, analyzing alignment between Fellow
candidates and CHC service communities through detailed data on language capabilities, race,
and ethnicity for both placed Fellows and the applicant pool. Early results demonstrate
successful matching between placed Fellows and the communities they serve in language
capabilities and cultural competencies.
All PMHNP Fellow applicants were asked to self-identify their non-English spoken languages,
race, and ethnicity. Applicants were also asked to respond yes, if they identify as Hispanic or
Latino.
The demographic outcomes of Graduate and Student Fellows who were matched to a CHC are
presented on pages 5 and 6 as of December 2025. A complete comparison of Graduate and
Student Applicant demographic data is provided on pages 7 and 8.

Appendix II: Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP) Fellowship Program
Eligibility
The Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP) Fellowship Program aims to recruit,
educate, and retain PMHNPs at community health centers (CHCs). All Massachusetts’ CHCs
that meet the criteria to qualify as a Federal Qualified Health Center (FQHC), a hospital-licensed
health center, or an FQHC look-alike are eligible to apply for funding. Current PMHNP Students
and recent Graduates are eligible to apply for placement. Student Fellows will complete a
semester-long placement while Graduate Fellows will be under a 12-month service
commitment.
CHC Prioritization
CHCs that serve diverse communities, rural communities, or are in a federally designated
Health Professional Shortage Area (HPSA) or a Mental Health Professional Shortage Area are
strongly encouraged to apply. At least one rural CHC will be funded during each year of the
program.
CHC Selection
CHCs will be selected and evaluated for funding based on their demonstrated capacity to
provide the following:
1) A commitment to hire Graduate PMHNP fellows who have passed the national
certification exam by the American Nurses Credentialing Center or the American
Association of Nurse Practitioners, have a Commonwealth of Massachusetts Advance
Practice Nursing License in good standing with the Board of Registration in Nursing, and
are eligible to apply for Mass Controlled Substance License/DEA by the start of the
Program.
2) A commitment to building culturally diverse psychiatric capacity both by prioritizing hiring
qualified PMHNP candidates who reflect the patient population's cultural and linguistic
backgrounds and providing services to communities that are underserved by limited
access to behavioral healthcare.
3) Capacity and capability to precept all Fellows.
4) Capacity to provide adequate prescribing Preceptor support for PMHNP Graduate
Fellows.
5) Ability to ensure adequate patient panels for Fellows in terms of size, diversity, and
clinical complexity.
6) Ability to ensure adequate care team support (e.g., medical assistants, registered
nurses, community health workers, behavioral health clinicians, psychiatrists, or
experienced PMHNPs for consult support, and support staff) for Fellows.
7) Ability to provide adequate physical space (e.g., exam/treatment rooms) for Fellows.
8) Ability to provide coverage and systems to ensure that the clinical needs of patients are
met in the Fellows’ absence (e.g., sick day, vacation time, etc.).
9) Ability to ensure that Fellows work as part of a multi-disciplinary care team.
11

Fellow Selection
All Fellow applicants must meet the minimum eligibility criteria listed below. Graduates and
Students who are bi-lingual and/or reflect the population served by a community health center
(CHC) (e.g., languages spoken, lived experience in urban or rural underserved communities)
are strongly encouraged to apply.
• Graduate Fellow Minimum Eligibility:
o Completed a PMHNP program
▪ Graduation dates within 18 months of application opening will receive
priority.
o Licensed registered nurse in Massachusetts and in good standing.
o Are either board certified as a PMHNP with an APRN license in Massachusetts
OR are eligible and will soon sit for board certification in Massachusetts.
▪ Fellow applicants who are not board certified at the time of application
must be eligible to apply for a Mass Controlled Substance License/DEA
prior to the fellowship start date.
• Student Fellow Minimum Eligibility:
o Licensed registered nurse in Massachusetts and in good standing.
o Are currently enrolled part-time or full-time in a PMHNP academic program.
o Have approval from their school’s program director or coordinator to pursue this
placement.
