An Act to expand the use of career and academic plans.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 71 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 official edition, is amended by
2 adding the following new section:-
3 Section 100. (a) For the purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the
4 following meanings:
5 “college and career pathway program”, a high school based program, designated by the
6 department of elementary and secondary education, which offers a sequenced and/or career-
7 themed set of academic and/or work-based learning experiences, including designated early
8 college programs, vocational-technical education programs, innovation pathways programs, and
1 of 3

9 STEM tech career academies which can lead to, in the department’s opinion, improved college
10 and career readiness outcomes.
11 “individual learning plan”, a plan devised by individual students with assistance from a
12 designated educator that provides a clear and detailed student academic pathway from secondary
13 to post- secondary education or career with regard to coursework, sequencing and
14 experiences beyond the classroom, beginning in the ninth grade or earlier.
15 “MyCAP”, an acronym for My Career and Academic Plan, a process for creating a
16 student’s individual learning plan as defined in this subsection, and supported by utilization of an
17 online platform,
18 (b) All public school districts shall ensure that all high school students, beginning in the
19 ninth grade, have developed, with the support of a designated educator and an online platform
20 approved by the department of elementary and secondary education, an individual learning plan
21 as defined in subsection a of this section. Said plans shall allow students to continue to adapt said
22 plans and to make reasonable and relevant changes to their plans through the course of their
23 secondary education that allow for a continuing process of alignment with the student’s needs
24 and planning goals. Districts will provide support to students to participate in the development
25 and refinement of their plans. Districts will also make every reasonable effort to offer students
26 the experiences identified in their plans to ensure alignment with, and accomplishment of, the
27 student’s goals for post-graduate success.
28 (c) Nothing contained herein will prevent districts from beginning the process of having
29 students develop individual learning plans prior to beginning high school, for students within
30 their jurisdictions.
2 of 3

31 (d) Districts shall report annually to the department of elementary and secondary
32 education on their progress in meeting the requirements contained in this section.
33 (e) The department of elementary and secondary education shall develop guidance for
34 districts to oversee and ensure their compliance with the provisions of this section, aligned with
35 their current guidance to districts utilizing the structure of the MyCAP system and its
36 component parts.
37 (f) This section will take effect on September 1, 2028.
