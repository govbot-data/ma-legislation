HOUSE . . . . . . . . No. 5200
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
________________________________________
HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, March 12, 2026.
The committee on State Administration and Regulatory Oversight, to
whom were referred the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3286) of
Brian M. Ashe for legislation to include insurance under Chapter 30B, the
Uniform Procurement Act; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
3363) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others relative to the procuring of
information technology goods or services by state agencies; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3367) of Patrick Joseph Kearney for
legislation to procure public products or services from businesses in
Massachusetts or the United States; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 3369) of Meghan K. Kilcoyne relative to; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3374) of Michael P. Kushmerek relative
to certain procurements through requests for proposals and cooperative
contracts; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3391) of Thomas
W. Moakley relative to government surplus supply; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3399) of Norman J. Orrall for legislation
to encourage in-state procurements by state agencies or governmental
bodies; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3434) of David T.
Vieira relative to the uniform procurement law; and the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3442) of Christopher J. Worrell that the
Supplier Diversity Office annually collect and report certain procurement
activity related to certified business enterprises and non-certified businesses
by businesses, nonprofit corporations, and state authorities, reports
recommending that the accompanying order (House, No. 5200) ought to be
adopted.
For the committee,
ANTONIO F. D. CABRAL.
