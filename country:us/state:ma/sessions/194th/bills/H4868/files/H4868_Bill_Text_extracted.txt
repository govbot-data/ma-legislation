An Act relative to chaperones for medical exams.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 111 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the
2 following section:-
3 Section 250. (a) As used in this section, the following words shall, unless the context
4 clearly requires otherwise, have the following meaning:
5 “Electronic health record”, an electronic record of patient health information generated
6 by 11 or more encounters in any care delivery setting.
7 “Familial chaperone”, a friend or family member of a patient who is chosen by a patient
8 to be physically present at the time of a sensitive examination when a medical chaperone is
9 unavailable.
10 “Health care facility”, an acute-care hospital licensed under section 51G, or a licensed
11 facility under the ownership or control of the acute-care hospital or its corporate parent, a long-
12 term care facility licensed under section 71, an urgent care center licensed under section 51O, a

13 participating healthcare facility as defined in section 9 of chapter 112, a hospital under section
14 51 and a physical therapy facility licensed under section 23M ½ of chapter 112.
15 “Medical chaperone”, a physician registered pursuant to section 2 of chapter 112, a nurse
16 registered pursuant to section 74 of said section 112, a licensed practical nurse licensed pursuant
17 to section 74A of said chapter 112, a physician assistant registered pursuant to section 9I of said
18 chapter 112,a physical therapist licensed pursuant to section 23B of said chapter 112, a physical
19 therapist assistant licensed pursuant to said section 23B of said chapter 112, an occupational
20 therapist licensed pursuant to said section 23B of said chapter 112, an occupational therapy
21 assistant licensed pursuant to said section 23B of said chapter 112, a nurses’ aide trained
22 pursuant to section 72W, a community health worker, as defined in section 80B of said chapter
23 112 or a certified medical assistant, as defined in section 265 of said chapter 112, who has
24 received health care facility medical chaperone training pursuant to subsection (j).
25 “Medical chaperone policy”, an institutional written policy offering a patient the option
26 to request a medical chaperone to observe and support the patient during the delivery of a
27 sensitive examination.
28 “Sensitive examination”, any physical examination, surgery or other medical procedure
29 or examination involving the patient’s genitalia, breasts, perianal region or rectum, or any other
30 examination or medical procedure that a patient finds uncomfortable undergoing without the
31 presence of a medical chaperone.
32 “Treating provider”, a medical professional delivering any physical medical procedure or
33 examination.

34 (b) Each health care facility shall have a medical chaperone policy. A medical chaperone
35 policy shall require each treating provider to disclose to a patient that they may request a medical
36 chaperone to observe and support a patient during a sensitive examination. Each medical
37 chaperone policy shall be developed in accordance with industry-recognized best practices,
38 including, but not limited to, the American Medical Society Code of Ethics and the University of
39 Michigan Health patient chaperone policy, in addition to any other appropriate medical
40 chaperone guidance as determined by the department. Each medical chaperone policy shall be
41 issued in English, Spanish, Chinese, Haitian Creole, Italian, Portuguese, Vietnamese, Laotian,
42 Khmer, Russian and any other language that is the primary language of at least 10,000 or one
43 half of 1 per cent of all residents of the commonwealth.
44 (c) A health care facility shall disclose the medical chaperone policy to a patient in
45 advance of an appointment and at the time a patient presents for their appointment. A treating
46 provider shall provide the disclosure verbally and by written or printed communication,
47 including, but not limited to, a communication digitally issued to a patient through an electronic
48 health record system.
49 (d) A treating provider shall document in a patient’s electronic health record the treating
50 provider’s disclosure of the medical chaperone policy. If the patient requests a medical
51 chaperone, the treating provider shall document in the patient’s electronic health record: (i) the
52 patient’s request for the presence of a medical chaperone; (ii) the procedure or examination that
53 the patient deemed sensitive and for which they requested the presence of a medical chaperone;
54 (iii) the name and any other applicable personal and professional information of the observing
55 medical chaperone; (iv) acknowledgement of a patient’s disclosure and understanding of
3 of 9

56 educational materials, as described in subsections (h) and (i); and (v) any other pertinent details
57 as required by the medical chaperone policy.
58 (e) Each health care facility shall submit annually or upon request by the department a
59 copy of its medical chaperone policy.
60 (f) Each health care facility shall annually report to the department on the use of medical
61 and familial chaperones for the purpose of monitoring and assessing the effectiveness of
62 chaperones and improving patient safety and comfort. Said report shall include the number of: (i)
63 medical and familial chaperones utilized for a sensitive examination, (ii) instances in which a
64 treating provider offered a medical chaperone and the patient declined; and (iii) uses of familial
65 chaperones when a medical chaperone was not available.
66 (g) If a patient requests a medical chaperone but a medical chaperone is unavailable at the
67 time of an appointment, a patient may request a familial chaperone to observe a sensitive
68 examination to prevent deferred access to care. In such cases, each treating provider shall
69 provide a familial chaperone with educational resources and materials that explain the nature of
70 the sensitive examination and the role of the chaperone prior to said sensitive examination. Each
71 treating provider shall document in the patient’s electronic health record: (i) that a patient
72 requested the presence of a medical chaperone; (ii) the reason or reasons for which a medical
73 chaperone was unavailable at the time of an appointment; (iii) the procedure or examination for
74 which the patient requested the presence of a medical chaperone; (iv) the name and any other
75 applicable personal identifying information of the familial chaperone; (v) the written consent of
76 both the patient and familial chaperone; (vi) a patient and familial chaperone’s acknowledgement
77 of the receipt of disclosure and understanding of educational materials, as described in
4 of 9

78 subsections (h) and (i); and (vii) any other any other pertinent details as determined by the
79 department.
80 (h) Each health care facility shall develop and implement an educational program for all
81 medical chaperones to ensure patient and provider comfort, privacy and safety during sensitive
82 examinations. Such programs shall be developed in accordance with industry recognized best
83 practices, including, but not limited to, the American Medical Society Code of Ethics and the
84 University of Michigan Health patient chaperone policy, in addition to any other appropriate
85 medical chaperone guidance as determined by the department. The educational program shall be
86 made available to all employees eligible to serve as a medical chaperone not less than once per
87 year or upon hiring.
88 (i) Each health care facility shall develop educational materials for all patients and
89 familial chaperones to ensure patient and provider comfort, privacy and safety during sensitive
90 examinations. Educational materials shall include, but not be limited to: (i) the components of a
91 sensitive examination; (ii) the role of a medical and familial chaperone; and (iii) how to report
92 concerns about inappropriate exams. Such materials shall be developed in accordance with
93 industry recognized best practices, including, but not limited to, the American Medical Society
94 Code of Ethics and the University of Michigan Health patient chaperone policy, in addition to
95 any other appropriate medical chaperone guidance as determined by the department. The
96 educational materials shall be provided to all patients and individuals serving as a familial
97 chaperone prior to the sensitive examination of the patient.
98 (j) Health care facilities shall require that all employed certified medical assistants, as
99 defined in section 265 of chapter 112, certified community health workers, as defined in section
5 of 9

100 80B of chapter 112 and nurses’ aides trained pursuant to section 72W receive 1-time training on
101 serving as a medical chaperone for sensitive examinations. Training shall be developed in
102 accordance with industry recognized best practices, including, but not limited to, the American
103 Medical Society Code of Ethics and the University of Michigan Health patient chaperone policy,
104 in addition to any other appropriate medical chaperone guidance as determined by the
105 department.
106 (k) A treating provider may not perform a sensitive examination on an anesthetized,
107 deeply sedated or unconscious patient or supervise the performance of a sensitive examination
108 on an anesthetized, deeply sedated or unconscious patient, unless: (i) the patient or the patient’s
109 representative has given specific informed consent in writing to the sensitive examination using
110 an authorized informed consent form; or (ii) the sensitive examination is necessary for diagnosis
111 or treatment of the patient and an emergency or urgent care situation prevented the securing of
112 consent in (i).
113 (l) The department shall develop authorized informed consent forms to be used by a
114 treating provider before performing or supervising the performance of a sensitive examination on
115 an anesthetized, deeply sedated or unconscious patient pursuant to subsection (i). The form may
116 be used in a paper or electronic format and shall be signed by the patient or the patient’s
117 representative before a sensitive examination is performed. The form shall be separate from any
118 other notice or agreement and shall clearly identify that it requests consent for a sensitive
119 examination. The form shall also clearly state the patient’s right to request and have a medical
120 chaperone present during a sensitive examination. In preparing the form, the department shall
121 consider similar forms used in other states and consult with providers, medical educators and
122 persons concerned about patient rights to autonomy.

124 inconsistent or harmful to good patient care and safety under subsection (a) of section 203.

126 administration and enforcement of this section.
