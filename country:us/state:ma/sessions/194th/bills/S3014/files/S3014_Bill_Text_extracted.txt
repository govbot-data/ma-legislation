4 SECTION 2. Chapter 23B of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting following
5 new section:-
6 Section 37. (a) For the purposes of this section, the term “pet” shall mean any
7 domesticated animal that is commonly kept in a household.
8 (b) The executive office of housing and livable communities shall establish a program of
9 pet ownership for residents of state-aided public housing for: (i) the elderly; and (ii) residents
10 who are able to reasonably care for a pet, in accordance with all applicable public health, animal
11 control animal-cruelty laws and regulations and the reasonable requirements of the state-aided
12 public housing; provided, however, that reasonable requirements may include, but shall not be
13 limited to: (i) requiring a pet deposit which shall be the lesser of $160 or one month’s rent; (ii)
1 of 8

14 limitations on the number of pets permitted in a unit, based on unit size; (iii) restrictions or
15 prohibitions based on size and type of building, project or unit; (iv) a requirement that pets be
16 spayed or neutered; (v) a requirement that a tenant maintain an adequate care plan for the pet,
17 including veterinarian care and designation of an emergency contact that can care for the pet in
18 the event the tenant is unable to do so; (vi) a requirement that the pet be registered with the state-
19 aided public housing facility; and (vii) any other requirements deemed necessary or appropriate
20 by the executive office of housing and livable communities.
21 (c) No state-aided public housing facility shall: (i) impose an outright prohibition on the
22 keeping of pets by tenants; (ii) discriminate solely based on a pet’s breed, size, weight or
23 appearance; (iii) require pet declawing as a condition of allowing a pet on the premises; or (iv)
24 evict a tenant based solely on the presence of a pet, where such tenant has otherwise adhered to
25 the facility’s requirements and policies pursuant to this section.
26 (d) Nothing in this section shall require a state-aided housing facility to allow for the
27 keeping of a dog that has been declared dangerous by a municipality pursuant to section 157 of
28 chapter 140 or has a known history of biting or injuring a person or domestic animal.
29 (e) The executive office of housing and livable communities shall promulgate regulations
30 to implement this section; provided, however, that the executive office shall consult with local
31 housing authorities and local tenant organizations in state-aided public housing in developing
32 said regulations.

55 SECTION 7. Section 174E of chapter 140 of the General Laws, as so appearing, is
56 hereby amended striking out subsections (f) and (g) and inserting in place thereof the following
57 subsections:-
58 (f) No person owning or keeping a household pet shall subject such pet to cruel
59 conditions.
60 For the purposes of this subsection, “cruel conditions” shall include, but shall not be
61 limited to, the following:
62 (i) exposure to excessive animal waste, garbage, non-potable water, excessive noxious
63 odors that create a health threat to people or animals, dangerous objects or other animals that
64 could injure or kill an animal upon contact, other circumstances that could cause harm to the
65 health or safety of the animal based on species, age or physical condition; or failure to provide
66 access to appropriate food and water based on the animal’s species, age or physical condition;
67 and
68 (ii) lack of protection when wind or environmental or weather conditions pose an adverse
69 risk to the health or safety of the animal based on the animal’s species, age or physical condition.
70 (g) A person who violates this section shall: (i) for a first offense, be issued a written
71 warning or punished by a fine of not more than $50; (ii) for a second offense, be punished by a
72 fine of not more than $200; and (iii) for a third or subsequent offense, be punished by a fine of
73 not more than $500; provided, however, that for a third or subsequent offense, the animal may be
74 subject to impoundment in a local shelter or appropriate facility at the owner’s, keeper’s or
75 guardian’s expense pending compliance with this section or the owner, keeper or guardian may
76 lose ownership of the animal.

86 SECTION 11. Said chapter 140 is hereby further amended by inserting after section
87 174H the following section:-
88 Section 174I.
89 (a) As used in this section, the following words shall have the following meanings unless
90 the context clearly requires otherwise:
91 “Alternative test method”, a process or procedure, implemented to obtain information
92 regarding the biological effects of exposure to a chemical or agent, that does not employ the use
93 of an animal and results in the gathering of information that would otherwise be acquired
94 through the use of a traditional animal test method, including, but not limited to, computational
95 toxicology, bioinformatics, high-throughput screening methods, chemical substances testing,
96 tiered testing methods, in vitro studies and systems biology.
97 “Animal”, a vertebrate nonhuman animal.
5 of 8

98 “Commissioner”, the commissioner of the department of public health.
99 “Contract testing facility”, a partnership, corporation, association or other legal entity that
100 tests chemicals, ingredients, product formulations or products.
101 “Manufacturer”, a partnership, corporation, association, or other legal entity that
102 produces products, product formulations, chemicals or ingredients.
103 “Medical research”, investigations, experiments or studies to discover, develop or verify
104 knowledge relating to the causes, diagnosis, treatment, prevention or control of physical or
105 mental diseases and impairments of humans or animals or relating to the development of
106 biomedical products, devices or pharmaceuticals.
107 “Traditional animal test method”, a process or procedure using animals to obtain
108 information regarding the biological effects of exposure to a chemical or agent.
109 (b) No manufacturer or contract testing facility shall use a traditional animal test method
110 for which a valid alternative test method exists when testing a cosmetic or household product,
111 product formulation, chemical or ingredient in the commonwealth.
112 (c) The commissioner shall promulgate regulations to implement, enforce and administer
113 this section and shall establish standards for alternative test methods to ensure safety and
114 comparable validity of results to traditional animal test methods. The commissioner shall
115 consider the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development’s guidelines for the
116 testing of chemicals when establishing said standards.
117 (d) This section shall not apply to a test method conducted for the purposes of medical
118 research.

135 SECTION 13. The executive office of housing and livable communities shall conduct a
136 study and develop recommendations regarding the prevalence and effects of pet-related fees
137 charged to residents of state-aided and privately-owned rental housing in the commonwealth.
138 The study shall include, but not be limited to: (i) the prevalence and range of such pet-related fee
139 practices among state-aided and privately-owned rental housing providers in the commonwealth;
140 (ii) the effects of such pet-related fees on housing stability and access for pet-owning
7 of 8

141 households, including households in state-aided public housing; (iii) the relationship between
142 such pet-related fees and rates of pet relinquishment to animal shelters in the commonwealth;
143 (iv) existing fee or security deposit limitations or prohibitions on pet ownership for tenants in
144 other states and their effects on housing access, animal shelter intake and landlord practices; and
145 (v) any other information deemed relevant by the executive office. For the purposes of this study,
146 the term “pet” shall mean any domesticated animal that is commonly kept in a household.
147 Not later than December 31, 2027, the executive office shall publish the results of the
148 study on its website and submit a copy to the clerks of the senate and house of representatives
149 and to the joint committee on housing.
