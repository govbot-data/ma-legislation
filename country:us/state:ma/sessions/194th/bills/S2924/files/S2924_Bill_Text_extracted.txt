27 SECTION 3. Chapter 69 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
28 section 1E the following 2 sections:-
29 Section 1E ½. (a) As used in this section and section 1E ¾, the following words shall
30 have the following meanings unless the context clearly requires otherwise:
31 “Evidence-based literacy instruction”, literacy instruction that: (i) is based on methods
32 backed by scientific research; and (ii) has a demonstrated record of success in increasing student
33 competency in reading ability, vocabulary, oral language and comprehension through well-
34 designed and well-implemented studies to produce measurable and positive effects on student
35 learning outcomes.
36 “Five research-based areas in reading instruction”, phonemic awareness, phonics,
37 fluency, vocabulary and comprehension; provided, however, that comprehension shall include
38 oral reading, oral communication skills and reading comprehension.
2 of 9

39 “High-quality curriculum”, a curriculum reviewed and approved by the department.
40 (b) A district shall select a high-quality curriculum to meet the curriculum frameworks
41 under section 1E for kindergarten through grade 3 literacy that: (i) includes instruction in the five
42 research-based areas in reading instruction and oral language development; and (ii) utilizes and
43 is based upon evidence-based literacy instruction.
44 (c)(1) A district may apply for and the department may grant a waiver for the district to
45 use a kindergarten through grade 3 literacy instruction curriculum that does not appear on the
46 department’s list of high-quality curricula and that has not already been reviewed by the
47 department; provided, however, that any such literacy instruction curriculum shall utilize and be
48 based on evidence-based literacy instruction and meet quality standards determined by the
49 department.
50 (2) A waiver granted pursuant to this subsection may be approved for a period of not
51 more than 5 years; provided, however, that the department may approve the renewal of such
52 waiver if the district continues to utilize evidence-based literacy practices and meet quality
53 standards as determined by the department. A district shall report to the department during the
54 waiver period any student data as requested by the department.
55 (3) A district that receives a waiver under this subsection shall publicly post to its website
56 any report submitted to the department with information required pursuant to paragraph (2) and
57 curriculum samples.
58 (4) The department may audit waivers approved pursuant to this subsection at any time.
59 The audit shall include, but not be limited to, a comprehensive review of the: (i) curriculum
60 materials; (ii) instructional documentation; and (iii) instructional practices in the classroom.

61 (5) The department may rescind approval of a waiver approved pursuant to this
62 subsection based on audit findings before the expiration of the waiver; provided, however, that if
63 a waiver is rescinded, the department shall assist the district with implementing a high-quality
64 curriculum.
65 (d) The department shall maintain and make available to school districts a free, complete
66 high-quality curriculum for kindergarten through grade 3 that meets frameworks under section
67 1E; provided, however, that such free, complete high-quality curriculum or a free curriculum

72 teachers, paraprofessionals and reading specialists in kindergarten through grade 3.
73 (b) The department shall create and maintain: (i) a list of department-approved
74 professional development programs and vendors aligned with evidence-based literacy instruction
75 best practices; (ii) a set of online training modules available at no cost to all teachers that provide
76 training on the foundational practices and pedagogy aligned with evidence-based literacy
77 instruction best practices; and (iii) a list of high-quality curricula. The department shall update
78 said lists and modules to account for developments in best practices and curriculum.

124 SECTION 11. Said chapter 71 is hereby further amended by inserting after section 57A
125 the following 2 sections:-
126 Section 57B. (a) For the purposes of this section and section 57C, the term “screening
127 assessments” shall mean tools and assessments that can be used to identify students at risk for
128 poor academic outcomes as determined by the department.
129 (b) The department of elementary and secondary education, in consultation with the
130 department of early education and care, shall issue regulations requiring districts to adopt
131 screening procedures and protocols for reading ability and progress in literacy skills for all
132 students in kindergarten through grade 3 using screening assessments. The regulations shall
133 include a threshold at which a student is determined to be significantly below the grade level
134 benchmark. The commissioner shall annually make publicly available on the department’s
135 website data on which screening assessments are employed by each district to conduct reading
136 screening.
137 Section 57C. (a) Each district shall assess each student’s reading ability and progress in
138 literacy skills from kindergarten through at least grade 3, using a screening assessment approved
139 by the department of elementary and secondary education, at such intervals as determined by the
140 commissioner but in no event less than twice per school year. The department shall update
141 approved screening assessments to remain current with best practices and evidence-based
142 assessments. The screening assessment of the student’s reading ability and progress shall be
143 consistent with section 2 of chapter 71B, the department’s dyslexia guidelines issued pursuant to
144 section 57A and literacy guidelines issued pursuant to section 57B.
7 of 9

145 (b) If a screening assessment determines that a student is significantly below relevant
146 grade level benchmarks in specific literacy skills, the district shall determine which actions
147 within the general education program will meet the student’s needs, including, but not limited to,
148 differentiated or supplementary evidence-based reading instruction and ongoing monitoring of
149 progress. Not later than 30 days after a screening result that determines that a student is
150 significantly below the relevant grade level benchmark, the district shall inform the student’s
151 parent or guardian of the screening results and the school’s response on what actions within the
152 general education program will meet the student’s needs and shall offer the student’s parent or
153 guardian the opportunity for a follow-up discussion.
