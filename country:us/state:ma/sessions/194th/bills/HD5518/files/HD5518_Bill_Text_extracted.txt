This report is responsive to two legislative mandates: an annual report on the state budget expenditure and a
quarterly report on the American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA) expenditures, as noted above. Both items are currently
combined into one report to provide a holistic illustration of how funds are being distributed. This is particularly
important for funds distributed to municipalities, as municipalities receive funding from both sources to provide
public health services. Additionally, providing an annual, rather than quarterly legislative report, both shares the
bigger picture as well is more streamlined from both programmatic and fiscal perspectives. Often, there can be
changes to contracts or amount per funding source throughout a fiscal year. By providing an annual report, there
can be one clear, final reporting of funding distributed.
BACKGROUND
Special Commission on Local and Regional Public Health
The Special Commission on Local and Regional Public Health (referred to hereafter as “the Commission”) was
established in August 2016 (Chapter 3 of the Resolves of 2016) to “assess the effectiveness and efficiency of
municipal and regional public health systems and to make recommendations regarding how to strengthen the
delivery of public health services and preventive measures.”
The Commission released its final report, “Blueprint for Public Health Excellence: Recommendations for Improved
Effectiveness and Efficiency of Local Public Health Protections,” (referred to hereafter as “the Blueprint report”) in
3

June 2019. The report documents the Commission’s findings and makes recommendations for strengthening local
public health services across the Commonwealth.
The initiative and legislation described below align with the Commission’s recommendations.
Public Health Excellence (PHE) Grant Program for Shared Services
In FY21, DPH established the Public Health Excellence for Shared Services Grant Program to promote and expand
cross-jurisdictional sharing arrangements statewide to support the Commission’s recommendations in the Blueprint
report. These are competitive grants that went through June 30, 2024, with the option to renew through 2038, and
subject to availability of funding. Grantee funding amounts differ based on the amount of funds applied for by the
municipality. Grantees were awarded initial grant funding and then were awarded additional funding after the
completion of a capacity assessment.
Multiple procurement rounds have been conducted to award PHE grants funds. At the end of FY24, the PHE grant
program had a total of 51 grantees representing 320 municipalities (91% of municipalities across the
Commonwealth). Total funding for FY24 for all grantees statewide was $29.5M.
American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA)
The Massachusetts Legislature has allocated funds ($200M) made available from the federal government through
ARPA to “support and enhance the commonwealth’s local and regional public health system” (Chapter 102 of the
Acts of 2021, Item 1599-2025). This funding prioritizes the expansion and enhancement of shared public health
services among municipalities. The language details three earmarked areas of focus: data systems and
performance tracking, workforce development, and capacity building of local and regional public health. In 2024, a
supplemental budget provided DPH with spending flexibility between these three earmarks to allow for more
efficient, effective use of funds (Chapter 206 of the Acts of 2024, Section 20). Nevertheless, for the purposes of this
report, DPH has outlined overarching goals for each of the earmarked areas, listed below (Table 1).

State Action for Public Health Excellence (SAPHE 2.0) Act
In November 2024, the Massachusetts Legislature passed “An Act Relative to Strengthening Massachusetts’
Economic Leadership.” Passage of Sections 224 and 307-309 of this legislation, also known as the State Action for
Public Health Excellence (SAPHE 2.0) Act, will strengthen local public health service delivery. SAPHE 2.0 promotes
service sharing, establishes a statewide data collection system, and requires local boards of health to meet certain
performance standards— all to support a more equitable public health system throughout the state. This legislation
not only aligns with the Commission’s recommendations but also reconvenes the Special Commission on Local and
Regional Public Health.

FUTURE EXPENDITURES
In the coming years, funds distributed to local public health will be direct investments in the implementation of the
recently passed State Action for Public Health Excellence (SAPHE 2.0) Act. State funds under 4512-2022 will
continue to be expended in full to support local public health and implementation of SAPHE 2.0. In addition, the
administration is also committing $42M to a local public health data solution (Metrik).
