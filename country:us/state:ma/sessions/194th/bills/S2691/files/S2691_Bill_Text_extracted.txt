An Act updating the charter net school spending cap.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Subsection (i) of section 89 of chapter 71 of the general laws, as appearing
2 in the 2022 official edition, is hereby amended by striking out the second, third and fourth
3 paragraphs and inserting in place the following paragraph:-
4 (2) In any fiscal year, no public school district's total charter school tuition payment to
5 commonwealth charter schools shall exceed 9 per cent of the district's net school spending;
6 provided, however, that a public school district whose total charter school tuition payment as of
7 June 30, 2026, exceeds said 9 per cent shall remain at the level necessary to support current and
8 previously authorized charter school enrollment, and provided further that no new charter school
9 seats shall be authorized by the board for said district so long as its total charter school tuition
10 payment exceeds said 9 per cent. The commonwealth shall incur charter school tuition payments
11 for siblings attending commonwealth charter schools to the extent that their attendance would
12 otherwise cause the school district's charter school tuition payments to exceed 9 per cent of the
13 school district's net school spending or the higher caps referenced herein.

14 SECTION 2. Subsection (j) of said section 89 of said chapter 71, as so appearing, is
15 hereby amended by inserting after the third sentence the following sentence:- The criteria shall
16 include, but not be limited to: (i) the projected impact on enrollment to the sending district or
17 districts; (ii) the projected financial impact on the sending district or districts; and (iii) the
18 projected impact on the overall short- and long-term viability of the sending district or districts.
19 SECTION 3. Subsection (n) of said section 89 of said chapter 71, as so appearing, is
20 hereby amended by inserting after the first paragraph, the following paragraph:-
21 A charter school may offer enrollment preferences for the following students: (i) students
22 with an individualized education plan; (ii) English learners; (iii) students in foster care; (iv)
23 students who are determined to be at risk of dropping out of school based on predictors
24 determined by the department; (v) students who have dropped out of school; or (vi) students
25 experiencing homelessness. Prior to offering an enrollment preference, a charter school shall (i)
26 seek approval from the department; and (ii) notify the sending district or districts upon seeking
27 approval from the department. Enrollment preferences shall apply both to students who are
28 admitted through the lottery and students admitted from the waitlist pursuant to this section.
29 SECTION 4. Subsection (dd) of said section 89 of said chapter 71, as so appearing, is
30 hereby amended by adding the following paragraph:-
31 The decision by the board to renew a charter shall be based upon the presentation of
32 affirmative evidence regarding the faithfulness of the school to the terms of its charter. Any
33 material produced by the department in relation to a charter school renewal decision shall be
34 made available to the public at least 60 days prior to a vote by the board to renew a school’s
35 charter. Furthermore, at least 60 days prior to a renewal vote, the board shall solicit public
2 of 3

36 comment on the renewal for a period of at least 30 days. The board shall review all material
37 submitted in support of or in opposition to the charter renewal prior to a renewal vote. If the
38 board delegates full or partial authority to the commissioner to make charter school renewal
39 decisions, the same public notice and public comment timelines shall apply.
40 SECTION 5. Said section 89 of said chapter 71, as so appearing, is hereby further
41 amended by inserting after subsection (dd) the following subsection:-
42 (dd1/2) In the case of a charter school seeking an expansion of seats from the board, there
43 shall be a public hearing on the expansion if a member of the school committee in any
44 potentially impacted district makes the request to the department within 30 days of public
45 notification of the expansion request. The board shall hold the hearing in the school district,
46 providing for the option to participate virtually, in which the proposed expansion would take
47 place, in accordance with the public hearing requirements outlined in subsection (h).
48 The board shall establish criteria for the approval of a charter school expansion request,
49 and recommendations to the board shall be based upon and reference those criteria. The criteria
50 shall include, but not be limited to: (i) the projected impact on enrollment to the sending district
51 or districts; (ii) the projected financial impact on the sending district or districts; (iii) the
52 projected impact on the overall short- and long-term viability of the sending district or districts;
53 and (iv) the degree to which the charter school seeking expansion serves and demonstrates an
54 intent to serve student populations representative of the sending district or districts.
