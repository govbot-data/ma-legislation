48 SECTION 7. Section 4 of said chapter 3 of said charter is hereby amended by striking out
49 subsections 3–4–1 to 3–4–3, inclusive, and inserting in place thereof the following 3
50 subsections:-
51 3-4-1 An elected officer of the Town may be recalled and removed from public office by
52 the voters of the Town as herein provided. Any voter of the Town may file with the Select Board
53 a petition containing the name and title of the elective officer whose removal is sought together
54 with a statement of the grounds for their removal. Said petition shall be filed with said Select
3 of 15

55 Board and shall be signed in ink or indelible pencil by qualified voters of the Town equal in
56 number to at least 12 per cent of the voters registered at the last regular municipal election;
57 provided, that no recall petition may be filed against any officer until the officer shall have held
58 the officer’s office before the taking of such vote. Said recall vote shall be called and conducted
59 in the same manner as is provided by the General Laws for the call and conduct of a special
60 election.
61 3-4-2 If, within 5 business days after receipt of the petition the Select Board shall
62 determine the petition and affidavits to be sufficient, a recall vote shall be taken within 110 days
63 but not sooner than 90 days after such final determination; provided, that no such vote shall be
64 taken if the term of office of such elective official shall expire within 150 days after such
65 determination, or if such elective officer shall resign from such office before the taking of such
66 vote. Said recall vote shall be called and conducted in the same manner as is provided by the
67 General Laws for the call and conduct of a special election.
68 3-4-3 The form of the question to be voted upon shall be substantially as follows: "Shall
69 (here insert the name and title of the elective officer whose recall is sought) be recalled?" A
70 majority vote of the voters to recall such elective officer shall not be effective unless a total of a
71 majority of 12 per cent of the electorate entitled to vote on the question shall be voted. Recall of
72 such elective officer shall become effective upon certification of the results of the voting thereon,
73 regardless of any technical deficiency in the recall petition. If any elective officer shall be
74 recalled, the vacancy created thereby shall be filled in accordance with the provisions of this
75 Charter and the General Laws.
