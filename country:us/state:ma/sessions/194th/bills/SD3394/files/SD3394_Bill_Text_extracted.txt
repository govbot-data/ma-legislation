1 SECTION 1. Section one of Chapter 258F of the General Laws are hereby amended by
2 adding the following definitions:
3 ''Certifying entity’ ‘All law enforcement, to include prosecutor or state or local entity
4 courts or administrative bodies that have the authority to detect, investigate, prosecute, or
5 adjudicate any of the crimes under section 2 of this chapter or severe forms of trafficking in
6 persons under 22 U.S.C. 7102(11).
7 “Exceptional circumstances” is defined to include: (i) when any victim of criminal
8 activity who intends to petition for a nonimmigrant visa under 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(U) or a
9 victim of severe forms of trafficking in persons is in immigration detention, is in removal
10 proceedings, or has been ordered removed; (ii) when any qualifying derivative family member is
11 in immigration detention, in removal proceedings, or has been ordered removed; and (iii) when

12 any qualifying derivative family member is at risk of aging out of the immigration benefit under
13 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(U)(ii) or 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(T)(ii).
14 A “qualifying derivative family member” is defined to include a qualifying family
15 member of a victim of criminal activity who intends to petition for a nonimmigrant visa under 8
16 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(U) or a victim of severe forms of trafficking in persons who intends to
17 petition for a nonimmigrant visa under 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(T), who is eligible to apply for U
18 nonimmigrant status under 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(U)(ii) or T nonimmigrant status under 8 U.S.C.
19 1101(a)(15)(T)(ii).
20 SECTION 2. Said chapter 258F is further amended by striking out section 2 and
21 inserting in place thereof the following section:
22 Section 2. Certifying entities shall complete and sign nonimmigrant status certification
23 forms for: (i) victims of any of the enumerated crimes under 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(U); and (ii)
24 victims of severe forms of trafficking in persons under 22 USC 7102(11), who intend to petition
25 for nonimmigrant visas under 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(T).
26 SECTION 3. Said chapter 258F is further amended by striking out section 3 and inserting
27 in place thereof the following section:
28 Section 3. A certifying entity shall respond to a nonimmigrant status certification request
29 from a victim who intends to petition for a nonimmigrant visa under 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(U) or
30 a victim of severe forms of trafficking in persons who intends to petition for a nonimmigrant visa
31 under 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(T) not later than 30 days after receiving the request for certification,
32 unless Section 4 applies. The certifying entity shall respond to the request by: (i) completing and
33 signing the certification forms; (ii) issuing a written denial of the request, without prejudice,
2 of 6

34 informing the victim of the reason that the request does not meet the requirements of the
35 certifying entity's policy under section 2; or (iii) in extenuating circumstances outside the control
36 of the certifying entity that prevent the certifying entity from responding to the certification
37 request in the required time period, issuing a written explanation of the delay, the process the
38 certifying entity will undertake to respond and a projected time frame for such response.
39 SECTION 4. Said chapter 258F is further amended by striking out section 4 and inserting
40 in place thereof the following section:
41 Section 4. If the requestor notifies the certifying entity in writing of exceptional
42 circumstances, a certifying entity shall respond to a nonimmigrant status certification request
43 from a victim of criminal activity who intends to petition for a nonimmigrant visa under 8 U.S.C.
44 1101(a)(15)(U) or from a victim of severe forms of trafficking in persons who intends to petition
45 for a nonimmigrant visa under 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15)(T) not later than 10 days or two (2) days
46 before the age-out of a qualifying derivative family member. The certifying entity shall respond
47 to the request by: (i) completing and signing the certification forms; (ii) issuing a written denial
48 of the request, without prejudice, informing the victim of the reason that the request does not
49 meet the requirements of the certifying entity's policy under section 2; or (iii) in extenuating
50 circumstances outside the control of the certifying entity that prevent the certifying entity from
51 responding to the certification request in the required time period, issuing a written explanation
52 of the delay, the process the certifying entity will undertake to respond and a projected time
53 frame for such response.

55 Section 5. (a) If an agency or municipality fails to comply with a requirement of sections
56 3 and 4, issues a response the requestor believes is in violation of sections 3 and 4, or fails to
57 issue a policy for completing and signing nonimmigrant status certification as required by
58 section 2 of chapter 258F of Massachusetts General Laws, the person who submitted the initial
59 request for certification may obtain judicial review through an action in superior court seeking
60 relief in the nature of certiorari under section 4 of chapter 249 and as prescribed in subsection
61 (c).
62 (b) A requestor may initiate a civil action to enforce the requirements of this chapter, and
63 section 2 of chapter 258F of Massachusetts General Laws. Any action under this subsection shall
64 be filed in Suffolk superior court with respect to agencies and, with respect to municipalities, in
65 the superior court in the county in which the municipality is located. The superior court shall
66 have available all remedies at law or in equity; provided, however, that any damages awarded
67 shall be consistent with subsection (c).
68 (c) In any action filed by a requestor pursuant to this section:
69 (1) the superior court shall have jurisdiction to enjoin agency or municipal action;
70 (2) the superior court shall determine the propriety of any agency or municipal action de
71 novo and may inspect the contents of any defendant agency or municipality record in camera,
72 provided, however, that the in camera review shall not waive any legally applicable privileges,
73 including without limitation, the attorney-client privilege and the attorney work product
74 privilege;
75 (3) the superior court shall, when feasible, expedite the proceeding;

76 (4) The superior court may award reasonable attorney fees and costs in any case in which
77 the requester obtains relief through a judicial order, consent decree, or the provision of requested
78 documents after the filing of a complaint. There shall be a presumption in favor of an award of
79 fees and costs unless the agency or municipality establishes that:
80 (i) the agency or municipality did not violate this chapter;
81 (ii) the agency or municipality reasonably relied upon a published opinion of an appellate
82 court of the commonwealth based on substantially similar facts; or
83 (iii) the agency or municipality reasonably relied upon a published opinion by the
84 attorney general based on substantially similar facts.
85 If the superior court determines that an award of reasonable attorney fees or costs is not
86 warranted, the judge shall issue written findings specifying the reasons for the denial.
87 (5) If a requestor has obtained judgment in superior court in a case under this section and
88 has demonstrated that the defendant agency or municipality, in failing to comply with a
89 requirement of sections 3 and 4, issuing a response the requestor believes is in violation of
90 sections 3 and 4, or failing to issue a policy for completing and signing nonimmigrant status
91 certification as required by section 2 of chapter 258F of Massachusetts General Laws, did not act
92 in good faith, the superior court may assess punitive damages against the defendant agency or
93 municipality in an amount not less than $1,000 nor more than $5,000, awarded to the person who

102 Section 6. (a) Annually, not later than February 1, each certifying entity shall report to the

112 the number of individuals that requested nonimmigrant status certification, delineated by

116 denials. The report shall not include information that would allow the public to ascertain the
