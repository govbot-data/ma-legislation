An Act to increase access to healthcare for ostomy patients.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 32A of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
2 section 17W the following section:-
3 Section 17X. (a) The commission shall provide to any person who is insured under the
4 group insurance commission coverage of all medical supplies for management of surgically
5 created or spontaneous fistulas and coverage of supplies related to ostomy care. The commission
6 shall not require the use of non-medical supplies for a patient to receive coverage under this
7 section. The commission shall make publicly available information about its coverage of
8 supplies related to ostomy care.
9 (b) If a person obtains new health insurance coverage, information about the person’s
10 ostomy care, patient history and prescriptions shall be transferred to the new insurer within 72
11 hours for continuity of care. Ostomy supply orders and shipments shall not be delayed during the
12 transition period.
1 of 9

13 (c) Healthcare payers shall reimburse suppliers of supplies related to ostomy care at a rate
14 not less than the Medicare reimbursement rate.
15 SECTION 2. Chapter 111 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
16 section 51M the following section:-
17 Section 51N. Any acute-care hospital licensed pursuant to this chapter that performs
18 ostomy surgery shall employ and have access to certified healthcare professionals who specialize
19 in ostomy care. The acute-care hospital shall ensure ongoing follow-up care offers ostomy care
20 in an outpatient setting, as appropriate, with certified healthcare professionals who specialize in
21 ostomy care.
22 SECTION 3. Chapter 112 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
23 section 12D the following section:-
24 Section 12D½. (a) A prescription issued in the commonwealth by a physician licensed
25 pursuant to this chapter for supplies related to an ostomy shall be valid for at least 1 year without
26 disruption in fulfillment and coverage for the duration of the prescription.
27 (b) Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, a physician may prescribe
28 supplies related to ostomy care in a quantity exceeding any limit imposed by law, regulation,
29 rule, guidance or insurance policy if the physician determines that it is necessary and expedient
30 for patient care.
31 (c) Fulfillment of prescriptions related to ostomy supplies shall not be delayed by any
32 approval or appeal process.
2 of 9

33 (d) Suppliers of ostomy supplies shall be required to provide 1 month's advanced
34 notification of prescription expiration to both patients and their prescribers.
35 (e) Fulfillment of prescriptions related to ostomy supplies shall be dispensed as written.
36 Suppliers of ostomy supplies shall be required to provide 1 month's advanced notification of
37 their intention to substitute ostomy supply brands or products, also known as non-medical
38 switching. Notification by mail shall include samples of the proposed product substitution for the
39 patient to try. If a substitute product fails to meet or exceed the quality of the original and
40 thereby compromises the patient's ostomy care, the patient shall have the option of returning to
41 their original product or shall receive a product that matches the quality of the original.
