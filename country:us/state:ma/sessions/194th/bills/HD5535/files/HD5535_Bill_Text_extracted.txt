Massachusetts Center for Employee Ownership 2025 Annual Report
Section 17 of Chapter 23D of the General Laws (“Authorizing Statute”) established the
Massachusetts Center for Employee Ownership (“Center”) within the Massachusetts Office of
Business Development (“MOBD”).
Pursuant to Section 17(b)(4) of the Authorizing Statute, the Center’s mission is to
"provide education, conduct outreach and promote efforts to create an overall environment in
the commonwealth to: (i) expand and enhance employee ownership; (ii) increase the number of
employee-owned companies; (iii) publicize and promote the benefits of employee involvement
and ownership to policy makers and the general public; (iv) encourage collaborative outreach
efforts regarding involvement and ownership in the workplace; (v) research and evaluate
employee involvement and employee ownership in the commonwealth; (vi) showcase employee
ownership initiatives in the commonwealth; (vii) facilitate and coordinate the sharing of existing
information and resources; and (viii) provide grants…”
The Center is required by the Authorizing Statute to annually submit a report to the
Legislature that describes the activities taken by the Center to support and promote the
transition of traditionally structured companies to an employee ownership model and an
inventory of employee-owned businesses in the state.
Activities Taken to Support and Promote Employee Ownership
Advisory Board
• Section 204 of Chapter 6 of the General Laws establishes an Advisory Board on
Employee Ownership (“Advisory Board”) consisting of 19 members, both appointed
and ex-officio, for the purpose of advising the Governor and the Director of the
Massachusetts Center for Employee Ownership on issues and policy matters
pertaining to employee involvement and ownership in the Commonwealth.
• The Advisory Board was organized in March 2024 and has been actively engaged
with the Center in 2025, with formal meetings held on January 30, February 27,
March 27, April 24, June 26, July 31, September 25, and November 20.

• Additionally, the Advisory Board has established three standing sub-committees:
Advisory Board Governance, Policy & Advocacy, and Operationalizing the Center.
Center for Employee Ownership
• MOBD Central MA Regional Director Kevin Kuros continues to serve as Interim
Director of the Center, having been appointed in April 2024.
• As the profile and visibility of the Center continue to grow, Interim Director Kuros was
invited in May to moderate a panel discussion at Harvard Business School’s
Conference on Worker Ownership, with attendees from around the world. Kuros also
attended the Equal Exchange Co-op Summit held at Wheaton College in June,
connecting with EO ecosystem participants from around the Country.
• The Center maintained its membership in The ESOP Association (“TEA”) and
participated in TEA’s Spring and Fall Conferences, not only as an attendee, but also
as the only New England state center with an exhibitor booth and Center-based
collateral materials. The Center also maintained memberships with the National
Center for Employee Ownership, the National Federation of Worker Cooperatives,
and the Employee Ownership Expansion Network (EOX), the co-sponsor of the
Center’s “Employee Ownership 101” webinar in November.
Marketing of Employee Ownership
• From January through June, the Center engaged an integrated marketing and event
management consultancy to assist with logo and branding development, production
of collateral materials and “Employee Ownership Success Story” 1-pagers,
production of a trade-show booth and promotional items, and event management for
the Center’s symposiums noted below.
• In October, the Governor formally announced the Center by launching a new website
(www.mass.gov/massceo) to promote the Center as a statewide hub to help
businesses explore and adopt employee ownership models.
• The Governor declared October “Employee Ownership Month” in the
Commonwealth. The Center launched a new initiative, “Massachusetts Employee
Owned” where owners can register their employee-owned business with MassCEO.
Regional Roadshow
• To raise awareness of both the Center and employee ownership in general, from
February through July, the Center held monthly regional symposiums on employee
ownership, one in each MOBD region across the Commonwealth.
• For each symposium, the Center partnered with the US Small Business
Administration (SBA)’s Boston District Office and regional economic development
agencies (Chambers, Regional Economic Development Organizations [REDOs],
etc.) to co-host the event.
• The symposiums consisted of an overview of employee ownership and the Center, a
discussion around funding employee ownership presented by the SBA district
director, and business profiles of successful regional ESOP and Co-op businesses,

culminating in a lively round-table discussion where the audience was able to
engage with the subject matter experts.
• The symposiums were each attended by 25-70 people, depending on location, and
received overwhelmingly positive feedback surveys.
• Roadshow Events included:
o Greater Boston – February 28
o Western MA – March 26
o Central MA – April 29
o South Coast – May 28
o North Shore – June 24
o MetroWest – July 30

Definitions and Sources:
ESOP: An ESOP is a federally regulated employee benefit plan that gives ownership interest to
workers by allocating shares from the ESOP trust.
Cooperative or Co-op: A worker cooperative is a member-owned business entity in which
worker-owners have a controlling interest, and who elect the governing body on a one-member-
one-vote basis.
EO Trust: An Employee Ownership Trust (EO Trust) is a newly emerging form of employee
ownership where a trust owns the business but distributes profits to the workers without
allocating the shares to the workers.
