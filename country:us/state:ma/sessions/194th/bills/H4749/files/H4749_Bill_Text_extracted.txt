An Act establishing a commission to study the financial abuse of elders.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 (a) The Massachusetts General Court shall initiate a commission to study and
2 draft policies to combat the financial abuse of elders in the Commonwealth.
3 (1) Said Commission shall have three members of the Massachusetts House of
4 Representatives, two appointed by the Speaker of the House and one appointed by the Minority
5 Leader;
6 (2) Said Commission shall have three members of the Massachusetts Senate,
7 two appointed by the President of the Senate and one appointed by the Minority Leader;
8 (3) Said commission shall have two attorneys, admitted to the Massachusetts
9 Bar, who specialize in elder and estate law;
10 (4) Said Commission shall have a representative from an advocacy group,
11 operating in Massachusetts, which has a history of advocating for the interests of the elder
12 population in the Commonwealth;
1 of 3

13 (5) Said Commission shall have two representatives who are employed by
14 Councils on Aging in the Commonwealth or any organization responsible for administering
15 protective services to elders in the Commonwealth; and
16 (6) Said Commission shall have a representative from an association or
17 advocacy group, operating in Massachusetts, which has a history of advocating on behalf of the
18 real estate industry.
19 (7) Said Commission shall have a representative from an association or
20 advocacy group, operating in Massachusetts, which has a background in the financial services
21 industry
22 (8) Said Commission shall have a representative from the Massachusetts Bankers
23 Association, Inc.
24 (9) Said Commission shall have a representative from the Cooperative Credit Union
25 Association, Inc.
26 (b) The commission will specifically study
27 (1) The economic impact and mechanisms of financial abuse on those over
28 age 60 in the Commonwealth;
29 (2) The lending and refinancing practices of companies who target customers
30 above the age of 60;
31 (3) Exploitative transfers of real estate or other valuable property from
32 dependent elders to fiduciaries for significantly less than market value;
2 of 3

33 (4) Fraudulent or deceptive schemes that target elderly persons in the
34 Commonwealth through telemarketing, the internet, or other means; and
35 (5) Any other form of elder financial abuse.
36 (c) Said commission shall file a report that includes policy recommendations
37 prevent or better protect elders from financial abuse.The report shall be filed with the clerks of
38 the house of representatives and the senate. chamber, as well as the Chairmen of the the joint
39 committee aging and independence and the joint committee on financial services, no later than
40 December 31st, 2026.
