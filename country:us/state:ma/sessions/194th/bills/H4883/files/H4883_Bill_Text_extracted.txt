HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, December 29, 2025.
The committee on Children, Families and Persons with Disabilities, to
whom were referred the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 198) of
James C. Arena-DeRosa for legislation to authorize video cameras to be
installed at entrances, exits, and common areas of certain private facilities
under the authority of the Department of Developmental Services; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 200) of Brian M. Ashe relative
to the rights of deaf children; the petition (accompanied by bill, House,
No. 203) of Christine P. Barber and Sally P. Kerans for legislation to
establish a protection and advocacy system for investigating abuse,
neglect, and financial exploitation of persons with disabilities occurring
and advocating for the civil and human rights of such persons; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 204) of Donald R. Berthiaume, Jr.,
relative to the Department of Children and Families providing official
documentation of child counsel appointments; the petition (accompanied
by bill, House, No. 205) of Donald R. Berthiaume, Jr., relative to
background checks for kinship foster care; the petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 206) of Donald R. Berthiaume, Jr., relative to providing
that certain foster care children have the right to be emancipated from their
biological parents; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 211) of
Mark J. Cusack for legislation to support the transition to adult services
for persons with disabilities; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
212) of Michael S. Day for legislation to promote employment for people
with disabilities; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 213) of
Marjorie C. Decker relative to ensuring access to behavioral health
services for children involved with state agencies; the petition
(accompanied by resolve, House, No. 218) of Carol A. Doherty and James
K. Hawkins for legislation to establish the harmony commission to study
the welfare of children in care and protection cases; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 228) of Tricia Farley-Bouvier and
Joanne M. Comerford for legislation to further regulate the Office of the
Child Advocate; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 229) of
