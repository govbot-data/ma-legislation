An Act establishing school zones for locomotives.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 160 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after section 19 the
2 following section:-
3 Section 19A. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, a city or town
4 may, by vote of the city council or other legislative body of a city, or town meeting or other
5 legislative body of a town, establish designated school zones as provided in section 2 of chapter
6 85 for passenger and freight locomotive engines operating on railroads within the city or town,
7 and may set speed limits for locomotives operating in said zones at a rate of speed not to exceed
8 20 miles per hour; provided, however, that no such designation or speed limit shall be effective
9 until the city or town; (i) notifies any railroad corporation with a railroad running through the
10 designated zone of such designation and speed limit, and (ii) erects signs indicating such
1 of 2

11 designation and speed limit, in a manner consistent with the department of highways’ current
12 manual on uniform traffic control devices and said department’s sample regulation for a standard
13 municipal traffic code, at such points as said department and the effected railroad corporations,
14 acting jointly, may designate. No railroad corporation, including the Massachusetts Bay
15 Transportation Authority, shall permit a passenger or freight locomotive to operate on a railroad
16 at a rate of speed greater than the rate posted while traveling through a designated school zone in
17 a city or town. Cities and towns shall be responsible for the cost of erecting and maintaining
18 signs pursuant to this section.
