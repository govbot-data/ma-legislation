An Act to support men's mental health.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. (1) As used in this section and the following sections the following terms
2 shall have the following meanings:
3 “MassMen Program”, the program tailored to male suicide prevention and mental health
4 needs as developed by the Division of Violence and Injury Prevention, within the Bureau of
5 Community Health and Prevention within the Department of Public Health within the Executive
6 Office of Health and Human Services.
7 “Circumstances that increase suicide risk”, as defined by the United States Centers for
8 Disease Control and Prevention, history of depression and other mental illnesses, serious illness
9 such as chronic pain, criminal or legal problems, job or financial problems or loss, impulsive or
10 aggressive tendencies, substance use, current or prior history of adverse childhood experiences,
11 sense of hopelessness, violence victimization or perpetration, bullying, family or loved one's
12 history of suicide, loss of relationships, high conflict or violent relationships, social isolation,
1 of 3

13 community risk factors, lack of access to healthcare, suicide cluster in the community, stress of
14 acculturation, community violence, historical trauma, discrimination, stigma associated with
15 help-seeking and mental illness, easy access to lethal means of suicide among people at risk, and
16 unsafe media portrayals of suicide.
17 SECTION 2. (1) Massachusetts agencies with direct online or in-person constituent
18 service interfaces that serve men known or likely to have a higher propensity to develop mental
19 health conditions or suicidal ideation, as indicated by the circumstances that increase suicide
20 risk, shall within 9 months of the passage of this act, (a) clearly display MassMen resources on
21 online and material constituent service interfaces in a way that is most accessible to the public
22 including publishing the MassMen Program website for use by constituents and (b) communicate
23 best practices derived from MassMen resources to staff members and relevant community
24 partners. The agencies tasked with formulating such a plan will include, but not be limited to: all
25 agencies supporting those with a history of depression or mental illness such as all facilities run
26 by the department of mental health; all agencies supporting those who are victimized by violence
27 or perpetrate violence such as the Massachusetts Parole Board within the Executive Office of
28 Public Safety and Security, the Victim and Witness Assistance Board within the Massachusetts
29 Office for Victim Assistance and the Massachusetts Department of Children and Families (DCF)
30 within the Executive Office of Health and Human Services; all agencies supporting those with a
31 history of or likelihood to engage in substance use including the Bureau of Substance Addiction
32 Services within the Department of Public Health, as overseen by the Executive Office of Health
33 and Human Services; all agencies supporting those with likely histories of occupational trauma
34 including programs within the Executive Office of Veterans Affairs; and all agencies supporting
35 those experiencing job loss or financial insecurity including the Department of Unemployment
2 of 3

36 Assistance within the Executive Office of Labor and Workforce Development and the
37 Department of Transitional Assistance within the Executive Office of Health and Human
38 Services.
39 2) The Department of Public Health shall have the authority to, within three months of
40 the passage of this act, promulgate resources as to how to best incorporate MassMen resources
41 into other agencies as outlined above.
42 SECTION 3. (1) Subject to appropriation, the Executive Office of Health and Human
43 Services in consultation with the Department of Public Health shall conduct a publicity
44 campaign to promote the materials and resources of the MassMen program. These funds can be
45 used for facilitating the connections described above or for a broader publicity campaign.
