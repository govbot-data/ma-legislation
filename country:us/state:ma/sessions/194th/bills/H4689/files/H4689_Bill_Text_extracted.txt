An Act establishing buffer zones for battery storage facilities.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 21A of the General Laws, as appearing in the most recent edition,
2 is hereby amended by adding the following new section:
3 Battery Energy Storage Facility Buffer Zones.
4 (a) Definitions. As used in this section, the following words shall have the following
5 meanings:
6 "Battery storage facility"-a facility that uses batteries to store and distribute electrical
7 energy.
8 "Buffer zone"- a designated distance from certain areas within which battery energy
9 storage facilities shall not be constructed or operated.
10 "Ecologically protected area"- any land designated under state or federal law as a
11 protected environmental zone, including wetlands, wildlife reserves, and conservation areas.
1 of 3

12 "Populated area"- any residentially zoned area, school, hospital, or other location with
13 high human occupancy.
14 (b) Buffer Zone Requirements- (1) No battery storage facility shall be constructed or
15 operated within 2000 feet of a populated area. (2) No battery storage facility shall be constructed
16 or operated within 3,000 feet of an ecologically protected area. (3) The Department of
17 Environmental Protection, in consultation with the Department of Fire Services, may adjust
18 buffer distances based on facility size, battery chemistry, and risk assessment.
19 (c) Fire Mitigation and Emergency Preparedness- (1) All potential battery storage
20 facilities shall submit a fire mitigation and emergency response plan to the Department of Fire
21 Services and the local fire department before starting building. (2) Facilities utilizing lithium-ion
22 or similarly volatile battery chemistries shall install fire suppression systems in accordance with
23 regulations set forth by the Department of Fire Services.
24 (d) Environmental Protection Measures- (1) Facilities shall implement groundwater and
25 soil contamination prevention measures, including secondary containment and spill response
26 plans. (2) The Department of Environmental Protection shall establish guidelines for monitoring
27 and mitigating environmental risks posed by battery storage facilities.
28 (e) Enforcement and Penalties- (1) The Department of Environmental Protection shall
29 have the authority to enforce this section and may impose fines of up to $50,000 per violation for
30 noncompliance. (2) Any facility found to be in violation of this section shall be subject to
31 suspension of operating permits until compliance is achieved.
32 SECTION 2. Chapter 148 of the General Laws, as appearing in the most recent edition, is
33 hereby amended by adding the following new section:
2 of 3

34 Battery Storage Facility Fire Safety Standards.
35 The State Fire Marshal, in coordination with the Department of Environmental
36 Protection, shall establish safety and fire prevention standards specific to battery energy storage
37 facilities, including but not limited to: fire suppression and containment requirements,
38 emergency response protocols, and mandatory inspections and risk assessments at regular
39 intervals.
40 SECTION 3. This act shall take effect immediately upon passage.
