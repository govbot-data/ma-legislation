HOUSE . . . . . . . . No. 5203
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
________________________________________
HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, March 12, 2026.
The committee on Revenue, to whom were referred the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3005) of James Arciero and Peter
Barbella relative to local taxes based on personal or business income instead
of local real property taxes; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
3011) of James C. Arena-DeRosa relative to delinquent municipal tax
interest rates; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3088) of
Michelle M. DuBois relative to creating a Chapter 62F calculations study
group of fiscal harms caused by excluding new local tax revenue from
"allowable state tax revenue" calculations and other such calculations; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3139) of Steven S. Howitt
relative to transparency in property deed requests; the petition
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 3141) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., Richard
M. Haggerty and others relative to the small commercial tax exemption; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3158) of Sally P. Kerans for
legislation to authorize an increase in the local sales tax upon the sale of
restaurant meals; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3211) of
Edward R. Philips and Dennis C. Gallagher relative to deeds excise receipts;
the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3215) of Orlando Ramos
relative to the withholding of property taxes by utility companies; the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3229) of John H. Rogers relative
to community preservation surcharges; the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 3239) of Adam J. Scanlon relative to municipal property tax
reform; the petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3244) of Michael J.
Soter and others relative to the amount and valuation for eligibility for a tax
exemption on commercial property; and the petition (accompanied by bill,
House, No. 4239) of Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others relative to a municipal
tax amnesty program, reports recommending that the accompanying order
(House, No. 5203) ought to be adopted.
For the committee,
ADRIAN C. MADARO.
