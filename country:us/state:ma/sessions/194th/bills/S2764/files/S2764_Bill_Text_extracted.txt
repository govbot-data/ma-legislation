5 SECTION 2. Chapter 23B of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the
6 following section:-
7 Section 37. (a) There shall be a reentry and formerly incarcerated persons program
8 administered by the executive office of housing and livable communities. The executive office
9 shall be the central coordinating agency for the program. The executive office, in coordination
10 with the department of correction, the office of probation, and the parole board shall help
11 incarcerated people: (i) understand housing options before and after their release; (ii) find
12 pathways to both short-term and permanent housing; and (iii) receive financial supports, such as
13 housing vouchers, from the commonwealth. For the purposes of this section the term
1 of 8

14 “understand housing options” shall include: (i) an ongoing effort by the executive office to
15 evaluate and ensure such understanding; (ii) the executive office’s establishment of tools for
16 instruction of such understanding; and (iii) evaluating outcomes in the understanding of housing
17 options for incarcerated people. Such tools and evaluations shall include oral and written surveys
18 and setting guidelines and goals for measurable success based on the frame of reference of a
19 formerly incarcerated person.
20 The executive office shall partner with established community-based organizations with a
21 record of working with reentry of incarcerated and formerly incarcerated persons to assist with
22 positive outcomes and impacts. Such community-based organizations should prioritize including
23 affected populations, such as formerly incarcerated persons and their families, in the leadership
24 of their organization.
25 The executive office, in coordination with other relevant state agencies, shall provide for
26 housing, supportive programs, and oversight of housing formerly incarcerated persons in any
27 housing receiving funds administered by any state agency or regulated by the executive office.
28 The executive office shall develop and implement outcome-based measurements for the success
29 of such housing, supportive programs, and oversight.
30 (b) For any funds administered by the executive office, any state agency, or any state
31 authority for the purposes of providing for affordable housing, the executive office shall review
32 the implementation of the program and the expenditure of funds, including tax expenditures, for
33 the program no less than annually. The executive office review shall include, but not be limited
34 to:
35 (i) The number and demographic data of persons served by the program;
2 of 8

36 (ii) The compliance of each beneficiary of state funds for the purposes of the program
37 and the state funds received regarding the preferences for persons served by the program; and
38 (iii) Any opportunities to improve the effectiveness of the program in meeting its
39 purposes, intent or goals based on feedback and data gathered from formerly incarcerated
40 persons, persons currently experiencing housing instability related to former incarceration, and
41 community-based organizations, staff participants, and partner agencies that serve such persons.
42 (c) Annually, the executive office shall submit a report of its review of the program,
43 including but not limited to, the program’s impacts and outcomes to the joint committee on
44 housing and the joint committee on community development and small businesses and the clerks
45 of the house of representatives and the senate.
