An Act authorizing amending, updating, and aligning the Commonwealth’s definition of veteran.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 7, 43rd clause of Chapter 4 of the General Laws, is hereby amended
2 by striking the 43rd clause in its entirety and inserting in its place the following:
3 Forty-third, ''Veteran'' shall mean (1) a person who served on active duty in the
4 Uniformed Services or on full time national guard or reserve duty, under Titles 10 or 32 of the
5 United States Code or under sections 38, 40 and 41 of chapter 33, and (2) who was discharged or
6 released therefrom under conditions other than dishonorable; (3) a member of the American
7 Merchant Marine who served in armed conflict between December 7, 1941 and December 31,
8 1946, and who was discharged or released therefrom under conditions other than dishonorable;
9 (4) a member of the reserve component who has served s minimum of six years as an active
10 drilling member of the reserve component; or (5) any person who served in the Uniformed
11 Services, and was awarded a service-connected disability by the U.S. Veterans Administration or
12 who died in such service under conditions other than dishonorable shall be deemed a veteran.
1 of 2

13 “Active Duty” shall include (1) full time service; and (2) any period of active or inactive
14 duty for training during which the individual was disabled or died.
15 “Armed Forces” shall include army, marine corps, navy, air force, space force, coast
16 guard and any additional military service created by the U.S. Department of Defense including
17 reserve components thereof.
18 “Uniformed Services” shall include armed forces, commissioned corps of the public
19 health service and commissioned corps of the national oceanic and atmospheric administration.
20 None of the following shall be deemed to be a ''veteran'':
21 (a) Any person who at the time of entering into the armed forces of the United States had
22 declared his intention to become a subject or citizen of the United States and withdrew his
23 intention under the provisions of the act of Congress approved July ninth, nineteen hundred and
24 eighteen.
25 (b) Any person who was discharged from the said armed forces on his own application or
26 solicitation by reason of his being an enemy alien.
27 (c) Any person who has been proved guilty of willful desertion.
28 (d) Any person whose only service in the armed forces of the United States consists of his
29 service as a member of the coast guard auxiliary or as a temporary member of the coast guard
30 reserve, or both.
31 (e) Any person whose last discharge or release from the armed forces is dishonorable.
