Massachusetts-Ireland Trade Commission 2025 Annual Report
Section 42 of Chapter 140 of the Acts of 2024 (“Authorizing Statute”) established the
Massachusetts-Ireland Trade Commission (“Commission”) within the Massachusetts Office of
International Trade and Investment (“MOITI”).
Pursuant to Section 13V of Chapter 23A of the General Laws, as inserted by the
Authorizing Statute, the Commission’s mission is to "evaluate the economic interests of, and
review ways to strengthen the relationship between, the commonwealth and the Republic of
Ireland. The Commission shall study, research, analyze and make recommendations related to:
(i) bilateral trade and investments between the commonwealth and the Republic of Ireland; (ii)
policy issues of interest to the commonwealth; (iii) business and academic exchanges between
the commonwealth and the Republic of Ireland; (iv) mutual economic support; (v) investments
in the infrastructure of the commonwealth and Republic of Ireland; (vi) the creation of
economic and educational opportunities for aid to communities in the commonwealth and the
Republic of Ireland; and (vii) other related issues as the commission deems necessary.” See
Appendix A.1 for full authorizing language.
The Commission is required by the Authorizing Statute to annually submit a report that
describes its findings, together with recommendations for any legislation, to the Governor, the
Clerks of the House of Representatives and the Senate and the Chairs of the Joint Committee
on Economic Development and Emerging Technologies.
The Commission consists of 15 members including legislators, gubernatorial
appointees, and representation from the Executive Office of Economic Development. See
Appendix A.2 for full Commission membership.

Activities Taken to Support the Massachusetts-Ireland Trade Commission:
Research:
• MOITI conducted research and discovered that Ireland was Massachusetts’ 6th largest
trading partner with over $3.3 billion worth of goods exchanged in 2024.
o In 2024, top export products from Massachusetts to Ireland included: (i)
pharmaceutical products; (ii) medical/surgical equipment; and (iii) industrial
machinery – totaling over $889 million.
o In 2024, top import products from Ireland to Massachusetts included: (i)
medical/surgical equipment; (ii) industrial machinery; and (iii) special
classification provisions – totaling over $2.4 billion.
See Appendix B: Massachusetts-Ireland Trade Relationship
Inaugural Meeting of the Massachusetts-Ireland Trade Commission:
• June 2025 -- MOITI Executive Director Jeevan Ramapriya chaired the Inaugural Meeting
of the Massachusetts-Ireland Trade Commission. After introductory remarks by
Commission Members, invited dignitaries, including Consul General of Ireland to New
England Síghle Fitzgerald, The Honorable Neale Richmond, Minister of State at the
Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade, and former U.S Ambassador to Ireland Claire
Page 2 of 11

Cronin shared their perspectives on the deep cultural, diplomatic, and commercial ties
between Massachusetts and Ireland.
o Representative John Lawn highlighted the growth of the Massachusetts Irish
Legislative Caucus and its international collaborations, while UMass President
Marty Meehan underscored academic partnerships with Irish universities and
workforce development initiatives, particularly in MEDtech.
o Irish Minister Neale Richmond and former U.S. Ambassador Claire Cronin,
reaffirmed the importance of fair trade, Ireland’s economic evolution, and the
strength of Ireland-Massachusetts ties, noting that Ireland is Massachusetts’ sixth-
largest trading partner.
o Key takeaways centered on expanding trade in pharmaceuticals and technology,
strengthening cultural exchanges, and advancing education and workforce
development initiatives.

Diplomatic Engagement:
• March 2025 -- Governor Healey, former Undersecretary Sarah Stanton, and MOITI
Executive Director Ramapriya met with the Minister of Enterprise and Trade, The
Hon. Peter Burke, and Irish Consul General to New England Sighle FitzGerald, to
discuss historic ties between the Commonwealth and the Republic of Ireland, both
cultural and economic. Also discussed were upcoming events such as the
Massachusetts Irish-Trade Commission Inaugural meeting in June alongside the 250th
Celebration of the American Revolution.

International Business Development:
• February 2025 --- MOITI met with Protex.AI, a Dublin-based artificial intelligence
company specializing in leveraging AI for workplace security. Protex.AI established
their US Headquarters in Boston, MA.
• June 2025 --- MOITI met with EclimAI, an Irish AI-driven energy efficiency company
focused primarily on applications in cleantech. Their systems are designed to reduce
waste and support global sustainability.
• July 2025 --- MOITI Executive Director Ramapriya meet with Professor William
Scanlon, CEO of the Cork-based Tyndall National Institute at a luncheon hosted by
UMass Lowell's Chancellor Julie Chen and the Senior Executive Director of the Rist
Institute for Sustainability and Energy and a Commissioner of the Massachusetts-Ireland
Trade Commission, Ruairi O'Mahony.
