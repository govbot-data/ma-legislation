Dear Mr. Larkin and Mr. McCarthy,
I am submitting a Tech Fund request to support the storage and relocation of our microfilm archive. We
currently have approximately 1,235 microfilm reels stored at Iron Mountain through Avenu Insights &
Analytics, now called Neumo. We propose to move this collection to the New England Archives Center
(NEAC) in Holyoke, MA, which offers more cost-effective and accessible archival storage.
Relocation from Iron Mountain to NEAC:
Based on the attached quote from Neumo, the total estimated cost to retrieve and ship the microfilm is
$6,270.00 plus approximately $300 for shipping.
Estimated 3-Year Storage Costs (from NEAC):
Open Shelving: 56 units @ $45/year = $7,560 for 3 years
We are requesting funding to cover both the retrieval and approximate three years of storage costs for a
total of $14,130. The transition to NEAC will improve access, reduce long-term costs, and ensure the
preservation of these records.
Please feel free to contact me if you have additional questions. For your reference, our current Tech
Fund balance as of September 1, 2025 (prior to this request) is approximately $205,264.46.
Thank you for your attention to this request.
Sincerely,
Paulo C DeOliveira, Register

Vault Description
Our vault was designed and manufactured by FIRELOCK, a division of Banc Technic, Inc. The
FIRELOCK Modular Vault is constructed of lightweight insulation panels that are far superior to concrete
or block vaults. The vault offers Class 125-2 hour magnetic media and Class 150-2 hour micrographic
media ratings in addition to the Class 350-4 hour paper rating. Our vault meets or exceeds the temperature
and hourly limits detailed within NFPA 75 “Protection of Electronic Computer/Data Processing
Equipment.”
The vault’s environment is strictly controlled. The temperature and humidity levels are maintained at
optimum levels for optical, microform, and magnetic media. Monitors record the environment’s
temperature and humidity levels. The records are maintained for customer inspection.
The vault is located in our record storage facility. Our facility is protected by state of the art intrusion and
fire protection systems. The systems are continuously monitored by a professional monitoring service.
Separate smoke and heat alarms and a FM-200 Gas Fire Suppression System also protect the vault.
Access to the vault is restricted to authorized NEAC employees that are assigned magnetically coded key
cards. These cards are part of a system that controls the lock on the door to the vault lobby area and
records the identities of the employees and the time they enter the vault area. The access records are kept.
You can be assured that your items are handled in a secure and confidential manner. We perform
background checks on our employees as part of their review and screening process. Our employees also
are required to sign a non-disclosure agreement as a condition of their employment. In addition, we carry
liability insurance to further protect our customers’ privacy.
We can schedule one of our drivers and vehicles to transport items for storage to our facility. Items can
also be shipped to us via common carrier, UPS, or U.S. mail. We will supply you with inventory forms
and labels at no charge.
Items received for storage are entered into our computer tracking system. Each container is assigned a
unique identification number and storage location. You can access your inventory of items in storage at
our secure website. You will be assigned a user name and password that allows you to view a read only
table of your items in storage. Users can search for boxes, place requests for service, initiate record
destruction, order supplies, or print inventory reports. In addition, usage reports and a spreadsheet of your
inventory can be provided with the monthly service invoices.
Requests can also be placed by calling our toll-free number, by fax, or by e-mail. Requests received by
2PM are usually pulled and prepared for delivery the same day. Requests can be delivered by our drivers
or third party service.
New England Archives Center is a member of Prism International, a professional records and information
services management trade association. New England Archives Center and Prism International are
dedicated to a philosophy of professionalism to our clients, fellow members, and the public. We strive to
provide our customers with timely, dependable storage services at competitive prices.
