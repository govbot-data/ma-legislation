An Act relative to nature as a prescription.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 32A of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2020 Official
2 Edition, is hereby amended by adding the following section:-
3 Section 33. The commission shall provide to any active or retired employee of the
4 commonwealth who is insured under the group insurance commission coverage for accessing
5 nature as a medical treatment when prescribed pursuant to section 42E of chapter 112, as mental
6 health services when prescribed pursuant to section 42F of said chapter 112 and as social
7 rehabilitation services when referred pursuant to section 42G of said chapter 112.
8 SECTION 2. Chapter 112 of the General Laws, as so appearing, is hereby amended by
9 inserting after section 42D the following 3 sections:- Section 42E. Any person authorized to
10 prescribe or dispense of controlled substances pursuant to section 9 of chapter 94C may, when
11 acting in accordance with any provision of this chapter and in good faith and in the course of a
12 professional practice for the alleviation of pain and suffering or for the treatment or alleviation of
1 of 5

13 disease, prescribe outdoor time for accessing nature as may reasonably be required for the
14 purpose of patient treatment.
15 Section 42F. Any persons licensed in the Commonwealth to provide counseling or other
16 mental health services pursuant to this chapter may, when acting in accordance with any
17 provision of this chapter and in good faith and in the course of a professional practice for the
18 treatment or alleviation of mental health symptoms, prescribe outdoor time for accessing nature
19 as may reasonably be required for the purpose of client treatment.
20 Section 42G. Any persons licensed to provide social services to individuals experiencing
21 social or emotional trauma including, but not limited to, job loss, divorce, food insecurity,
22 immigration or probation may, when acting in good faith and in the course of a professional
23 practice to meet the needs of community members, refer clients to outdoor time for accessing
24 nature as may reasonably be beneficial to their social rehabilitation.
25 SECTION 3. Chapter 118E of the General Laws, as so appearing, is hereby amended by
26 inserting after section 10Q the following section:-
27 Section 10R. The division and its contracted health insurers, health plans, health
28 maintenance organizations, behavioral health management firms and third-party administrators
29 under contract to a Medicaid managed care organization or primary care clinician plan shall
30 provide coverage for accessing nature as a medical treatment when prescribed pursuant to
31 section 42E of chapter 112, as mental health services when prescribed pursuant to section 42F of
32 said chapter 112 and as social rehabilitation services when referred pursuant to section 42G of
33 said chapter 112.

74 SECTION 9. (a) Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, the
75 commissioner of the department of public health and the commissioner of the department of
76 conservation and recreation shall jointly review existing programs and budgets to identify
77 opportunities for collaboration on developing and implementing outdoor environmental,

78 ecological, agricultural and other natural resource or outdoor-based therapy programs. This
79 review shall include evaluation of the equitable access to existing natural resource areas and
80 strategies for ensuring that black, indigenous and people of color, environmental justice and
81 other underrepresented community members feel safe and welcome when visiting these natural
82 resource areas.
