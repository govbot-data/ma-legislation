SENATE, November 17, 2025.
The committee on Education, to whom was referred the petitions (accompanied by bill,
Senate, No. 326) of John J. Cronin and John F. Keenan for legislation to expand student
opportunities in career technical education; (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 329) of John J.
Cronin for legislation to construct vocational education annexes in gateway cities; (accompanied
by bill, Senate, No. 330) of John J. Cronin and Vanna Howard for legislation to ensure that all
students have an equal chance of being admitted to vocational programs; (accompanied by bill,
Senate, No. 332) of John J. Cronin for legislation to expand student access to a vocational-
technical education; (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 358) of Paul R. Feeney, Steven George
Xiarhos, James B. Eldridge, Michael D. Brady and other members of the General Court for
legislation to improve access, opportunity, and capacity in Massachusetts vocational-technical
education; and (accompanied by resolve, Senate, No. 384) of Edward J. Kennedy that provisions
be made for an investigation and study by a special commission (including members of the
General Court) to establish a commission to examine expanding vocational and technical high
school access, report the accompanying bill (Senate, No. 2690).
For the committee,
Jason M. Lewis

An Act to improve access, opportunity, and capacity in Massachusetts vocational-technical
education.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 69 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition,
2 is hereby amended by adding the following sections:-
3 Section 38. For the purposes of sections 38 and 39, inclusive, the following terms shall
4 have the following meanings, unless the context clearly requires otherwise:-
5 “District of residence”, shall mean the school district of the city or town where a student
6 resides.
7 “School of residence”, shall mean the middle school or junior high school a student
8 attends within their district of residence.
9 Section 39. (a) The department of elementary and secondary education shall develop and
10 implement policies and promulgate regulations to promote, enhance, and expand vocational-
11 technical education programs, as defined in Chapter 74 of the General Laws, in the
12 commonwealth.
1 of 6

13 (b) The department shall:
14 (i) oversee all Chapter 74 approved programs and ensure compliance with M.G.L.
15 Chapter 74 and related regulations;
16 (ii) work to increase awareness of vocational-technical education and career opportunities
17 among students in elementary schools, junior high schools, and middle schools;
18 (iii) ensure that schools offering Chapter 74 programs are provided reasonable access
19 during the school day at schools of residence to meet with all students and distribute information
20 about vocational-technical education and careers to said students, including English language
21 learners, students with disabilities, students of color, and other student populations, to ensure that
22 such information is provided equitably to all learners;
23 (iv) ensure that schools offering Chapter 74 programs are given the opportunity during
24 the school day to host middle school tours, with transportation costs paid by the school hosting
25 the tour, for all middle school students in member communities, including English language
26 learners, students with disabilities, students of color, and other student populations to ensure that
27 such opportunities are provided equitably to all learners; and further ensure that the school of
28 residence may not count middle school student tours of vocational schools or programs during
29 the school day as unexcused absences if the vocational school or program confirms the student's
30 participation, and may not unreasonably withhold student access to tours of vocational schools
31 and programs during the school day.
32 (v) require that schools offering Chapter 74 programs are given the opportunity to
33 provide middle and junior high school students with information about vocational-technical
34 programs and careers through mail and email.
2 of 6

35 (vi) require all middle schools and junior high schools in member communities to
36 establish and implement a Chapter 74 Access Policy, in accordance with state requirements
37 promoting equitable access to Chapter 74 programs, outlining specific ways in which the middle
38 schools will collaborate with regional vocational-technical high schools and agricultural high
39 schools to: provide staff members from Chapter 74 schools with direct school day access to all
40 middle school students, to inform them about opportunities in vocational-technical and
41 agricultural education and to distribute materials about such opportunities to them; provide all
42 middle school students an opportunity to tour, during regular middle school hours, the regional
43 vocational-technical high school and/or county agricultural school of which the middle school’s
44 city or town is a member, with the transportation costs of all such tours being borne by the
45 school hosting the tours; provide contact information for all seventh grade and eighth-grade
46 middle school students, including a student’s name and mailing address, a student’s personal
47 email address, and the parent’s/guardian’s email address by October 15 of each school year;
48 (vii) establish, in addition to the minimum requirements outlined in the preceding
49 subsections, such additional requirements for Chapter 74 Access Policies as the office deems
50 reasonable and necessary to promote equitable access by all students to information about
51 vocational-technical and agricultural education;
52 (viii) require all sending school districts which are members of a regional-vocational
53 school district or whose community is located in the county or district of an agricultural high
54 school to submit the Chapter 74 Access Policy annually to the department; annually attest in
55 writing that the Chapter 74 Access Policy is being implemented equitably and that all students
56 are being provided with information, access, and tours in accordance with this section and with
3 of 6

57 federal and state civil rights laws, regulations, and policies; and post the Chapter 74 Access
58 Policy on its district website and provide written copies to students and parents, upon request;
59 (ix) create a mechanism to enforce timely implementation of Chapter 74 Access
60 Policies;
61 (x) establish a system to ensure that students who live in communities that are not
62 members of or affiliated with a regional vocational-technical high school district or agricultural
63 high school annually are provided with information about their high school options, including
64 their option to seek an education in a vocational-technical or agricultural high school;
65 (xi) provide technical support to schools seeking to offer Chapter 74 programs that meet
66 regional labor market demands and do not duplicate existing programs in the region;
67 (xii) support the continuation of state grant programs that provide funding for equipment
68 purchases and facility expansion; and
69 (xiii) support the continuation of demonstration programs that provide opportunities in
70 vocational-technical education for students unable to secure a seat in an approved Chapter 74
71 program due to lack of enrollment capacity.
