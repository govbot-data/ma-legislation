An Act protecting personal identifying information on records of death.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 46 of the General Laws is hereby amended by striking section 2A,
2 as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition, and inserting in place thereof the following section:-
3 (a) Examination of records and returns of fetal deaths or of copies of such records in the
4 department of public health, shall not be permitted except upon proper judicial order, or upon
5 request of a parent, guardian, or conservator, or a person whose official duties, in the opinion of
6 the town clerk or the commissioner of public health, as the case may be, entitle them to the
7 information contained therein, nor shall certified copies thereof be furnished except upon such
8 order, or the request of such person. The provisions of this section shall not apply to such
9 records, returns or notices recorded or filed prior to January 1st, 1841 or to such copies thereof.
10 (b) It shall be unlawful for any person to permit inspection of, or to disclose information
11 contained in, certificates of death or to copy or issue a copy of all or part of any such certificate
12 except as authorized by this section and any accompanying regulations or by order of a court of
1 of 4

13 competent jurisdiction. The provisions of subsections (b) through (i), inclusive, shall only apply
14 to certificates of death.
15 (c) The state registrar or town clerk, upon receipt of a written application, shall issue:
16 (i) A certified copy of a certificate of death to:
17 (1) The decedent’s spouse, parent, child, sibling, grandparent, grandchild, next of kin or
18 legal guardian at the time of death;
19 (2) A legal representative for the decedent or any of the parties listed in the preceding
20 subclause;
21 (3) A court of competent jurisdiction upon its order or subpoena;
22 (4) Any governmental agency or entity, provided that such copy shall be needed for
23 official purposes;
24 (5) An employee or agent of a funeral home or person acting as a funeral service
25 practitioner who is named in the certificate of death;
26 (6) A person who demonstrates to the satisfaction of the state registrar or custodian that
27 the certified copy is necessary for a determination related to or the protection of a personal or
28 property right of the applicant;
29 (7) Any applicant whom the state registrar or custodian of the certificate of death deems
30 having a direct and tangible interest in the certificate; and
31 (ii) A noncertified informational copy of a certificate of death in that registrar’s or
32 custodian’s custody or abstract thereof to any applicant, provided that the informational copy
2 of 4

33 shall have the decedent’s social security number and cause of death redacted. A noncertified
34 informational copy may not comply with the security features pursuant to sections 16, 19C and
35 33 and shall not be used for legal purposes; provided, however, that the registrar shall
36 promulgate regulations or guidelines related to permissible uses of a noncertified informational
37 copy of a certificate of death. Said noncertified informational copy shall contain a clear and
38 conspicuous watermark stating the copy cannot be used for legal purposes.
39 (d) Each certified copy issued shall show the date of registration; provided, that
40 duplicates issued from records marked “delayed” or “amended” shall be similarly marked,
41 including the effective date. The documentary evidence used to establish a delayed certificate of
42 birth shall be shown on all duplicates issued. All forms and procedures used in the issuance of
43 certified copies of vital records in the commonwealth shall be provided or approved by the state
44 registrar.
45 (e) The state registrar may transmit duplicates of certificates of death and other reports
46 required by this section to offices of vital records outside of this state when such certificates or
47 other reports relate to residents of those jurisdictions or persons born in those jurisdictions. The
48 state registrar shall require that the duplicates be used for statistical and administrative purposes
49 only and shall establish terms for the retention and disposition of such duplicates. Duplicates
50 received by the department from offices of vital statistics in other states shall be handled in the
51 same manner as prescribed in this section.
52 (f) No person shall prepare or issue any certificate which purports to be an original,
53 certified copy or duplicate of a certificate of death except as authorized in this section or
54 accompanying regulations.
3 of 4

55 (g) No duplicates or parts thereof of a certificate of death shall be reproduced or
56 information copied for commercial or speculative purposes. This subsection shall not apply to
57 published results of research.
58 (h) The provisions outlined in this section shall not apply to fetal deaths and those
59 provisions outlined in section 202 of chapter 111.
60 (i) The state registrar shall promulgate regulations or guidelines for the implementation of
61 this section, including, but not limited to authorization of the disclosure of information contained
62 in certificates of death for research purposes.
63 SECTION 2. Subsection (h) of section 13 of said chapter 46 is hereby amended by
64 striking out, in lines 174 to 183, inclusive, the words “not be restricted, except for records or
65 other items of documentary evidence submitted in support of the affidavit which are considered
66 medical records for purposes of paragraph (c) of clause twenty-sixth of section 7 of chapter 4 are
67 restricted by section 2A of this chapter, or are restricted by judicial order” and inserting in place
68 thereof the following:- “be restricted pursuant to section 2A of this chapter.”
