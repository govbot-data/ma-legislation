Dear Clerks of the Massachusetts Senate and House of Representatives:
The Embodied Carbon Intergovernmental Coordinating Council (Council), established pursuant
to "An Act promoting a clean energy grid, advancing equity and protecting ratepayers," St. 2024,
c. 239 ("Act"), convened in March of 2025 and has been meeting on a monthly basis since then.
Attached here is a list of the appointed members and their professional affiliations.
Section 4(c) of the Act requires the Council, by January I, 2026, to prepare an embodied carbon
reduction plan, including specific recommendations for submission to the Legislature. See
Section 4(f). To that end, the Council has been reviewing "data, input and advice," from experts
and stakeholders, and held the first two of three required public hearings, see Section 4(d ), on
July 8, in Lexington; the second was held on September 29, in Amherst; and the final will be
held in late October, location to be determined.
Section 115 of the Act requires the Council to submit an initial embodied carbon reduction plan
by July 31, 2025, pursuant to M.G.L. c. 7C §73. As set forth below, the Council provides this
interim report on the work completed to date and an initial summary of the embodied carbon

reduction plan currently under development. I note that Section 4(c) requires our consideration
of availability and cost ofmaterials. See Section 4(c ) .
• The Council meets on a monthly basis; co-chairs and their staff have organized several
presentations from external experts and from within the Council on topics including
Environmental Product Declarations (EPDs), Buy Clean laws across the country,
procurement procedures within DCAMM, MassDOT, and OSD, building and stretch
code creation, and recommendations from the concrete industry.
• In March, DCAMM published a webpage to publicize the formation of the Council. This
webpage includes information on the Council, its membership and mission, and
announcements; it also hosts publicly available documents and recordings. Embodied

• Three subcommittees were formed that meet regularly: (1) Procurement, Procedure,
Deconstruction & Reuse; (2) EPDs and GWPs; and (3) Technology, Market Incentives,
and Building Codes.
• The Council has begun drafting the embodied carbon reduction plan and
recommendations, with a goal of completing a first draft by late October. The Council is
on track to submit these recommendations to the Clerks' offices before the end of the
calendar year. The current outline includes the following:
• Recommendations for requiring EPDs for eligible products and materials;
• Suggestion for initial GWP limits and a process for reviewing and
modifying limits over time;
• Proposal for DCAMM to require Whole Building Life Cycle Assessment
for projects exceeding a certain size;
• Proposal for consideration of the BBRS to include embodied carbon in
code.
The Council respectfully looks forward to submitting its final and complete embodied carbon
reduction plan and recommendations on or before January I, 2026. Please do not hesitate to
contact me should you have any questions or concerns. Thank you for your consideration.
